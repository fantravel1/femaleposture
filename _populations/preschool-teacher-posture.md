# Preschool Teacher Posture

Preschool teaching involves constant engagement with very young children at their physical level. Female preschool teachers spend significant time on the floor, bending, lifting, and moving quickly to keep up with active toddlers and preschoolers. The physical demands are substantial and deserve attention.

## Young Child Demands

### Floor Time
Extensive floor-level activities.

### Constant Bending
Getting to child height.

### Lifting
Picking up children and equipment.

### Active Movement
Keeping up with energetic children.

## Physical Activities

### Circle Time
Floor sitting for group activities.

### Active Play
Outdoor and movement activities.

### Art Activities
Hands-on project work.

### Care Routines
Diapering, toileting, and mealtime.

## Common Physical Strains

### Lower Back
Bending and lifting.

### Knees
Floor work and getting up and down.

### Shoulders
Lifting children.

### Voice
Projection and constant talking.

## Floor Work Strategies

### Squatting vs. Bending
Using legs not back.

### Position Variety
Changing floor positions.

### Adult Seating
Using low chairs when possible.

### Rising Technique
Safe way to stand from floor.

## Lifting Children

### Proper Technique
Lifting with legs.

### Child Cooperation
Encouraging climbing help.

### Limit Awareness
Knowing when not to lift.

### Alternative Strategies
Getting to child level instead.

### Furniture Height
Age-appropriate but teacher-considerate.

### Supply Organization
Accessible arrangement.

### Movement Flow
Room layout for efficiency.

### Adult Space
