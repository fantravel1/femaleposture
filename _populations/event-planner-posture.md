# Event Planner and Coordinator Posture

> **Planning Is Desk Work; Events Are Marathons:** Event planning means computer-intensive office work punctuated by extremely physical event days. Both demand attention.

---

## The Event Planner Challenge

### Dual Physical Demands

**Office work:**
- Computer-intensive planning
- Long seated sessions
- Phone and email heavy
- Detail-focused work

**Event days:**
- On feet for 10-16+ hours
- Walking constantly
- Lifting and arranging
- Physical problem-solving
- High stress

### Common Problems

- Lower back pain (from both sitting and standing)
- Foot and leg fatigue
- Shoulder tension
- Stress-related body patterns
- Boom and bust physical cycles

---

## Office Planning Work

### Desk Ergonomics

**Standard setup:**
- Proper monitor height
- Keyboard at elbow level
- Supportive chair
- See [Desk Work Posture](/activities/desk-work/)

### Long Planning Sessions

**During intense phases:**
- Regular breaks still essential
- Movement between tasks
- Not marathoning at desk
- Variety when possible

---

## Event Day Survival

### Extended Standing

**10+ hours on feet:**
- Best possible footwear
- Compression socks
- Brief sitting when possible
- Weight shifting constantly

### Moving Constantly

**Walking venue:**
- Comfortable, supportive shoes
- Pace yourself when possible
- Know the venue layout
- Efficient movement

### Lifting and Moving

**Setup tasks:**
- Proper lifting technique
- Get help for heavy items
- Don't be the hero
- Protect your back

---

## Footwear Critical

### Event Day Shoes

**Requirements:**
- Supportive
- Comfortable for extended wear
- Appropriate for venue
- Broken in before event

**Consider:**
- Multiple pairs for different phases
- Insoles for additional support
- Know when fashion must yield to function

### Recovery

**After events:**
- Foot soak
- Elevation
- Massage
- Recovery time

---

## Managing Event-Day Stress

### Physical Stress Response

**High-pressure events:**
- Shoulder tension
- Jaw clenching
- Shallow breathing
- Adrenaline running

### Quick Resets

**When possible:**
- Deep breath
- Shoulder drops
- Brief stillness
- Body awareness check

---

## Pre-Event Preparation

### Day Before

**Physical prep:**
- Rest
- Hydrate well
- Stretch
- Pack appropriate shoes/clothes

### Morning Of

**Setup for success:**
- Good breakfast
- Comfortable clothes
- Supportive shoes on
- Stretch before heading out

---

## During Events

### Micro-Breaks

**When possible:**
- Brief sitting moments
- Deep breaths
- Water and food
- Body check-ins

### Managing Demands

**Prioritize:**
- Essential movement first
- Delegate when possible
- Know your limits
- Not everything requires running

---

## Post-Event Recovery

### Same Day

**After event:**
- Gentle movement (don't just collapse)
- Stretching
- Hydration
- Appropriate rest

### Next Day

**Recovery priorities:**
- Gentle activity
- Stretching
- Address any pain
- Not back to sitting immediately

---

## Boom and Bust Pattern

### The Problem

**Event work pattern:**
- Intense event days
- Followed by desk-heavy recovery
- Physical whiplash
- Hard on the body

### Smoothing It Out

**Strategies:**
- Regular exercise (builds resilience)
- Consistent stretching
- Movement on desk days too
- Preparation for event days

---

## Building Resilience

### Regular Exercise

**Supports both modes:**
- Stamina for events
- Strength for demands
- Recovery capacity
- Overall resilience

### Stretching Routine

**Consistent practice:**
- Lower back and hips
- Legs and feet
- Shoulders and neck
- Daily, not just post-event

---

## Client Meetings

### Varied Environments

**Site visits:**
- Walking venues
- Different seating
- Standing discussions
- Variable conditions

### Office Meetings

**Standard positioning:**
- Good meeting posture
- Comfortable seating
- Not marathon meetings
