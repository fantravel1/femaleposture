# Posture Guide for Students

> **Study Smart, Sit Smart:** Students spend long hours studying, often in less-than-ideal positions. Here's how to protect your body during your academic years.

---

## The Student Challenge

### Unique Issues

- Long study sessions
- Laptop on lap or bed
- Library and dorm furniture
- Heavy backpacks
- Stress-related tension
- Limited budget for equipment

---

## Study Posture Basics

### At a Desk

- Screen at eye level (use books to raise laptop)
- External keyboard if possible
- Chair supporting back
- Feet flat on floor
- Take breaks every 30-50 minutes

### On the Go

- Avoid studying in bed
- Use table, not lap
- Sit upright at library
- Change locations for movement

---

## Laptop Challenges

### The Problem

Laptops force:
- Looking down (screen too low)
- OR reaching up (keyboard too high)
- No good position

### Solutions

- External monitor or stand
- External keyboard
- Stack books to raise screen
- Use laptop at desk, not lap

---

## Backpack Guide

- Keep under 15% of body weight
- Use both straps
- Position at mid-back
- Use lockers when possible
- Go digital when you can

[See full backpack guide](/life-stages/teens/backpack-guide/)

---

## Dorm Room Tips

### Make It Work

- Desk lamp (reduce leaning)
- Chair cushion for support
- Raised laptop setup
- Resist bed studying

---

## Study Break Routine

**Every 45-60 minutes:**
1. Stand up
2. Walk briefly
3. Stretch neck and shoulders
4. Roll shoulders
5. Reset posture when returning

---

## Managing Stress

Exam stress affects posture:
- Shoulders rise
- Jaw clenches
- Shallow breathing

**Counter with:**
- Breathing exercises
- Shoulder rolls
- Regular movement
- Sleep priority
