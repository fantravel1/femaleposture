# Veterinarian and Vet Tech Posture

> **Caring for Animals, Protecting Yourself:** Veterinary work combines unpredictable patients with challenging physical demands. Lifting, restraining, and treating animals takes a toll on your body.

---

## Unique Challenges of Veterinary Work

### Physical Demands

- **Animal handling** - Unpredictable, varying sizes
- **Lifting** - Patients of all weights
- **Restraint** - Holding for procedures
- **Surgery** - Long periods in fixed positions
- **Bending** - Exam tables often too low
- **Floor work** - Large animal, fear-free handling

### Unpredictability Factor

- Animals move unexpectedly
- Bracing against resistance
- Sudden bursts of strength
- Can't fully plan each interaction

---

## Animal Handling

### Small Animals

**Lifting dogs:**
- Assess weight before lifting
- Get help for large dogs (>40-50 lbs)
- Keep animal close to body
- Squat, don't bend
- Use lifts/ramps when available

**Restraint:**
- Get comfortable position first
- Use minimal restraint needed
- Distribute effort across body
- Take breaks when possible
- Trade off with colleagues

**Floor handling (fear-free):**
- Kneel rather than bend
- Use cushioned knee pad
- Change positions frequently
- Support your back

### Large Animals

**Additional challenges:**
- Can't liftâ€”must work with animal
- More awkward positions
- Higher injury risk

**Safety principles:**
- Proper facilities and equipment
- Position yourself safely
- Escape routes planned
- Never get between animal and wall

## Examination and Treatment

### Exam Table Height

**The problem:**
- Most tables are too low
- Constant bending forward
- Neck and back strain

**Solutions:**
- Height-adjustable tables
- Hydraulic lift tables
- Step stools for tall staff
- Advocate for proper equipment

### During Exams

**Position:**
- Stand close to table
- Adjust table to your height if possible
- Core engaged
- Switch sides when possible

**Avoid:**
- Leaning over for extended periods
- Twisting to reach supplies
- Hunching over patients

### Treatment Procedures

**Blood draws, injections, etc.:**
- Comfortable position before starting
- Animal properly restrained
- Everything ready and accessible
- Adequate lighting

---

## Surgery

### Surgical Suite Ergonomics

**Table height:**
- Adjust for your height
- Elbows at comfortable angle
- Not reaching up or hunching down

**Positioning:**
- Stand squarely
- Both feet planted
- Don't lean to one side
- Core engaged

### Long Surgeries

**Managing fatigue:**
- Shift weight periodically
- Micro-movements when possible
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Supportive shoes

**Breaks:**
- Between procedures
- Stretch and move
- Don't schedule back-to-back long surgeries

### Loupes and Microscopes

**If you use them:**
- Proper declination angle
- Custom fit for your anatomy
- Reduce neck flexion
- See dental professional guidance (similar issues)

---

## Imaging

### X-Ray Positioning

**Challenges:**
- Holding animals in position
- Awkward angles
- Lead protection adds weight

**Technique:**
- Minimal manual restraint when possible
- Use positioning aids
- Sedation when appropriate
- PPE that fits properly

### Ultrasound

**Long exams:**
- Seated when possible
- Monitor at good height
- Arm supported
- Regular breaks

- Similar to human dentistry challenges

## Throughout the Day

### Pacing

**If possible:**
- Mix appointment types
- Alternate physical and less physical
- Build in recovery time

### Microbreaks

**Between appointments:**
- Stand and stretch
- Shoulder rolls
- Neck movement
- Back extensions

### Team Approach

- Share physically demanding work
- Help each other with large animals
- Communicate about fatigue
- Don't be a hero

---

## Stretches for Veterinary Professionals

### Essential Stretches

**Neck (constant looking down):**
- Chin tucks
- Neck tilts
- Rotation stretches

**Shoulders:**
- Doorway stretch
- Across body stretch
- Shoulder rolls

**Lower back:**
- Cat-cow
- Standing back extension
- Knee to chest

**Hips (from standing):**
- Hip flexor stretch
- Glute stretch
- Figure-4 stretch

---

## Strengthening

### Key Areas

**Core:**
- Essential for lifting
- Protects back
- Maintains posture

**Back:**
- Upper back for posture
- Lower back for lifting
- Rows, extensions

**Legs:**
- For lifting power
- Squats, lunges

---

## Equipment and Environment

### What to Advocate For

- Height-adjustable exam tables
- Lift tables for surgery
- Proper restraint equipment
- Anti-fatigue flooring
- Adequate staffing

### Personal Equipment

- Good shoes
- Knee pads
- Back support if needed
- Proper PPE that fits

---

## Career Longevity

### Prevention Focus

- Don't wait for injury
- Build good habits now
- Invest in fitness
- Take care of yourself like you take care of patients

### If Injured

- Report early
- Get proper treatment
- Modified duty if needed
- Don't push through
