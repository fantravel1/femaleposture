# Project Manager Posture

Project management is meeting-intensive work that also requires significant desk time for planning, documentation, and communication. Female project managers move between conference rooms, team spaces, and their own desks throughout the day. This varied environment requires adaptable ergonomic awareness.

## Meeting-Heavy Days

### Extended Sitting
Back-to-back meetings mean hours of sitting.

### Various Venues
Conference rooms, team rooms, virtual meetings.

### Presentation Delivery
Standing to present project updates.

### Client Meetings
In-person and virtual client interactions.

## Desk Work Demands

### Project Planning
Creating and updating project plans.

### Documentation
Writing status reports, project documents.

### Email Volume
High volume of stakeholder communication.

### System Updates
Project management software and tracking.

## Varied Work Locations

### Conference Rooms
Different seating in different rooms.

### Team Spaces
Working near team members.

### Home Office
Remote work considerations.

### Client Sites
Visiting client locations.

## Meeting Posture

### Active Listening
Body language for engaged attention.

### Note-Taking Position
Comfortable position for capturing information.

### Facilitating
Standing or moving when leading meetings.

### Virtual Presence
Camera positioning for video meetings.

## Desk Setup

### Dual Monitors
Managing multiple documents and systems.

### Keyboard and Mouse
Proper positioning for extensive use.

### Chair Selection
Quality seating for desk work periods.

### Stand Option
Ability to alternate sitting and standing.
