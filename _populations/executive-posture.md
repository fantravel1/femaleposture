# Executive and Leadership Posture

> **Presence Is Physical:** Leadership presence begins with how you hold yourself. Good posture projects confidence while protecting your health during demanding days.

---

## The Executive Challenge

### Physical Demands

- **Back-to-back meetings** - Hours seated in discussion
- **High stress** - Responsibilities create tension
- **Travel** - Flights, cars, hotels
- **Long hours** - Extended workdays
- **Presentation demands** - Standing, projecting, engaging
- **Decision fatigue** - Mental exhaustion affects body

### What Posture Communicates

**Your posture says:**
- Confidence or uncertainty
- Engagement or disinterest
- Energy or fatigue
- Authority or deference

---

## Executive Presence Through Posture

### Standing Presence

**Commanding position:**
- Feet hip-width apart
- Weight evenly distributed
- Shoulders back and down
- Head balanced, chin neutral
- Open body language

**What to avoid:**
- Crossed arms (closed)
- Weight on one leg (casual)
- Hands in pockets (disengaged)
- Hunched shoulders (diminished)

### Seated Presence

**In meetings:**
- Sit tall, back from chair back
- Both feet on floor
- Lean in slightly for engagement
- Open chest and shoulders

**Avoid:**
- Slouching back
- Crossed arms
- Looking down at phone
- Appearing disengaged

## The Corner Office Setup

### Your Primary Workspace

**Invest in:**
- Quality executive chair
- Proper desk height
- Monitor at eye level
- Complete ergonomic setup

**You spend hours there:**
- Setup should be perfect
- Model for organization
- Worth the investment
- Prevents problems

### Standing Desk Option

**Benefits:**
- Position variety
- Projects energy
- May improve focus
- Health benefits

---

## Meeting Marathon Management

### Back-to-Back Meetings

**The reality:**
- Hour after hour seated
- Different rooms, different seating
- Little break time
- Cumulative strain

**Strategies:**
- Walk between meetings
- Stand for some meetings
- Brief stretches in transitions
- Position changes during meetings

### Conference Room Posture

**Whatever the chair:**
- Sit well
- Presence matters
- Core engaged
- Not collapsing

---

## Presentation Posture

### Commanding the Room

**Standing to present:**
- Grounded stance
- Open body language
- Purposeful movement
- Confident gestures

**Avoid:**
- Swaying or pacing nervously
- Closed body position
- Looking down at notes constantly
- Diminishing gestures

### Boardroom Dynamics

**At the head of the table:**
- Posture reflects leadership
- Open, confident position
- Engage visually around room
- Project calm authority

## High-Stress Tension

### Where Executives Hold Stress

**Common patterns:**
- Jaw clenching
- Shoulder elevation
- Neck tension
- Shallow breathing
- Overall tightness

### Managing Physical Stress

**Throughout the day:**
- Conscious breath
- Shoulder drops
- Jaw release
- Brief resets

**Between meetings:**
- Quick stretch
- Position reset
- Tension release
- Physical transition
