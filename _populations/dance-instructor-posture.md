# Dance Instructor Posture

Dance instruction combines physical demonstration with teaching. Female dance instructors spend hours on their feet, demonstrating movements, correcting students, and maintaining their own practice. The cumulative physical demands can take a toll on the body.

## Teaching Demands

### Demonstration
Showing movements repeatedly.

### Correction
Physically adjusting students.

### Class Leadership
Extended active classes.

### Multiple Classes
Back-to-back teaching.

## Physical Requirements

### Standing Duration
Hours on feet.

### Movement Repetition
Same movements demonstrated.

### Floor Work
Getting up and down.

### Impact
Jumping and landing.

## Common Physical Strains

### Joint Stress
Knees, ankles, hips.

### Back Problems
Repetitive movements.

### Shoulder Issues
Arm work.

### Foot Pain
Extended standing and movement.

## Teaching Techniques

### Verbal Instruction
Reducing demonstration needs.

### Partial Demonstration
Marking instead of full-out.

### Student Demonstration
Using advanced students.

### Video Use
Supplementary instruction.

## Studio Environment

### Floor Surface
Appropriate dance flooring.

### Temperature
Muscle-friendly warmth.

### Mirror Use
Observation without strain.

### Sound System
Hearing without damage.

## Body Maintenance

### Warm-Up
Personal preparation.

### Cool-Down
Post-class care.

### Cross-Training
Balanced conditioning.

### Rest Days
Recovery integration.

## Self-Care Practices

### Injury Prevention
Proactive care.

### Ice and Heat
Treatment as needed.

### Massage
Recovery support.

### Sleep
Adequate rest.

## Career Health

### Physical Conditioning
Maintaining capability.

### Teaching Evolution
Less physical instruction methods.

### Injury Management
Working with limitations.

### Long-Term Planning
Sustainable teaching career.

Dance instruction shares the joy of movement. Protecting your body ensures you can continue this inspiring physical art.
