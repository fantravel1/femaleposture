# Optometrist Posture

Optometry involves precise examinations using specialized equipment in specific positioning. The work requires sustained close-range focus, repetitive movements, and maintaining position relative to patients and instruments. Female optometrists face unique ergonomic challenges in their diagnostic work.

## Examination Demands

### Instrument Positioning
Working with slit lamps and other equipment.

### Patient Alignment
Adjusting to each patient's position.

### Close Examination
Near-focus work throughout the day.

### Repetitive Sequences
Same examination pattern repeated.

## Equipment-Based Work

### Slit Lamp Use
Sustained positions at the slit lamp.

### Phoropter Adjustments
Arm movements during refraction.

### Ophthalmoscopy
Direct and indirect examination positioning.

### Special Testing
Various diagnostic equipment.

## Common Physical Strains

### Neck Pain
Forward flexion during examinations.

### Back Strain
Positioning relative to equipment.

### Shoulder Tension
Sustained arm positions.

### Eye Fatigue
Close examination focus.

Appropriate lighting for examinations.
