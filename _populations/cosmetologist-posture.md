# Cosmetologist Posture

Cosmetology is physically demanding work requiring extended standing, repetitive arm movements, and sustained positions while styling and treating clients. Female cosmetologists must develop body awareness and protective practices to sustain long careers in beauty services.

## Hair Services Demands

### Standing Duration
Hours on feet at styling stations.

### Arm Elevation
Sustained arm positions while cutting and styling.

### Fine Motor Work
Precise movements for cutting and coloring.

### Client Positioning
Working around seated clients.

## Styling Station Setup

### Chair Height
Client chair adjustable to appropriate height.

### Tool Accessibility
Efficient arrangement of supplies.

### Mirror Position
Proper sightlines for work.

### Lighting
Adequate lighting for detailed work.

## Cutting and Coloring

### Scissor Technique
Ergonomic cutting methods.

### Arm Position
Managing elevated arm fatigue.

### Color Application
Positioning for application work.

### Blow-Drying
Managing heavy dryer and sustained position.

## Common Physical Issues

### Shoulder Pain
Elevated arm positions.

### Back Strain
Standing and leaning.

### Hand Problems
Repetitive gripping and cutting.

### Foot Pain
Extended standing.

## Protective Strategies

### Client Chair Adjustment
Raising clients to appropriate height.

### Tool Selection
Ergonomic scissors and equipment.

### Position Variety
Moving around clients during services.

### Break Integration
Resting between clients.

## Shampoo Services

### Bowl Positioning
Proper height for shampooing.

### Body Mechanics
Using body weight not arm strength.

### Wrist Protection
Neutral wrist positions.

### Shoulder Care
Avoiding overreaching.
