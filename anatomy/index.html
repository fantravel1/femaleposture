Posture Anatomy

Understanding the muscles, bones, and structures that control your posture empowers you to make lasting changes. Learn how your body works—with attention to women-specific anatomy.

The Posture System

Good posture isn't about one muscle or one position—it's a coordinated system involving:

  • Muscles that support, stabilize, and move
  • Fascia that connects and transmits force
  • Bones and joints that provide structure
  • Nervous system that coordinates it all

When any component is dysfunctional, posture suffers. Understanding anatomy helps you identify what needs attention.

Women's Anatomy & Posture

Female anatomy creates unique postural considerations:

Structural Differences

  • Wider pelvis → Different hip mechanics and Q-angle
  • Lower center of gravity → Different balance point
  • Breast tissue → Forward pull affecting upper body
  • Generally more flexible → May need stability over mobility work

Hormonal Influences

  • Relaxin → Joint laxity during menstruation and pregnancy
  • Estrogen changes → Affect tissue quality throughout life
  • Menstrual cycle → Some women notice postural changes

Common Muscle Imbalances

Upper Cross Syndrome

Tight: Chest, upper traps, suboccipitals
Weak: Deep neck flexors, lower traps, serratus
Result: Forward head, rounded shoulders

Lower Cross Syndrome

Tight: Hip flexors, back extensors
Weak: Glutes, abdominals
Result: Anterior pelvic tilt, excessive lumbar curve

Layer Syndrome

Combination of both patterns—common in prolonged sitting.

Applying Anatomy Knowledge
