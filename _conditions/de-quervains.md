# De Quervain's Tenosynovitis

De Quervain's tenosynovitis causes pain at the base of the thumb and wrist, affecting the tendons that move the thumb. It's very common in new mothers (sometimes called "mommy thumb") and those with repetitive hand activities.

## Understanding De Quervain's

### What Happens

**The condition:**
- Tendons on thumb side of wrist affected
- Sheaths thicken
- Pain with thumb movement
- Often sudden onset

### Why Women Are Affected More

**Common in:**
- New mothers (lifting baby)
- Hormonal changes postpartum
- Repetitive texting
- Computer work

## Symptoms

### Typical Presentation

**Signs:**
- Pain at base of thumb
- Worsens with thumb use
- Difficulty gripping
- Swelling possible
- Catching or snapping

## The Posture Connection

### Hand and Wrist Use

**Contributing factors:**
- Phone holding position
- Baby lifting technique
- Computer mouse use
- Repetitive thumb motions

## Treatment

### Rest and Splinting

**Initial:**
- Thumb spica splint
- Avoid aggravating activities
- Ice for comfort

### Modification

**Changes:**
- Baby lifting technique
- Phone use habits
- Grip changes

### Stretching

**Gentle:**
- Wrist stretches
- Thumb stretches
- Not aggressive

## When to Seek Help

**If not improving:**
- May need injection
- Rarely surgery
- Usually responds to conservative care

## Key Points

1. **Common in new mothers** — "Mommy thumb"
2. **Splinting helps** — Thumb spica
3. **Modify activities** — Baby lifting, phone use
4. **Usually responds** — To conservative care
