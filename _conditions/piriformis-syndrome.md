# Piriformis Syndrome

Piriformis syndrome occurs when this deep hip muscle irritates the sciatic nerve, causing buttock and leg symptoms.

## Understanding Piriformis Syndrome

### What Happens

- Piriformis muscle tightens
- Sciatic nerve compressed
- Buttock pain develops
- May radiate down leg

### Posture Connection

- Prolonged sitting aggravates
- Cross-legged sitting problematic
- Wallet in back pocket
- Hip rotation patterns

## Symptoms

### Common Presentations

**Typical findings:**
- Deep buttock pain
- Worse with sitting
- Pain down back of thigh
- Tender spot in buttock

### Aggravating Factors

**Often worse with:**
- Sitting on hard surfaces
- Driving long distances
- Climbing stairs
- Hip rotation

## Management

### Stretching Focus

**Key stretches:**
- Figure-4 stretch
- Pigeon pose
- Supine piriformis stretch
- Hip rotation stretches

### Activity Modifications

**Helpful changes:**
- Avoid prolonged sitting
- Remove wallet from pocket
- Vary sitting positions
- Regular movement breaks

## Key Points

1. **Sitting related** — Modify positions
2. **Stretching helps** — Regular piriformis stretches
3. **More common in women** — Hip anatomy factors
4. **Address posture** — Sitting habits matter
