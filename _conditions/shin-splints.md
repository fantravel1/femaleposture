# Shin Splints

Shin splints cause pain along the inner shin bone, often from overuse and related to foot mechanics and posture.

## Understanding Shin Splints

### What Happens

- Stress on shin bone
- Muscle attachment irritation
- Pain with activity
- Gradual onset typical

### Posture Connection

- Overpronation contributes
- Hip weakness involved
- Gait mechanics matter
- Training errors common

## Causes

### Contributing Factors

**Common causes:**
- Too much too soon
- Hard surfaces
- Worn footwear
- Flat feet

### Biomechanical Factors

**Often involved:**
- Overpronation
- Weak hip abductors
- Tight calves
- Poor running form

## Management

### Initial Treatment

**Options:**
- Relative rest
- Ice application
- Activity modification
- Cross-training

### Prevention

**Key strategies:**
- Gradual progression
- Proper footwear
- Hip strengthening
- Calf flexibility

## Key Points

1. **Usually overuse** — Training errors common
2. **Check foot mechanics** — Pronation matters
3. **Strengthen hips** — Affects leg alignment
4. **Gradual return** — Don't rush back
