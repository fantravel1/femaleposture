# Scoliosis and Posture

> **Understanding Curves:** Scoliosis is a lateral curvature of the spine. While it can't be "fixed" by posture exercises alone, understanding the condition helps manage it effectively.

---

## What Is Scoliosis?

### Definition

- Lateral (side-to-side) curve of spine
- Usually includes rotation
- Measured in degrees (Cobb angle)
- Mild: <20°, Moderate: 20-40°, Severe: >40°

### Types

**Idiopathic:**
- Most common (80%)
- Cause unknown
- Often develops in adolescence
- More common in females

**Other types:**
- Congenital (present at birth)
- Neuromuscular (from other conditions)
- Degenerative (adult-onset)

---

## Scoliosis in Women

### Why More Common

- Idiopathic scoliosis more prevalent in females
- Hormonal factors may contribute
- Progression more common in females
- Screened for in school-age girls

### Through Life Stages

**Adolescence:**
- When often diagnosed
- Monitoring during growth
- Treatment if progressing

**Pregnancy:**
- Mild scoliosis usually not problem
- Severe cases need monitoring
- Back pain may increase

**Aging:**
- Curves may progress
- Degenerative changes add complexity
- Activity remains important

---

## Does Posture Work Help?

### What Exercise Can Do

- Strengthen supporting muscles
- Improve overall function
- Reduce pain
- Maintain flexibility
- Support quality of life

### What Exercise Cannot Do

- Straighten the curve
- Replace medical treatment when needed
- "Cure" scoliosis

### Specialized Approaches

- Schroth method
- SEAS approach
- Specialized physical therapy
- Individualized programs

---

## General Exercise Guidance

### Usually Beneficial

- Symmetrical strengthening
- Core stability
- Flexibility work
- Swimming and water exercise
- Walking

### Approach with Care

- Heavy asymmetric loading
- Extreme rotation under load
- Activities that increase pain

### Work with Professionals

Ideally find:
- Physical therapist experienced with scoliosis
- Possible Schroth-certified therapist
- Understanding of your specific curve

---

## Daily Life with Scoliosis

### Awareness

- Know your curve pattern
- Notice what increases/decreases symptoms
- Understand your body

### Ergonomics

- May need customized setup
- Chair and desk adjustments
- Support where needed
- Same principles, possibly modified

### Activity

- Stay active
- Swimming often well-tolerated
- Walking excellent
- Find what works for you

---

## When to Seek Help

### See a Specialist If

- New diagnosis
- Pain increasing
- Curve progressing
- Neurological symptoms
- Considering treatment options

### Ongoing Care

- Regular monitoring
- Age-appropriate screening
- Bone health important (especially post-menopause)

---

## Important Notes

- Scoliosis varies greatly person to person
- General advice doesn't replace individual guidance
- Many people live full, active lives with scoliosis
- Treatment advances continue
