# Jaw Clenching and Bruxism

Jaw clenching and teeth grinding (bruxism) are common and often connected to posture. Forward head posture and neck tension can contribute to increased jaw muscle activity and dysfunction.

## Understanding Jaw Clenching

### What Happens

**The problem:**
- Excessive jaw muscle contraction
- May occur day and/or night
- Damages teeth
- Causes jaw pain and dysfunction

### Types

**Awake bruxism:**
- Clenching during day
- Often stress-related
- May be posture-related

**Sleep bruxism:**
- Grinding at night
- Less conscious control
- May need night guard

## The Posture Connection

### Forward Head and Jaw

**How they connect:**
- Forward head changes jaw position
- Neck muscles connect to jaw
- Stress affects both posture and jaw
- Tension patterns overlap

### Common Pattern

**What develops:**
- Forward head posture
- Neck muscle tension
- Jaw muscle overactivity
- Headaches, TMJ issues

## Treatment

### Awareness

**Throughout day:**
- Check jaw position regularly
- Teeth should be slightly apart
- Tongue on roof of mouth
- Relaxed jaw

### Posture Correction

**Helps jaw:**
- Chin tucks
- Neck stretches
- Upper back strengthening
- Correct forward head

### Jaw Relaxation

**Exercises:**
- Gentle jaw stretches
- Massage jaw muscles
- Relaxation techniques

### Night Guard

**For sleep bruxism:**
- Protects teeth
- Custom from dentist best
- OTC available

## Key Points

1. **Posture connected** — Forward head contributes
2. **Stress factor** — Affects both
3. **Awareness helps** — Check jaw during day
4. **Address posture** — Can reduce jaw tension
5. **Night guard** — If grinding at night
