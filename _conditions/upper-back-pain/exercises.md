# Exercises for Upper Back Pain: Relief and Prevention

> **Target the Problem Area:** Upper back pain between the shoulder blades often comes from muscle fatigue, poor posture, and stiffness. The right exercises address all three.

---

## Understanding Upper Back Pain

### Common Causes

- Poor posture (rounded shoulders, forward head)
- Muscle fatigue from prolonged positions
- Weakness in postural muscles
- Thoracic spine stiffness
- Trigger points in upper back muscles

### Exercise Approach

1. Release muscle tension
2. Mobilize stiff thoracic spine
3. Stretch tight front muscles
4. Strengthen upper back muscles
5. Improve posture habits

---

## Stretching Exercises

### Thread the Needle

**Why:** Rotational stretch for thoracic spine

**How to:**
1. Start on hands and knees
2. Take right arm and thread under body to left
3. Lower right shoulder and head toward floor
4. Feel rotation in upper back
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Switch sides

### Cat-Cow

**Why:** Gentle mobilization through flexion and extension

**How to:**
1. Hands and knees position
2. **Cow:** Drop belly, lift head and tailbone
3. **Cat:** Round back, tuck chin and tailbone
4. Move slowly between positions
5. 10-15 cycles

### Child's Pose

**Why:** Gentle stretch and relaxation for back

**How to:**
1. From hands and knees, sit back toward heels
2. Arms extended forward or by sides
3. Forehead on floor
4. Breathe and relax
5. Hold 30-60 seconds

### Doorway Stretch

**Why:** Opens tight chest that contributes to upper back pain

**How to:**
1. Stand in doorway
2. Forearm on door frame, elbow at shoulder height
3. Step through with one foot
4. Feel stretch in chest
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

### Lat Stretch

**Why:** Tight lats contribute to upper back issues

**How to:**
1. Hold onto doorframe or sturdy object
2. Step back and hinge at hips
3. Let arms straighten
4. Feel stretch along side and back
5. Hold 30 seconds each side

## Mobility Exercises

### Thoracic Extension on Foam Roller

**Why:** Mobilizes stiff thoracic spine into extension

**How to:**
1. Place foam roller under upper back
2. Support head with hands
3. Extend backward over roller
4. Move roller to different segments
5. 10-15 extensions at each level

### Open Book

**Why:** Thoracic rotation mobility

**How to:**
1. Lie on side, knees stacked and bent
2. Arms extended in front
3. Rotate top arm open, following with eyes
4. Return slowly
5. 10 reps each side

### Seated Thoracic Rotation

**Why:** Rotation work in seated position

**How to:**
1. Sit on chair, feet flat
2. Cross arms over chest
3. Rotate upper body one direction
4. Return and rotate other direction
5. 10 reps each side

### Quadruped Thoracic Rotation

**Why:** Rotation with core engagement

**How to:**
1. Hands and knees position
2. Place one hand behind head
3. Rotate elbow toward floor
4. Then rotate to open up toward ceiling
5. 10 reps each side

## Strengthening Exercises

### Shoulder Blade Squeeze

**Why:** Activates muscles between shoulder blades

**How to:**
1. Sit or stand tall
2. Squeeze shoulder blades together
3. Hold 5 seconds
4. Release
5. 15-20 reps

### Rows

**Why:** Strengthens middle back

**How to (with band):**
1. Anchor band at chest height
2. Grip handles
3. Pull toward lower ribs
4. Squeeze shoulder blades
5. Return with control
6. 12-15 reps

### Face Pulls

**Why:** Works rear delts and external rotators

**How to:**
1. Band at face height
2. Pull toward face, elbows high
3. Externally rotate at end
4. Squeeze upper back
5. 12-15 reps

### Prone Y-T-W

**Why:** Comprehensive upper back work

**How to:**
1. Lie face down
2. **Y:** Arms at 45 degrees, thumbs up, lift
3. **T:** Arms to sides, lift
4. **W:** Elbows bent, squeeze and lift
5. 8-10 of each

### Wall Slides

**Why:** Scapular control and mobility

**How to:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Arms in goalpost position
3. Slide arms up the wall
4. Maintain wall contact
5. 10-15 reps

## Self-Release Techniques

### Tennis Ball Release

**For trigger points:**
1. Lie on back
2. Place tennis ball between shoulder blades
3. Apply body weight to pressure points
4. Hold 30-60 seconds per spot
5. Move to different spots

### Foam Roller Upper Back

**For general release:**
1. Roller under upper back (perpendicular)
2. Support head, cross arms over chest
3. Roll slowly up and down
4. Pause on tight spots
5. 1-2 minutes

## Sample Routines

### Quick Relief (10 minutes)

When pain is present:

1. Cat-cow: 10 cycles
2. Child's pose: 1 minute
3. Thread the needle: 30 sec each
4. Tennis ball release: 1-2 minutes
5. Shoulder blade squeeze: 10 reps
6. Gentle neck stretches

### Prevention Routine (15 minutes)

For regular practice:

1. Cat-cow: 10 cycles
2. Thoracic extension (roller): 3 positions, 10 each
3. Open book: 10 each side
4. Doorway stretch: 30 sec each side
5. Prone Y-T-W: 8 each
6. Rows: 15 reps
7. Shoulder blade squeeze: 15 reps

### Comprehensive Routine (25 minutes)

3x per week:

1. Warm-up movement: 3 minutes
2. Cat-cow: 10 cycles
3. Thread the needle: 30 sec × 2 each
4. Thoracic extension: All positions
5. Open book: 10 each side
6. Doorway stretch: 45 sec each
7. Lat stretch: 30 sec each
8. Rows: 3 × 12
9. Face pulls: 3 × 12
10. Prone Y-T-W: 2 rounds
11. Wall slides: 15 reps
12. Foam rolling: 2 minutes

## Workplace Exercises

### At Your Desk

Every 30-60 minutes:
- Shoulder blade squeeze: 10 reps
- Neck stretches: 15 sec each direction
- Seated rotation: 5 each side
- Stand and move

### Standing Break

- Doorway stretch (find doorframe)
- Shoulder rolls: 10 each direction
- Cat-cow motion against wall
- Walk around

---

## What to Avoid

### During Acute Pain

- Heavy lifting
- Intense exercise
- Overhead work
- Positions that worsen pain

### In General

- Prolonged slouching
- Static positions too long
- Ignoring pain signals
- Overstretching

---

## When to Seek Help

See a professional if:
- Pain persists more than 2-3 weeks
- Pain is severe
- Pain with breathing
- Numbness or tingling
- Other concerning symptoms
