# Spondylolisthesis

Spondylolisthesis involves one vertebra slipping forward on another, affecting spinal alignment and stability.

## Understanding Spondylolisthesis

### What Happens

- Vertebra shifts forward
- Most common in lower back
- Varying degrees of slip
- May affect nerve roots

### Posture Effects

- Increased lumbar curve often
- Core stability important
- Movement patterns affected
- May limit extension

## Types

### Isthmic

**Characteristics:**
- Stress fracture in pars
- Common in young athletes
- Activities with extension

### Degenerative

**Characteristics:**
- Age-related changes
- More common in women
- Usually after 50

## Management

### Exercise Focus

**Key elements:**
- Core stabilization crucial
- Avoid excessive extension
- Hip flexibility
- Neutral spine training

### Movement Guidelines

**Helpful strategies:**
- Maintain neutral spine
- Strengthen deep core
- Careful with backbends
- Stay active within limits

## Key Points

1. **Stability key** — Core strength essential
2. **Avoid hyperextension** — May worsen symptoms
3. **Keep neutral spine** — During activities
4. **Stay active** — Within guidelines
