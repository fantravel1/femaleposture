# Heel Spurs

Heel spurs are bony growths on the heel bone, often associated with plantar fasciitis and affecting gait patterns.

## Understanding Heel Spurs

### What Happens

- Calcium deposits form
- Often with plantar fasciitis
- May or may not cause pain
- Develops over time

### Posture Effects

- Altered heel strike
- Compensation patterns
- Changed gait
- May favor other foot

## Causes

### Contributing Factors

**Common causes:**
- Plantar fascia strain
- Flat feet or high arches
- Excess weight
- Standing occupations

### Associated Conditions

**Often seen with:**
- Plantar fasciitis
- Achilles tendinopathy
- Tight calf muscles

## Management

### Conservative Care

**Options:**
- Supportive footwear
- Orthotics
- Stretching program
- Ice for inflammation

### Prevention

**Key strategies:**
- Proper footwear
- Calf stretching
- Gradual activity increases
- Maintain healthy weight

## Key Points

1. **Often asymptomatic** — Spur itself may not hurt
2. **Address underlying cause** — Usually plantar fasciitis
3. **Stretching important** — Calf and plantar fascia
4. **Footwear matters** — Support and cushioning
