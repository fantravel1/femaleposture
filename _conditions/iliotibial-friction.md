# IT Band Friction Syndrome

IT band friction syndrome causes lateral knee pain from the IT band rubbing over the outer knee, common in runners.

## Understanding IT Band Issues

### What Happens

- IT band tightens
- Friction over knee
- Inflammation develops
- Pain with activity

### Posture Connection

- Hip weakness contributes
- Leg alignment matters
- Foot mechanics involved
- Running form affects

## Symptoms

### Common Presentations

**Typical symptoms:**
- Outer knee pain
- Worse with running
- Starts after certain distance
- May click or snap

### Activity Pattern

**Characteristic pattern:**
- Fine initially
- Pain builds with activity
- Forces stopping
- Resolves with rest

## Causes

### Contributing Factors

**Common factors:**
- Weak hip abductors
- Training errors
- Worn shoes
- Hill running

### Biomechanical Issues

**Often involved:**
- Hip drop when running
- Knee valgus
- Overpronation
- Cross-body arm swing

## Management

### Hip Strengthening

**Critical exercises:**
- Side-lying leg raises
- Clamshells
- Single-leg exercises
- Hip stability work

### Foam Rolling

**Self-treatment:**
- Lateral thigh
- TFL muscle
- Glute muscles
- Quadriceps

## Key Points

1. **Hip weakness key factor** — Strengthen abductors
2. **Training errors common** — Gradual progression
3. **Check running form** — Hip stability matters
4. **Responds to rehab** — Conservative treatment works
