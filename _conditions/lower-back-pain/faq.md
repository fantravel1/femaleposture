# Lower Back Pain FAQ

> **Quick Answers:** Common questions about lower back pain, how posture affects it, and what you can do for relief.

---

## Understanding Lower Back Pain

### How common is lower back pain in women?

Very common. About 80% of people experience lower back pain at some point, and women report it more frequently than men. Hormonal factors, pregnancy, and structural differences contribute to higher rates in women.

### Is my lower back pain caused by bad posture?

It could be contributing, but the relationship is complex. Poor posture can:
- Increase muscle tension
- Create uneven load on spinal structures
- Lead to muscle weakness over time
- Cause fatigue and strain

However, many factors contribute to back pain. Posture improvement often helps but may not be the only solution.

### What's the difference between acute and chronic back pain?

- **Acute:** Sudden onset, lasting less than 6 weeks
- **Subacute:** 6-12 weeks
- **Chronic:** Lasting more than 12 weeks

Most acute back pain resolves on its own. Chronic pain needs more comprehensive treatment.

### Why does my back hurt when I sit but not when I stand?

Sitting puts more pressure on spinal discs than standing. Other factors:
- Chair may not support you properly
- Hip flexors tighten in seated position
- Core muscles disengage
- You may be slouching

Improving sitting posture and taking regular breaks often helps.

## Causes & Risk Factors

### What causes lower back pain?

Common causes include:
- Muscle strain or spasm
- Poor posture over time
- Weak core muscles
- Tight hip flexors
- Disc issues (bulging, herniated)
- SI joint dysfunction
- Arthritis/degenerative changes
- Pregnancy and postpartum changes

### Why is lower back pain worse during my period?

Prostaglandins (hormones that trigger uterine contractions) can also cause back muscle spasms. Relaxin, released during menstruation, can also affect ligament stability. This is a normal variation for many women.

### Can high heels cause lower back pain?

Yes. High heels:
- Shift your center of gravity forward
- Increase the curve in your lower back
- Shorten calf muscles and hip flexors
- Change your walking pattern

If you must wear heels, choose lower options and limit wear time. Stretch afterward.

### Does being overweight cause lower back pain?

Excess weight can contribute by:
- Adding load to the spine
- Shifting your center of gravity
- Weakening core muscles (often)
- Changing posture to compensate

Weight management combined with exercise often improves back pain, but thin people get back pain too.

### Can stress cause lower back pain?

Yes. Stress can:
- Cause muscle tension
- Reduce blood flow to muscles
- Increase pain perception
- Lead to poor posture habits

Stress management is an often-overlooked part of back pain treatment.

## Symptoms & Warning Signs

### When should I see a doctor for lower back pain?

See a healthcare provider if you have:
- Pain lasting more than 4-6 weeks
- Severe pain that doesn't improve with rest
- Numbness or tingling in legs
- Weakness in legs
- Bladder or bowel changes
- Unexplained weight loss
- Pain that wakes you from sleep
- History of cancer
- Pain after trauma/fall

These could indicate something more serious than muscle strain.

### Is it normal for back pain to go into my legs?

Pain that radiates into the legs (sciatica) suggests nerve involvement. This is common but should be evaluated, especially if you have:
- Pain below the knee
- Numbness or tingling
- Weakness
- Pain that doesn't improve with rest

### What does it mean if my back hurts on one side?

One-sided pain can indicate:
- Muscle strain on that side
- SI joint dysfunction
- Nerve irritation
- Kidney issues (especially with fever)
- Posture habits (always crossing same leg, carrying bag on same side)

If severe or persistent, get it checked.

## Treatment & Relief

### Should I rest or keep moving with lower back pain?

Keep moving (gently). Modern guidelines recommend:
- Avoid prolonged bed rest (makes things worse)
- Stay as active as tolerable
- Gentle movement helps healing
- Avoid activities that significantly worsen pain

Complete rest weakens muscles and slows recovery.

### What are the best exercises for lower back pain?

Evidence-supported exercises include:
- Cat-cow (gentle spinal mobility)
- Bird-dog (core stability)
- Glute bridges (strengthens glutes and core)
- Dead bug (core without spinal load)
- Hip flexor stretches
- Walking

Avoid exercises that increase your pain.

### Does core strengthening help lower back pain?

Yes, research supports this. Strong core muscles:
- Support the spine
- Reduce load on passive structures
- Improve posture
- Allow better movement patterns

Focus on deep core muscles (transverse abdominis) not just "six-pack" muscles.

### Is stretching or strengthening more important for back pain?

Both matter, but strengthening is often more important long-term. A good approach:
- Stretch what's tight (hip flexors, hamstrings often)
- Strengthen what's weak (core, glutes often)
- Build endurance for postural muscles

### Do heating pads help lower back pain?

They can provide temporary relief. Heat:
- Relaxes muscle tension
- Increases blood flow
- Feels soothing

Use for 15-20 minutes at a time. Heat is generally better for muscle tension; ice is often better for acute injury with inflammation.

## Posture & Daily Life

### What's the best posture for lower back pain?

"Neutral spine" is ideal:
- Natural curve in lower back (not flat, not excessive)
- Weight evenly distributed
- Core gently engaged
- Avoid extremes of flexion or extension

The best posture is also a changing posture—don't stay in any position too long.

### How should I sit to avoid back pain?

- Sit back in your chair (not on the edge)
- Use lumbar support (built-in or rolled towel)
- Feet flat on floor or footrest
- Knees at or slightly below hip level
- Take breaks every 30-60 minutes
- Consider a standing desk

### What's the best sleeping position for lower back pain?

- **Side sleeping:** With pillow between knees to keep hips aligned
- **Back sleeping:** With pillow under knees to reduce spinal curve
- **Stomach sleeping:** Generally avoid—increases back arch

Your mattress matters too—medium-firm is often best.

### Is standing better than sitting for lower back pain?

It can be, but not always. Prolonged standing can also cause back pain. The key is:
- Vary your position throughout the day
- Use good posture whether sitting or standing
- Take regular movement breaks
- Stand on a supportive surface if standing long periods

## Products & Tools

### Do back braces help lower back pain?

For short-term support during acute pain, possibly. Long-term:
- Can weaken muscles
- Creates dependency
- Doesn't address underlying issue

If you use one, limit wear and combine with exercise. Use as temporary support, not a solution.

### Is a standing desk worth it for back pain?

It can help IF:
- You alternate between sitting and standing
- You maintain good posture while standing
- You don't just trade sitting pain for standing pain

Standing all day isn't better than sitting all day. Movement and variety are key.

### What type of mattress is best for lower back pain?

Generally, medium-firm mattresses are best for most people with back pain. But the "best" mattress depends on:
- Your sleeping position
- Your body weight
- Personal preference

The best mattress is one you wake up comfortable on.

## Women-Specific Questions

### Why does lower back pain increase with pregnancy?

Multiple factors:
- Weight gain (especially in front)
- Center of gravity shifts
- Hormones loosen ligaments
- Posture changes to compensate
- Abdominal muscles stretch and weaken

Pregnancy back pain is common but can be managed with exercise and posture awareness.

### Does lower back pain improve after pregnancy?

Usually, but not always immediately. Factors that help:
- Core and glute strengthening postpartum
- Addressing diastasis recti if present
- Proper baby-carrying posture
- Gradual return to exercise

Some women need focused rehabilitation.

### Can breastfeeding cause lower back pain?

The positions used for breastfeeding often do:
- Looking down for long periods
- Holding baby without support
- Hunching over baby
- Sitting in suboptimal positions

Using pillows, varying positions, and doing stretches between feeds helps.

### How does menopause affect lower back pain?

Menopause can increase back pain due to:
- Decreased bone density
- Reduced estrogen (affects pain perception)
- Changes in muscle mass
- Weight changes

Strength training is especially important during and after menopause.

## Exercise & Activity

### Is walking good for lower back pain?

Yes! Walking is one of the best activities for back pain:
- Low impact
- Promotes blood flow to spine
- Gentle movement without loading
- Improves mood
- Maintains fitness

Start with short walks and gradually increase distance.

### Can yoga help lower back pain?

Research supports yoga for back pain. Helpful aspects:
- Improves flexibility
- Builds core strength
- Reduces stress
- Promotes body awareness

Choose a class designed for back issues. Avoid extreme poses. Listen to your body.

### Should I avoid lifting weights with lower back pain?

Not necessarily. Strength training can help back pain, but:
- Learn proper form first
- Avoid exercises that worsen symptoms
- Start with light weights
- Focus on compound movements with good form
- Deadlifts (properly done) often help, not hurt

When in doubt, consult a qualified trainer or physical therapist.

### Is running bad for lower back pain?

Not inherently. Running can be fine if:
- You don't have acute pain
- You have adequate core and hip strength
- You wear appropriate shoes
- You run on suitable surfaces
- Your running form is good

If running worsens your pain, address the underlying issue first.
