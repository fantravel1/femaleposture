# Hyperkyphosis: Excessive Upper Back Curve

> **Understanding the Curve:** Hyperkyphosis refers to excessive forward curvature of the thoracic spine. While some curve is normal, too much can cause problems.

---

## What Is Hyperkyphosis?

### Normal vs. Excessive

- Normal thoracic curve: 20-45 degrees
- Hyperkyphosis: >45-50 degrees
- Visible as rounded upper back

### Types

**Postural kyphosis:**
- From habits and posture
- Flexible and correctable
- Most common in younger people

**Structural kyphosis:**
- Changes in bone structure
- Less flexible
- More common with aging

---

## Causes

### In Younger People

- Poor posture habits
- Weak back muscles
- Screen time positions
- Scheuermann's disease (adolescents)

### With Aging

- Vertebral compression fractures
- Osteoporosis effects
- Disc degeneration
- Muscle weakness

---

## Concerns

### Physical Effects

- Affects breathing capacity
- May cause back pain
- Impacts balance
- Changes appearance

### Why It Matters

- Can progress over time
- Affects quality of life
- Prevention is easier than reversal

---

## Management

### For Postural Hyperkyphosis

- Posture awareness
- Extension exercises
- Back strengthening
- Stretching tight muscles

### For Structural Concerns

- Professional evaluation
- Bone health assessment
- Appropriate exercise (some modifications)
- May need medical management

---

## Exercises That May Help

- Thoracic extension
- Wall angels
- Upper back strengthening
- Cat-cow (extension focus)
- Swimming (backstroke)

---

## When to See a Professional

- Significant visible change
- Pain associated with curve
- Balance problems
- Breathing difficulties
- Rapid changes
