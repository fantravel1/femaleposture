# Hyperlordosis: Excessive Lower Back Curve

> **Understanding the Arch:** Hyperlordosis refers to an exaggerated inward curve of the lower back. While some curve is normal, too much can cause problems.

---

## What Is Hyperlordosis?

### Normal vs. Excessive

- Some lumbar curve is normal
- Excessive = hyperlordosis
- Also called "swayback" in some contexts

### How It Looks

- Pronounced lower back arch
- Belly pushed forward
- Buttocks pushed backward
- Often accompanies anterior pelvic tilt

---

## Causes

### Common Contributors

- Tight hip flexors
- Weak core muscles
- Weak glutes
- Prolonged sitting
- Poor postural habits
- Pregnancy (temporarily)

### Other Factors

- Obesity
- High heels
- Certain medical conditions

---

## Problems It Can Cause

- Lower back pain
- Muscle fatigue
- Pressure on spinal structures
- Altered biomechanics
- Associated with disc issues

---

## Corrective Approach

### Stretch

- Hip flexors
- Lower back (gently)
- Quadriceps

### Strengthen

- Core (especially deep core)
- Glutes
- Hamstrings

### Awareness

- Learn neutral spine
- Practice pelvic tilts
- Mind posture in daily life

---

## Key Exercises

1. Pelvic tilts
2. Dead bug
3. Glute bridges
4. Hip flexor stretches
5. Planks
