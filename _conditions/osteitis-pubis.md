# Osteitis Pubis

Osteitis pubis causes pain at the pubic symphysis joint, affecting pelvic stability and movement patterns.

## Understanding Osteitis Pubis

### What Happens

- Pubic joint inflamed
- Bone stress develops
- Central pelvic pain
- Movement affects

### Posture Connection

- Pelvic stability important
- Core function affected
- Hip mechanics involved
- Movement patterns change

## Symptoms

### Common Presentations

**Typical symptoms:**
- Central groin pain
- Worse with activity
- Tender over pubic bone
- Adductor pain

### Aggravating Activities

**Often worse with:**
- Running
- Kicking
- Quick direction changes
- Single-leg activities

## Causes

### Contributing Factors

**Common causes:**
- Overuse
- Muscle imbalances
- Core weakness
- Hip stiffness

### Risk Groups

**Higher prevalence in:**
- Runners
- Athletes
- Postpartum women
- Those with hip issues

## Management

### Initial Approach

**Key strategies:**
- Activity modification
- Core strengthening
- Adductor exercises
- Hip mobility

### Progressive Rehab

**Focus on:**
- Pelvic stability
- Gradual loading
- Movement quality
- Return to activity

## Key Points

1. **Core strength essential** — Pelvic stability
2. **Balance muscles** — Adductors and abdominals
3. **Gradual return** — Don't rush back
4. **Address hip mobility** — Contributes to overload
