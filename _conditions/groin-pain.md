# Groin Pain

Groin pain in women can stem from multiple sources including hip, pelvic, and spine issues. Posture and pelvic alignment often play a role in both cause and treatment.

## Understanding Groin Pain

### Possible Causes

**Musculoskeletal:**
- Hip joint problems
- Adductor strain
- Pubic symphysis dysfunction
- Referred from spine
- SI joint issues

### Posture Connection

**How alignment affects:**
- Pelvic tilt changes loading
- Hip muscle imbalances
- Asymmetrical stance
- Leg length differences

## Symptoms

### Location Patterns

**Where you feel it:**
- Inner thigh
- Lower abdomen
- Pubic area
- May radiate

## When Women Experience It

### Common Scenarios

**Frequent in:**
- Pregnancy and postpartum
- Athletic activities
- Hip issues
- After childbirth

## Treatment

### Depends on Cause

**General approaches:**
- Rest from aggravating activities
- Gentle stretching
- Pelvic stability exercises
- Address imbalances

### Hip Strengthening

**Often helpful:**
- Adductor exercises
- Hip stability work
- Core strengthening

## When to Seek Help

**Important if:**
- Sudden onset
- After trauma
- Significant dysfunction
- Concerns about cause

## Key Points

1. **Multiple possible causes** — Need proper diagnosis
2. **Posture may contribute** — Pelvic alignment
3. **Common in pregnancy** — Pubic symphysis issues
4. **Strengthening helps** — Hip and core
5. **Seek evaluation** — If uncertain of cause
