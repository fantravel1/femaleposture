# Gym Posture Guide

> **Exercise Should Help, Not Hurt:** The gym builds strength that supports posture—but only if exercises are done well. Poor form reinforces bad patterns and risks injury.

---

## Foundational Positions

### Neutral Spine

**What it means:**
- Natural curves maintained
- Not excessively arched
- Not rounded
- Stable core engagement

**How to find it:**
1. Stand tall
2. Slight lumbar curve
3. Ribs over pelvis
4. Core gently engaged
5. This is your default for most exercises

### Hip Hinge

**Essential for:**
- Deadlifts
- Rows
- Kettlebell swings
- Any bending movement

**How to do it:**
1. Feet hip-width apart
2. Slight knee bend
3. Push hips back
4. Torso comes forward
5. Back stays straight
6. Weight in heels/mid-foot

### Shoulder Position

**Shoulders packed:**
1. Roll shoulders back
2. Pull shoulder blades slightly together and down
3. Create stable base
4. Maintain during pressing and pulling

## Lower Body Exercises

### Squat

**Setup:**
- Feet shoulder-width or wider
- Toes slightly out (15-30°)
- Core engaged
- Chest up

**Movement:**
- Hips and knees bend together
- Knees track over toes
- Descend to comfortable depth
- Spine neutral throughout
- Drive through whole foot to stand

**Common mistakes:**
- Knees caving inward
- Excessive forward lean
- Rounding lower back
- Losing core engagement

### Deadlift

**Setup:**
- Feet hip-width
- Bar over mid-foot
- Hip hinge to grip bar
- Shoulders over or slightly ahead of bar
- Back flat, core engaged

**Movement:**
- Push floor away with legs
- Keep bar close to body
- Hips and shoulders rise together
- Lock out by squeezing glutes
- Reverse movement to lower

**Common mistakes:**
- Rounding lower back
- Bar drifting away from body
- Jerking the lift
- Hyperextending at top

### Lunge

**Setup:**
- Upright torso
- Step forward
- Front foot flat
- Back heel lifted

**Movement:**
- Lower straight down
- Front knee tracks over ankle
- Back knee approaches floor
- Push through front foot to return

**Common mistakes:**
- Front knee shooting forward
- Leaning too far forward
- Hip dropping/tilting
- Loss of balance

### Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

**Setup:**
- Feet hip-width
- Slight knee bend (fixed)
- Hold weight at thighs

**Movement:**
- Hip hinge backward
- Lower weight along legs
- Feel hamstring stretch
- Neutral spine entire time
- Return by driving hips forward

**Common mistakes:**
- Rounding back
- Bending knees too much
- Not hinging at hips
- Going too low

## Upper Body Pushing

### Overhead Press

**Setup:**
- Feet hip-width
- Core braced
- Bar at shoulders
- Elbows slightly forward of bar

**Movement:**
- Press straight up
- Move head out of way
- Lock out overhead
- Bar over mid-foot at top
- Lower controlled

**Common mistakes:**
- Excessive back arch
- Pressing forward (not straight up)
- Flaring elbows excessively
- Not bracing core

### Bench Press

**Setup:**
- Eyes under bar
- Shoulder blades pinched together
- Small arch in lower back (feet grounded)
- Grip just outside shoulders

**Movement:**
- Unrack with straight arms
- Lower bar to lower chest
- Elbows at ~45-75 degrees
- Press straight up
- Maintain upper back tightness

**Common mistakes:**
- Flaring elbows too wide
- Bouncing off chest
- Losing shoulder blade position
- Butt lifting off bench

### Push-Up

**Setup:**
- Hands slightly wider than shoulders
- Body in straight line
- Core engaged
- Shoulders over wrists

**Movement:**
- Lower body as unit
- Elbows at ~45 degrees
- Touch chest to floor (or close)
- Push to full extension

**Common mistakes:**
- Hips sagging
- Hips piking up
- Elbows flaring 90 degrees
- Head dropping

## Upper Body Pulling

### Barbell Row

**Setup:**
- Hip hinge position
- Back flat, parallel-ish to floor
- Grip just outside knees
- Shoulders packed

**Movement:**
- Pull bar to lower chest/upper belly
- Elbows back (not out)
- Squeeze shoulder blades
- Lower controlled

**Common mistakes:**
- Rounding upper back
- Using momentum
- Standing too upright
- Not pulling to body

### Lat Pulldown

**Setup:**
- Grip wider than shoulders
- Sit tall
- Chest up
- Shoulders depressed (away from ears)

**Movement:**
- Pull bar to upper chest
- Lead with elbows
- Squeeze lats at bottom
- Control return

**Common mistakes:**
- Leaning back excessively
- Pulling behind head (avoid this)
- Shrugging shoulders up
- Using momentum

### Seated Cable Row

**Setup:**
- Sit with slight knee bend
- Upright torso
- Grip handle
- Shoulders down and back

**Movement:**
- Pull handle to lower ribs
- Squeeze shoulder blades together
- Pause briefly
- Extend arms fully
- Don't sway forward/backward

**Common mistakes:**
- Swinging body for momentum
- Rounding shoulders forward
- Not pulling all the way back
- Shrugging shoulders

## Core Exercises

### Plank

**Setup:**
- Forearms on floor
- Body straight line
- Core engaged
- Glutes squeezed

**Position:**
- Hips level (not piking or sagging)
- Shoulder blades slightly protracted
- Neutral neck
- Breathe normally

**Common mistakes:**
- Hips too high
- Hips sagging
- Holding breath
- Neck craning up

### Dead Bug

**Setup:**
- On back
- Arms toward ceiling
- Knees bent 90°
- Lower back pressed to floor

**Movement:**
- Lower opposite arm and leg
- Maintain back position
- Return and switch
- Move slowly

**Common mistakes:**
- Back arching off floor
- Moving too fast
- Not breathing
- Legs going too low

## Posture-Building Exercises

### Face Pulls

**Why:** Counters forward head and rounded shoulders

**Setup:**
- Cable at face height
- Rope attachment
- Stand facing machine

**Movement:**
- Pull to face level
- Separate rope at end
- External rotation at end
- Squeeze rear delts
- Control return

### Prone Y Raises

**Why:** Strengthens upper back extensors

**Setup:**
- Lie face down
- Arms in Y position

**Movement:**
- Lift arms toward ceiling
- Squeeze upper back
- Lower controlled
- Keep forehead on ground

### Wall Angels

**Why:** Thoracic mobility and awareness

**Setup:**
- Back against wall
- Arms in goalpost position
- Back of hands on wall

**Movement:**
- Slide arms up and down
- Keep contact with wall
- Move through available range

## General Form Tips

### Breathing

**The rule:**
- Exhale on exertion
- Inhale on easier phase
- Never hold breath on max effort

**For heavy lifts:**
- Brace with air
- Hold briefly
- Exhale through sticking point or after

### Mirrors

**Use for:**
- Form check
- Not for admiring yourself mid-set
- Quick glances, not staring

**Don't:**
- Crane neck to watch (compromises form)
- Check every rep

### When to Stop

- Pain (not muscle burn)
- Form breakdown
- Dizziness
- Unable to complete rep safely
