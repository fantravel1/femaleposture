# Quick Posture Fixes: 60-Second Resets

> **For Those "I Need Help NOW" Moments:** When you've been slouching for hours and suddenly notice, or your neck is killing you between meetings—these instant fixes help.

---

## The 10-Second Full-Body Reset

**Use when:** You need an immediate posture check

1. **Stand tall** - Imagine string pulling crown of head up
2. **Roll shoulders** - Back and down, 3 circles
3. **Engage core** - Gentle 20% contraction
4. **Unlock knees** - Soft, not locked
5. **Ground feet** - Feel even weight distribution

**That's it.** Takes 10 seconds. Do it 10+ times daily.

---

## Neck Emergency Fixes

### The Chin Tuck Reset (15 seconds)

For forward head position and neck strain:

1. Pull chin straight back (make double chin)
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Release
4. Repeat 3 times

### The Neck Release (30 seconds)

For neck tension and stiffness:

1. Drop ear toward shoulder (15 seconds each side)
2. Add gentle hand pressure for deeper stretch
3. Breathe into the stretch

### The Tension Breaker (20 seconds)

For that knot at the base of skull:

1. Interlace fingers behind head
2. Let head drop forward
3. Gentle pull adds stretch to upper neck
4. 20 seconds, then slowly release

---

## Shoulder Emergency Fixes

### Shoulder Blade Squeeze (15 seconds)

For rounded shoulders:

1. Squeeze shoulder blades together
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Release slowly
4. Repeat 3 times

### The Wall Reset (30 seconds)

For full upper body realignment:

1. Stand with back against wall
2. Raise arms to "goalpost" position
3. Slide up and down wall 5 times
4. Step away maintaining position

### The Doorway Opener (30 seconds each side)

For chest tightness:

1. Arm on door frame at 90°
2. Step through doorway
3. Feel chest stretch
4. Hold 30 seconds each side

---

## Lower Back Emergency Fixes

### The Standing Back Release (15 seconds)

For lower back compression:

1. Hands on hips
2. Lean backward gently
3. Hold 5-10 seconds
4. Return to neutral

### The Hip Shift (20 seconds)

For one-sided standing tension:

1. Notice if you're leaning on one leg
2. Shift weight evenly to both feet
3. Engage glutes gently
4. Maintain for 20 seconds

### The Desk Chair Rescue (30 seconds)

For seated lower back pain:

1. Sit at edge of chair
2. Tilt pelvis forward (arch back slightly)
3. Then tilt back (tuck pelvis)
4. Find neutral in middle
5. Engage core to maintain

## Full-Body Quick Resets

### The Ragdoll Reset (45 seconds)

Full spine decompression:

1. Stand, bend forward from hips
2. Let arms and head hang
3. Gently sway side to side
4. Slowly roll up, stacking vertebrae
5. 45 seconds total

### The Cat-Cow Standing (30 seconds)

Spine mobility anywhere:

1. Hands on thighs
2. Round spine (cat)
3. Arch spine (cow)
4. Flow 5-10 times

### The Full Reset (60 seconds)

Complete posture restart:

1. Ragdoll forward fold (10 sec)
2. Roll up slowly (10 sec)
3. Shoulder rolls back x5 (10 sec)
4. Neck circles each direction (10 sec)
5. Wall test check (10 sec)
6. Walk away with awareness (10 sec)

## Situation-Specific Fixes

### After Long Sitting

1. Stand and reach arms overhead
2. Side bend each direction
3. Hip circles
4. Calf raises x10
5. Walk for 30 seconds if possible

### After Phone Use

1. Chin tucks x5
2. Look up at ceiling (10 sec)
3. Ear to shoulder stretch each side
4. Shoulder blade squeezes x5

### After Carrying Bags/Baby

1. Shoulder shrugs and releases x5
2. Arm circles both directions
3. Stand on opposite leg briefly
4. Reset posture fully

### Before Important Meeting/Photo

1. Wall check for alignment
2. Shoulder blade squeeze
3. Chin tuck
4. Deep breath
5. Confident stance

## Building the Habit

### Trigger Points

Do a quick fix:
- Every time you stand up
- When you grab your phone
- At every red light
- After using the bathroom
- When your timer/reminder goes off

### Signs You Need a Fix NOW

- Neck feeling heavy
- Shoulders creeping toward ears
- Lower back aching
- Feeling "scrunched"
- Shallow breathing
