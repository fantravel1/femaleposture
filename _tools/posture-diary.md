# Posture Diary: Track Your Progress

> **What Gets Measured Gets Managed:** Tracking your posture journey helps you stay aware, build habits, and see progress over time.

---

## Why Keep a Posture Diary

### Benefits

- Increases awareness
- Builds accountability
- Shows progress
- Identifies patterns
- Motivates continued effort

### What to Track

- Daily exercises completed
- Pain or discomfort levels
- Posture check-ins
- Habits and behaviors
- Weekly photos (optional)

---

### Quick Daily Check

- Pain level (1=none, 5=severe): ___

## Tracking Patterns

### Notice Connections

- When does posture worsen?
- What activities cause pain?
- What helps you feel better?
- What time of day is challenging?

### Common Patterns

**Pain increases with:**
- Prolonged sitting
- Stressful days
- Skipped exercises
- Poor sleep

**Pain decreases with:**
- Regular exercise
- Movement breaks
- Good sleep
- Lower stress

---

## Simple vs. Detailed Tracking

### Minimal Tracking

**If you don't like logging:**
- Weekly check-in only
- Monthly photo
- Brief notes

**Still helpful** for awareness.

### Detailed Tracking

**If you like data:**
- Daily exercises logged
- Pain levels tracked
- Time of day noted
- Detailed observations

## Tools for Tracking

### Paper Options

- Notebook
- Printed template

### Digital Options

- Notes app on phone

---

## Making It Stick

### Keep It Simple

- Don't make it complicated
- 1-2 minutes is enough
- Consistency matters more than detail

### Habit Stack

- Link to existing routine
- Right after exercise
- Before bed
- Part of morning routine

### Review Regularly

- Weekly review (5 minutes)
- Monthly review (15 minutes)
- Celebrate progress

---

## Using Your Data

### Identify What Works

- More exercise = less pain?
- Which exercises help most?
- What time of day works?

### Adjust Your Plan

- Do more of what works
- Reduce what doesn't help
- Try new approaches
- Be flexible

### Celebrate Progress

- Acknowledge improvements
- Compare to starting point
- Not just physical changes
- Habits and awareness count too

## Sample First Week

### Example Entries

**Monday:**
- Exercises: Morning routine (10 min), desk stretches (3x)

**Tuesday:**

- Pain: 3/5 (neck stiff from phone use)
