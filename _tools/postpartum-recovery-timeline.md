# Postpartum Recovery Timeline

> **Healing Takes Time:** Your body grew a humanâ€”recovery isn't instant. This timeline helps you understand what to expect and when to reintroduce activity safely.

---

## Important Notes First

### Individual Variation

- Recovery varies widely between women
- Birth method affects timeline
- Previous fitness matters
- Listen to YOUR body
- Get provider clearance for exercise

### C-Section Recovery

- Major abdominal surgery
- Generally slower timeline
- Different precautions
- Usually 6-8 weeks before cleared for exercise
- Sometimes longer

---

## Week 1: Rest and Recovery

### What's Happening

- Uterus contracting back
- Bleeding (lochia) heavy initially
- Hormones shifting dramatically
- Sleep disruption begins
- Soreness from birth

### What You Can Do

**Movement:**
- Gentle walking (as tolerated)
- Deep breathing
- Ankle circles in bed
- Getting up carefully

**Posture focus:**
- Feeding positions that support you
- Support for perineum when sitting
- Pillows for comfort
- Rest in good positions

### Avoid

- Lifting anything heavier than baby
- Stairs (if possible/C-section)
- Strenuous activity
- Rushing recovery

---

## Weeks 2-3: Beginning to Move

### What's Happening

- Bleeding decreasing
- Some energy returning (hopefully)
- Sleep deprivation ongoing
- Body still very healing

### What You Can Do

**Movement:**
- Longer gentle walks
- Pelvic floor awareness (gentle)
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Light stretching

**C-section:**
- Incision healing
- Walking encouraged
- Supporting incision when moving
- Very gentle only

### Posture Focus

- Nursing/feeding posture critical
- Baby-holding awareness
- Getting in/out of bed properly
- Standing feeds if helpful

---

## Weeks 4-6: Building Foundation

### What's Happening

- Body continuing to heal
- May have 6-week checkup
- Energy still variable
- Feeding/baby care demanding

### What You Can Do

**With provider approval:**
- Longer walks
- Gentle pelvic floor exercises (if appropriate)
- Basic core connection (not crunches)
- Light stretching
- Body weight squats

**Exercises:**
- Pelvic tilts
- Belly breathing
- Heel slides
- Gentle cat-cow
- Wall angels

### Not Yet

- Running or jumping
- Heavy lifting
- Ab crunches/planks
- High-intensity exercise
- Return to pre-pregnancy routine

---

## 6-Week Checkup

### What Gets Assessed

- Uterus returned to size
- Healing from birth
- Bleeding stopped
- General recovery

### What to Ask About

- Exercise clearance
- Pelvic floor assessment referral
- Specific limitations for you
- When to return if concerns

### Getting Cleared

"Cleared for exercise" means:
- Light-moderate activity okay
- Not "back to normal"
- Still need gradual progression
- Listen to your body

---

## Weeks 6-12: Gradual Return

### What's Happening

- More healed
- May have more energy (or not)
- Baby may have patterns emerging
- Body still adapting

### What You Can Do

**Exercise:**
- Longer walks
- Swimming (when bleeding stopped)
- Light resistance training
- Beginner yoga
- Pelvic floor exercises
- Core rehabilitation (not crunches)

**Start slowly:**
- 10-15 minutes initially
- Gradually increase
- Stop if pain
- Rest as needed

### Watch For

- Pain during/after exercise
- Leaking urine with activity
- Feeling of heaviness in pelvis
- Bleeding returning
- Coning of abdomen during exercise

---

## Months 3-6: Building Strength

### What's Happening

- More consistent energy
- Body continuing to adapt
- May be ready for more
- Sleep may improve (or not!)

### What You Can Do

**Exercise:**
- Longer duration sessions
- Moderate intensity
- Strength training progression
- Core training continuation
- Some women: begin running (gradually)

**Progression:**
- Add resistance
- Increase duration
- Increase frequency
- Still respect limits

### Individual Factors

- Breastfeeding affects recovery
- Sleep affects recovery
- Stress affects recovery
- Be patient with yourself

---

## Months 6-12: Continued Recovery

### What's Happening

- Many women feel more like themselves
- Physical recovery ongoing
- Some issues may emerge
- Return to activities possible

### What You Can Do

**Most can:**
- Most pre-pregnancy activities (modified)
- Higher intensity exercise
- Full strength training
- Running/HIIT (if pelvic floor ready)

**Continue:**
- Pelvic floor maintenance
- Core strengthening
- Gradual progression
- Listening to body

### When to Reassess

- Persistent pelvic floor symptoms
- Diastasis not improving
- Pain with activity
- Plateau in recovery

---

## Special Considerations

### Diastasis Recti

**What it is:**
- Separation of rectus abdominis
- Common in pregnancy
- Doesn't always resolve alone

**What helps:**
- Proper core rehabilitation
- Avoiding exercises that worsen
- PT assessment and guidance
- Time (months to improve)

### Pelvic Floor Dysfunction

**Signs:**
- Leaking urine
- Urgency
- Heaviness/prolapse feeling
- Pain

**What helps:**
- Pelvic floor PT
- Appropriate exercises
- Addressing root cause
- Time and consistency

### C-Section Recovery

**Additional considerations:**
- Scar tissue mobilization
- Core reconnection takes longer
- Nerve sensation changes
- Gentler progression

---

## Red Flags

### See Provider If

- Fever
- Excessive bleeding
- Foul-smelling discharge
- Signs of infection
- Depression/anxiety severe
- Pain that's worsening

### During Exercise

- Pain (stop activity)
- Heavy bleeding
- Leaking urine regularly
- Severe fatigue
- Dizziness

---

## Realistic Expectations

### What's Normal

- Feeling different than before
- Taking 6-12+ months for "recovery"
- Some things changing permanently
- Need for modifications
- Body looking/feeling different

### What's Not Normal

- Persistent significant pain
- Unable to control bladder/bowel
- Progressive symptoms
- Severe mental health struggles

### Be Kind to Yourself

- You created life
- Recovery is not a race
- Comparison is unhelpful
- Progress is not linear
- Rest is productive

---

## Week-by-Week Summary

| Timeframe | Focus | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Week 1 | Rest, gentle breathing | Everything strenuous |
| Weeks 2-3 | Walking, breathing | Lifting, exercise |
| Weeks 4-6 | Light movement | High intensity |
| Weeks 6-12 | Gradual return | Rushing progress |
| Months 3-6 | Building strength | Ignoring symptoms |
| Months 6-12 | Most activities | Still respect limits |
