# When to See a Doctor

> **Most Posture-Related Pain Is Manageable:** But some symptoms require professional evaluation. Here's how to know when to seek help and what to expect.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs

### Seek Immediate Care If

**Spinal cord symptoms:**
- Sudden numbness or weakness in legs
- Loss of bladder or bowel control
- Inability to urinate
- Numbness in groin/saddle area

**After trauma:**
- Severe pain after fall or accident
- Inability to move normally after injury
- Visible deformity

**Systemic symptoms:**
- Back pain with fever
- Unexplained weight loss with pain
- Severe night pain that wakes you
- Pain that doesn't change with position

**GO TO ER** for these symptoms.

---

## See Doctor Soon (Within Days)

### Pain Characteristics

- Pain lasting more than 4-6 weeks
- Pain that's getting progressively worse
- Pain that doesn't respond to self-care
- Pain that interferes with sleep
- Pain that limits daily activities

### Neurological Symptoms

- Numbness in arm or leg
- Tingling or "pins and needles"
- Weakness in muscles
- Radiating pain down limb
- Changes in reflexes

### Other Concerns

- History of cancer with new back pain
- History of osteoporosis with new pain
- Recent infection with spine pain
- Pain that started after medical procedure
- Unexplained symptoms

---

## Consider Professional Help

### When Self-Care Isn't Enough

- 2+ weeks of consistent effort with no improvement
- Symptoms that keep returning
- Pain that affects work/life quality
- Uncertainty about what's wrong
- Want guidance for specific condition

### For Prevention

- Want professional posture assessment
- Starting new exercise program
- Returning to activity after injury
- Pregnancy-related changes
- Age-related changes

---

## Types of Providers

### Primary Care Physician

**Good first stop for:**
- Initial evaluation
- Ruling out serious causes
- Referrals to specialists
- Medication if needed
- General guidance

### Orthopedic Doctor

**Specializes in:**
- Spine conditions
- Bone and joint issues
- Surgical options if needed
- Complex musculoskeletal problems

### Physical Therapist

**Specializes in:**
- Posture assessment
- Movement evaluation
- Exercise prescription
- Manual therapy
- Rehabilitation

**Note:** In many states, you can see a PT without referral.

### Chiropractor

**Offers:**
- Spinal adjustments
- Posture analysis
- Some exercise guidance
- Alternative approach

**Consider if:**
- You prefer this approach
- Problem is appropriate for chiropractic care
- Licensed, reputable practitioner

### Physiatrist (PM&R)

**Specializes in:**
- Non-surgical spine treatment
- Rehabilitation medicine
- Injection procedures
- Complex pain cases

### Pelvic Floor Physical Therapist

**Specializes in:**
- Pelvic floor dysfunction
- Pregnancy/postpartum issues
- Core and pelvic integration
- Women's health concerns

### Massage Therapist

**Offers:**
- Muscle tension relief
- Complementary care
- Relaxation
- Some therapeutic techniques

## What to Expect at Appointment

### Be Prepared

**Bring:**
- Pain journal/tracking (if you have it)
- List of symptoms
- Timeline of when symptoms started
- List of what you've tried
- Current medications
- Questions written down

### Typical Evaluation

**History:**
- When did it start?
- What makes it better/worse?
- Previous episodes?
- Other health conditions?
- What have you tried?

**Physical exam:**
- Posture assessment
- Range of motion testing
- Strength testing
- Neurological exam
- Special tests for specific conditions

**Possibly:**
- Imaging (X-ray, MRI, etc.)
- Blood tests
- Referral to specialist

---

## Imaging: When and Why

### Not Always Needed

**Most musculoskeletal pain:**
- Doesn't require imaging initially
- Diagnosis based on history and exam
- Images don't always correlate with pain
- Many "findings" are normal for age

### When Imaging Is Appropriate

- Red flag symptoms present
- Not improving with conservative care
- Neurological symptoms
- History suggesting specific condition
- Planning for procedure/surgery

### Types

**X-ray:**
- Bones
- Alignment
- Quick and accessible

**MRI:**
- Soft tissues (discs, muscles, nerves)
- More detailed
- No radiation
- More expensive

**CT:**
- Detailed bone images
- Certain conditions
- Radiation involved

## Questions to Ask Your Provider

### About Diagnosis

- What do you think is causing my pain?
- What are other possible causes?
- Do I need any tests?
- What is the likely outcome?

### About Treatment

- What are my treatment options?
- What do you recommend and why?
- What are the risks and benefits?
- How long until I should expect improvement?

### About Activity

- Should I rest or stay active?
- Are there activities I should avoid?
- What exercises might help?
- When can I return to normal activity?

### About Follow-Up

- When should I come back?
- What should prompt earlier return?
- When should I see improvement?
- Who else should I see?

## Red Flags by Body Part

### Neck

- Severe pain after trauma
- Progressive weakness in arms
- Difficulty with balance/walking
- Bowel/bladder changes
- Severe headache (new type)

### Upper Back

- Chest pain (rule out heart)
- Breathing difficulties
- Pain with swallowing
- Severe unexplained pain

### Lower Back

- Leg weakness
- Saddle numbness
- Bladder/bowel dysfunction
- Fever
- Night pain
- Significant trauma

### General

- Unexplained weight loss
- Cancer history with new pain
- Severe pain not relieved by any position
- Progressive symptoms

---

## Building a Care Team

### For Chronic Issues

You may benefit from:
- Primary care for overall coordination
- Specialist for specific condition
- Physical therapist for exercise/function
- Possibly massage for maintenance
- Mental health support if pain affects mood

### Communication

- Keep all providers informed
- Share what's working/not working
- Ask about coordination
- Be honest about all treatments

---

## When Pain Is "Just" Muscular

### Most Pain Is Manageable

- Not dangerous
- Will improve with time and care
- Doesn't require extensive treatment
- Responds to self-care

### But Get Help If

- You're unsure
- Self-care isn't working
- You want professional guidance
- Pain is affecting quality of life
