# Workplace Ergonomics Checklist

> **Set Up for Success:** Use this comprehensive checklist to create an ergonomic workspace that supports good posture all day.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Go through each section
2. Check off items you've addressed
3. Note items that need improvement
4. Make changes and reassess
5. Review periodically (monthly)

---

## Chair Setup

### Seat Height
☐ Feet flat on floor (or footrest)
☐ Thighs parallel to floor
☐ Knees at 90-100 degree angle
☐ No pressure behind knees

### Seat Depth
☐ 2-3 finger widths between back of knees and seat edge
☐ Full thigh support
☐ Sit all the way back in chair

### Lumbar Support
☐ Lower back supported
☐ Natural curve maintained
☐ Add lumbar pillow if chair lacks support
☐ Not sitting slumped

### Armrests (If Present)
☐ Shoulders relaxed when using
☐ Elbows at 90 degrees
☐ Arms not reaching up or pressing down
☐ Can be removed or adjusted if problematic

### Overall
☐ Chair allows you to sit back
☐ Can move and adjust throughout day
☐ Chair appropriate for your height and size

---

## Desk/Surface Setup

### Desk Height
☐ Forearms parallel to floor when typing
☐ Shoulders relaxed (not shrugged)
☐ Wrists in neutral position
☐ Elbows at about 90 degrees

### Desk Space
☐ Enough room for equipment
☐ Frequently used items within easy reach
☐ Infrequently used items can require reaching
☐ Surface organized to prevent overreaching

### Under Desk
☐ Room for legs to move
☐ No obstacles blocking foot placement
☐ Can stretch legs
☐ Footrest if feet don't reach floor

---

## Monitor Setup

### Monitor Height
☐ Top of screen at or slightly below eye level
☐ Can view screen without looking up
☐ Can view screen without looking down significantly
☐ Neck neutral when viewing

### Monitor Distance
☐ Approximately arm's length away
☐ Can read text without leaning forward
☐ Not too far that you squint
☐ Can be adjusted if needed

### Monitor Angle
☐ Screen tilted slightly back (10-20 degrees)
☐ No glare on screen
☐ Not reflecting windows or lights
☐ Brightness appropriate for environment

### Multiple Monitors
☐ Primary monitor directly in front
☐ Secondary monitor adjacent (not requiring twist)
☐ Both at same height
☐ Distance equal for both

### Laptop Considerations
☐ Raised to eye level if used for extended periods
☐ External keyboard and mouse added
☐ Not hunching over laptop on desk
☐ Laptop stand or books to elevate

---

## Keyboard and Mouse

### Keyboard Position
☐ Directly in front of you
☐ At elbow height or slightly below
☐ Close enough that you don't reach
☐ Wrists neutral (not bent up or down)

### Keyboard Angle
☐ Flat or slightly negative angle
☐ Keyboard legs NOT extended (usually)
☐ Wrist rest used only during pauses (not while typing)

### Mouse Position
☐ Next to keyboard (same level)
☐ Close enough to not require reaching
☐ Can use without extending arm
☐ Wrist neutral when mousing

### Mouse/Keyboard Type
☐ Appropriate size for your hands
☐ Comfortable to use
☐ Consider ergonomic options if issues persist
☐ Split keyboard if experiencing symptoms

---

## Lighting

### General Lighting
☐ Adequate light to see work
☐ Not too bright (causing squinting)
☐ Even lighting (no harsh shadows)
☐ Can be adjusted if needed

### Monitor and Light Interaction
☐ No glare on screen
☐ Window to the side (not behind or in front)
☐ Blinds/curtains available if needed
☐ Monitor not brighter than surroundings

### Task Lighting
☐ Available for reading/paperwork if needed
☐ Positioned to not cause glare
☐ Adjustable

---

## Document/Reference Materials

### Document Holder
☐ Documents at same height as monitor (if looking at frequently)
☐ Between keyboard and monitor, or beside monitor
☐ Don't need to look down at desk for documents
☐ Reduces neck rotation

### Phone Use
☐ Phone on same side as dominant hand
☐ Headset if on phone frequently
☐ Not cradling phone between ear and shoulder
☐ Phone within reach without stretching

---

## Movement and Breaks

### Timer/Reminders
☐ Reminder set for breaks (every 30-60 minutes)
☐ Uses app, watch, or other reminder system
☐ Actually takes breaks when reminded
☐ Variety in break activities

### Standing Options
☐ Sit-stand desk available (ideal)
☐ High counter/surface for standing work (alternative)
☐ Standing mat if standing on hard floor
☐ Transitions between sitting and standing

### Movement Opportunities
☐ Printer/supplies require getting up
☐ Can walk to meetings
☐ Restroom breaks used as movement breaks
☐ Walking meetings when possible

---

## Posture Habits

### Sitting Posture
☐ Sits back in chair
☐ Uses lumbar support
☐ Feet flat (or on footrest)
☐ Shoulders relaxed

### Posture Checks
☐ Regular self-checks throughout day
☐ Uses posture cues or reminders
☐ Corrects when notice slouching
☐ Stretches when tight

### Work Positions
☐ Varies positions throughout day
☐ Not locked in one position for hours
☐ Reclines occasionally
☐ Stands if possible

---

## Common Problem Areas

### Eye Strain
☐ 20-20-20 rule followed (every 20 min, look 20 feet away, for 20 seconds)
☐ Screen brightness appropriate
☐ Font size adequate
☐ Blue light filter if needed

### Neck Tension
☐ Monitor height correct
☐ Not looking down at phone
☐ Phone/headset for calls
☐ Documents at eye level

### Lower Back Pain
☐ Lumbar support adequate
☐ Not sitting slumped
☐ Takes movement breaks
☐ Sits all the way back in chair

### Wrist/Hand Issues
☐ Wrists neutral when typing
☐ Not resting wrists while typing
☐ Mouse appropriate size
☐ Taking breaks from repetitive motions

### Shoulder Tension
☐ Shoulders relaxed (not shrugged)
☐ Arms supported or relaxed at sides
☐ Not reaching for mouse/keyboard
☐ Takes shoulder stretch breaks

---

## Home Office Additional Considerations

☐ Dedicated workspace (not couch)
☐ Same ergonomic principles as office
☐ Proper equipment (chair, desk, monitor)
☐ Good lighting
☐ Minimal distractions
☐ Separation between work and living space if possible

---

## Quick Daily Setup Check

Before starting work each day:

1. ☐ Chair adjusted correctly
2. ☐ Feet on floor or footrest
3. ☐ Monitor at right height
4. ☐ Keyboard and mouse positioned well
5. ☐ Shoulders relaxed
6. ☐ Break timer/reminder set

---

## When to Seek Help

Consider consulting an ergonomist or occupational health professional if:
- Pain persists despite adjustments
- Multiple problem areas
- Complex setup needs
- Employer offers assessment
- Returning from injury
