# Tibialis Posterior

The tibialis posterior is the deepest muscle of the posterior leg. It's the primary dynamic support for the medial arch and commonly involved in flat foot development.

## Anatomy

### Location

**Deep calf:**
- Behind tibia and fibula
- Deepest posterior muscle
- Long tendon wrapping behind ankle

### Attachments

**Origin:**
- Posterior tibia
- Posterior fibula
- Interosseous membrane

**Insertion:**
- Navicular bone
- Multiple foot bones

## Function

### Primary Actions

**Plantarflexion:**
- Pointing foot down

**Inversion:**
- Turning sole inward

**Arch support:**
- Primary dynamic support
- Critical for foot function

### During Walking

**Support phase:**
- Maintains arch
- Controls pronation
- Propulsion assistance

## Tibialis Posterior Dysfunction

### What Happens

**PTTD:**
- Tendon weakens
- Arch support decreases
- Flat foot develops progressively

### Stages

1. Pain and swelling
2. Arch begins to flatten
3. Significant deformity
4. Fixed deformity

### Risk Factors

- Age over 40
- Female gender
- Obesity
- Previous injury

## Prevention & Treatment

### Strengthen

- Heel raises
- Single-leg balance
- Resistance band exercises
- Toe raises

### Support

- Proper footwear
- Orthotics if needed
- Avoid barefoot on hard surfaces
