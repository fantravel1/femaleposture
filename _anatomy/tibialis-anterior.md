# Tibialis Anterior

The tibialis anterior is the main muscle on the front of the shin. It's essential for lifting the foot during walking and supporting the arch.

## Anatomy

### Location

**Anterior shin:**
- Lateral to tibia
- Easily palpable
- Long tendon to foot

### Attachments

**Origin:**
- Lateral tibial condyle
- Upper 2/3 of tibia
- Interosseous membrane

**Insertion:**
- Medial cuneiform
- First metatarsal base

## Function

### Primary Actions

**Dorsiflexion:**
- Lifts foot up
- Clears foot during walking
- Heel strike control

**Inversion:**
- Turns sole inward
- Supports medial arch

### Walking Role

**During gait:**
- Lifts foot in swing phase
- Controls foot at heel strike
- Prevents slapping

## Common Issues

### Shin Splints

**Medial tibial stress:**
- Overuse
- Running on hard surfaces
- Weak tibialis anterior

### Foot Drop

**When weak or damaged:**
- Can't lift foot
- Trip hazard
- May be neurological

### Tightness

**From:**
- Hiking
- Excessive walking
- Running uphill

## Exercises

### Strengthening

- Heel walks
- Toe raises
- Resistance band dorsiflexion

### Stretching

- Point toes and curl
- Kneel with toes pointed
- Wall stretch (reverse)
