# Hip Muscles

The hip is surrounded by powerful muscles controlling movement in all directions. Hip muscle balance is essential for pelvic positioning and spinal alignment.

## Muscle Groups

### Hip Flexors

**Lift thigh toward trunk:**
- Iliopsoas (primary)
- Rectus femoris
- Sartorius
- TFL

[See detailed: Hip Flexors](/anatomy/hip-flexors/)

### Hip Extensors

**Push thigh backward:**
- Gluteus maximus (primary)
- Hamstrings
- Adductor magnus (posterior)

[See detailed: Glute Muscles](/anatomy/glute-muscles/)

### Hip Abductors

**Lift leg out to side:**
- Gluteus medius
- Gluteus minimus
- TFL

### Hip Adductors

**Bring leg toward midline:**
- Adductor longus
- Adductor brevis
- Adductor magnus
- Gracilis
- Pectineus

### Hip Rotators

**External rotators (deep six):**
- Piriformis
- Obturator internus/externus
- Gemelli (superior/inferior)
- Quadratus femoris

**Internal rotators:**
- TFL
- Gluteus medius/minimus (anterior fibers)
- Adductors

## Function

### Movement Production

**Multi-directional:**
- Walking and running
- Climbing
- Squatting
- Lunging
- Dancing

### Pelvic Stability

**During single-leg activities:**
- Gluteus medius prevents hip drop
- Critical for walking
- Affects whole chain

### Pelvic Position

**Anterior/posterior tilt:**
- Hip flexors vs. extensors
- Abdominals vs. erectors
- Balance determines tilt

## Women's Considerations

### Wider Pelvis

**Effects:**
- Different muscle angles
- Q-angle at knee
- May need more hip strengthening

### Common Patterns

**Frequently weak:**
- Gluteus medius
- Gluteus maximus
- Deep rotators

**Frequently tight:**
- Hip flexors
- TFL
- Piriformis

## Assessment

### Hip Mobility

**Flexion:** ~120 degrees
**Extension:** ~20-30 degrees
**Abduction:** ~45 degrees
**Adduction:** ~30 degrees
**External rotation:** ~45 degrees
**Internal rotation:** ~45 degrees
