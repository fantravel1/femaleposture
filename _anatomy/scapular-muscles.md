# Scapular Muscles

The scapula (shoulder blade) is controlled by multiple muscles that position it for optimal shoulder function. Scapular stability is fundamental to shoulder health and upper body posture.

## Muscles Controlling the Scapula

### Serratus Anterior

**"Boxer's muscle":**
- Wraps around ribs
- Protracts scapula
- Upward rotation
- Holds scapula to ribs

**When weak:** Winging scapula

### Trapezius

**Three parts:**
- Upper: Elevates
- Middle: Retracts
- Lower: Depresses, upward rotates

[See detailed: Trapezius](/anatomy/trapezius/)

### Rhomboids

**Retraction:**
- Pull scapulae together
- Downward rotation

[See detailed: Rhomboids](/anatomy/rhomboids/)

### Levator Scapulae

**Elevation:**
- Lifts scapula
- Often tight
- Connects to neck

### Pectoralis Minor

**Protraction and depression:**
- Tilts scapula forward
- Often short/tight
- Contributes to rounded shoulders

## Scapular Movements

### Six Movements

1. **Elevation** - Shoulder shrug up
2. **Depression** - Shoulders down
3. **Protraction** - Forward (rounding)
4. **Retraction** - Back (squeezing)
5. **Upward rotation** - For overhead reach
6. **Downward rotation** - Return from overhead

## Common Problems

### Scapular Winging

**Scapula lifts off ribs:**
- Serratus anterior weakness
- Nerve injury possible
- Affects arm function

### Scapular Dyskinesis

**Abnormal movement:**
- Poor muscle control
- Compensation patterns
- Shoulder impingement risk

## Importance for Posture

### Foundation for Shoulder

**If scapula unstable:**
- Shoulder works harder
- Compensation patterns
- Injury risk increases

### Upper Body Alignment

**Scapular position affects:**
- Neck posture
- Thoracic posture
- Arm function
- Breathing

## Exercises

### Strengthen

- Push-ups plus (serratus)
- Face pulls
- Rows
- Y-T-I raises
- Scapular push-ups

### Stabilize

- Planks
- Farmer carries
- Overhead holds
- Crawling patterns
