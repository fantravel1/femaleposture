# Forearm Muscles

The forearm contains numerous muscles controlling the wrist and fingers. Their health is essential for computer work, carrying, and daily activities.

## Anatomy

### Flexor Compartment (Front)

**Superficial:**
- Pronator teres
- Flexor carpi radialis
- Palmaris longus
- Flexor carpi ulnaris
- Flexor digitorum superficialis

**Deep:**
- Flexor digitorum profundus
- Flexor pollicis longus
- Pronator quadratus

### Extensor Compartment (Back)

**Superficial:**
- Brachioradialis
- Extensor carpi radialis longus/brevis
- Extensor digitorum
- Extensor carpi ulnaris

**Deep:**
- Supinator
- Abductor pollicis longus
- Extensor pollicis brevis/longus
- Extensor indicis

## Function

### Wrist Movements

**Flexion:** Bending palm toward forearm
**Extension:** Bending back of hand toward forearm
**Radial deviation:** Toward thumb side
**Ulnar deviation:** Toward pinky side

### Hand Function

**Gripping:** Flexors
**Releasing:** Extensors
**Fine motor:** Coordination of both

## Common Problems

### Overuse Issues

**From repetitive work:**
- Typing
- Mouse use
- Gripping tools

**Conditions:**
- Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis)
- Golfer's elbow (medial epicondylitis)
- Carpal tunnel syndrome

### Prevention

- Regular breaks
- Stretching
- Ergonomic setup
- Strengthening

## Stretching

### Flexor Stretch

- Extend arm, palm up
- Pull fingers down with other hand

### Extensor Stretch

- Extend arm, palm down
- Pull hand down
