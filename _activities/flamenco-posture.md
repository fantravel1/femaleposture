# Flamenco Posture

Flamenco demands proud carriage and precise footwork. Proper posture enables authentic expression.

## Body Carriage

### Stance

**Proud posture:**
- Tall spine
- Chest lifted
- Shoulders back
- Chin elevated
- Strong presence

### Core Position

**Center of power:**
- Very engaged
- Stable platform
- Power source
- Upper/lower connection
- Movement foundation

## Arms

### Port de Bras

**Arm carriage:**
- Lifted from back
- Wrists articulate
- Fingers alive
- Sustained energy
- Expressive movement

### Hand Movements

**Floreo:**
- Wrist rotation
- Finger articulation
- Sequential movement
- Expressive quality
- Personal styling

## Footwork

### Zapateado

**Rhythmic footwork:**
- Weight in standing leg
- Precise strikes
- Clean sounds
- Rhythmic patterns
- Musical expression

### Technique

**Sound production:**
- Ball strikes
- Heel strikes
- Full foot
- Toe taps
- Complex patterns

## Upper Body

### Chest

**Torso movement:**
- Isolation possible
- Held high
- Rotates with expression
- Power and grace
- Character expression

### Head

**Dramatic movement:**
- Controlled turns
- Sharp looks
- Dramatic pauses
- Passionate expression
- Musical accents

## Expression

### Duende

**Emotional depth:**
- Inner fire
- Passionate expression
- Authentic emotion
- Musical connection
- Personal truth

Good flamenco posture creates the proud, passionate presence flamenco demands.
