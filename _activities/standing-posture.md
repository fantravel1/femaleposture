# Standing Posture

Standing seems simple, but hours on your feet can strain the body. Proper standing posture protects your back, legs, and feet while maintaining comfort and energy.

## Standing Alignment

### Proper Position

**Aligned stance:**
- Weight evenly distributed
- Feet hip-width apart
- Knees soft, not locked
- Pelvis neutral
- Shoulders relaxed

### Head Position

**Upper body:**
- Head balanced
- Chin level
- Eyes forward
- Neck relaxed
- Shoulders down

## Standing for Work

### Standing Desk

**Workstation setup:**
- Screen at eye level
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Footrest option
- Sit-stand alternation

### Standing Jobs

**Occupational standing:**
- Supportive footwear
- Compression if needed
- Regular breaks
- Position changes
- Floor surface matters

## Managing Standing Fatigue

### Movement While Standing

**Staying comfortable:**
- Weight shifting
- Small steps
- Calf raises
- Posture resets
- Regular movement

### Break Strategies

**Rest opportunities:**
- Sit when possible
- Lean when appropriate
- Elevate feet
- Walk around
- Stretch regularly

## Common Standing Issues

### Lower Back Pain

**Addressing:**
- Core engagement
- Pelvic position
- Footwear check
- Standing surface
- Position variety

### Leg and Foot Pain

**Relief strategies:**
- Quality shoes
- Compression wear
- Foot exercises
- Elevation when resting
- Massage and care

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Footwear

**Shoe choices:**
- Supportive options
- Heel height impact
- Cushioning needs
- Fit considerations
- Work-appropriate options

Good standing posture is an active practice that protects your body through long hours on your feet.
