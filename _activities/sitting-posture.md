# Sitting Posture

Most women spend significant hours sittingâ€”at work, while commuting, and at home. Proper sitting posture protects your body from the health effects of sedentary time.

## Chair Positioning

### Basic Setup

**Proper alignment:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Knees at 90 degrees
- Thighs parallel to floor
- Back supported
- Shoulders relaxed

### Chair Adjustments

**Optimizing seat:**
- Height appropriate
- Lumbar support
- Seat depth
- Armrest positioning
- Tilt if available

## Sitting Alignment

### Lower Body

**Base position:**
- Weight on sit bones
- Thighs supported
- Feet grounded
- Hips at or above knees
- Legs not crossed for long

### Upper Body

**Torso position:**
- Spine tall
- Core gently engaged
- Shoulders back and down
- Head balanced
- Chin level

## Movement While Sitting

### Position Variety

**Changing up:**
- Shift positions regularly
- Cross legs briefly
- Sit forward, then back
- Use different chairs
- Stand intermittently

### Seated Exercises

**Desk movements:**
- Seated marching
- Ankle circles
- Shoulder rolls
- Neck stretches
- Core engagement

## Breaking Up Sitting

### Regular Breaks

**Movement goals:**
- Stand every 30-60 minutes
- Walking breaks
- Stretch routine
- Posture reset
- Movement snacks

### Standing Options

**Alternating:**
- Standing desk periods
- Walking meetings
- Standing phone calls
- Active commute if possible
- Evening activity

## Common Sitting Issues

### Lower Back Pain

**Solutions:**
- Lumbar support
- Chair adjustment
- Frequent breaks
- Core strengthening
- Flexibility work

Good sitting posture combined with regular movement protects your body from sedentary-related problems.
