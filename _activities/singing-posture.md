# Singing Posture

Singing requires specific body alignment for optimal breath support and vocal production. Proper singing posture is essential for both technique and vocal health.

## Standing Position

### Basic Alignment

**Singer's stance:**
- Feet hip-width apart
- Weight balanced
- Knees soft
- Pelvis neutral
- Spine tall

### Upper Body

**Chest and shoulders:**
- Chest lifted naturally
- Shoulders back and down
- Neck long
- Head balanced
- Jaw relaxed

## Breath Support

### Body for Breathing

**Support posture:**
- Ribcage expanded
- Core engaged for support
- Low breath
- No chest heaving
- Relaxed throat

### Abdominal Engagement

**Breath control:**
- Core stability
- Diaphragm freedom
- Low belly involvement
- Back expansion
- Controlled release

## Performance Posture

### Stage Presence

**Performing stance:**
- Confident position
- Grounded feet
- Open chest
- Expressive face
- Whole body engaged

### Movement Integration

**While singing:**
- Gesture freely
- Maintain support
- Move naturally
- Return to home position
- Stay grounded

## Common Issues

### Tension Areas

**What to release:**
- Jaw tension
- Tongue tension
- Shoulder lifting
- Neck strain
- Locked knees

### Prevention

**Healthy singing:**
- Warm-up properly
- Body awareness
- Regular practice
- Vocal rest
- Teacher guidance

Good singing posture creates the physical conditions for your best vocal production while protecting your voice for years of singing.
