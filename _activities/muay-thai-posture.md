# Muay Thai Posture

Muay Thai uses eight striking points. Proper posture enables powerful, balanced technique.

## Fighting Stance

### Thai Stance

**Basic position:**
- Square to narrow
- Weight evenly distributed
- Hands high
- Chin protected
- Core engaged

### Guard Position

**Defensive ready:**
- Elbows close
- Hands protecting face
- Chin tucked
- Shoulders slightly raised
- Ready for action

## Strikes

### Punches

**Western influence:**
- Jab and cross
- Hooks and uppercuts
- Similar to boxing
- Set up for kicks
- Core rotation

### Elbows

**Close range:**
- Various angles
- Devastating power
- Clinch weapon
- Controlled training
- Proper safety

### Knees

**Core weapon:**
- Drive from hip
- Core engagement
- Balance on support leg
- Various angles
- Clinch essential

### Kicks

**Thai round kick:**
- Full body rotation
- Shin connection
- Hip turnover
- Follow through
- Power generation

## Clinch

### Position

**Control position:**
- Collar ties
- Elbow control
- Core engaged
- Balance fighting
- Knee opportunity

### Offense

**From clinch:**
- Knee strikes
- Elbow opportunities
- Off-balancing
- Control fighting
- Strategic position

## Training

### Pad Work

**Skill development:**
- Technique refinement
- Power building
- Timing development
- Conditioning
- Real application

Good Muay Thai posture enables powerful eight-limb striking.
