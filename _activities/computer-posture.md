# Computer Posture

Computer work dominates many women's days, making proper screen ergonomics essential for long-term health. Understanding workstation setup protects your body through hours of screen time.

## Workstation Setup

### Monitor Position

**Screen placement:**
- Top of screen at eye level
- Arm's length distance
- Directly in front
- Reduce glare
- Proper brightness

### Keyboard and Mouse

**Input devices:**
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Wrists neutral
- Mouse close to keyboard
- Elbow at 90 degrees
- Relaxed shoulders

### Chair Setup

**Seating position:**
- Feet flat or on footrest
- Knees at 90 degrees
- Back supported
- Armrests at elbow height
- Comfortable seat depth

## Body Position

### Upper Body

**Posture elements:**
- Head balanced over spine
- Chin level
- Shoulders relaxed
- Upper arms at sides
- Elbows at 90 degrees

### Lower Body

**Foundation:**
- Hips at or above knees
- Thighs supported
- Feet grounded
- Weight on sit bones
- Core gently engaged

## Laptop Considerations

### Ergonomic Challenges

**Laptop issues:**
- Screen too low
- Keyboard at wrong height
- Encourages looking down
- Not ideal long-term
- Modifications needed

### Solutions

**Better laptop setup:**
- External keyboard
- External mouse
- Laptop stand or riser
- External monitor
- Proper accessories

## Movement and Breaks

### Regular Breaks

**Break routine:**
- 20-20-20 rule for eyes
- Stand every 30-60 minutes
- Stretch and move
- Walk if possible
- Position changes

### Desk Exercises

**Seated movements:**
- Neck stretches
- Shoulder rolls
- Wrist circles
- Seated twists
- Core engagement

Good computer posture combined with regular movement protects your body through workdays of screen time.
