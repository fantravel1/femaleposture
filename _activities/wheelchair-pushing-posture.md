# Wheelchair Pushing Posture

Pushing wheelchairs requires proper technique. Proper posture protects caregiver while ensuring safe transport.

## Handle Position

### Grip Technique

**Holding handles:**
- Both hands
- Relaxed grip
- Arms at comfortable angle
- Elbows slightly bent
- Control maintained

### Standing Position

**Behind wheelchair:**
- Upright stance
- Core engaged
- Close to chair
- Balanced position
- Ready to push

## Pushing Technique

### Forward Motion

**Moving chair:**
- Push from core
- Steady pace
- Smooth movement
- Watch ahead
- Controlled motion

### Turning

**Directional changes:**
- Slow for turns
- Both hands steering
- Gradual curves
- Announce turns
- Safe navigation

## Terrain Considerations

### Slopes and Ramps

**Inclines:**
- Extra care
- Controlled descent
- Backward down steep ramps
- Brake awareness
- Safe handling

### Curbs and Obstacles

**Navigation:**
- Tip chair back for curbs
- Get assistance
- Know limits
- Safety first
- Careful navigation

## Communication

### With Person

**Care recipient:**
- Communicate movements
- Announce direction
- Ask preferences
- Respectful care
- Connected assistance

## Extended Pushing

### Long Distances

**Sustained pushing:**
- Pace yourself
- Take breaks
- Sustainable effort
- Know limits
- Quality assistance

Good wheelchair pushing posture ensures safe transport and caregiver protection.
