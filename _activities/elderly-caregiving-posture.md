# Elderly Caregiving Posture

Caregiving involves physical assistance. Proper posture protects both caregiver and care recipient.

## Assisting Standing

### Help Rising

**Assisting up:**
- Face the person
- Wide stance
- Bend at knees
- Core engaged
- Lift with legs

### Support Walking

**Gait assistance:**
- Walk beside
- Arm support
- Match their pace
- Steady presence
- Safe ambulation

## Transfer Techniques

### Bed to Chair

**Safe transfers:**
- Proper training essential
- Use transfer belt
- Lift with legs
- Core braced
- Smooth movement

### Lifting Cautions

**Protect yourself:**
- Know limits
- Get help
- Use equipment
- Proper technique
- Injury prevention

## Bending and Reaching

### Personal Care

**Assisting ADLs:**
- Kneel or squat
- Get close
- Don't overreach
- Comfortable positioning
- Sustainable care

### Dressing Assistance

**Helping dress:**
- Comfortable position
- Patient approach
- Efficient technique
- Protective posture
- Quality assistance

## Extended Caregiving

### Sustained Care

**Long hours:**
- Take breaks
- Self-care essential
- Sustainable pace
- Get help
- Protect yourself

### Emotional and Physical

**Whole person care:**
- Physical health matters
- Body care for caregiver
- Sustainable practice
- Support systems
- Long-term caregiving

Good caregiving posture protects caregiver health while providing quality care.
