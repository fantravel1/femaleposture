# Sleeping Posture

You spend a third of your life sleepingâ€”how you position your body during those hours significantly affects your waking posture and pain levels. Understanding proper sleeping posture improves both sleep quality and daytime comfort.

## Sleep Position Basics

### Side Sleeping

**Most common position:**
- Knees slightly bent
- Pillow between knees
- Spine aligned
- Pillow height matching shoulder
- Arms comfortable

### Back Sleeping

**Neutral position:**
- Pillow supporting head
- Small pillow under knees
- Neutral spine
- Arms at sides or on chest
- Relaxed position

### Stomach Sleeping

**Challenging position:**
- Generally not recommended
- Neck rotation strain
- Low back compression
- If necessary, flat pillow
- Pillow under pelvis option

## Pillow Selection

### For Side Sleepers

**Proper support:**
- Higher loft
- Fills shoulder gap
- Neutral neck
- Firm enough support
- Quality materials

### For Back Sleepers

**Lower profile:**
- Medium loft
- Supports natural curve
- Not pushing head forward
- Comfortable width
- Appropriate firmness

## Mattress Considerations

### Firmness Selection

**Finding right level:**
- Body weight factor
- Sleep position factor
- Pain considerations
- Personal preference
- Trial period use

### Women-Specific Needs

**Female considerations:**
- Hip support for side sleepers
- Shoulder accommodation
- Temperature regulation
- Weight distribution
- Changing needs over time

## Waking Up

### Morning Transition

**Safe rising:**
- Roll to side first
- Use arms to push up
- Swing legs over
- Sit before standing
- Stretch gently

## Common Sleep Issues

### Pain on Waking

**Addressing problems:**
- Position evaluation
- Pillow adjustment
- Mattress assessment
- Sleep environment
- Professional consultation if persistent

Good sleeping posture supports restorative rest and prevents the back and neck pain that can result from poor nighttime positioning.
