# Racewalking Posture

Racewalking requires specific technique for speed and rules. Proper form distinguishes from running.

## Basic Requirements

### Rule Compliance

**Legal walking:**
- Straight leg at contact
- Contact with ground maintained
- No visible flight phase
- Judge observation
- Technical compliance

### Stance Phase

**Ground contact:**
- Heel strike
- Leg straightens
- Roll through
- Push off
- Continuous contact

## Hip Movement

### Rotation

**Key to speed:**
- Hip drops on swing side
- Rotation creates stride
- Core controls motion
- Smooth undulation
- Maximum efficiency

### Range of Motion

**Developing flexibility:**
- Hip mobility work
- Progressive range
- Natural movement
- Efficient mechanics
- Practice needed

## Arm Action

### Driving Arms

**Upper body contribution:**
- Bent at 90 degrees
- Drive back powerfully
- Hands relaxed
- Shoulder rotation
- Coordinated with hips

### Cadence

**Arm speed:**
- Quick turnover
- Matches leg cadence
- Power generator
- Rhythm maintained
- Efficient motion

## Core Engagement

### Stability

**Center of body:**
- Strong core
- Controls rotation
- Transfers power
- Maintains form
- Prevents injury

## Training

### Technique Development

**Building form:**
- Drills for elements
- Video analysis
- Coach feedback
- Progressive speed
- Long term development

Good racewalking posture creates legal, efficient, and fast walking.
