# Snow Skiing Posture

Snow skiing demands dynamic, balanced positioning. Proper posture enables controlled, confident skiing.

## Basic Stance

### Athletic Position

**Ready stance:**
- Feet hip-width
- Knees bent
- Ankles flexed
- Hips over feet
- Core engaged

### Arm Position

**Pole ready:**
- Hands forward
- Elbows bent
- Poles angled back
- Relaxed shoulders
- Ready for plants

## Weight Distribution

### Balanced Center

**On skis:**
- Weight centered
- Slight forward lean
- Shin pressure on boot
- Hips over bindings
- Balanced position

### Terrain Adaptation

**Varied conditions:**
- More forward on steep
- Centered on flats
- Active adjustment
- Terrain responsive
- Dynamic balance

## Turning

### Initiation

**Starting turns:**
- Weight shift
- Edge engagement
- Core rotation
- Pole plant
- Smooth transition

### Carved Turn

**Edge work:**
- Angulation
- Pressure control
- Arc through turn
- Edge angle
- Speed control

## Moguls

### Bump Technique

**Uneven terrain:**
- Absorption
- Quick turns
- Line selection
- Pole plants
- Lower body active

## Safety

### Controlled Skiing

**Safe practices:**
- Speed appropriate
- Look ahead
- Ski in control
- Know limits
- Progressive learning

Good snow skiing posture creates dynamic, controlled downhill skiing.
