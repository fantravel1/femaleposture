# Foam Rolling Posture

Foam rolling provides self-massage for muscle recovery. Proper positioning makes rolling effective and comfortable.

## Basic Principles

### Body Weight Control

**Pressure management:**
- Control how much weight on roller
- Start lighter
- Increase gradually
- Breathe through
- Not too intense

### Movement Pattern

**Rolling technique:**
- Slow movements
- Pause on tender spots
- Not too fast
- Controlled pressure
- Breathe throughout

## Upper Body Rolling

### Upper Back

**Thoracic spine:**
- Roller under upper back
- Support head
- Core engaged
- Roll slowly
- Avoid lower back

### Lats

**Side body:**
- Arm extended
- Roll from armpit down
- Control pressure
- Both sides
- Slow movement

## Lower Body Rolling

### Quads

**Front thigh:**
- Face down position
- Support with arms
- Roll from hip to knee
- Control pressure
- Both legs

### IT Band

**Outer thigh:**
- Side position
- Support with arms
- Roll slowly
- Very tender area
- Moderate pressure

### Hamstrings

**Back of thigh:**
- Sit on roller
- Support with arms
- Roll from hip to knee
- Control pressure
- Both legs

### Calves

**Lower leg:**
- Sit on floor
- Roller under calves
- Roll from knee to ankle
- Cross legs for more pressure
- Slow movement

Good foam rolling posture allows effective self-massage while protecting joints and spine.
