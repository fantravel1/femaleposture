# TRX Training Posture

TRX suspension training uses body weight and angles. Proper posture enables effective, scalable workouts.

## Principles

### Body Angle

**Resistance control:**
- Steeper angle harder
- Shallower easier
- Adjust by stepping
- Scalable intensity
- Personal progression

### Core Engagement

**Throughout:**
- Always engaged
- Plank-like body
- No sagging
- Controlled motion
- Foundation of TRX

## Upper Body

### Rows

**Pulling:**
- Lean back
- Arms extend
- Pull to chest
- Squeeze back
- Lower controlled

### Chest Press

**Pushing:**
- Facing away
- Arms extend
- Lower with control
- Push through
- Core stable

### Tricep Press

**Arm work:**
- Facing away
- Bend elbows
- Extend arms
- Controlled motion
- Isolation focus

## Lower Body

### Squats

**Leg work:**
- Hold handles
- Sit back
- Use for balance
- Full depth
- Stand strong

### Lunges

**Single leg:**
- Foot in strap
- Stable standing leg
- Controlled lunge
- Core engaged
- Balance challenge

## Core

### Plank Variations

**Core stability:**
- Feet in straps
- Plank position
- Various movements
- Instability challenge
- Core strength

## Benefits

### TRX Advantages

**Why suspension:**
- Portable
- Scalable
- Core constant
- Functional
- Joint friendly

Good TRX posture creates effective bodyweight training with scalable challenge.
