# Calligraphy Posture

Calligraphy demands precise control and sustained focus. Proper posture enables beautiful letterforms while protecting your body.

## Seated Position

### Body Alignment

**At the desk:**
- Upright spine
- Feet flat
- Arms at desk level
- Shoulders relaxed
- Core engaged

### Paper Position

**Work surface:**
- Slight angle ideal
- Proper distance
- Good lighting
- Comfortable reach
- Minimal glare

## Arm and Hand Position

### Brush/Pen Hold

**Proper grip:**
- Relaxed fingers
- Vertical or angled hold
- Movement from shoulder and elbow
- Wrist stable
- Minimal tension

### Arm Movement

**Writing motion:**
- Whole arm involvement
- Not just fingers
- Smooth strokes
- Breathing coordination
- Flow focus

## Eastern vs Western

### Asian Brush

**Brush calligraphy:**
- Often standing
- Arm from shoulder
- Vertical brush
- Breathing important
- Meditative pace

### Western Pen

**Nib work:**
- Usually seated
- Angled hold
- Arm and hand work
- Paper angle
- Traditional techniques

## Common Issues

### Tension Problems

**From focus:**
- Shoulder awareness
- Breath monitoring
- Regular breaks
- Stretching routine
- Mindful practice

Good calligraphy posture supports the flow and precision that makes beautiful lettering possible.
