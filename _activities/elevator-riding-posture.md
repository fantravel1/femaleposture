# Elevator Riding Posture

Elevator riding involves brief standing. Proper posture supports comfortable confined space presence.

## Entering Elevator

### Entry Etiquette

**Getting on:**
- Wait for others to exit
- Step in smoothly
- Position yourself
- Face front typically
- Quick transition

### Finding Position

**In elevator:**
- Allow space for others
- Face door usually
- Comfortable stance
- Balanced position
- Social positioning

## Standing Position

### Brief Ride

**During travel:**
- Balanced stance
- Core engaged lightly
- Relaxed posture
- Patient waiting
- Brief duration

### Crowded Elevators

**Limited space:**
- Minimal space okay
- Stable footing
- Respect boundaries
- Patient tolerance
- Brief discomfort

## Motion Awareness

### Starting and Stopping

**Movement:**
- Slight knee flex
- Absorb motion
- Maintain balance
- Core stability
- Smooth adaptation

## Exiting

### Getting Off

**At floor:**
- Excuse yourself if needed
- Step out smoothly
- Clear doorway
- Move away
- Smooth transition

## Personal Space

### Proximity Management

**Close quarters:**
- Accept proximity
- Maintain composure
- Brief duration
- Patient tolerance
- Professional bearing

Good elevator riding posture supports comfortable confined space presence.
