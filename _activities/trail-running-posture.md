# Trail Running Posture

Trail running demands adaptable posture. Proper form handles varied terrain safely.

## Basic Stance

### Adaptable Posture

**On trails:**
- Shorter stride
- Lower center of gravity
- Eyes scanning ahead
- Quick foot turnover
- Responsive body

### Upper Body

**Stability:**
- Arms wider for balance
- Slight forward lean
- Core very engaged
- Reactive shoulders
- Relaxed grip

## Terrain Adaptation

### Uphill

**Climbing technique:**
- Forward lean into hill
- Shorter stride
- Drive with arms
- Power hike steep sections
- Sustained effort

### Downhill

**Descent form:**
- Slight back lean
- Quick feet
- Arms for balance
- Controlled speed
- Eyes well ahead

## Technical Trail

### Rocks and Roots

**Obstacle navigation:**
- Light, quick feet
- Eyes scanning
- Ankle stability
- Quick reactions
- Confident steps

### Single Track

**Narrow paths:**
- Precise foot placement
- Core stability
- Arms for balance
- Focus forward
- Confident movement

## Safety

### Awareness

**Trail running safety:**
- Know the trail
- Watch weather
- Carry essentials
- Tell someone route
- Start conservative

### Falling

**If you fall:**
- Roll with it
- Protect head
- Get up carefully
- Assess injuries
- Adjust pace

Good trail running posture creates stable, adaptable movement on varied terrain.
