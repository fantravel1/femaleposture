# Interval Running Posture

Interval running alternates fast and easy efforts. Proper posture enables effective speed training.

## Fast Intervals

### Speed Form

**During fast effort:**
- More upright
- Higher knee drive
- Powerful arm swing
- Quick turnover
- Efficient motion

### Body Position

**Sprinting posture:**
- Slight forward lean
- Core very engaged
- Relaxed shoulders
- Full extension
- Powerful movement

## Recovery Intervals

### Easy Jogging

**Between efforts:**
- Relaxed form
- Easy effort
- Heart rate recovery
- Loose movement
- Catch breath

### Walking Recovery

**If needed:**
- Upright posture
- Natural walking
- Full recovery
- Prepare for next
- Listen to body

## Track vs Road

### Track Intervals

**On oval:**
- Consistent surface
- Measured distance
- Precise timing
- Banking on turns
- Controlled environment

### Road Intervals

**On streets:**
- Variable surface
- Estimated distance
- Flexible approach
- Traffic awareness
- Convenient access

## Workout Structure

### Warm-up

**Before intervals:**
- Easy jogging
- Dynamic stretching
- Build-up strides
- Prepare body
- Mental focus

### Cool-down

**After intervals:**
- Easy jogging
- Gradual slowing
- Static stretching
- Recovery start
- Complete workout

Good interval running posture enables effective speed development.
