# Bottle Feeding Posture

Bottle feeding requires holding baby comfortably. Proper posture supports both caregiver and infant.

## Holding Baby

### Cradle Position

**Traditional hold:**
- Baby in arm crook
- Head elevated
- Body supported
- Comfortable angle
- Eye contact possible

### Upright Position

**More vertical:**
- Baby upright
- Less reflux risk
- Head supported
- Good for older babies
- Comfortable hold

## Your Position

### Seated Feeding

**Comfortable seat:**
- Back supported
- Arms supported
- Relaxed shoulders
- Not hunched
- Sustainable position

### Pillow Support

**Arm relief:**
- Nursing pillow helps
- Regular pillow works
- Elevates baby
- Reduces arm strain
- Extended feeding comfort

## Arm and Shoulder

### Preventing Fatigue

**During feeding:**
- Switch arms
- Use support
- Relaxed grip
- Shoulders down
- Rest when possible

### Wrist Position

**Bottle holding:**
- Neutral wrist
- Relaxed grip
- Proper bottle angle
- Not strained
- Comfortable feeding

## Night Feeding

### Safe Positioning

**Tired feeding:**
- Stay alert
- Comfortable but awake
- Safe hold
- Well-supported
- Complete feeding

### Quick Return to Sleep

**Efficient process:**
- Supplies ready
- Minimal lighting
- Quick burping
- Calm return
- Rest restored

Good bottle feeding posture supports comfortable, connected feeding experiences.
