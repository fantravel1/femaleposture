# Stair Climbing Posture

Stair climbing provides intense exercise. Proper posture maximizes benefits and protects joints.

## Climbing Technique

### Basic Form

**Going up:**
- Upright torso
- Core engaged
- Drive through whole foot
- Push with glutes
- Controlled motion

### Foot Placement

**On each step:**
- Whole foot on step
- Drive from heel
- Knees track toes
- Stable base
- Controlled power

## Pacing

### Sustainable Effort

**Energy management:**
- Consistent rhythm
- Breathing controlled
- Sustainable intensity
- Long term effort
- No burnout

### Speed Variation

**Training options:**
- Slow and steady
- Interval bursts
- Every other step
- Single steps
- Variable intensity

## Muscle Engagement

### Lower Body

**Working muscles:**
- Glutes primary
- Quads assist
- Calves push off
- Core stabilizes
- Full lower body

### Arm Use

**If handrails:**
- Light touch only
- Balance assistance
- Don't pull up
- Own your effort
- Core stability

## Descent

### Coming Down

**Safe technique:**
- Controlled steps
- Core engaged
- Absorb impact
- Use handrail lightly
- Patient descent

## Stair Machine

### Gym Equipment

**Machine technique:**
- Upright posture
- Minimal handrail
- Steady pace
- Full steps
- Core engaged

Good stair climbing posture creates effective exercise with joint protection.
