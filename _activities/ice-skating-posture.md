# Ice Skating Posture

Ice skating demands precise balance on a slippery surface. Understanding proper skating posture improves control and reduces the fear of falling.

## Basic Stance

### Standing on Ice

**Balanced position:**
- Feet hip-width
- Knees bent
- Weight slightly forward
- Arms out for balance
- Core engaged

### Center of Gravity

**Balance point:**
- Low center of gravity
- Weight over blades
- Not too far back
- Controlled lean
- Stable base

## Skating Technique

### Forward Stroke

**Basic glide:**
- Push to side
- Glide on one foot
- Recover foot
- Alternate sides
- Smooth motion

### Backward Skating

**Reverse movement:**
- Weight transfer
- Push with inside edge
- Look over shoulder
- Controlled speed
- More challenging

### Stopping

**Control methods:**
- Snowplow stop
- T-stop
- Hockey stop (advanced)
- Speed control
- Essential skill

## Figure Skating Position

### Artistic Posture

**Performance stance:**
- Elegant carriage
- Arms positioned
- Core strong
- Lifted chest
- Pointed toes

### Spins and Jumps

**Advanced positions:**
- Centered rotation
- Arms placement
- Core engagement
- Landing position
- Balance recovery

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female skaters:**
- Flexibility advantage
- Artistry emphasis
- Costume considerations
- Cold protection
- Skate fit

### Safety

**Protection:**
- Gloves always
- Proper clothing
- Helmet if learning
- Wrist guards optional
- Fall recovery skills

Good ice skating posture creates graceful, controlled movement on the ice surface.
