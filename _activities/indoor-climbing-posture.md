# Indoor Climbing Posture

Indoor climbing offers controlled training. Proper posture builds technique safely.

## Gym Environment

### Wall Angles

**Various surfaces:**
- Vertical walls
- Slab (less than vertical)
- Overhang
- Each requires technique
- Progressive challenge

### Hold Types

**Training variety:**
- Jugs for beginners
- Crimps for strength
- Slopers for technique
- Various shapes
- Progressive difficulty

## Basic Technique

### Body Position

**On the wall:**
- Hips close
- Weight on feet
- Arms straight when possible
- Core engaged
- Efficient movement

### Reading Routes

**Before climbing:**
- Study the problem
- Identify holds
- Plan sequence
- Visualize movement
- Strategic approach

## Bouldering

### Short Problems

**Without rope:**
- Powerful moves
- Problem solving
- Quick attempts
- Good rest
- Technique focus

### Falling and Landing

**Safe falling:**
- Controlled descent
- Crash pad awareness
- Bent knee landing
- Roll if needed
- Safe practice

## Roped Climbing

### Top Rope

**Learning security:**
- Rope from above
- Safe environment
- Technique building
- Endurance training
- Confidence building

### Lead Climbing

**Advanced skill:**
- Clipping technique
- Fall awareness
- Mental challenge
- Progressive learning
- Safety essential

## Training

### Skill Building

**Regular practice:**
- Technique drills
- Strength building
- Endurance routes
- Problem variety
- Progressive challenge

Good indoor climbing posture builds technique in a controlled environment.
