# Olympic Lifting Posture

Olympic weightlifting demands precise technique. Proper posture enables maximum weight with safety.

## Start Position

### Setup

**On the platform:**
- Feet hip-width
- Bar over mid-foot
- Grip established
- Back set
- Ready for pull

### First Pull

**Off the floor:**
- Knees back
- Maintain back angle
- Controlled speed
- Bar stays close
- Leg drive

## Snatch

### Second Pull

**Power position:**
- Bar at hip
- Explosive extension
- Triple extension
- Bar travels up
- Aggressive pull under

### Catch Position

**Receiving the bar:**
- Full squat or power
- Overhead lockout
- Bar behind ears
- Core braced
- Stable receive

### Recovery

**Standing up:**
- Controlled rise
- Bar overhead stable
- Complete lift
- Down safely
- Reset for next

## Clean

### Pull Pattern

**Similar to snatch:**
- Different grip width
- Bar to shoulders
- Front rack position
- Full clean or power
- Strong catch

### Front Rack

**Catch position:**
- Elbows high
- Bar on shoulders
- Upright torso
- Stable position
- Ready for jerk

## Jerk

### Dip and Drive

**Power generation:**
- Vertical dip
- Explosive drive
- Bar momentum
- Under the bar
- Lockout overhead

### Split or Squat

**Catch style:**
- Split jerk common
- Squat jerk option
- Overhead stability
- Recovery to standing
- Complete lift

Good Olympic lifting posture enables safe, powerful barbell movements.
