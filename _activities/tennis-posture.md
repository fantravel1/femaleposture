# Tennis Posture

Tennis demands quick movement and powerful strokes, both requiring excellent posture for performance and injury prevention. Understanding proper court positioning protects your body while improving your game.

## Court Posture Basics

### Ready Position

**Athletic stance:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees bent
- Weight on balls of feet
- Core engaged
- Racket ready

### Movement Posture

**Court coverage:**
- Split step
- Quick first step
- Low center of gravity
- Balanced movement
- Recovery position

## Stroke Mechanics

### Forehand

**Proper form:**
- Unit turn
- Shoulder rotation
- Hip drive
- Kinetic chain
- Follow through

### Backhand

**Technique focus:**
- Shoulder alignment
- Core rotation
- Arm extension
- Balance maintenance
- Clean finish

### Serve

**Powerful position:**
- Trophy position
- Full extension
- Core involvement
- Shoulder mechanics
- Follow through

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Biomechanical Factors

**Female tennis:**
- Shoulder stability
- Core strength needs
- Hip mechanics
- Injury patterns
- Equipment fit

### Common Issues

**Frequent problems:**
- Tennis elbow
- Shoulder injuries
- Back strain
- Knee issues
- Wrist problems

## Injury Prevention

### Protective Practices

**Staying healthy:**
- Proper warm-up
- Technique focus
- Strength training
- Flexibility work
- Recovery attention

Good tennis posture improves performance while protecting your body from the sport's repetitive demands.
