# Shoulder Bag Posture

Shoulder bags create asymmetric load. Proper posture minimizes strain from one-sided carrying.

## Single Shoulder

### One Strap Carry

**Single shoulder:**
- Don't hike shoulder
- Relaxed under strap
- Switch sides regularly
- Light weight
- Limited duration

### Shoulder Tension

**Preventing strain:**
- Conscious relaxation
- Take breaks
- Alternate shoulders
- Lighten load
- Watch for strain

## Crossbody

### Better Option

**Across body:**
- Weight distributed
- More comfortable
- Hands free
- Less shoulder strain
- Preferred method

### Strap Adjustment

**Crossbody fit:**
- Bag at hip or higher
- Not too low
- Comfortable length
- Easy access
- Secure position

## Weight Management

### Bag Contents

**What you carry:**
- Essential only
- Regular cleanout
- Lighter better
- Digital alternatives
- Mindful packing

### Heavy Days

**When loaded:**
- Crossbody essential
- Take breaks
- Set down often
- Consider backpack
- Protect body

## Posture Awareness

### While Carrying

**Body position:**
- Stand balanced
- Don't lean
- Both shoulders level
- Core engaged
- Aware carrying

Good shoulder bag posture minimizes asymmetric strain from one-sided carrying.
