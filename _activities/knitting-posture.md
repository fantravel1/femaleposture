# Knitting Posture

Knitting and crocheting involve repetitive hand movements that can strain hands, wrists, and neck. Proper posture protects your body while you create.

## Seated Position

### Chair Setup

**Comfortable seating:**
- Good back support
- Feet grounded
- Arms at comfortable height
- Not slouching
- Relaxed shoulders

### Body Position

**Proper alignment:**
- Upright torso
- Shoulders back
- Work held up
- Not hunched over
- Core engaged

## Hand and Arm Position

### Needle/Hook Holding

**Grip technique:**
- Relaxed grip
- Not too tight
- Wrist neutral
- Varied tension
- Comfortable hold

### Arm Support

**Reducing strain:**
- Elbows supported
- Arms at sides
- Pillows for support
- Armrests use
- Relaxed position

## Common Issues

### Hand Strain

**Repetitive stress:**
- Vary grip
- Take breaks
- Hand stretches
- Ice if sore
- Modify technique

### Neck and Shoulder

**From looking down:**
- Elevate work
- Good lighting
- Regular breaks
- Stretching routine
- Posture awareness

## Prevention Tips

### Break Schedule

**Movement intervals:**
- Every 20-30 minutes
- Put down work
- Stretch hands
- Move shoulders
- Look away

### Hand Care

**Protecting hands:**
- Warm hands before
- Stretch between sessions
- Strengthen hands
- Rest when sore
- Ergonomic tools

Good knitting posture allows you to enjoy your craft without the pain that often accompanies long sessions.
