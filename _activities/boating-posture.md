# Boating Posture

Boating requires adapting to vessel movement. Proper posture enables safe, comfortable time on water.

## Seated Position

### On Boat Seats

**When seated:**
- Hold secure point
- Feet braced
- Core engaged
- Ready for motion
- Life vest worn

### Absorbing Motion

**In waves:**
- Knees act as shock absorbers
- Relaxed body
- Go with motion
- Don't fight it
- Stay centered

## Standing

### When Moving

**Standing on boat:**
- Hold secure point always
- Wide stance
- Knees bent
- Core engaged
- Ready for unexpected

### Docking

**Careful movement:**
- Secure footing
- Deliberate steps
- Hold rails
- Watch obstacles
- Safe movement

## Operating

### At Helm

**Driving boat:**
- Secure standing or seating
- Good visibility
- Access to controls
- Comfortable position
- Sustained alertness

### Viewing Conditions

**Watching water:**
- Scan constantly
- Sunglasses helpful
- Comfortable neck
- Regular checks
- Safety priority

## Safety

### Life Vest

**Essential gear:**
- Wear always
- Proper fit
- Doesn't restrict
- Safety first
- Required for many

### Rough Water

**Challenging conditions:**
- Seated position
- Hold secure
- Lower stance
- Core very engaged
- Safety priority

Good boating posture adapts to vessel movement safely.
