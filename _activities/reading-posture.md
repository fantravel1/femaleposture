# Reading Posture

Reading, whether books or screens, involves prolonged static positions that can strain the neck, eyes, and back. Proper reading posture protects your body while you enjoy your reading.

## Book Reading

### Chair Positioning

**Comfortable setup:**
- Supported back
- Feet flat
- Armrests if available
- Good lighting
- Book at angle

### Book Position

**Proper holding:**
- Book elevated
- Arms supported
- Not too close
- Tilted toward eyes
- Reduce looking down

## Screen Reading

### Device Position

**Screen ergonomics:**
- Eye level for monitor
- Elevated for tablets
- Not in lap for long periods
- Appropriate distance
- Glare reduction

### E-Reader Position

**Digital reading:**
- Elevated position
- Case or stand use
- Arms supported
- Not too bright
- Appropriate text size

## Reading Positions

### Seated Reading

**Chair options:**
- Upright with support
- Reclined slightly
- Footstool option
- Armrest use
- Regular repositioning

### Bed Reading

**If reading in bed:**
- Propped sitting
- Support behind back
- Device on pillow
- Limit duration
- Neck protection

## Eye Care

### Preventing Strain

**Eye protection:**
- Adequate lighting
- Regular breaks
- 20-20-20 rule
- Appropriate distance
- Blink frequently

## Long Reading Sessions

### Taking Breaks

**Rest strategies:**
- Stand and stretch
- Eye rest
- Walk around
- Position change
- Hydration

Good reading posture lets you enjoy your books and articles without neck, back, or eye strain.
