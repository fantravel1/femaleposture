# Guitar Playing Posture

Guitar playing requires balanced positioning. Proper posture enables comfortable, effective playing.

## Seated Position

### Classical Style

**Formal position:**
- Left leg elevated
- Guitar on left thigh
- Neck elevated
- Upright posture
- Traditional form

### Casual Style

**Relaxed position:**
- Guitar on right thigh
- Neck angled up
- Comfortable hold
- Personal preference
- Common approach

## Body Position

### Spine Alignment

**While playing:**
- Upright posture
- Don't hunch over
- Neck forward minimal
- Shoulders relaxed
- Sustainable position

### Arm Position

**Fretting arm:**
- Elbow down
- Wrist comfortable
- Thumb behind neck
- Fingers curved
- Natural position

## Standing Position

### With Strap

**Standing playing:**
- Strap adjusted properly
- Guitar at comfortable height
- Weight distributed
- Freedom of movement
- Performance ready

### Strap Height

**Personal choice:**
- Too low strains
- Comfortable reach
- Various styles
- Personal preference
- Playability priority

## Hand Technique

### Fretting Hand

**On neck:**
- Curved fingers
- Thumb placement
- Efficient pressure
- Relaxed grip
- Clean notes

### Picking Hand

**On body:**
- Relaxed wrist
- Various techniques
- Arm support
- Natural motion
- Style dependent

## Extended Practice

### Long Sessions

**Practice duration:**
- Regular breaks
- Hand stretches
- Position changes
- Sustainable effort
- Build stamina

Good guitar posture enables comfortable, expressive string playing.
