# Crafting Posture: Knitting, Sewing, and Handwork

> **Craft Without Pain:** Handwork hobbies require focus and often prolonged positioning. Here's how to protect your body while enjoying your craft.

---

## Common Crafting Issues

- Looking down for extended periods
- Rounded shoulders
- Neck strain
- Wrist and hand fatigue
- Upper back tension

---

## General Crafting Principles

### Workspace Setup

- Good lighting (reduces leaning)
- Work at appropriate height
- Support for arms if possible
- Chair with back support

### Body Position

- Bring work up, not head down
- Support forearms when possible
- Shoulders relaxed
- Take regular breaks

---

## Specific Activities

### Knitting and Crochet

- Use pillow or cushion to raise work
- Support elbows
- Don't hunch over
- Set timer for breaks

### Hand Sewing

- Good lighting essential
- Magnification if needed
- Work supported on lap desk or table
- Frequent position changes

### Machine Sewing

- Chair at right height
- Proper table height
- Take breaks from foot pedal
- Stretch hands and neck

---

## Break Routine

**Every 20-30 minutes:**
1. Set work down
2. Shoulder rolls
3. Neck stretches
4. Hand and wrist stretches
5. Stand and move
