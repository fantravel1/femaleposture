# Crochet Posture

Crochet requires similar care to knitting. Proper posture enables comfortable hook work.

## Basic Position

### Seated Setup

**Comfortable seating:**
- Supportive chair
- Back support
- Feet on floor
- Arms free to move
- Relaxed position

### Body Alignment

**While crocheting:**
- Upright spine
- Shoulders relaxed
- Not hunched
- Core lightly engaged
- Sustainable posture

## Hook Technique

### Grip Styles

**Holding hook:**
- Knife grip
- Pencil grip
- Personal preference
- Relaxed hold
- Efficient motion

### Hand Position

**Working yarn:**
- Tensioning comfortable
- Not too tight
- Fingers relaxed
- Sustainable grip
- Various methods

## Working Position

### Project Placement

**While working:**
- Close to body
- Don't reach forward
- Support weight
- Comfortable angle
- Good visibility

### Lighting

**See your work:**
- Adequate light
- No shadows
- Reduce eye strain
- Daylight helpful
- Adjustable lighting

## Extended Sessions

### Long Projects

**Hours of work:**
- Regular breaks
- Stand and stretch
- Hand exercises
- Eye rest
- Mindful practice

### Hand Care

**Preventing issues:**
- Stretch fingers
- Wrist circles
- Arm movements
- Shoulder rolls
- Stay loose

## Common Issues

### Tension and Pain

**Problem areas:**
- Neck from looking down
- Shoulders from tension
- Hands from gripping
- Wrists from repetition
- Address early

Good crochet posture enables comfortable fiber arts practice.
