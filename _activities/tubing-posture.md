# Tubing Posture

Water tubing provides fun behind a boat. Proper positioning enables secure, safe rides.

## Riding Position

### On the Tube

**Basic position:**
- Centered on tube
- Grip secure
- Elbows bent
- Core engaged
- Head up

### Grip Style

**Holding on:**
- Firm grip on handles
- Arms with give
- Not locked elbows
- Sustainable hold
- Prepared for forces

## Body Position

### Prone Riding

**Face down:**
- Chest on tube
- Legs trailing
- Core engaged
- Head lifted
- Controlled position

### Seated Riding

**Sitting up:**
- Bottom secure
- Handles gripped
- Core engaged
- Knees up or out
- Balanced position

## Crossing Wakes

### Handling Bumps

**Wake riding:**
- Grip tighter
- Core very engaged
- Absorb impact
- Stay centered
- Enjoy the ride

### Anticipation

**Prepared riding:**
- Watch water ahead
- Expect bumps
- Adjust grip
- Core ready
- Maintain position

## Falling Off

### Safe Release

**When you fall:**
- Let go cleanly
- Cover head
- Relax body
- Signal OK
- Wait for pickup

## Safety

### Essentials

**Safe tubing:**
- Wear PFD
- Know hand signals
- Communicate with driver
- Know your limits
- Have fun safely

Good tubing posture enables secure, enjoyable rides with minimal strain.
