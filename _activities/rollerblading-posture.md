# Rollerblading Posture

Rollerblading requires balance on a narrower wheel base. Proper posture enables controlled, efficient skating.

## Basic Stance

### Ready Position

**Standing on blades:**
- Knees well bent
- Weight centered
- Forward lean
- Core engaged
- Arms forward

### Balance Point

**Finding stability:**
- Over wheel center
- Hips back slightly
- Low center of gravity
- Active core
- Ready for movement

## Stride Technique

### Basic Stride

**Forward movement:**
- Push to side
- Full extension
- Recovery swing
- Weight transfer
- Rhythm maintained

### Power Stride

**Efficient skating:**
- Hip engagement
- Full push
- Quick recovery
- Core stability
- Speed building

## Stopping Techniques

### Heel Brake

**Basic stop:**
- Scissor stance
- Lift toe of brake foot
- Controlled pressure
- Weight centered
- Gradual stop

### T-Stop

**Advanced stop:**
- Trailing foot perpendicular
- Edge drag
- Balance on front foot
- Controlled deceleration
- Smooth finish

## Turning

### Basic Turn

**Direction change:**
- Weight shift
- Lean into turn
- Arms for balance
- Core engaged
- Smooth arc

### Crossover Turn

**Tight turns:**
- Step over
- Inside leg leads
- Continuous motion
- Core stability
- Speed maintained

Good rollerblading posture creates the stability for confident inline skating.
