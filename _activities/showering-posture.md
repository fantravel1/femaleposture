# Showering Posture

Showering involves reaching and standing in wet conditions. Proper posture enables safe, efficient bathing.

## Safety First

### Non-Slip Surface

**Essential:**
- Non-slip mat
- Secure footing
- Careful movement
- Grab bars helpful
- Safety priority

### Stable Stance

**Standing safely:**
- Feet planted
- Weight balanced
- Careful turns
- Slow movements
- Aware of water

## Washing

### Upper Body

**Arms and torso:**
- Natural reaching
- Comfortable motion
- Alternate sides
- Easy access
- Normal movement

### Lower Body

**Legs and feet:**
- Bend carefully
- Use walls for support
- Foot rest if available
- Secure position
- Safe reaching

### Hair Washing

**Head positioning:**
- Face spray or away
- Comfortable neck
- Natural position
- Rinse thoroughly
- Relaxed motion

## Extended Showers

### Relaxation Time

**Taking time:**
- Enjoy the water
- Don't stand too long
- Comfortable temperature
- Self-care moment
- Listen to body

## Special Considerations

### Mobility Limitations

**Adaptive equipment:**
- Shower chair
- Grab bars
- Handheld showerhead
- Non-slip surfaces
- Safety priority

### Fatigue

**When tired:**
- Shorter showers
- Seated option
- Essential washing
- Energy conservation
- Safety first

Good showering posture enables safe, comfortable daily bathing.
