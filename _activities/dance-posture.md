# Dance Posture

Dance demands exquisite body awareness and control. Proper dance posture varies by style but shares common alignment principles that protect the body while enabling beautiful movement.

## Dance Alignment Basics

### Core Principles

**Foundation elements:**
- Aligned spine
- Engaged core
- Placed shoulders
- Lifted head
- Grounded feet

### Dynamic Alignment

**Movement considerations:**
- Alignment through motion
- Transitions
- Balance points
- Weight transfers
- Floor connection

## Style-Specific Posture

### Ballet

**Classical form:**
- Elongated spine
- Lifted sternum
- Turned out from hips
- Placed arms
- Long neck

### Contemporary/Modern

**Fluid form:**
- Responsive spine
- Core-driven
- Floor work ready
- Weight play
- Breath connection

### Jazz/Commercial

**Dynamic form:**
- Strong center
- Isolated movements
- Sharp or fluid
- Performance posture
- Energized presence

### Social Dance

**Partner form:**
- Frame maintenance
- Connection posture
- Lead/follow position
- Balance sharing
- Communication stance

## Common Dance Injuries

### Posture-Related Issues

**Risk areas:**
- Lower back strain
- Knee problems
- Ankle injuries
- Shoulder issues
- Neck tension

### Prevention

**Protective practices:**
- Proper warm-up
- Technique focus
- Strength training
- Flexibility work
- Adequate rest

## Training for Dance

### Building Dance Posture

**Development work:**
- Core strengthening
- Flexibility
- Balance training
- Body awareness
- Cross-training

Dance posture is an ongoing practice that protects the body while enabling the fullest expression of movement artistry.
