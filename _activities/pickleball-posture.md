# Pickleball Posture

Pickleball combines elements of tennis, badminton, and ping-pong. Proper pickleball posture supports the unique demands of this growing sport.

## Ready Position

### Base Stance

**Court-ready:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees bent
- Weight forward
- Paddle up
- Eyes on ball

### Kitchen Line Position

**Non-volley zone:**
- Close to line
- Low stance
- Paddle ready
- Quick reactions
- Balanced position

## Shot Mechanics

### Dink Shot

**Soft game:**
- Low stance
- Paddle face open
- Soft grip
- Touch and control
- Minimal backswing

### Volley

**Net play:**
- Compact motion
- Paddle in front
- Quick punch
- Limited swing
- Ready recovery

### Drive and Groundstroke

**Power shots:**
- Preparation early
- Paddle back
- Rotate through
- Follow through
- Balance maintained

## Footwork

### Court Coverage

**Movement patterns:**
- Split step
- Side shuffle
- Forward approach
- Quick backward
- Balanced movement

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female players:**
- All ages welcome
- Joint-friendly sport
- Social aspects
- Technique over power
- Great exercise

### Injury Prevention

**Protection:**
- Proper footwear
- Court surface awareness
- Shoulder care
- Overuse prevention
- Proper warm-up

Good pickleball posture supports the strategic finesse game that makes this sport so enjoyable.
