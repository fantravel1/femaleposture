# Circuit Training Posture

Circuit training moves between exercise stations. Proper posture at each station ensures effective workout.

## Circuit Structure

### Station Setup

**Organization:**
- Multiple stations
- Different exercises
- Timed intervals
- Quick transitions
- Full body coverage

### Transitions

**Moving between:**
- Quick setup
- Equipment ready
- Minimal rest
- Start on time
- Efficient movement

## Station Categories

### Strength Stations

**Resistance work:**
- Free weights
- Machines
- Bodyweight
- Resistance bands
- Various implements

### Cardio Stations

**Heart rate:**
- Jump rope
- Step-ups
- Battle ropes
- Rowing
- Cycling bursts

### Core Stations

**Midsection:**
- Planks
- Crunches
- Rotational work
- Stability exercises
- Core focus

### Power Stations

**Explosive:**
- Box jumps
- Medicine ball throws
- Kettlebell swings
- Plyometrics
- Dynamic movements

## Form Priority

### Each Station

**Technique:**
- Quality reps
- Full range
- Controlled motion
- Safe execution
- Time efficient

### Fatigue Management

**As circuits progress:**
- Maintain form
- Reduce intensity slightly
- Keep moving
- Quality focus
- Safe completion

## Benefits

### Efficiency

**Circuit advantages:**
- Time efficient
- Full body
- Cardio and strength
- Variety
- Group friendly

Good circuit training posture maintains form across varied exercise stations.
