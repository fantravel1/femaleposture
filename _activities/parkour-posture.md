# Parkour Posture

Parkour demands efficient, safe movement over obstacles. Proper posture protects your body through dynamic movement.

## Basic Movement

### Running

**Efficient running:**
- Controlled pace
- Quiet landing
- Core engaged
- Efficient form
- Environment awareness

### Landing

**Safe absorption:**
- Soft landings
- Knee bend
- Core engaged
- Roll if needed
- Gradual height increase

## Vaults

### Speed Vault

**Quick over:**
- Hand placement
- Hip drive
- Core tight
- Quick execution
- Smooth exit

### Kong Vault

**Dive through:**
- Dive position
- Hands push off
- Hips high
- Controlled landing
- Progressive training

### Other Vaults

**Technique variations:**
- Safety vault
- Lazy vault
- Dash vault
- Proper progression
- Environment appropriate

## Climbing

### Wall Work

**Going up:**
- Efficient movement
- Core engagement
- Multiple techniques
- Safe descent
- Strength foundation

## Training Philosophy

### Progressive Approach

**Building safely:**
- Condition first
- Low heights start
- Proper technique
- Gradual increase
- Know limits

## Risk Management

### Safety Mindset

**Smart training:**
- Assess before attempt
- Know your skills
- Weather and surface
- Have exit strategy
- Train with others

Good parkour posture enables efficient movement while prioritizing the safety that allows long-term practice.
