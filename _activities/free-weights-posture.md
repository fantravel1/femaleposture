# Free Weights Posture

Free weights demand proper form without machine guidance. Correct posture is essential for safety and effectiveness.

## Foundational Positions

### Standing Exercises

**Base stance:**
- Feet hip to shoulder-width
- Knees soft
- Core braced
- Shoulders set
- Spine neutral

### Seated Exercises

**Bench position:**
- Feet grounded
- Back appropriate to exercise
- Core engaged
- Weight balanced
- Stable base

## Dumbbell Technique

### Upper Body

**Pressing movements:**
- Shoulder blade stability
- Neutral wrist
- Controlled path
- Full range
- Breathing pattern

### Lower Body

**Leg exercises:**
- Proper squat/lunge form
- Dumbbells add challenge
- Core extra important
- Balance demands
- Form priority

## Barbell Technique

### Major Lifts

**Compound movements:**
- Bar path control
- Core bracing
- Joint alignment
- Full range when safe
- Spotter when needed

### Setup Importance

**Before lifting:**
- Proper rack height
- Safety bars set
- Space cleared
- Weight confirmed
- Form reviewed

## Common Errors

### What to Avoid

**Dangerous form:**
- Ego lifting
- Rushed movement
- Bouncing weight
- Poor alignment
- Breath holding

### Corrections

**Safe lifting:**
- Appropriate weight
- Controlled tempo
- Perfect alignment
- Proper breathing
- Progressive overload

Good free weight posture provides the stability necessary for safe, effective resistance training.
