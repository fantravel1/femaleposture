# Stretching Posture

Stretching improves flexibility and feels good, but poor stretching form can cause injury. Understanding proper stretching posture maximizes benefits while protecting your body.

## Stretching Basics

### General Principles

**Safe stretching:**
- Warm muscles first
- Never bounce
- Breathe through stretches
- Feel stretch, not pain
- Hold appropriate time

### Body Position

**Alignment in stretches:**
- Stable base
- Neutral spine when appropriate
- Controlled movement
- Proper joint position
- Targeted stretch

## Upper Body Stretches

### Neck Stretches

**Safe technique:**
- Gentle tilts only
- No full circles
- Slow movement
- Hold gently
- Breathe throughout

### Shoulder Stretches

**Proper form:**
- Cross-body reach
- Doorway stretches
- Arm circles
- Shoulder blade movement
- Support when needed

### Chest and Back

**Opening stretches:**
- Door frame stretch
- Cat-cow
- Back extensions
- Twist variations
- Proper breathing

## Lower Body Stretches

### Hip Stretches

**Flexibility work:**
- Hip flexor lunge
- Figure-4 stretch
- Hip circles
- Pigeon pose
- Butterfly stretch

### Leg Stretches

**Proper technique:**
- Hamstring variations
- Quad stretches
- Calf stretches
- Adductor work
- ITB attention

## Common Stretching Mistakes

### What to Avoid

**Problematic practices:**
- Bouncing in stretches
- Overstretching
- Ignoring pain
- Cold stretching
- Breath holding

### Better Practices

**Safe approach:**
- Warm up first
- Gradual stretch
- Appropriate hold time
- Breathing focus
- Progressive approach

## Stretching Routines

### Daily Stretching

**Maintenance work:**
- Morning wake-up stretches
- Work break stretches
- Evening unwinding
- Post-exercise stretching
- Regular routine

Proper stretching form makes flexibility work safe and effective, supporting overall posture and mobility.
