# Hiking Posture

Hiking challenges the body differently than flat-ground walking. Proper hiking posture protects your body on varied terrain while allowing you to enjoy the trail.

## Trail Posture Basics

### Uphill Technique

**Climbing form:**
- Shorter steps
- Lean slightly forward
- Engage glutes
- Steady pace
- Controlled breathing

### Downhill Technique

**Descending form:**
- Slightly bent knees
- Shorter steps
- Weight back slightly
- Controlled pace
- Pole use if available

### Level Trail

**Cruising form:**
- Upright posture
- Natural stride
- Relaxed arms or poles
- Eyes on trail
- Efficient movement

## Pack Carrying

### Backpack Fit

**Proper loading:**
- Hip belt on hip bones
- Weight on hips, not shoulders
- Shoulder straps adjusted
- Sternum strap secure
- Load balanced

### Carrying Posture

**With pack:**
- Maintain upright stance
- Don't lean forward excessively
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Regular adjustment

## Terrain Navigation

### Rocky Ground

**Foot placement:**
- Careful steps
- Flat foot landings
- Ankle stability
- Slower pace
- Constant attention

### Wet/Slippery Terrain

**Careful movement:**
- Shorter steps
- Lower center of gravity
- Pole use
- Careful foot placement
- Patience

## Injury Prevention

### Common Hiking Injuries

**Risk areas:**
- Ankle sprains
- Knee pain
- Back strain
- Blisters
- Muscle fatigue

### Prevention Strategies

**Safe hiking:**
- Proper footwear
- Gradual conditioning
- Pole use when helpful
- Rest breaks
- Hydration and nutrition

Good hiking posture allows you to cover more miles with less fatigue while protecting your body for the journey.
