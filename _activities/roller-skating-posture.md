# Roller Skating Posture

Roller skating—whether quad or inline—requires specific body positioning for balance and control. Proper skating posture improves your skills while preventing falls.

## Basic Stance

### Standing Position

**Balanced standing:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees bent
- Weight forward slightly
- Arms out for balance
- Core engaged

### Ready Position

**Prepared to move:**
- Athletic stance
- Lower center of gravity
- Soft knees
- Eyes forward
- Relaxed upper body

## Skating Technique

### Forward Stride

**Basic movement:**
- Push to side
- Glide and recover
- Weight transfer
- Smooth motion
- Arms swing naturally

### Stopping

**Control skills:**
- T-stop
- Plow stop
- Heel stop (inline)
- Controlled speed
- Practice essential

### Turning

**Direction change:**
- Lean into turn
- Weight shift
- Crossover if able
- Eyes lead
- Smooth execution

## Balance Skills

### Finding Balance

**Stability practice:**
- One-foot glides
- Weight shifts
- Recovery moves
- Progressive challenge
- Confidence building

### Falling Safely

**When it happens:**
- Fall forward if possible
- Protect with pads
- Roll with fall
- Get up safely
- Learn from it

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female skaters:**
- Hip width consideration
- Skate fit
- Protective gear
- Community fun
- All ages welcome

Good roller skating posture creates flowing, controlled movement that looks as good as it feels.
