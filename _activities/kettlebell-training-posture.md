# Kettlebell Training Posture

Kettlebell training combines strength and conditioning. Proper posture enables safe, effective movement.

## Foundational Movements

### Deadlift

**Base pattern:**
- Hip hinge
- Back flat
- Bell close
- Core braced
- Full extension

### Swing

**Ballistic movement:**
- Hip hinge power
- Arm guide only
- Explosive extension
- Bell to shoulder height
- Core throughout

## Intermediate Movements

### Clean

**Bell to rack:**
- Swing start
- Catch in rack
- Wrist straight
- Bell rests on arm
- Ready for press

### Press

**Overhead movement:**
- From rack position
- Core braced
- Lockout overhead
- Controlled descent
- Return to rack

## Advanced Movements

### Snatch

**One motion overhead:**
- Swing path
- High pull
- Hand insertion
- Overhead catch
- Full lockout

### Turkish Get-Up

**Floor to standing:**
- Multiple positions
- Core engaged throughout
- Controlled movement
- Eyes on bell
- Complex sequence

## Goblet Movements

### Goblet Squat

**Holding at chest:**
- Two-hand hold
- Chest up
- Deep squat
- Core engaged
- Controlled motion

## Training Approach

### Skill Development

**Building competence:**
- Light weight first
- Movement mastery
- Progressive loading
- Technique priority
- Long-term development

Good kettlebell training posture enables powerful, controlled movement with the bell.
