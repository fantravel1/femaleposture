# Carrying Posture

How you carry things affects your posture throughout the day. From purses to children to groceries, proper carrying technique prevents strain and pain.

## Bag Carrying

### Purse and Shoulder Bags

**One-shoulder carrying:**
- Alternate shoulders
- Lighten load
- Cross-body option
- Proper strap length
- Core engagement

### Backpacks

**Two-shoulder carrying:**
- Both straps used
- Weight distributed
- Straps adjusted
- Load on upper back
- Hip belt for heavy loads

### Briefcases and Totes

**Hand carrying:**
- Alternate hands
- Keep close to body
- Lighten when possible
- Proper grip
- Core stability

## Carrying Children

### Infant Carrying

**Baby carrying:**
- Carrier fit
- Weight distribution
- Posture maintenance
- Frequent position changes
- Support needs

### Toddler Carrying

**Bigger child:**
- Hip carry considerations
- Alternate sides
- Core strength
- Limit duration
- Let them walk

### Proper Technique

**Safe carrying:**
- Close to body
- Balanced weight
- Core engaged
- Upright posture
- Strength building

## Grocery Carrying

### Multiple Bags

**Load management:**
- Balance both sides
- Multiple trips
- Cart to car
- Proper grip
- Helper if available

### Heavy Items

**Single heavy loads:**
- Close to body
- Core braced
- Squat to set down
- Rest as needed
- Know limits

## Work Carrying

### Equipment

**Professional carrying:**
- Work bags
- Tools
- Supplies
- Proper distribution
- Ergonomic options

Good carrying posture distributes load appropriately and protects your back and shoulders through daily tasks.
