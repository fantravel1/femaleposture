# Table Tennis Posture

Table tennis demands quick reflexes and precise movements in a compact space. Proper posture enables the fast reactions the game requires.

## Ready Position

### Base Stance

**Table-ready:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Weight on balls of feet
- Knees bent
- Slightly crouched
- Paddle ready

### Body Position

**Optimal posture:**
- Slightly forward lean
- Core engaged
- Arms free
- Shoulders relaxed
- Quick reactions ready

## Stroke Mechanics

### Forehand

**Drive and loop:**
- Paddle back
- Rotate through
- Forward brush
- Quick recovery
- Consistent contact

### Backhand

**Opposite side:**
- Elbow bent
- Wrist action
- Quick motion
- Touch and spin
- Ready position

### Serve

**Starting play:**
- Ball in palm
- Toss up
- Contact behind line
- Variety of spins
- Prepared for return

## Footwork

### Movement Patterns

**Around table:**
- Small steps
- Side to side
- Forward and back
- Weight transfers
- Quick adjustments

### Balance

**Stability:**
- Low center of gravity
- Core engagement
- Smooth transitions
- Ready stance return
- Efficient movement

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female players:**
- All fitness levels
- Quick reaction development
- Low impact
- Social activity
- Competition available

Good table tennis posture enables the lightning-fast play that makes the sport exciting.
