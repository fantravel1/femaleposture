# Floor Mopping Posture

Floor mopping involves standing and arm work. Proper posture prevents back and shoulder strain.

## Mop Handling

### Handle Length

**Proper fit:**
- Long enough to stand upright
- No hunching needed
- Adjustable handle ideal
- Comfortable grip
- Efficient cleaning

### Grip Position

**Holding mop:**
- Both hands
- Comfortable spacing
- Relaxed grip
- Control maintained
- Efficient handling

## Mopping Motion

### Body Movement

**Cleaning action:**
- Core rotation
- Not just arms
- Full body motion
- Back straight
- Efficient mopping

### Pattern

**Floor coverage:**
- Systematic approach
- Move backward
- Don't mop yourself in
- Efficient pattern
- Complete coverage

## Standing Position

### Upright Posture

**While mopping:**
- Back straight
- Shoulders relaxed
- Core engaged
- Eyes on floor
- Comfortable stance

### Avoiding Strain

**Prevention:**
- Proper handle length
- Core engagement
- Regular breaks
- Alternate sides
- Sustainable cleaning

## Wringing Mop

### Bucket Work

**Wringing technique:**
- Squeeze mechanism helps
- Not hand wringing
- Efficient tool
- Less strain
- Effective cleaning

## Large Areas

### Extended Mopping

**Big floors:**
- Take breaks
- Split into sections
- Sustainable pace
- Quality over speed
- Complete job

Good mopping posture prevents strain during floor cleaning.
