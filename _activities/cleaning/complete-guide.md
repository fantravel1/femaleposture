# Cleaning Posture Guide

> **Housework Is Work:** Cleaning involves bending, lifting, reaching, and repetitive motionsâ€”often for hours. Good technique protects your body.

---

## General Cleaning Principles

### Body Mechanics

1. **Bend at hips and knees, not back**
2. **Keep loads close**
3. **Face your work** - don't twist
4. **Use your legs**
5. **Switch sides and positions**

### Pacing

- Break into sessions
- Rest between tasks
- Don't marathon clean
- Spread heavy work across days

---

## Vacuuming

### Common Problems

- Forward bending
- Repetitive pushing
- One-sided motion
- Reaching under furniture

### Better Technique

**Position:**
- Stand upright (don't hunch)
- Lunge stance (one foot forward)
- Keep vacuum close to body

**Movement:**
- Move with your feet
- Don't just reach arms
- Use whole body, not just arms
- Switch leading leg/hand

**Tips:**
- Lightweight vacuum if possible
- Extend handle to reduce bending
- Move furniture rather than strain to reach
- Take breaks every 15-20 minutes

---

## Mopping

### Common Problems

- Hunching over mop
- One-sided pushing
- Twisting motions

### Better Technique

**Position:**
- Mop handle at proper height
- Stand upright
- Use whole body

**Movement:**
- Figure-8 pattern reduces repetition
- Step into strokes
- Switch hands periodically
- Move with the mop

---

## Scrubbing (Tubs, Floors, etc.)

### Common Problems

- Extended bending
- Kneeling on hard surfaces
- Reaching into awkward spaces

### Better Technique

**Kneeling:**
- Use thick knee pad
- Cushioned mat
- Switch kneeling positions

**Reaching:**
- Move closer rather than stretching
- Use long-handled brushes
- Break into sections

**Tub cleaning:**
- Kneel beside tub with cushioning
- Long-handled tools
- Consider spray cleaners that reduce scrubbing
- Take breaks

---

## Toilet Cleaning

### Common Problems

- Bending over
- Awkward angles
- Hard to reach areas

### Better Technique

- Kneel rather than bend
- Long-handled toilet brush
- Knee pad helpful
- Don't contort to reach behind

---

## Dusting and Surface Cleaning

### High Surfaces

- Use extension duster
- Step stool for reach
- Don't overextend
- Lower arms frequently

### Low Surfaces

- Squat or kneel
- Don't bend repeatedly at waist
- Work in sections

### Mirrors and Windows

- Stand close
- Work at eye level and up first
- Step stool for high areas
- Switch arms regularly

---

## Laundry

### Washer/Dryer

**Front-loader:**
- Squat or kneel to reach in
- Don't bend and twist
- Pull items forward

**Top-loader:**
- Use stool if needed to reach
- Don't lean over edge
- Take items in batches

### Carrying Laundry

- Don't overload basket
- Hold close
- Lift with legs
- Multiple trips are fine

### Folding

- Stand at waist-height surface
- Or sit comfortably
- Not on floor/bed (bending)

### Hanging Clothes

- Use step stool for high rods
- Lower rods if possible
- Don't strain overhead

---

## Organizing and Decluttering

### Moving Items

- Lift properly
- Multiple trips
- Get help for heavy items

### Working in Closets/Storage

- Clear paths
- Kneel for low areas
- Stool for high areas
- Don't contort into tight spaces

---

## Kitchen Cleaning

### Dishes

- Stand at comfortable height
- Wide stance if sink is low
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Take sitting breaks if long session

### Counter Wiping

- Don't reach across
- Move around counter
- Stand close

### Appliance Cleaning

- Kneel for ovens/refrigerator bottoms
- Pull racks out to access
- Eye level work where possible

---

## Bathroom Deep Cleaning

### Multiple Positions Required

**Vary positions:**
- Standing for mirrors/high surfaces
- Kneeling for tub/toilet/floor
- Squatting for quick reaches

### Knee Protection

- Thick knee pad essential
- Move it with you
- Consider knee pads you wear

### Ventilation

- Opens windows/fans
- Reduces need to rush
- Can take breaks

---

## Seasonal/Deep Cleaning

### Pacing

- Multiple sessions
- Days apart
- Mix heavy and light tasks
- Listen to body

### Recovery

- Stretch after sessions
- Epsom salt bath if sore
- Rest before next session

---

## Quick Checklist

### Before Cleaning

- [ ] Stretches
- [ ] Gather supplies (reduce trips)
- [ ] Plan order of tasks
- [ ] Knee pad ready

### During Cleaning

- [ ] Change positions frequently
- [ ] Switch hands/sides
- [ ] Take breaks
- [ ] Hydrate

### After Cleaning

- [ ] Stretch
- [ ] Note any pain
- [ ] Rest
