# Handbag Carrying Posture

Handbags are daily accessories. Proper carrying technique prevents asymmetric strain.

## Shoulder Bags

### Single Shoulder

**Common carry:**
- Alternate shoulders
- Not too heavy
- Strap length matters
- Close to body
- Regular switching

### Shoulder Tension

**Preventing strain:**
- Don't hike shoulder
- Relax under strap
- Take breaks
- Lighten load
- Switch sides

## Crossbody Bags

### Best Distribution

**Across body:**
- Weight distributed
- Hands free
- Less shoulder hiking
- Comfortable position
- Preferred style

### Strap Adjustment

**Proper fit:**
- Bag at hip or higher
- Not too low
- Comfortable length
- Easy access
- Secure position

## Tote Bags

### Hand Carrying

**By handles:**
- Alternate hands
- Not too heavy
- Close to body
- Manageable weight
- Arm at side

## Backpack Style

### Two Straps

**Best option:**
- Both shoulders
- Weight distributed
- Most ergonomic
- Consider this style
- Body-friendly

## Weight Management

### Lightening Load

**Essential only:**
- Clean out regularly
- Essential items only
- Smaller bags
- Digital alternatives
- Lighter is better

### Heavy Bag Days

**When needed:**
- Crossbody or backpack
- Take breaks
- Set down often
- Don't overdo
- Plan for weight

Good handbag carrying posture prevents daily asymmetric strain.
