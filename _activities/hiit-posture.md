# HIIT Posture

High-intensity interval training pushes the body hard, making proper form crucial. Maintaining good posture during HIIT prevents injury while maximizing results.

## Exercise Form Under Fatigue

### Maintaining Technique

**As you tire:**
- Form awareness priority
- Quality over quantity
- Rest when needed
- Core engagement
- Proper breathing

### Common Breakdowns

**What happens:**
- Form deteriorates
- Rounding back
- Knees collapsing
- Shortened range
- Compensations

## Common HIIT Movements

### Burpees

**Proper form:**
- Controlled drop
- Core engaged
- Push-up form
- Explosive jump
- Soft landing

### Jump Squats

**Technique focus:**
- Proper squat depth
- Explode upward
- Soft landing
- Knees aligned
- Core engaged

### Mountain Climbers

**Form points:**
- Plank position
- Core stable
- Hips level
- Controlled speed
- Full range

### High Knees

**Running form:**
- Upright torso
- Core engaged
- Arm swing
- Soft landing
- Knee height

## Rest Intervals

### Active Recovery

**Between sets:**
- Hands on thighs
- Controlled breathing
- Quick form reset
- Mental preparation
- Posture recovery

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female HIIT:**
- Core and pelvic floor
- Impact modifications
- Recovery needs
- Hormonal considerations
- Appropriate scaling

Good HIIT posture means knowing when to push and when to prioritize form over intensity.
