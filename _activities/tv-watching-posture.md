# TV Watching Posture

Television watching often involves prolonged sitting in non-ergonomic positions. Understanding proper viewing posture allows relaxation without strain.

## Viewing Setup

### TV Position

**Screen placement:**
- Eye level when seated
- Comfortable distance
- Centered on seating
- Reduced glare
- Appropriate size for distance

### Seating Position

**Where to sit:**
- Centered on TV
- Appropriate distance
- Comfortable seat
- Head facing forward
- Supported body

## Couch Posture

### Proper Sitting

**Supported position:**
- Sit back in cushions
- Feet on floor or ottoman
- Back supported
- Head neutral
- Arms comfortable

### Common Problems

**Typical issues:**
- Slumping
- Neck craning
- Legs tucked under
- Twisted position
- Poor cushion support

## Lounging Safely

### Reclined Positions

**If reclining:**
- Head supported
- Spine alignment
- Legs elevated appropriately
- Regular position changes
- Time limits

### Side Lying

**On couch:**
- Pillow for head
- Spine aligned
- Legs supported
- Not too long
- Position changes

## Long Viewing Sessions

### Taking Breaks

**Movement during:**
- Stand during commercials
- Stretch periodically
- Walk around
- Position changes
- Get up and move

### Time Awareness

**Limiting impact:**
- Set viewing limits
- Active during commercials
- Consider standing options
- Breaks between shows
- Balance with activity

## Alternative Viewing

### Active Options

**Moving while watching:**
- Stationary bike
- Stretching routine
- Light exercises
- Standing periods
- Exercise ball sitting

Proper TV watching posture lets you enjoy entertainment while protecting your body from sedentary strain.
