# Fencing Posture

Fencing demands precise body positioning for both attack and defense. Proper fencing posture enables quick movement and accurate blade work.

## En Garde Position

### Basic Stance

**Ready position:**
- Feet at right angle
- Shoulder-width apart
- Knees bent
- Weight centered
- Weapon arm forward

### Body Alignment

**Proper posture:**
- Sideways to opponent
- Small target
- Back straight
- Chin up
- Core engaged

## Blade Position

### Guard Position

**Weapon ready:**
- Arm extended appropriately
- Point threatening
- Hand at proper height
- Relaxed grip
- Quick reactions ready

### Attack Position

**Extending:**
- Arm extends first
- Point on target
- Body follows
- Lunge preparation
- Recovery ready

## Footwork

### Advance and Retreat

**Basic movement:**
- Front foot leads forward
- Back foot follows
- Reverse for retreat
- Maintain distance
- Stay in guard

### Lunge

**Attack movement:**
- Push from back leg
- Extend front leg
- Arm extended
- Body follows
- Recovery essential

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female fencers:**
- Speed advantage
- Flexibility use
- Strategy emphasis
- Equipment fit
- All ages welcome

### Weapon Choice

**Three weapons:**
- Foil
- Épée
- Sabre
- Different rules
- Different techniques

Good fencing posture creates the foundation for both offensive and defensive excellence.
