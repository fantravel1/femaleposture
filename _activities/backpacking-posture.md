# Backpacking Posture

Backpacking carries home on your back. Proper posture enables comfortable multi-day travel.

## Pack Fit

### Hip Belt

**Weight on hips:**
- Snug on hip bones
- Carries majority weight
- Essential adjustment
- Check fit regularly
- Comfortable padding

### Shoulder Straps

**Upper body:**
- Snug but not tight
- No gaps
- Don't carry too much weight
- Load lifters adjusted
- Comfortable fit

### Load Lifters

**Fine tuning:**
- Pull toward shoulders
- 45 degree angle
- Reduces pulling back
- Important adjustment
- Re-check during hike

## Packing

### Weight Distribution

**Organizing pack:**
- Heavy items center and close to back
- Medium items around
- Light items outside
- Balanced left to right
- Access considered

### Pack Weight

**Managing load:**
- Lighter is better
- Base weight matters
- Multi-use items
- Leave luxury
- Carry essentials

## Trail Posture

### Hiking with Pack

**On trail:**
- Upright posture
- Core engaged
- Trekking poles help
- Steady pace
- Regular breaks

### Terrain Adaptation

**Varied ground:**
- Lean into climbs
- Careful descents
- Rocky terrain slow
- Cross-country cautious
- Appropriate pace

## Rest and Breaks

### Taking Pack Off

**Rest stops:**
- Remove properly
- Set down gently
- Stretch and rest
- Hydrate and eat
- Resume refreshed

### Adjustments

**During hike:**
- Readjust as needed
- Reposition weight
- Tighten or loosen
- Respond to discomfort
- Fine tune fit

Good backpacking posture enables comfortable wilderness travel.
