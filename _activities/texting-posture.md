# Texting Posture

Texting is frequent daily activity. Proper posture prevents neck and hand strain.

## Phone Position

### Raised Viewing

**While texting:**
- Bring phone up
- Eyes forward
- Not head down
- Brief position
- Neck neutral

### Supported Arms

**Arm position:**
- Elbows supported
- Relaxed shoulders
- Comfortable position
- Sustainable typing
- Less fatigue

## Thumb Technique

### Two-Thumb Typing

**Standard method:**
- Both thumbs
- Relaxed grip
- Phone supported
- Efficient typing
- Comfortable motion

### Hand Fatigue

**Extended texting:**
- Take breaks
- Stretch hands
- Vary grip
- Rest thumbs
- Prevent strain

## Voice Option

### Speech to Text

**Alternative:**
- Speak messages
- Reduce typing
- Neck relief
- Efficient method
- When appropriate

## Quick Replies

### Brief Messages

**Short texting:**
- Quick position
- Efficient typing
- Resume normal posture
- Brief interruption
- Minimal impact

Good texting posture prevents neck and hand strain during messaging.
