# Bootcamp Training Posture

Bootcamp combines strength and cardio. Proper posture maintains form during high-intensity work.

## Common Movements

### Burpees

**Full body:**
- Squat position
- Jump back to plank
- Push-up option
- Jump forward
- Stand or jump up

### Mountain Climbers

**Core cardio:**
- Plank position
- Alternating knees
- Quick tempo
- Core stable
- Controlled movement

### Squat Jumps

**Explosive:**
- Deep squat
- Explosive jump
- Soft landing
- Immediate squat
- Continuous motion

## Strength Components

### Push-ups

**Proper form:**
- Plank position
- Core engaged
- Full range
- Modify as needed
- Quality over quantity

### Lunges

**Leg work:**
- Forward or reverse
- Knee tracking
- Core stable
- Controlled motion
- Alternating legs

## Cardio Intervals

### High Knees

**Running in place:**
- Quick tempo
- Arms driving
- Core engaged
- Light on feet
- Sustained effort

### Jumping Jacks

**Full body cardio:**
- Coordinated motion
- Light landing
- Arms and legs together
- Continuous rhythm
- Sustained effort

## Class Structure

### Interval Management

**Work and rest:**
- High intensity work
- Recovery periods
- Repeated rounds
- Progressive challenge
- Full body workout

## Form Priority

### Fatigue Management

**When tired:**
- Maintain form
- Reduce intensity
- Modify movements
- Keep moving
- Stay safe

Good bootcamp posture maintains form during high-intensity group workouts.
