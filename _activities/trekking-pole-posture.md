# Trekking Pole Posture

Trekking poles add stability and reduce strain. Proper technique maximizes their benefit.

## Pole Height

### Setting Height

**Adjustment:**
- Forearm parallel to ground
- Elbow at 90 degrees
- Adjust for terrain
- Comfort priority
- Individual fit

### Terrain Adjustment

**Variable height:**
- Shorter uphill
- Longer downhill
- Flat for normal
- Quick adjust helpful
- Terrain responsive

## Pole Technique

### Flat Ground

**Normal walking:**
- Opposite pole/foot
- Natural swing
- Light plant
- Rhythmic motion
- Efficient movement

### Uphill

**Climbing:**
- Poles shortened
- Plant ahead
- Pull assistance
- Arm engagement
- Power addition

### Downhill

**Descending:**
- Poles lengthened
- Plant ahead
- Braking assistance
- Stability addition
- Joint protection

## Grip and Strap

### Hand Position

**Holding pole:**
- Grip relaxed
- Use strap for support
- Strap through properly
- Not too tight
- Efficient hold

### Strap Use

**Support benefit:**
- Push down through strap
- Relaxed grip
- Efficient force
- Proper technique
- Less hand fatigue

## Benefits

### Why Use Poles

**Advantages:**
- Joint protection
- Balance assistance
- Upper body workout
- Stability on terrain
- Confidence building

Good trekking pole posture maximizes stability and reduces hiking strain.
