# Flute Playing Posture

Flute playing requires balanced horizontal positioning. Proper posture enables comfortable, quality tone.

## Standing Position

### Basic Stance

**While playing:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Weight balanced
- Knees soft
- Upright spine
- Stable base

### Head Position

**While playing:**
- Looking forward
- Chin slightly down
- Neck relaxed
- Natural alignment
- No strain

## Holding the Flute

### Instrument Position

**Horizontal hold:**
- Parallel to floor
- To the right
- Elbows lifted
- Wrists comfortable
- Balanced support

### Arm Position

**Supporting flute:**
- Right arm elevated
- Left arm comfortable
- Shoulders relaxed
- Not raised
- Sustainable position

## Hand Technique

### Finger Position

**On keys:**
- Curved fingers
- Light pressure
- Efficient coverage
- Quick motion
- Relaxed hands

### Embouchure

**Mouth position:**
- Jaw relaxed
- Lips formed
- Air direction
- Consistent position
- Quality tone

## Breathing

### Diaphragmatic

**Breath support:**
- Deep breathing
- Core engagement
- Controlled exhale
- Sustained notes
- Breath management

### Posture Impact

**On breathing:**
- Good posture enables
- Full lung capacity
- Efficient breath
- Better sound
- Essential connection

## Extended Playing

### Practice Sessions

**Long practice:**
- Regular breaks
- Arm rest
- Stretch shoulders
- Sustainable effort
- Gradual building

Good flute posture enables comfortable, quality woodwind playing.
