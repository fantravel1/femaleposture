# Gaming Posture Guide

> **Level Up Your Setup:** Gaming sessions can be long and intense. Proper ergonomics keep you playing without pain.

---

## The Gaming Challenge

### Physical Demands

- **Extended sitting** - Hours of gameplay
- **Intense focus** - Losing body awareness
- **Repetitive motions** - Controller/mouse use
- **Static positions** - Not moving for long periods
- **Forward lean** - Getting into the game
- **Tension** - Competitive intensity

### Common Problems

- Neck and upper back pain
- Lower back issues
- Wrist and hand pain
- Shoulder tension
- Eye strain
- Headaches

---

## PC Gaming Setup

### Monitor Position

**Height:**
- Top of screen at or slightly below eye level
- Not looking up or down
- Neutral neck position

**Distance:**
- Arm's length away (roughly)
- Can see without leaning in
- Appropriate for screen size

**Angle:**
- Slight tilt back (10-15 degrees)
- No glare
- Comfortable viewing

### Desk Height

**Keyboard and mouse level:**
- Elbows at about 90 degrees
- Wrists neutral (not bent)
- Shoulders relaxed
- Arms at comfortable height

### Chair

**Gaming chair or office chair:**
- Lumbar support
- Adjustable height
- Armrests that fit under desk
- Comfortable for long sessions

**Your position:**
- Back against chair back
- Feet flat or on footrest
- Knees at about 90 degrees
- Not perching on edge

---

## Console Gaming

### TV Position

**Viewing angle:**
- Eye level or slightly below
- Not looking up at wall-mount
- Appropriate distance for screen size

**Seating:**
- Couch with support
- Not sinking into too-soft cushions
- Back support (pillow if needed)
- Comfortable viewing angle

### Controller Position

**Holding controller:**
- Arms supported on lap/armrests
- Elbows comfortable
- Not hunching over controller
- Shoulders relaxed

### Common Problems

**Couch gaming:**
- Tends to slouching
- Less structured setup
- Easy to lose posture

**Solutions:**
- Pillow behind lower back
- Sit back, don't sink
- Position changes
- Regular breaks

---

## Mobile Gaming

### Phone Position

**The problem:**
- Looking down at screen
- Neck strain (text neck)
- Arms unsupported

**Better approach:**
- Raise phone toward eye level
- Support elbows on something
- Shorten sessions
- Take breaks

### Tablet Gaming

**Similar principles:**
- Raise screen up
- Use stand when possible
- Support arms
- Don't hunch over device

---

## Keyboard and Mouse

### Keyboard

**Position:**
- Flat or negative tilt (not raised back)
- Wrists neutral
- Fingers fall naturally on keys
- Keyboard at edge of desk

**Typing position:**
- Wrists floating (not resting)
- Or soft wrist rest for breaks
- Light touch
- Not pounding keys

### Mouse

**Position:**
- Same height as keyboard
- Close to body
- Mouse size fits hand
- Appropriate sensitivity

**Movement:**
- From shoulder/elbow for large movements
- Not just wrist
- Arm supported between movements
- Consider ergonomic mouse

### WASD Fatigue

**PC gaming specific:**
- Same hand position for hours
- Stretch fingers between matches
- Vary key bindings if possible
- Rest hand periodically

---

## Taking Breaks

### During Gameplay

**Between matches/levels:**
- Stand up briefly
- Look away from screen
- Stretch shoulders and neck
- Move differently

**Reminders:**
- Set timer if needed
- Use loading screens
- Game breaks for bio = body breaks

### Eye Care

**20-20-20 rule:**
- Every 20 minutes
- Look 20 feet away
- For 20 seconds

**Other tips:**
- Blink consciously
- Reduce screen brightness if needed
- Consider blue light settings

---

## Intense Gaming Sessions

### Long Sessions

**Reality of gaming:**
- Sometimes you play for hours
- Competitive matches take time
- Want to enjoy the experience

**Harm reduction:**
- Best possible setup
- Movement during any breaks
- Stretching before and after
- Recovery afterward

### Tournament/Competitive

**Extended competitive play:**
- Setup is critical
- Breaks where possible
- Hydration and food
- Sleep matters for performance
- Long-term health enables long-term play

## Stretches for Gamers

### Between Matches

**Neck:**
- Chin tucks
- Side tilts
- Gentle rotation

**Shoulders:**
- Shoulder rolls
- Shoulder blade squeezes
- Arm circles

**Wrists and Hands:**
- Wrist circles
- Finger spreads
- Prayer stretch

### After Sessions

**More thorough:**
- Full upper body stretch
- Lower back stretches
- Hip flexor stretches (from sitting)
- Walk around

## Gaming Chair Reality

### Marketing vs. Function

**The truth:**
- Racing-style doesn't equal ergonomic
- Look for actual support features
- Adjustability matters most
- Price doesn't equal quality

### What Actually Matters

- Lumbar support (adjustable)
- Height adjustment
- Armrest adjustment
- Seat comfort for your body
- Quality construction

### Office Chair Alternative

**May be better:**
- Designed for long sitting
- Ergonomic features proven
- Worth considering

## Building Good Habits

### Setup Once, Benefit Always

**Invest in setup:**
- Proper monitor height
- Good seating
- Correct desk height
- Quality peripherals

### Make Breaks Automatic

**Build into routine:**
- Bio break = movement break
- Loading screen = quick stretch
- Match end = stand up
- Session end = full stretch

## Special Considerations

### VR Gaming

**Different demands:**
- Standing/moving
- Arm reaching
- Balance challenges
- Different strain patterns

**Tips:**
- Clear play space
- Warm up first
- Take breaks (motion sickness too)
- Listen to body

### Streaming

**Extra demands:**
- Camera awareness
- Longer sessions
- Talking while playing
- Posture on display
