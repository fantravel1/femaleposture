# Childcare Posture

Childcare involves constant lifting, carrying, and bending. Proper posture protects your body through the physically demanding early years.

## Lifting Children

### Infant Lifting

**Safe technique:**
- Bend at knees
- Keep baby close
- Core engaged
- Rise with legs
- Support baby's head

### Toddler Lifting

**Heavier children:**
- Squat down
- Child holds on
- Close to body
- Leg power
- Core stability

## Carrying Positions

### Baby Carrying

**Various holds:**
- Hip carry awareness
- Alternate sides
- Carrier use
- Core engagement
- Posture maintenance

### Baby Wearing

**Carrier posture:**
- Proper fit
- Weight distributed
- Core engaged
- Shoulder relief
- Duration limits

## Floor Play

### Getting Down

**Safe technique:**
- Use hands
- Kneel first
- Controlled descent
- Cushioning
- Variety of positions

### Playing Low

**Floor positions:**
- Change positions often
- Use support
- Core engagement
- Getting up safely
- Listen to body

## Feeding Positions

### Breastfeeding

**Nursing posture:**
- Support for arm
- Baby at breast level
- Pillow support
- Back supported
- Comfortable duration

### Bottle Feeding

**Holding baby:**
- Arm supported
- Baby elevated
- Comfortable position
- Alternate arms
- Posture awareness

Good childcare posture protects your body through the physically demanding job of caring for children.
