# Kettlebell Posture

Kettlebell training combines strength and cardio. Proper posture is essential for the dynamic movements involved.

## Basic Position

### Athletic Stance

**Starting position:**
- Feet shoulder-width or wider
- Toes slightly out
- Knees soft
- Core engaged
- Eyes forward

### Hip Hinge

**Foundation movement:**
- Push hips back
- Soft knee bend
- Flat back
- Weight in heels
- Chest up

## The Swing

### Setup

**Swing preparation:**
- Bell ahead of feet
- Hinge to grip
- Shoulders packed
- Lats engaged
- Core braced

### Movement

**Swing execution:**
- Hip drive primary
- Arm guides bell
- Bell to shoulder height
- Control descent
- Repeat smoothly

## Other Exercises

### Goblet Squat

**Squat form:**
- Bell at chest
- Elbows between knees
- Full depth
- Core engaged
- Upright torso

### Clean

**Powerful lift:**
- Hip drive
- Elbow close
- Bell rotation
- Rack position
- Soft catch

### Press

**Overhead movement:**
- From rack position
- Core stability
- Lockout overhead
- Controlled descent
- Safe shoulder path

## Common Errors

### Swing Mistakes

**What to avoid:**
- Squat instead of hinge
- Arms doing work
- Rounded back
- Hyperextension
- Poor timing

Good kettlebell posture creates powerful, safe movement through the unique demands of bell training.
