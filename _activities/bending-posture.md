# Bending Posture

Bending is one of the most frequent movements in daily life, yet improper bending is a leading cause of back pain. Learning proper bending technique protects your spine.

## Bending Basics

### Hip Hinge

**Primary technique:**
- Hinge at hips
- Push hips back
- Keep spine neutral
- Soft knees
- Chest up

### Squat Bend

**Deep bending:**
- Feet wider
- Full squat down
- Weight in heels
- Spine neutral
- Rise with legs

## When to Use Each

### Hip Hinge Use

**Best for:**
- Shallow bending
- Quick reaches
- Light objects
- Good flexibility
- Repeated bending

### Squat Use

**Best for:**
- Low objects
- Heavy items
- Prolonged ground work
- Deep reaching
- Leg strength use

## Common Bending Tasks

### Picking Up Items

**Proper technique:**
- Assess weight first
- Choose bend type
- Get close
- Keep spine safe
- Lift with legs

### Low Cabinet Access

**Safe reaching:**
- Squat or half-kneel
- Use door for balance
- Don't round back
- Take time
- Rise carefully

### Child Care Tasks

**Baby and toddler:**
- Frequent bending required
- Vary technique
- Use furniture height
- Build strength
- Core engagement

## Building Bending Ability

### Flexibility Work

**Mobility needs:**
- Hip flexibility
- Hamstring length
- Ankle mobility
- Thoracic movement
- Overall flexibility

### Strength Building

**Supporting muscles:**
- Glutes
- Hamstrings
- Core
- Back extensors
- Quads

Proper bending technique protects your back through countless daily tasks while building functional fitness.
