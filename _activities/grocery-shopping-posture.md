# Grocery Shopping Posture

Grocery shopping involves walking, reaching, lifting, and carrying. Proper posture protects your body through this regular activity.

## Cart Use

### Pushing Position

**Cart posture:**
- Stand upright
- Arms relaxed
- Don't lean on cart
- Keep cart close
- Push with core

### Cart Height

**Handle level:**
- Elbows slightly bent
- Shoulders relaxed
- Wrists neutral
- Comfortable grip
- Not hunched

## Reaching for Items

### High Shelves

**Safe reaching:**
- Ask for help or step stool
- Don't overreach
- Face item directly
- Use core
- Stable base

### Low Shelves

**Safe bending:**
- Squat or lunge
- Core engaged
- Don't round back
- Use cart for support
- Rise carefully

## Loading and Unloading

### At Checkout

**Belt loading:**
- Keep items close
- Don't twist
- Even distribution
- Manageable weight
- Bag properly

### Into Car

**Trunk loading:**
- Items close to edge
- Squat if needed
- Core engaged
- Don't overreach into trunk
- Multiple loads

## Carrying Bags

### From Car to Home

**Safe carrying:**
- Balance both sides
- Multiple trips
- Manageable weight
- Core engaged
- Safe stairs

Good grocery shopping posture protects your back through this regular household task.
