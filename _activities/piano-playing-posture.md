# Piano Playing Posture

Piano playing requires sustained positioning. Proper posture enables comfortable, effective playing.

## Bench Position

### Height

**Seat level:**
- Forearms parallel to floor
- Elbows slightly above keys
- Adjust bench height
- Personal preference
- Comfortable position

### Distance

**From piano:**
- Arms reach comfortably
- Not too close
- Elbows clear body
- Full keyboard access
- Natural position

## Body Position

### Sitting Posture

**On bench:**
- Forward on bench
- Feet flat on floor
- Core engaged
- Upright spine
- Balanced weight

### Shoulders

**Upper body:**
- Relaxed down
- Not raised
- Open chest
- Natural position
- Freedom of movement

## Hand Position

### Basic Form

**On keys:**
- Curved fingers
- Relaxed hands
- Wrist level
- Thumb natural
- Efficient position

### Arm Weight

**Playing technique:**
- Weight from arm
- Not finger tension
- Relaxed wrist
- Flowing motion
- Sound production

## Pedal Use

### Foot Position

**At pedals:**
- Heel on floor
- Ball of foot on pedal
- Ankle flexible
- Smooth motion
- Sustained comfort

## Practice Sessions

### Extended Playing

**Long practice:**
- Regular breaks
- Stand and stretch
- Variety of pieces
- Hand care
- Sustainable practice

Good piano posture enables comfortable, expressive playing.
