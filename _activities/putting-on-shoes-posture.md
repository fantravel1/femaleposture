# Putting On Shoes Posture

Putting on shoes requires balance or bending. Proper posture supports daily footwear routine.

## Seated Approach

### Sitting Down

**Seated method:**
- Sit on chair or bed
- Bring foot up
- Easy access
- No balance needed
- Comfortable method

### Cross-Legged

**Foot to knee:**
- Sit comfortably
- Foot across knee
- Easy shoe access
- Tie laces easily
- Stable position

## Standing Approach

### With Support

**Holding something:**
- Wall or chair
- Lift foot behind
- Slip shoe on
- Maintain balance
- Efficient method

### One-Legged Balance

**Without support:**
- Challenging balance
- Quick motion
- Core engaged
- Brief duration
- Skill required

## Tying Laces

### Best Position

**Lace tying:**
- Seated preferred
- Foot elevated
- Easy access
- Comfortable tying
- Quality knot

### Shoe Bench

**Entryway setup:**
- Dedicated seat
- Shoe storage
- Convenient setup
- Good habit
- Organized entry

## Slip-On Shoes

### Easier Option

**No lacing:**
- Quick on
- Less bending
- Convenient
- Style option
- Time saver

Good putting on shoes posture supports comfortable, balanced footwear routine.
