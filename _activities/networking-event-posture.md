# Networking Event Posture

Networking events combine standing and socializing. Proper posture projects approachable confidence.

## Standing Presence

### Open Stance

**Approachable posture:**
- Shoulders back
- Open body
- Welcoming stance
- Not closed off
- Ready to engage

### Confident Bearing

**Professional presence:**
- Upright posture
- Balanced stance
- Head high
- Relaxed confidence
- Capable appearance

## Conversation Position

### One-on-One

**Individual conversations:**
- Face person
- Open body
- Comfortable distance
- Engaged posture
- Active listening

### Group Circles

**Multiple people:**
- Part of circle
- Open to group
- Engaged with speakers
- Inclusive positioning
- Connected presence

## Extended Standing

### Fatigue Management

**Long events:**
- Shift weight
- Find spots to lean
- Brief sitting
- Pace yourself
- Comfortable shoes essential

### Strategic Breaks

**Rest periods:**
- Bathroom breaks
- Refreshment table
- Brief sits
- Recharge moments
- Sustained energy

## Drink and Food

### Holding Items

**With refreshments:**
- One hand holds drink
- Other free for handshakes
- Not overeating
- Manageable items
- Professional handling

Good networking event posture supports effective professional relationship building.
