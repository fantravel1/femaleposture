# Curling Posture

Curling requires unique delivery positioning. Proper posture enables accurate, controlled play.

## Delivery Position

### Hack Setup

**Starting position:**
- Foot in hack
- Stone in hand
- Balance position
- Ready for push
- Focused aim

### Slide

**Delivery motion:**
- Push from hack
- Low glide position
- Trailing leg extends
- Stone in front
- Balance maintained

### Release

**Letting go:**
- Smooth release
- Handle turn
- Controlled velocity
- Target line
- Follow through

## Slide Position

### Low Form

**During delivery:**
- Very low stance
- Front leg bent deep
- Back leg straight trailing
- Chest over front knee
- Balanced glide

### Balance

**Maintaining stability:**
- Core engaged
- Quiet upper body
- Smooth motion
- Controlled position
- Practice essential

## Sweeping

### Sweeping Stance

**Working the stone:**
- Low athletic position
- Both hands on broom
- Weight on brush
- Quick motion
- Endurance required

### Sweeping Technique

**Effective sweeping:**
- Pressure on stone path
- Quick strokes
- Communication
- Sustained effort
- Team coordination

## Skip Position

### Strategic Position

**Calling shots:**
- At far end
- Broom for target
- Reading ice
- Team communication
- Strategic thinking

## Fitness

### Demands

**Physical requirements:**
- Flexibility for delivery
- Endurance for sweeping
- Core strength
- Balance
- Progressive development

Good curling posture enables accurate delivery and effective sweeping.
