# Video Calls Posture

Video calls are workplace standard. Proper posture enables comfort and professional appearance.

## Camera Position

### Eye Level

**Camera placement:**
- At eye level
- Straight on view
- No looking down at laptop
- Elevate if needed
- Professional angle

### Distance

**Framing:**
- Not too close
- Not too far
- Head and shoulders
- Some background
- Comfortable viewing

## Body Position

### Seated Posture

**During calls:**
- Upright in chair
- Shoulders back
- Core engaged
- Professional posture
- Comfortable position

### Screen Distance

**Viewing:**
- Arms length
- Reduce eye strain
- Clear viewing
- Comfortable focus
- Natural position

## Long Meetings

### Fatigue Prevention

**Extended calls:**
- Camera breaks if appropriate
- Stretch between calls
- Change position
- Eye rest
- Movement when off camera

### Multi-Hour Days

**Back-to-back calls:**
- Schedule breaks
- Stand between
- Stretch and move
- Hydrate
- Sustainable schedule

## Professional Appearance

### On Camera

**Visual presentation:**
- Good posture projects confidence
- Eye contact with camera
- Appropriate framing
- Background consideration
- Lighting awareness

## Equipment

### Setup Optimization

**Hardware helps:**
- External webcam
- Proper monitor height
- Good lighting
- Quality audio
- Comfortable workspace

Good video call posture maintains comfort and professional presence.
