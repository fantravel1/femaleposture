# Vacuuming Posture

Vacuuming involves pushing and walking. Proper posture prevents strain during floor cleaning.

## Vacuum Handling

### Handle Position

**Holding vacuum:**
- Upright posture
- Relaxed grip
- Handle at comfortable height
- Not hunching
- Efficient control

### Power Settings

**Vacuum type:**
- Lightweight preferred
- Cordless convenient
- Power appropriate
- Easy maneuverability
- Comfortable use

## Movement Pattern

### Pushing Motion

**Forward and back:**
- Core engaged
- Arms relaxed
- Steady rhythm
- Smooth strokes
- Efficient cleaning

### Direction Changes

**Turning:**
- Turn whole body
- Not twisting spine
- Smooth transitions
- Systematic pattern
- Complete coverage

## Body Mechanics

### Upright Position

**While vacuuming:**
- Stand tall
- Shoulders relaxed
- Core engaged
- Back straight
- Comfortable stance

### Reaching Areas

**Under furniture:**
- Kneel or squat
- Extension wands
- Don't overreach
- Safe positioning
- Thorough cleaning

## Extended Cleaning

### Large Spaces

**Multiple rooms:**
- Take breaks
- Alternate areas
- Sustainable pace
- Complete job
- Manage fatigue

Good vacuuming posture enables efficient, comfortable floor cleaning.
