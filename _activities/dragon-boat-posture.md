# Dragon Boat Posture

Dragon boat racing demands synchronized team paddling. Proper posture enables powerful, coordinated strokes.

## Seated Position

### On the Bench

**Team position:**
- Upright torso
- Core engaged
- Feet braced
- Knees bent
- Stable base

### Rotation Ready

**Prepared for power:**
- Shoulders square
- Torso mobile
- Core activated
- Arms ready
- Full attention

## Paddle Grip

### Hand Position

**Holding paddle:**
- Top hand on T-grip
- Lower hand on shaft
- Comfortable spacing
- Relaxed grip
- Blade angle correct

## Stroke Phases

### The Catch

**Entry:**
- Reach forward
- Rotate torso
- Arms extended
- Blade enters cleanly
- Bury fully

### The Pull

**Power phase:**
- Core rotation
- Engage back
- Arms guide
- Blade stays vertical
- Pull to hip

### The Exit

**Recovery:**
- Clean blade exit
- Quick recovery
- Controlled return
- Prepare for catch
- Match team timing

## Synchronization

### Timing

**Team unity:**
- Watch stroke caller
- Match timing exactly
- Unified motion
- Collective power
- Rhythm maintained

### Power Together

**Team effort:**
- Same stroke rate
- Equal power
- Balanced boat
- No individual heroes
- Team success

Good dragon boat posture creates the synchronized power needed for racing success.
