# Agility Training Posture

Agility training improves quickness and coordination. Proper posture enables fast, controlled movement.

## Athletic Stance

### Ready Position

**Base position:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Weight on balls of feet
- Knees bent
- Hips low
- Ready to move

### Movement Readiness

**Prepared:**
- Core engaged
- Arms ready
- Eyes forward
- Quick reaction
- Multi-directional

## Ladder Drills

### Quick Feet

**In and out:**
- Light foot strikes
- Quick rhythm
- Core stable
- Arms coordinated
- Pattern practice

### Lateral Movement

**Side patterns:**
- Side steps
- Crossovers
- In and out
- Quick feet
- Coordinated motion

## Cone Drills

### Direction Changes

**Cutting:**
- Plant and cut
- Low center
- Quick pivot
- Explosive restart
- Controlled motion

### Figure Eight

**Continuous motion:**
- Flowing curves
- Smooth transitions
- Speed control
- Footwork precision
- Rhythm maintained

## Reactive Drills

### Response Training

**Quick reaction:**
- Verbal cues
- Visual signals
- Decision making
- Quick execution
- Sport specificity

### Chase Drills

**Partner work:**
- React to partner
- Direction following
- Competitive element
- Fun training
- Sport application

## Benefits

### Athletic Transfer

**Sport application:**
- Game speed
- Quick reactions
- Coordination
- Confidence
- Performance improvement

Good agility training posture enables quick, controlled movement in all directions.
