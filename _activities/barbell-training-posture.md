# Barbell Training Posture

Barbell training builds foundational strength. Proper posture enables heavy, safe lifting.

## Squat Variations

### Back Squat

**Bar on back:**
- Bar position (high/low)
- Core braced
- Controlled descent
- Depth achieved
- Strong drive up

### Front Squat

**Bar at shoulders:**
- Rack position
- Elbows high
- Upright torso
- Full depth
- Core engaged

## Deadlift Variations

### Conventional Deadlift

**Standard pull:**
- Hip-width stance
- Mixed or hook grip
- Back set
- Bar close
- Full lockout

### Sumo Deadlift

**Wide stance:**
- Feet wide
- Grip inside legs
- Upright torso
- Leg drive
- Full lockout

## Pressing

### Bench Press

**Horizontal press:**
- Shoulder blade retraction
- Arch position
- Bar path
- Controlled motion
- Lockout

### Overhead Press

**Standing press:**
- Bar at shoulders
- Tight core
- Press overhead
- Full lockout
- Bar behind ears

## Rowing

### Bent Over Row

**Back builder:**
- Hip hinge position
- Back flat
- Pull to stomach
- Controlled return
- Back squeeze

## Form Essentials

### Core Principles

**Lifting guidelines:**
- Warm up thoroughly
- Progressive loading
- Perfect form first
- Breathe properly
- Rest appropriately

Good barbell training posture enables safe, effective strength building.
