# Hanging Pictures Posture

Hanging pictures involves reaching and precision. Proper posture ensures safe, accurate placement.

## Planning Position

### Visualization

**Before hanging:**
- Hold at wall
- Check height
- View from distance
- Mark position
- Partner helps

### Measuring

**Precise placement:**
- Level surface
- Measured marks
- Step back check
- Accurate positioning
- Quality result

## Reaching for Height

### Standard Heights

**Eye level work:**
- Comfortable reach
- Natural position
- Minor stretching
- Easy access
- Optimal height

### Above Eye Level

**Higher placement:**
- Step stool needed
- Stable platform
- Controlled reaching
- Safe positioning
- Careful mounting

## Using Tools

### Hammer Work

**Driving nails:**
- Steady swing
- Controlled force
- Eye protection
- Accurate strikes
- Patient work

### Drill Use

**For anchors:**
- Two hands stable
- Controlled pressure
- Eye level or step stool
- Safe operation
- Secure mounting

## Heavy Frames

### Large Artwork

**Heavy pieces:**
- Get help
- Two people safer
- Secure hardware
- Strong anchors
- Careful lifting

## Adjustment

### Fine-Tuning

**Leveling:**
- Small adjustments
- Level check
- Step back view
- Final positioning
- Satisfying result

Good picture hanging posture enables safe, accurate wall art placement.
