# Drawing Posture

Drawing requires sustained focus and fine motor control. Proper posture prevents the neck and hand strain common to visual artists.

## Workspace Setup

### Drawing Surface

**Table position:**
- Tilted surface ideal
- Appropriate height
- Good lighting
- Comfortable reach
- Paper positioned well

### Seating

**Chair setup:**
- Adjustable height
- Back support
- Feet grounded
- Arms comfortable
- Core engaged

## Body Position

### Seated Drawing

**Proper posture:**
- Upright spine
- Shoulders relaxed
- Work surface tilted
- Arms supported
- Regular breaks

### Hand Position

**Drawing grip:**
- Relaxed hold
- Wrist movement varies
- Draw from shoulder too
- Pressure awareness
- Varied techniques

## Common Issues

### Neck Strain

**Looking down:**
- Tilt drawing surface
- Proper chair height
- Eye-level work when possible
- Regular breaks
- Neck stretches

### Hand and Wrist

**Fine motor work:**
- Grip variation
- Wrist rests
- Hand stretches
- Arm movement too
- Rest periods

## Prevention Strategies

### Workspace Optimization

**Ergonomic setup:**
- Drafting table or tilted surface
- Proper lighting
- Organized tools
- Comfortable chair
- Good ventilation

### Break Schedule

**Movement intervals:**
- Every 20-30 minutes
- Stand and stretch
- Walk around
- Eye rest
- Full body movement

Good drawing posture protects your neck, shoulders, and hands while supporting your creative practice.
