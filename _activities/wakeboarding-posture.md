# Wakeboarding Posture

Wakeboarding combines balance and power on water. Proper posture enables control and progression.

## Getting Up

### Start Position

**In water:**
- Knees to chest
- Arms straight
- Board perpendicular
- Patience
- Let boat pull

### Standing

**Rising up:**
- Stay low initially
- Let boat do work
- Core engaged
- Balanced weight
- Gradual stand

## Riding Position

### Basic Stance

**On board:**
- Knees bent
- Weight centered
- Arms relaxed
- Eyes forward
- Core engaged

### Edge Control

**Cutting:**
- Weight transfer
- Edge pressure
- Core stability
- Hip rotation
- Speed control

## Tricks

### Jumping Wake

**Getting air:**
- Edge approach
- Controlled pop
- Body position
- Soft landing
- Core engaged

### Landing

**Safe return:**
- Knees absorb
- Weight centered
- Core engaged
- Rope tension
- Controlled descent

## Women-Specific

### Physical Factors

**Female wakeboarding:**
- Lower center of gravity
- Board sizing
- Boot fit
- Strength building
- Confidence progression

Good wakeboarding posture creates the stability needed for control and trick progression on the water.
