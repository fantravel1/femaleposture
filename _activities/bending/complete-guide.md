# Bending Posture Guide

## Why Bending Technique Matters

### The Frequency

**You bend constantly:**
- Picking things up
- Reaching low drawers
- Putting on shoes
- Cleaning
- Countless daily motions

### The Risk

**Poor bending creates:**
- Disc pressure
- Ligament strain
- Muscle overload
- Cumulative damage
- Acute injury potential

---

## The Hip Hinge

### The Foundation

**The hip hinge:**
- Bending at the hips, not waist
- Spine stays neutral
- Sends butt back
- Essential movement pattern

### How to Hip Hinge

1. **Stand with feet hip-width**
2. **Soft knees (slightly bent)**
3. **Push hips back**
4. **Let torso tilt forward**
5. **Back stays flat**
6. **Feel it in hamstrings**

### Practice

**Building the pattern:**
- Practice against wall (butt touches wall as you hinge)
- Dowel on spine (head, upper back, sacrum contact)
- Daily practice
- Make automatic

## Bending Options

### Hip Hinge

**When to use:**
- Light objects
- Brief bends
- Counter-height and below
- Good mobility required

### Squat

**When to use:**
- Heavier objects
- Need to get low
- Sustained low position
- When hinge isn't enough

**How:**
- Feet wider than hips
- Squat down (butt drops)
- Back stays straight
- Stand using legs

### Lunge

**When to use:**
- Accessing low spaces
- One object pickup
- Getting to floor level
- Variety of depths

**How:**
- Step one foot forward
- Lower back knee toward floor
- Back stays upright
- Push through front leg to stand

### Kneel

**When to use:**
- Extended ground work
- Deep access needed
- Sustained low position
- Can't squat long

## Common Bending Situations

### Picking Up From Floor

**Best approach:**
- Get close to object
- Choose squat, lunge, or golfer's pickup
- Object close to body
- Stand with legs

### Loading Dishwasher

**Frequently problematic:**
- Repeated bending
- Reaching into machine
- Twisting

**Better approach:**
- Squat in front
- Wide stance for stability
- Minimize repetitions (stack items first)
- Core engaged

### Making Bed

**Common strain:**
- Reaching across
- Repeated bending
- Tucking sheets

**Better approach:**
- Move around bed (don't reach across)
- Kneel at edges when tucking
- Good technique each time

### Laundry

**From washer/dryer:**
- Squat at front loaders
- Pull items to you first
- Don't lean and twist
- See [Cleaning Guide](/activities/cleaning/)

### Gardening

**Extensive bending:**
- Use kneeling pad
- Mix positions (kneel, squat, sit)
- Tools that reduce bending
- See [Gardening Guide](/activities/gardening/)

## The Golfer's Pickup

### What It Is

**Single-leg counterbalance:**
- One leg swings back as counterweight
- Other hand holds support
- Quick light pickups only
- Reduces forward bend depth

### When to Use

- Light objects
- Quick pickup
- Have something to hold
- Good balance

---

## Red Flags

### When Bending Is Risky

**Be extra careful if:**
- Existing back pain
- Recent injury
- Carrying extra weight
- First thing in morning
- After prolonged sitting

### Signs You Bent Wrong

**Stop and note:**
- Sharp pain
- Catch or lock
- Muscle spasm
- Numbness
- Weakness

## Building Better Patterns

### Awareness First

**Notice:**
- How you currently bend
- When you feel strain
- Your default patterns
- Opportunities to improve

### Habit Change

**Process:**
- Practice correct patterns
- Conscious effort initially
- Repetition builds automatic
- Eventually natural

### Strength Supports

**Helps bending:**
- Core strength
- Glute strength
- Hamstring flexibility
- Hip mobility

---

## Special Situations

### Pregnancy

**Modified bending:**
- Wider stance
- More squatting
- Less forward bending
- Ask for help more

### Back Pain History

**Extra precautions:**
- More conservative
- Hip hinge emphasis
- Get low with squat/kneel
- Avoid problematic movements

### Morning Bending

**First hours after waking:**
- Discs more hydrated
- More vulnerable
- More careful bending
- Warm up first

## Quick Summary

1. **Bend at hips, not waist**
2. **Keep back neutral**
3. **Squat for heavy/low**
4. **Get close to objects**
5. **Use legs to return**
6. **Variety of techniques**
7. **Practice good patterns**
