# Skiing Posture

Skiing requires specific body positioning for control and safety. Proper skiing posture protects your body while improving performance on the slopes.

## Downhill Skiing Position

### Basic Stance

**Foundation elements:**
- Feet hip-width apart
- Ankles flexed forward
- Knees bent
- Hips slightly back
- Hands forward

### Athletic Position

**Ready to ski:**
- Weight centered
- Core engaged
- Shoulders level
- Eyes forward
- Balanced stance

## Turning Mechanics

### Proper Turn Form

**Technique focus:**
- Weight shift
- Edge control
- Core rotation
- Upper body stability
- Pole plant

### Common Errors

**What to avoid:**
- Leaning back
- Sitting too low
- Stiff stance
- Arms back
- Looking down

## Cross-Country Form

### Classic Technique

**Gliding position:**
- Upright stance
- Diagonal stride
- Arm swing coordination
- Weight transfer
- Pole timing

### Skating Technique

**Dynamic form:**
- V-position
- Hip drive
- Core power
- Balance on one ski
- Rhythmic movement

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Equipment Fit

**Proper gear:**
- Women-specific boots
- Appropriate ski length
- Binding adjustment
- Pole length
- Clothing comfort

### Injury Prevention

**Staying safe:**
- Proper conditioning
- Technique lessons
- Warming up
- Knowing limits
- Rest when needed

Good skiing posture keeps you safe and in control while allowing you to enjoy the mountain experience fully.
