# Spinning Posture

Indoor cycling offers intense cardio workout, but poor bike setup and form can cause injury. Proper spinning posture protects your body while maximizing the workout.

## Bike Setup

### Seat Height

**Proper adjustment:**
- Slight knee bend at bottom
- Not too high or low
- Hip stability
- Comfortable pedaling
- Adjustable during class

### Handlebar Position

**Handle setup:**
- Comfortable reach
- Slight elbow bend
- Shoulders relaxed
- Multiple positions
- Personal preference

### Fore/Aft Position

**Seat position:**
- Knee over pedal spindle
- Proper distance to bars
- Comfortable reach
- Power position
- Individual fit

## Riding Position

### Seated Position

**In saddle:**
- Hips stable
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Hands light on bars
- Smooth pedal stroke

### Standing Position

**Out of saddle:**
- Hips over pedals
- Light hands
- Controlled movement
- Core stability
- Smooth transition

## Common Errors

### What to Avoid

**Problematic form:**
- Bouncing in saddle
- Death grip on bars
- Hunched shoulders
- Excessive movement
- Poor resistance

### Corrections

**Better technique:**
- Smooth pedaling
- Relaxed grip
- Open chest
- Controlled movement
- Appropriate resistance

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Comfort Issues

**Female-specific:**
- Saddle comfort
- Appropriate shorts
- Proper fit
- Hydration
- Recovery

Good spinning posture allows high-intensity training without the strain of poor positioning.
