# Airplane Posture

Air travel challenges the body with cramped seats and limited movement. Proper positioning and strategies protect your body during flights.

## Seat Setup

### Getting Comfortable

**Initial positioning:**
- Adjust seat and headrest
- Lumbar support (pillow if needed)
- Recline appropriately
- Feet positioned
- Belt positioned

### Support Props

**Helpful items:**
- Neck pillow
- Lumbar pillow or rolled jacket
- Footrest or bag under feet
- Blanket for comfort
- Travel pillow options

## Body Position

### Seated Posture

**In-flight positioning:**
- Back supported
- Feet flat or slightly elevated
- Shoulders relaxed
- Neck supported
- Core gently engaged

### Sleeping Position

**If resting:**
- Neck pillow use
- Lean toward window if available
- Blanket support
- Upright or slight recline
- Eye mask helps

## Movement

### In-Seat Exercises

**Circulation helpers:**
- Ankle circles
- Foot pumps
- Knee lifts
- Shoulder rolls
- Neck stretches

### Getting Up

**Walk when possible:**
- Every hour if able
- Aisle walks
- Bathroom breaks
- Standing stretches
- Circulation priority

## Long-Haul Flights

### Extended Strategies

**Multi-hour flights:**
- Compression socks
- Hydration priority
- Regular movement
- Position changes
- Sleep strategy

### Post-Flight

**After landing:**
- Stretch thoroughly
- Walk if possible
- Gentle movement
- Hydrate
- Allow adjustment time

Good airplane posture makes the difference between arriving stiff and sore versus ready to go.
