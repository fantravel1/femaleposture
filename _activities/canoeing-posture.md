# Canoeing Posture

Canoeing allows different positions for paddling. Proper posture enables efficient, comfortable travel.

## Seated Position

### On the Seat

**Basic sitting:**
- Upright torso
- Back straight
- Knees bent
- Feet braced
- Core engaged

### High Kneeling

**Traditional position:**
- Knees on padding
- Thighs against seat
- Upright posture
- Lower center of gravity
- More control

## Paddle Grip

### Hand Position

**Single blade:**
- Top hand on grip
- Shaft hand lower
- Comfortable spacing
- Relaxed grip
- Blade oriented correctly

## Stroke Technique

### Forward Stroke

**Basic paddling:**
- Reach forward
- Plant blade fully
- Pull alongside hull
- Exit at hip
- Recovery forward

### J-Stroke

**Tracking straight:**
- Forward stroke
- Outward pry at end
- Boat stays straight
- Subtle motion
- One-sided paddling

## Power Application

### Efficient Movement

**Using core:**
- Torso rotation
- Core engagement
- Arms guide
- Full body paddle
- Sustainable effort

## Partner Paddling

### Tandem

**Two paddlers:**
- Bow paddles
- Stern steers
- Opposite sides
- Synchronized rhythm
- Communication essential

Good canoeing posture enables comfortable, efficient paddling for extended trips.
