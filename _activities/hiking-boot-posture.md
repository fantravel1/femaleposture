# Hiking Boot Posture

Hiking boots require technique adaptation. Proper posture works with boot features.

## Boot Characteristics

### Ankle Support

**Higher boot:**
- Supports ankle
- May limit motion
- Heavier weight
- Protection benefit
- Proper use

### Stiff Sole

**Platform:**
- Less flex
- More support
- Different roll
- Edge on rocks
- Terrain handling

## Walking Technique

### Boot Walk

**Modified gait:**
- Flat foot plant
- Less toe-off
- Deliberate steps
- Secure placement
- Boot-friendly motion

### Break In

**New boots:**
- Gradual wearing
- Short walks first
- Check hot spots
- Allow adjustment
- Prevent blisters

## Terrain Handling

### Rocky Ground

**On rocks:**
- Flat placement
- Use sole edge
- Secure footing
- Deliberate steps
- Ankle protection

### Loose Terrain

**Scree and gravel:**
- Flat foot
- Controlled sliding
- Short steps
- Balance maintained
- Boot advantage

## Lacing

### Proper Lacing

**Fit adjustment:**
- Heel locked
- Comfortable forefoot
- Ankle support
- Varied terrain needs
- Adjust during hike

### Different Zones

**Lace adjustment:**
- Tighter for descent
- Looser for ascent
- Heel lock important
- Comfort priority
- Prevent blisters

## Extended Hikes

### Long Days

**All day comfort:**
- Proper fit essential
- Quality socks
- Lace adjustment
- Foot care breaks
- Blister prevention

Good hiking boot posture maximizes boot benefits for trail comfort.
