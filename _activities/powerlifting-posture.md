# Powerlifting Posture

Powerlifting focuses on three lifts. Proper posture maximizes strength with competition rules.

## Squat

### Setup

**Under the bar:**
- Bar position (high or low)
- Grip width
- Core braced
- Unrack controlled
- Walk out efficiently

### Descent

**Going down:**
- Hip hinge initiation
- Knees track out
- Maintain back angle
- Controlled speed
- Depth to parallel or below

### Ascent

**Coming up:**
- Drive through feet
- Maintain posture
- No forward lean
- Full lockout
- Controlled rack

## Bench Press

### Setup

**On the bench:**
- Arch position
- Shoulder blades retracted
- Feet planted
- Grip established
- Stable foundation

### Descent

**Bar to chest:**
- Controlled lower
- Touch point consistent
- Elbows positioned
- Pause (competition)
- Ready to press

### Press

**Driving up:**
- Leg drive
- Bar path
- Lockout complete
- Controlled rack
- Lift complete

## Deadlift

### Setup

**Conventional or Sumo:**
- Stance established
- Grip taken
- Back set
- Slack pulled
- Ready to lift

### Pull

**Off the floor:**
- Leg drive
- Bar close
- Back angle maintained
- Hip and knee extension
- Full lockout

## Competition

### Rules and Standards

**Meet preparation:**
- Commands knowledge
- White light lifts
- Equipment requirements
- Weight selection
- Competition strategy

Good powerlifting posture maximizes lift totals within competition standards.
