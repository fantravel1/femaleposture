# Writing Posture

Writing—whether by hand or keyboard—requires sustained static position. Proper writing posture prevents strain while supporting focus and productivity.

## Handwriting Position

### Paper Placement

**Optimal setup:**
- Paper angled slightly
- Proper distance
- Good lighting
- Surface height
- Comfortable reach

### Body Position

**Seated writing:**
- Feet flat
- Back supported
- Arms at desk height
- Shoulders relaxed
- Head balanced

### Hand Position

**Grip technique:**
- Relaxed grip
- Three-finger hold
- Wrist neutral
- Arm movement too
- No excessive pressure

## Typing Position

### Keyboard Setup

**Ergonomic typing:**
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Wrists neutral
- Fingers curved
- Shoulders relaxed
- Eyes at screen level

### Body Alignment

**At keyboard:**
- Upright torso
- Core engaged
- Feet grounded
- Arms at sides
- Head balanced

## Writing Environment

### Desk Setup

**Optimal workspace:**
- Proper height
- Good lighting
- Chair adjusted
- Minimal clutter
- Tools within reach

### Break Routine

**Movement intervals:**
- Every 30 minutes
- Stand and stretch
- Walk around
- Eye rest
- Hand stretches

## Common Issues

### Hand Fatigue

**From writing:**
- Grip awareness
- Regular breaks
- Hand exercises
- Varied tools
- Strength building

Good writing posture supports your creative work while protecting your body through long sessions.
