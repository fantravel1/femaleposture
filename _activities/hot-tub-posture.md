# Hot Tub Posture

Hot tub soaking offers relaxation. Proper posture maximizes benefits while ensuring safety.

## Entry and Exit

### Getting In

**Safe entry:**
- Use handrails
- Step carefully
- Wet surfaces slippery
- Controlled entry
- Safe positioning

### Getting Out

**Careful exit:**
- Move slowly
- Hold support
- Watch for dizziness
- Careful standing
- Safe exit

## Soaking Position

### Seated Relaxation

**In hot tub:**
- Seated on bench
- Back supported
- Shoulders at water level
- Comfortable position
- Relaxed posture

### Jet Positioning

**Using jets:**
- Target sore areas
- Comfortable distance
- Not too forceful
- Therapeutic placement
- Enjoy relief

## Duration

### Time Limits

**Safe soaking:**
- 15-30 minutes typical
- Don't overheat
- Exit if uncomfortable
- Hydrate before/after
- Know limits

### Temperature Awareness

**Heat safety:**
- Monitor feeling
- Exit if dizzy
- Cool down gradually
- Stay hydrated
- Safe soaking

## Relaxation

### Mental State

**While soaking:**
- Deep relaxation
- Stress release
- Mindful moment
- Enjoy warmth
- Therapeutic time

## Social Soaking

### With Others

**Shared spa:**
- Comfortable positioning
- Respectful space
- Enjoyable conversation
- Relaxed socializing
- Pleasant experience

Good hot tub posture supports safe, relaxing hydrotherapy experiences.
