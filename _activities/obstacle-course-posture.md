# Obstacle Course Posture

Obstacle course racing challenges the body with varied obstacles. Proper posture helps you tackle each challenge safely.

## Running Between

### Trail Running

**Uneven terrain:**
- Shortened stride
- Eyes on ground
- Core engaged
- Quick feet
- Balanced running

### Pacing

**Race strategy:**
- Sustainable pace
- Energy conservation
- Obstacle preparation
- Recovery between
- Smart racing

## Climbing Obstacles

### Walls

**Getting over:**
- Technique before power
- Core engagement
- Efficient movement
- Safe dismount
- Help if allowed

### Ropes

**Climbing up:**
- Foot lock technique
- Core engagement
- Arm pulls
- Pacing yourself
- Safe descent

## Crawling

### Low Obstacles

**Under barriers:**
- Efficient movement
- Protect skin if possible
- Breathing
- Pacing
- Mud management

### Bear Crawl

**Carrying body:**
- Core engaged
- Efficient movement
- Pacing
- Surface awareness
- Endurance

## Carrying

### Weighted Objects

**Moving loads:**
- Proper lifting
- Core braced
- Sustainable pace
- Safe put-down
- Technique over speed

## Women-Specific

### Racing Considerations

**Female OCR:**
- Grip strength focus
- Upper body training
- Technique emphasis
- Community support
- Have fun

Good obstacle course posture adapts to each challenge while maintaining efficiency and safety.
