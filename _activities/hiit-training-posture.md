# HIIT Training Posture

HIIT delivers maximum benefit in minimal time. Proper posture ensures safety during intense efforts.

## Principles

### Work Intensity

**Maximum effort:**
- High heart rate
- Full exertion
- Short duration
- Sustainable bursts
- Recovery needed

### Recovery Quality

**Rest periods:**
- Active or passive
- Heart rate drops
- Breath recovery
- Prepare for next
- Strategic rest

## Common Exercises

### Cardio Movements

**Heart rate builders:**
- Jumping jacks
- High knees
- Burpees
- Sprints
- Skaters

### Strength Movements

**Resistance work:**
- Squats
- Push-ups
- Lunges
- Rows
- Compound movements

## Form Under Fatigue

### Quality Priority

**When tired:**
- Form first
- Reduce speed slightly
- Full range maintained
- Injury prevention
- Modified if needed

### Breathing

**Oxygen delivery:**
- Don't hold breath
- Rhythmic breathing
- Recovery breaths
- Controlled effort
- Sustainable pace

## Programming

### Ratio Options

**Work to rest:**
- 20:10 (Tabata)
- 30:30
- 40:20
- 60:30
- Variable options

### Exercise Selection

**Movement choice:**
- Full body variety
- Push-pull balance
- Upper and lower
- Core included
- Varied patterns

## Safety

### Appropriate Intensity

**Smart training:**
- Build gradually
- Know your limits
- Quality over quantity
- Recovery days
- Long term progress

Good HIIT posture maintains form during intense interval training.
