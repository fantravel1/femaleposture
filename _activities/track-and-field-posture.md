# Track and Field Posture

Track and field encompasses many events with specific posture demands. Proper positioning optimizes performance in each discipline.

## Running Events

### Sprint Form

**Explosive running:**
- Drive phase
- Upright phase
- Arm drive
- High knees
- Toe push-off

### Distance Form

**Efficient running:**
- Relaxed but tall
- Economical movement
- Arm swing
- Breathing pattern
- Sustainable pace

## Jumping Events

### Long Jump

**Approach and takeoff:**
- Speed approach
- Penultimate plant
- Explosive takeoff
- Flight position
- Landing technique

### High Jump

**Fosbury Flop:**
- Curved approach
- Plant and drive
- Back arch
- Clearance
- Safe landing

## Throwing Events

### Shot Put

**Glide technique:**
- Starting position
- Glide movement
- Hip drive
- Arm extension
- Follow through

### Discus

**Rotational:**
- Starting position
- Wind-up
- Rotation
- Release
- Safe follow through

## Hurdles

### Hurdling Form

**Barrier clearance:**
- Approach rhythm
- Lead leg drive
- Trail leg snap
- Quick ground contact
- Maintained speed

Good track and field posture is event-specific but always emphasizes body control and injury prevention.
