# Child Lifting Posture

Lifting children occurs countless times. Proper technique protects your back as they grow.

## Infant Lifting

### From Crib

**Reaching in:**
- Lower side if possible
- Lean close
- Secure hold
- Lift with legs
- Close to body

### From Floor

**Ground level:**
- Squat down
- Get close
- Secure hold
- Stand with legs
- Smooth motion

## Toddler Lifting

### Request Help

**When possible:**
- Have child climb up
- Use step stool
- Meet in the middle
- Teach cooperation
- Reduce lifts

### Lifting Technique

**Growing weight:**
- Squat down
- Child assists
- Close to body
- Leg power
- Core engaged

## Preschool Age

### Getting Heavier

**Managing weight:**
- Fewer pickups
- Encourage climbing
- Use furniture
- Proper technique
- Know limits

### Special Moments

**When lifting:**
- Good technique always
- Child cooperates
- Quick and controlled
- Set down safely
- Protect your back

## High Repetition

### Many Lifts Daily

**Reducing strain:**
- Alternate positions
- Request cooperation
- Kneel to their level
- Reduce need to lift
- Strategic approach

## Setting Down

### Safe Placement

**Putting child down:**
- Squat with child
- Controlled lowering
- Don't bend at waist
- Stable surface
- Complete motion

Good child lifting posture protects parents during years of picking up children.
