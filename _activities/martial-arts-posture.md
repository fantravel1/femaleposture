# Martial Arts Posture

Martial arts demand precise body positioning for both effectiveness and safety. Understanding proper martial arts posture improves technique while preventing injury.

## Basic Stances

### Fighting Stance

**Ready position:**
- Feet shoulder-width or wider
- Weight distributed
- Knees bent
- Hands up
- Chin protected

### Grounding

**Stable base:**
- Connected to floor
- Rooted stance
- Core engaged
- Balance ready
- Quick to move

## Strike Mechanics

### Punches

**Proper form:**
- Hip rotation
- Core power
- Arm extension
- Wrist alignment
- Return to guard

### Kicks

**Kicking posture:**
- Balanced base
- Hip chamber
- Extension control
- Return control
- Balance recovery

## Defense Posture

### Blocking

**Protective position:**
- Guards up
- Core protected
- Chin tucked
- Ready to counter
- Stable base

### Evasion

**Movement:**
- Slips and weaves
- Footwork
- Distance management
- Balance maintenance
- Quick recovery

## Specific Arts

### Striking Arts

**Boxing, kickboxing:**
- Active guard
- Movement footwork
- Power generation
- Defense integration
- Conditioning

### Grappling Arts

**Judo, BJJ:**
- Posture control
- Base maintenance
- Weight distribution
- Leverage use
- Defensive positioning

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female martial artists:**
- Technique over power
- Flexibility advantage
- Core strength focus
- Self-defense applications
- Training adaptations

### Injury Prevention

**Common issues:**
- Joint protection
- Impact conditioning
- Overtraining prevention
- Proper progression
- Protective equipment

Good martial arts posture creates effective technique while protecting your body through training and application.
