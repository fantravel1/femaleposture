# Scuba Diving Posture

Scuba diving requires specific body positioning underwater. Proper posture enables efficient movement and safety.

## Trim

### Horizontal Position

**Streamlined diving:**
- Flat body position
- Legs slightly elevated
- Arms at sides or in front
- Head neutral
- Efficient movement

### Buoyancy Control

**Neutral buoyancy:**
- Proper weighting
- Breath control
- Minimal movement
- Relaxed body
- Controlled ascent/descent

## Finning

### Efficient Kick

**Propulsion:**
- From hips, not knees
- Flutter or frog kick
- Controlled movement
- Minimal silting
- Energy conservation

### Fin Position

**Leg placement:**
- Legs straight for flutter
- Knees bent for frog
- Core engaged
- Streamlined position
- Efficient movement

## Equipment Handling

### Pre-Dive

**Gearing up:**
- Tank lifting technique
- Buddy assistance
- Weight distribution
- Safe entry
- Proper checks

### During Dive

**Equipment management:**
- Streamlined profile
- Nothing dangling
- Accessible essentials
- Comfortable fit
- Buoyancy compensation

## Safety

### Ascent Position

**Coming up:**
- Slow controlled ascent
- Look up
- Hand up
- Safety stop
- Proper exit

## Women-Specific

### Equipment Fit

**Female considerations:**
- BCD fit
- Wetsuit sizing
- Weight placement
- Comfort underwater
- Rental vs owned

Good scuba diving posture creates efficient, comfortable underwater movement while maintaining safety.
