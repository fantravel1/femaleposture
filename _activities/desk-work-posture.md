# Desk Work Posture

Desk work dominates many careers. Proper posture prevents pain and maintains health.

## Seated Position

### Chair Setup

**Proper adjustment:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Knees at 90 degrees
- Hips at or above knees
- Back supported
- Arms at desk height

### Spine Alignment

**Neutral spine:**
- Natural curves maintained
- Lower back supported
- No slouching
- Shoulders relaxed
- Head balanced

## Screen Position

### Monitor Height

**Eye level:**
- Top of screen at eye level
- Arms length distance
- No neck strain
- Reduced eye strain
- Centered view

### Multiple Screens

**If dual monitors:**
- Primary centered
- Secondary angled
- Equal height
- Reduce neck turning
- Alternate focus

## Keyboard and Mouse

### Arm Position

**Comfortable typing:**
- Elbows at 90 degrees
- Wrists neutral
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Mouse close
- Relaxed shoulders

## Movement Breaks

### Regular Breaks

**Avoiding static posture:**
- Stand every 30-60 minutes
- Short walks
- Stretch breaks
- Eye breaks
- Movement integration

## Home Office

### Work From Home

**Home setup:**
- Dedicated space
- Proper equipment
- Avoid bed or couch
- Same principles apply
- Consistent routine

Good desk work posture prevents musculoskeletal issues from prolonged sitting.
