# Beginner Yoga Routine for Better Posture

> **No Experience Needed:** This 20-minute routine uses basic yoga poses to address common posture issues. Perfect for those new to yoga who want to improve how they stand and sit.

---

## What You'll Need

- Yoga mat or carpet
- 2 pillows or a yoga block (optional)
- Comfortable clothing
- 20 minutes of quiet time

---

## Before You Start

### Mindset

- This is not about perfection
- Move slowly and gently
- Never push into pain
- Breathing is as important as the poses
- Rest whenever you need to

### Breathing Basics

Throughout this practice:
- Breathe in through your nose
- Breathe out through your nose (or mouth if more comfortable)
- Let your breath be slow and steady
- If you hold your breath, you're trying too hard

---

## The 20-Minute Routine

### 1. Seated Centering (1 minute)

**Starting position:**
- Sit cross-legged or on a chair
- Hands on knees or thighs
- Close your eyes

**Practice:**
1. Take 5 deep breaths
2. Feel your sitting bones rooted down
3. Imagine a string pulling the top of your head up
4. Let shoulders drop away from ears
5. Set an intention to be present

---

### 2. Neck Stretches (2 minutes)

**Right side:**
1. Drop right ear toward right shoulder
2. Keep left shoulder down
3. Hold for 30 seconds
4. Breathe deeply

**Left side:**
1. Drop left ear toward left shoulder
2. Keep right shoulder down
3. Hold for 30 seconds

**Chin to chest:**
1. Drop chin toward chest
2. Feel stretch in back of neck
3. Hold for 30 seconds

**Look up gently:**
1. Lift chin slightly
2. Don't drop head back completely
3. Hold briefly, then return to neutral

---

### 3. Shoulder Rolls (1 minute)

**Practice:**
1. Roll shoulders up toward ears
2. Back (squeeze shoulder blades)
3. Down
4. Repeat 5 times slowly
5. Reverse direction: up, forward, down
6. Repeat 5 times

**Focus:** Really exaggerate the movements; feel each direction.

---

### 4. Cat-Cow (2 minutes)

**Starting position:**
- Come to hands and knees
- Hands under shoulders, knees under hips
- Spine neutral

**Cow (inhale):**
1. Drop belly toward floor
2. Lift chest forward
3. Lift tailbone up
4. Look slightly up

**Cat (exhale):**
1. Round spine up toward ceiling
2. Tuck chin toward chest
3. Tuck tailbone under
4. Push floor away

**Practice:**
- Flow between cow and cat
- Move with your breath
- 10-12 cycles
- Make movements smooth and slow

---

### 5. Child's Pose (1.5 minutes)

**Position:**
1. From hands and knees, sit hips back toward heels
2. Keep arms extended forward OR bring them alongside body
3. Rest forehead on mat (or pillow)
4. Let body relax completely

**Modifications:**
- Knees wide if hips are tight
- Pillow under forehead if it doesn't reach floor
- Pillow between hips and heels if needed

**Practice:** Hold for 1.5 minutes, breathing deeply into back.

---

### 6. Thread the Needle (2 minutes)

**Starting position:**
- Come back to hands and knees

**Right side:**
1. Reach right arm under left arm
2. Let right shoulder and cheek rest on floor
3. Left hand can stay in place or reach forward
4. Feel stretch in right shoulder and upper back
5. Hold for 1 minute

**Left side:**
1. Come back to hands and knees
2. Reach left arm under right arm
3. Hold for 1 minute

---

### 7. Sphinx Pose (1.5 minutes)

**Position:**
1. Lie on your belly
2. Place forearms on floor, elbows under shoulders
3. Press forearms down, lift chest
4. Keep pelvis on floor
5. Lengthen neck (don't crunch it back)

**Hold:** 1-1.5 minutes, breathing normally.

**Modification:** Lower to a smaller lift if this feels intense.

---

### 8. Cobra Pose - Gentle (1 minute)

**Position:**
1. Hands beside shoulders
2. Elbows close to body
3. On an inhale, lift head and chest slightly
4. Use back muscles, not arms, to lift
5. Keep lift small—just a few inches

**Practice:** Hold 3 breaths, lower, repeat 2-3 times.

---

### 9. Downward Dog (1 minute)

**Position:**
1. From hands and knees, tuck toes
2. Lift hips up and back
3. Arms straight, head between upper arms
4. Knees can be bent—that's okay!
5. Push floor away, reach hips high

**Modifications:**
- Bend knees as much as needed
- Heels don't need to touch floor
- Take breaks as needed

**Hold:** 5-10 breaths, or come in and out.

---

### 10. Low Lunge (2 minutes)

**Right side:**
1. Step right foot forward between hands
2. Lower left knee to floor
3. Keep right knee over right ankle
4. Lift torso upright
5. Hands can rest on right thigh
6. Feel stretch in left hip flexor
7. Hold for 1 minute

**Left side:**
1. Switch sides
2. Hold for 1 minute

**Tip:** Tuck tailbone slightly to increase hip flexor stretch.

---

### 11. Seated Twist (2 minutes)

**Right side:**
1. Sit with legs extended
2. Bend right knee, place right foot outside left thigh
3. Left hand on right knee, right hand behind you
4. Inhale, sit tall
5. Exhale, twist gently right
6. Hold for 1 minute

**Left side:**
1. Switch sides
2. Hold for 1 minute

---

### 12. Figure-4 Stretch (2 minutes)

**Right side:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent
2. Cross right ankle over left knee
3. Reach hands around left thigh
4. Pull left knee toward chest
5. Feel stretch in right hip/glute
6. Hold for 1 minute

**Left side:**
1. Switch sides
2. Hold for 1 minute

---

### 13. Final Relaxation - Savasana (2 minutes)

**Position:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Let legs extend naturally, feet falling open
3. Arms at sides, palms up
4. Close your eyes

**Practice:**
1. Let your body be heavy
2. Let your breath be natural
3. Scan body for any remaining tension, let it go
4. Simply rest for 2 minutes

**To finish:**
1. Deepen your breath
2. Wiggle fingers and toes
3. Roll to one side
4. Slowly press up to seated
5. Take a moment before returning to your day

## Tips for Success

### Consistency Over Perfection

Doing this routine 3-4 times per week imperfectly beats doing it once perfectly.

### Progress, Not Pressure

If something feels too hard, modify it. You'll progress over time.

### Combine with Other Practices

This routine works well:
- In the morning to start your day
- After work to release tension
- Before bed to unwind
- As a break from sitting
