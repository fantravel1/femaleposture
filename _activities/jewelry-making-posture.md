# Jewelry Making Posture

Jewelry making requires intense focus on small details. Proper posture prevents the eye strain, neck pain, and hand problems common to this craft.

## Workspace Setup

### Table Height

**Proper level:**
- Work at comfortable height
- Arms supported
- Minimal stooping
- Good lighting crucial
- Adjustable if possible

### Seating

**Chair setup:**
- Supportive chair
- Proper height
- Feet grounded
- Back support
- Arms at work level

## Fine Detail Work

### Eye Position

**Visual ergonomics:**
- Good task lighting
- Magnification when needed
- Proper distance
- Eye-level work
- Regular eye breaks

### Hand Position

**Fine motor work:**
- Supported arms
- Relaxed hands
- Varied grip
- Regular breaks
- Hand stretches

## Common Issues

### Neck Strain

**From looking down:**
- Elevate work
- Magnifier stands
- Regular breaks
- Neck stretches
- Posture awareness

### Hand Fatigue

**From fine work:**
- Ergonomic tools
- Grip variation
- Hand exercises
- Rest periods
- Strength building

## Prevention Strategies

### Break Schedule

**Movement intervals:**
- Every 20 minutes
- Eye rest
- Hand stretches
- Stand and move
- Posture reset

Good jewelry making posture protects your vision and hands while supporting your creative work.
