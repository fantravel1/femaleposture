# Stroller Pushing Posture

Stroller pushing is frequent for parents. Proper posture prevents back and shoulder strain.

## Handle Height

### Proper Adjustment

**Setting up:**
- Handles at waist height
- Elbows slightly bent
- No hunching
- Relaxed shoulders
- Comfortable grip

### Fixed Handles

**Non-adjustable:**
- Add handle extenders
- Adjust your position
- Walk close or far
- Find comfortable distance
- Consider stroller choice

## Pushing Technique

### Basic Push

**Moving forward:**
- Upright posture
- Arms relaxed
- Push from legs
- Smooth motion
- Core engaged

### One-Handed

**When necessary:**
- Alternate hands
- Other hand useful
- Brief periods only
- Return to two hands
- Minimize asymmetry

## Terrain

### Uneven Surfaces

**Rough ground:**
- Both hands
- Core engaged
- Shorter steps
- Watch carefully
- Steady control

### Hills

**Up and down:**
- Lean in going up
- Core very engaged
- Careful descent
- Brake use
- Controlled speed

## Loading and Unloading

### Lifting Child

**In and out:**
- Face stroller
- Lift properly
- Close to body
- Controlled placement
- Safe and secure

### Folding Stroller

**For transport:**
- Bend at knees
- Proper lifting
- Into car carefully
- Manage weight
- Practice mechanism

Good stroller pushing posture protects parents during daily outings.
