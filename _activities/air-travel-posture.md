# Air Travel Posture

Air travel confines you to limited space. Proper posture enables comfortable, healthy flights.

## Seat Setup

### Initial Positioning

**When seated:**
- Sit fully back
- Use lumbar support
- Feet flat if possible
- Recline when appropriate
- Organize space

### Headrest Use

**Neck support:**
- Adjust height
- Head supported
- Reduce neck strain
- Travel pillow optional
- Comfortable position

## Long Flights

### Position Changes

**During flight:**
- Shift position regularly
- Adjust recline
- Move in seat
- Stretch when possible
- Stay mobile

### Movement Breaks

**When seatbelt sign off:**
- Stand up
- Walk the aisle
- Simple stretches
- Reduce stiffness
- Circulation support

## Legroom Strategies

### Limited Space

**Managing cramped space:**
- Aisle seat for movement
- Under-seat storage
- Footrest if available
- Compression socks
- Circulation priority

### Deep Vein Prevention

**DVT awareness:**
- Ankle circles
- Calf raises seated
- Flex and point feet
- Hydration
- Movement essential

## Entertainment Posture

### Screen Viewing

**Watching movies:**
- Screen at eye level
- Don't crane neck
- Take breaks
- Change focus
- Eye rest

### Reading

**Books or tablet:**
- Raise reading material
- Don't look down extended
- Alternate activities
- Rest eyes
- Comfortable positioning

## Sleep Position

### Resting on Plane

**If sleeping:**
- Neck support essential
- Recline seat
- Eye mask
- Comfortable position
- Brief naps better

Good air travel posture enables comfortable, healthy flying.
