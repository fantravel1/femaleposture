# Lacrosse Posture

Lacrosse demands running, stick handling, and quick direction changes. Proper posture protects your body while enhancing play.

## Athletic Stance

### Ready Position

**Field ready:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees bent
- Weight forward
- Stick ready
- Core engaged

### Movement Ready

**Quick reactions:**
- Low center of gravity
- Explosive potential
- Balanced stance
- Eyes on play
- Athletic position

## Stick Work

### Cradling

**Ball control:**
- Stick close to body
- Wrist movement
- Arm position
- Core stability
- Running while cradling

### Passing and Catching

**Technique:**
- Step into throws
- Follow through
- Soft hands for catch
- Core rotation
- Balanced position

## Shooting

### Form

**Power and accuracy:**
- Step toward goal
- Hip rotation
- Arm whip
- Follow through
- Balanced recovery

## Defense

### Defensive Stance

**Guarding:**
- Low position
- Active stick
- Quick feet
- Body positioning
- Core engaged

Good lacrosse posture creates effective play while protecting your body through the sport's demands.
