# Roller Derby Posture

Roller derby combines skating with strategy. Proper posture enables effective, safe play.

## Derby Stance

### Low Position

**Basic stance:**
- Very low center
- Knees deeply bent
- Weight forward
- Core engaged
- Stable base

### Skating Form

**Moving around:**
- Low and stable
- Quick crossovers
- Agile movement
- Constant awareness
- Pack position

## Blocking

### Wall Formation

**Team blocking:**
- Side by side
- Low stance
- Impenetrable wall
- Communication
- Moving together

### Individual Blocking

**Stopping jammer:**
- Hip checks
- Shoulder blocks
- Legal contact zones
- Effective stopping
- Quick recovery

## Jamming

### Breaking Through

**Scoring points:**
- Speed bursts
- Agility moves
- Small openings
- Quick decisions
- Point scoring

### Speed Skating

**On breakaways:**
- Low and fast
- Powerful strides
- Efficient motion
- Sustained speed
- Quick laps

## Contact

### Legal Hits

**Safe contact:**
- Shoulder to hip
- Legal zones only
- Square contact
- Controlled force
- Sport specific

### Falling Safely

**When hit:**
- Fall small
- Slide don't stop
- Quick recovery
- Get back up fast
- Continuous action

## Safety

### Protective Gear

**Essential equipment:**
- Helmet always
- Mouth guard
- Knee pads
- Wrist guards
- Full protection

Good roller derby posture enables effective, exciting contact play.
