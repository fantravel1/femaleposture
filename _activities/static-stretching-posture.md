# Static Stretching Posture

Static stretching improves flexibility. Proper posture enables safe, effective stretching.

## Principles

### Hold Duration

**Time under stretch:**
- 15-30 seconds minimum
- Up to 60 seconds
- Sustained position
- Relaxed breathing
- Progressive depth

### Breathing

**Relaxation:**
- Deep breaths
- Exhale to deepen
- No holding breath
- Relaxed body
- Mental calm

## Lower Body

### Hamstring Stretch

**Back of thigh:**
- Straight leg
- Hinge at hip
- Flat back
- Reach forward
- Feel the stretch

### Quadriceps Stretch

**Front of thigh:**
- Standing or lying
- Heel to glute
- Knees together
- Hips forward
- Balance maintained

### Hip Flexor Stretch

**Front of hip:**
- Lunge position
- Back knee down
- Hips forward
- Torso upright
- Deep stretch

### Glute Stretch

**Hip muscles:**
- Figure four position
- Crossed ankle
- Pull to chest
- Relaxed breathing
- Hold position

## Upper Body

### Chest Stretch

**Pectoral muscles:**
- Doorway or wall
- Arm positioned
- Lean forward
- Feel across chest
- Both sides

### Shoulder Stretch

**Shoulder muscles:**
- Arm across body
- Gentle pressure
- Hold position
- Both sides
- Relaxed breathing

## Timing

### Post-Workout

**Best time:**
- After activity
- Muscles warm
- Cool down phase
- Flexibility focus
- Recovery benefit

Good static stretching posture enables safe flexibility development.
