# Painting Walls Posture

Wall painting involves reaching and rolling. Proper posture prevents strain during projects.

## Rolling Technique

### Roller Handling

**Using paint roller:**
- Extension pole helps
- Less reaching needed
- Relaxed grip
- Core engaged
- Efficient strokes

### Wall Coverage

**Rolling motion:**
- W or M pattern
- Smooth strokes
- Consistent pressure
- Comfortable reach
- Systematic approach

## Reaching High

### Upper Walls

**Above eye level:**
- Use extension pole
- Step stool for edges
- Don't overreach
- Controlled motion
- Safe reaching

### Ceiling Work

**Overhead painting:**
- Extension pole essential
- Neck protection
- Frequent breaks
- Eye protection
- Challenging work

## Lower Walls

### Near Floor

**Low painting:**
- Kneel on pad
- Squat positions
- Alternate positions
- Protect knees
- Rise carefully

## Cutting In

### Edge Work

**Brush technique:**
- Steady hand
- Close to work
- Comfortable position
- Controlled strokes
- Patient work

## Project Duration

### Long Sessions

**Extended painting:**
- Take breaks
- Stretch frequently
- Hydrate
- Ventilate area
- Pace work

### Multiple Days

**Large projects:**
- Split sensibly
- Don't rush
- Quality over speed
- Sustainable effort
- Proper drying time

Good wall painting posture enables quality results without body strain.
