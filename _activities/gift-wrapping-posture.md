# Gift Wrapping Posture

Gift wrapping involves reaching and cutting. Proper posture prevents strain during holiday prep.

## Work Surface

### Table Height

**Optimal setup:**
- Standing table height
- Or seated at desk
- Comfortable reach
- Materials organized
- Efficient workspace

### Floor Wrapping

**Common alternative:**
- Sit comfortably
- Cross-legged or kneeling
- Pad underneath
- Everything within reach
- Take breaks

## Wrapping Technique

### Cutting Paper

**Using scissors:**
- Stable surface
- Smooth cutting
- Comfortable grip
- Controlled motion
- Sharp scissors help

### Folding and Taping

**Forming package:**
- Close to work
- Both hands engaged
- Organized supplies
- Efficient technique
- Quality result

## Multiple Gifts

### Assembly Line

**Many presents:**
- Organize materials
- Systematic approach
- Efficient process
- Take breaks
- Pace yourself

### Fatigue Prevention

**Long sessions:**
- Alternate positions
- Stretch breaks
- Standing and sitting
- Comfortable duration
- Don't rush

## Ribbon and Bows

### Finishing Touches

**Decorating:**
- Fine motor work
- Comfortable position
- Good lighting
- Relaxed hands
- Creative focus

## Supplies Organization

### Workspace Setup

**Materials ready:**
- Paper accessible
- Tape dispenser ready
- Scissors at hand
- Ribbons organized
- Efficient wrapping

Good gift wrapping posture enables comfortable, enjoyable present preparation.
