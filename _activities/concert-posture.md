# Concert Posture

Concerts and events often require hours of standing. Proper posture protects your body while you enjoy the experience.

## Standing Position

### Basic Stance

**Concert standing:**
- Weight balanced
- Knees soft
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Position shifts

### Extended Duration

**Hours of standing:**
- Regular weight shifts
- Foot movements
- Posture resets
- Sit when possible
- Movement dancing

## Footwear

### Shoe Choice

**Critical decision:**
- Comfort over style
- Supportive shoes
- Appropriate for venue
- Broken in
- Extra padding

### Foot Care

**During event:**
- Weight distribution
- Rest feet when possible
- Stretching discretely
- Stand on flat surfaces
- Know your limits

## Crowd Situations

### Dense Crowds

**Close quarters:**
- Stable base
- Core engaged
- Awareness of surroundings
- Protect personal space
- Safe movement

### Navigation

**Moving through:**
- Watch footing
- Steady pace
- Core stable
- Eyes ahead
- Polite progress

## Dancing

### Movement Posture

**While dancing:**
- Upright posture
- Core engaged
- Joint protection
- Safe movements
- Pace yourself

## Recovery

### After Event

**Post-standing care:**
- Elevate feet
- Stretch legs and back
- Hydrate
- Rest
- Foot massage

Good concert posture lets you enjoy the show without the next-day pain of poor standing habits.
