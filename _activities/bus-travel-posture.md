# Bus Travel Posture

Bus travel varies from local to long-distance. Proper posture enables comfortable rides.

## Short Trips

### City Bus

**Quick rides:**
- May stand or sit
- Hold rail if standing
- Core engaged
- Ready for stops
- Balance awareness

### Seated Briefly

**Short duration:**
- Sit upright
- Feet on floor
- Ready to exit
- Bags managed
- Quick trips

## Standing on Bus

### Rush Hour

**Standing techniques:**
- Hold rail securely
- Feet wide for balance
- Knees bent slightly
- Core engaged
- Absorb movement

### Movement of Bus

**Anticipating:**
- Stops and starts
- Turns
- Bumps
- Brace appropriately
- Stay stable

## Long-Distance

### Coach Travel

**Extended journeys:**
- Similar to airplane
- Use recline
- Movement breaks
- Stretch when stopped
- Hydration

### Seat Selection

**Choosing:**
- Near front for less motion
- Aisle for movement
- Window for rest
- Personal preference
- Comfort priority

## Commuting

### Daily Transit

**Regular riders:**
- Consistent routine
- Minimize carrying
- Comfortable standing
- Rest when possible
- Sustainable approach

### Heavy Bags

**Managing belongings:**
- Keep light
- Backpack preferred
- On lap if seated
- Secure when standing
- Don't strain

Good bus travel posture enables comfortable transit experiences.
