# Sprinting Posture

Sprinting demands explosive power and perfect mechanics. Proper posture maximizes speed.

## Start Position

### Block Setup

**Ready position:**
- Hands shoulder-width
- Back flat
- Core engaged
- Eyes down
- Coiled for explosion

### First Movement

**On command:**
- Explosive push
- Low drive angle
- Arms drive hard
- Full extension
- Maximum force

## Drive Phase

### Acceleration

**Building speed:**
- Forward lean
- Powerful arm drive
- High knees
- Full hip extension
- Gradually rising

### Body Angle

**During acceleration:**
- Approximately 45 degrees
- Rising gradually
- Head aligned
- Core tight
- Patient transition

## Maximum Velocity

### Upright Sprinting

**Top speed:**
- Tall posture
- Relaxed shoulders
- High knees
- Full hip extension
- Minimal ground contact

### Arm Action

**At speed:**
- 90 degree bend
- Drive from shoulders
- Relaxed hands
- Opposite coordination
- Fast turnover

## Maintaining Speed

### Relaxation

**Staying fast:**
- Relaxed face
- Loose shoulders
- Efficient motion
- No wasted energy
- Trust the training

## Finish

### Through the Line

**Final meters:**
- Maintain form
- Don't reach
- Lean at line
- Full speed through
- Complete the race

Good sprinting posture maximizes speed through efficient, powerful mechanics.
