# Movie Watching Posture

Movie watching involves extended sitting. Proper positioning prevents discomfort during viewing.

## Home Viewing

### Couch Position

**Comfortable seating:**
- Sit back in cushions
- Feet up if comfortable
- Lumbar support
- Head supported
- Relaxed position

### Screen Height

**TV position:**
- Eye level or slightly below
- Not too high
- No neck strain
- Comfortable viewing
- Optimal distance

## Recliner Use

### Reclined Viewing

**In recliner:**
- Feet elevated
- Back supported
- Neck supported
- Comfortable angle
- Excellent for movies

### Getting Up

**From reclined:**
- Lower footrest first
- Sit up gradually
- Stand carefully
- Avoid rushing
- Careful transition

## Theater Seating

### Movie Theater

**Public viewing:**
- Sit back fully
- Use arm rests
- Comfortable position
- Accept seat limits
- Adjust as possible

### Snack Holding

**Popcorn and drinks:**
- Cup holders help
- Don't lean for snacks
- Keep close
- Balanced holding
- Enjoy the movie

## Extended Viewing

### Binge Watching

**Long sessions:**
- Position changes
- Stand during breaks
- Stretch between episodes
- Get up regularly
- Sustainable watching

### Eye Breaks

**Screen rest:**
- Look away periodically
- 20-20-20 rule
- Reduce strain
- Blink more
- Eye health

## Bed Watching

### From Bed

**If watching in bed:**
- Propped up well
- Pillows support
- Screen positioned
- Not lying flat
- Comfortable arrangement

Good movie watching posture enables extended viewing without discomfort.
