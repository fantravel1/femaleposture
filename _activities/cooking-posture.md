# Cooking Posture

Hours in the kitchen can strain the body, especially with counters at wrong heights. Understanding cooking posture protects your back and makes food preparation more enjoyable.

## Standing at Counter

### Proper Height

**Ergonomic positioning:**
- Counter at elbow height
- Minimal bending
- Relaxed shoulders
- Arms at comfortable angle
- Feet flat

### When Counter Is Wrong Height

**Adaptations:**
- Step stool if too high
- Cutting board on box if too low
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Alternate positions
- Take breaks

## Kitchen Posture Basics

### Standing Position

**Healthy stance:**
- Weight evenly distributed
- Soft knees
- Core gently engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Head balanced

### Task Positioning

**Reducing strain:**
- Face work directly
- Don't twist while working
- Move feet to turn
- Bring work close
- Use proper tools

## Common Kitchen Tasks

### Chopping

**Knife work posture:**
- Proper counter height
- Stable cutting board
- Relaxed grip
- Arm from shoulder
- Steady pace

### Stirring and Mixing

**Repetitive motion care:**
- Alternate hands
- Whole arm movement
- Relaxed grip
- Break up long tasks
- Switch positions

### Lifting Pots

**Safe handling:**
- Squat for low pots
- Two hands when heavy
- Keep close to body
- Clear path to destination
- Controlled movement

## Kitchen Environment

### Floor Care

**Standing surface:**
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Supportive shoes
- Soft flooring if possible
- Avoid hard standing
- Move frequently

Good cooking posture makes kitchen time more enjoyable and prevents the back and leg pain that comes from prolonged standing.
