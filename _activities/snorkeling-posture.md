# Snorkeling Posture

Snorkeling allows underwater viewing from the surface. Proper posture creates comfortable, efficient movement.

## Surface Position

### Face Down

**Viewing position:**
- Relaxed float
- Face in water
- Snorkel up
- Body flat
- Minimal effort

### Breathing

**Through snorkel:**
- Slow breaths
- Full exhale
- Relaxed rhythm
- Clear tube if needed
- Calm breathing

## Finning

### Efficient Kick

**Propulsion:**
- From hips
- Flutter or dolphin kick
- Relaxed movement
- Energy conservation
- Quiet kicks

### Speed Control

**Moving through water:**
- Sustainable pace
- Relaxed movement
- Enjoy the view
- Energy management
- Don't rush

## Equipment Use

### Mask

**Proper fit:**
- Comfortable seal
- Clear vision
- Defog as needed
- Strap position
- Jaw relaxed

### Fins

**Proper use:**
- Efficient kicks
- No cramping
- Comfortable fit
- Not too much effort
- Relaxed movement

## Safety

### Awareness

**Snorkeling safely:**
- Know your location
- Current awareness
- Buddy system
- Sun protection
- Hydration

Good snorkeling posture creates relaxed, efficient surface swimming for enjoying underwater views.
