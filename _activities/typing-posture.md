# Typing Posture

Typing is essential for modern work. Proper posture prevents repetitive strain injuries.

## Arm Position

### Elbow Placement

**Proper angle:**
- 90 degree angle
- At side of body
- Relaxed shoulders
- Not reaching forward
- Natural position

### Forearm Support

**Rest position:**
- Parallel to floor
- Supported or floating
- No reaching
- Relaxed muscles
- Comfortable position

## Wrist Position

### Neutral Alignment

**Wrist posture:**
- Straight wrist
- Not bent up or down
- Not angled to side
- Floating or supported
- Relaxed position

### Typing Motion

**Keystroke technique:**
- Fingers move
- Not wrists
- Light touch
- Avoid pounding
- Efficient motion

## Hand Position

### Home Row

**Finger placement:**
- Proper key assignment
- Curved fingers
- Relaxed hands
- Light touch
- Balanced position

### Thumb Use

**Space bar:**
- Light touch
- Natural position
- Not strained
- Relaxed motion
- Comfortable reach

## Equipment

### Keyboard Options

**Ergonomic choices:**
- Split keyboards
- Tented designs
- Mechanical keys
- Personal preference
- Pain reduction

### Wrist Rests

**Support options:**
- Use between typing
- Not during typing
- Gel or foam
- Proper height
- Optional tool

## Breaks

### Prevention

**Avoiding injury:**
- Regular breaks
- Stretch hands
- Change tasks
- Reduce duration
- Listen to body

Good typing posture prevents repetitive strain injuries during keyboard work.
