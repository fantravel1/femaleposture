# Gardening Posture

Gardening involves repetitive bending, kneeling, and lifting that can strain the body. Proper gardening posture protects your back and joints while allowing you to enjoy your time in the garden.

## Bending and Stooping

### Proper Technique

**Safe bending:**
- Hinge at hips
- Bend knees
- Keep back neutral
- Core engaged
- Avoid prolonged flexion

### Alternatives

**Reducing bend time:**
- Raised beds
- Kneeling pads
- Long-handled tools
- Sitting options
- Varied positions

## Kneeling Work

### Knee Protection

**Safe kneeling:**
- Use knee pads
- Kneel on soft surface
- Alternate positions
- Take breaks
- Use kneeling bench

### Getting Up and Down

**Safe transitions:**
- Use hands for support
- Rise gradually
- Don't twist while rising
- Stretch after
- Use assistance if needed

## Lifting in the Garden

### Proper Lifting

**Safe technique:**
- Close to load
- Squat down
- Lift with legs
- Keep load close
- Avoid twisting

### Heavy Items

**Managing weight:**
- Use wheelbarrow
- Ask for help
- Divide loads
- Use proper tools
- Know limits

## Repetitive Tasks

### Weeding

**Sustainable approach:**
- Vary positions
- Use tools
- Take breaks
- Stretch regularly
- Time limits

### Pruning

**Arm-intensive work:**
- Shoulder relaxation
- Tool quality
- Alternate hands
- Rest periods
- Proper technique

## Garden Ergonomics

### Tool Selection

**Body-friendly options:**
- Long handles
- Ergonomic grips
- Appropriate weight
- Well-maintained tools
- Task-appropriate tools

Good gardening posture turns yard work into enjoyable activity rather than source of pain.
