# Pet Care Posture

Pet care involves walking, bending, and lifting that can strain the body. Proper posture protects you while caring for your animal companions.

## Dog Walking

### Leash Handling

**Proper technique:**
- Relaxed arm
- Hand at waist
- Core engaged
- Prepared for pulls
- Comfortable grip

### Walking Form

**While walking dog:**
- Upright posture
- Normal gait
- Aware and responsive
- Comfortable shoes
- Varied routes

## Feeding and Watering

### Bending to Bowls

**Safe technique:**
- Squat or kneel
- Elevated bowls option
- Core engaged
- Don't round back
- Quick task

### Litter Box

**Scooping safely:**
- Kneel or squat
- Elevated box option
- Core engaged
- Regular cleaning
- Position variety

## Grooming

### Bathing

**Dog bath posture:**
- Raised tub if possible
- Kneel with padding
- Core engagement
- Position changes
- Take breaks

### Brushing and Combing

**Pet on lap or table:**
- Elevated surface
- Arms at comfortable height
- Pet secured
- Posture awareness
- Regular breaks

## Lifting Pets

### Safe Technique

**Proper lifting:**
- Squat down
- Pet close to body
- Rise with legs
- Core engaged
- Know weight limits

Good pet care posture protects your body while ensuring your furry friends get the care they need.
