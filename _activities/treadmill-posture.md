# Treadmill Posture

Treadmill exercise offers controlled walking and running. Proper posture maintains good form on the moving surface.

## Walking Form

### Basic Position

**Treadmill walking:**
- Upright posture
- Natural arm swing
- Eyes forward
- Not holding rails
- Centered on belt

### Arm Position

**While walking:**
- Natural swing
- Not holding handles long-term
- Light touch for balance only
- Relaxed shoulders
- Opposite arm to leg

## Running Form

### Proper Technique

**Treadmill running:**
- Upright torso
- Not leaning forward excessively
- Arms bent 90 degrees
- Eyes forward
- Natural stride

### Foot Strike

**Landing pattern:**
- Appropriate to your style
- Soft landings
- Not overstriding
- Centered on belt
- Consistent pattern

## Incline Walking

### Uphill Form

**Walking on incline:**
- Stay upright
- Don't hold rails
- Shorter steps
- Core engaged
- Natural lean slight only

### Common Errors

**What to avoid:**
- Holding rails and leaning back
- Exaggerated lean
- Too steep too soon
- Poor form for speed
- Overgripping

## Safety Considerations

### Belt Awareness

**Safe movement:**
- Stay centered
- Know emergency stop
- Start slow
- Stop gradually
- Rails for mount/dismount only

Good treadmill posture maintains outdoor walking/running form on the moving belt surface.
