# Drum Playing Posture

Drumming requires balanced, mobile positioning. Proper posture enables comfortable, powerful playing.

## Throne Position

### Height Setting

**Seat level:**
- Thighs slightly angled down
- Comfortable pedal reach
- Balanced position
- Adjustable throne
- Personal preference

### Sitting Position

**On throne:**
- Forward on seat
- Core engaged
- Upright spine
- Weight balanced
- Ready to play

## Upper Body

### Torso Position

**While playing:**
- Upright posture
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Free movement
- Stable center

### Arm Position

**For playing:**
- Elbows comfortable
- Natural angle
- Relaxed shoulders
- Efficient motion
- Power from core

## Foot Technique

### Pedal Position

**At pedals:**
- Ball of foot
- Heel up or down technique
- Comfortable reach
- Efficient motion
- Both feet active

### Hi-Hat Technique

**Left foot:**
- Same principles
- Foot control
- Timing work
- Coordination
- Essential skill

## Stick Technique

### Grip

**Holding sticks:**
- Match grip common
- Relaxed hold
- Fulcrum point
- Finger control
- Various grips

### Stroke Motion

**Playing technique:**
- Wrist primary
- Arm for power
- Finger finesse
- Efficient motion
- Relaxed movement

## Extended Playing

### Long Sessions

**Practice and performance:**
- Regular breaks
- Stretch and move
- Stay loose
- Hydrate
- Sustainable practice

Good drum posture enables comfortable, powerful percussion playing.
