# Ice Hockey Posture

Ice hockey demands athletic skating posture. Proper form enables speed, control, and power.

## Skating Stance

### Basic Position

**On ice:**
- Knees bent deep
- Low center of gravity
- Weight on balls of feet
- Core engaged
- Athletic ready

### Forward Skating

**Building speed:**
- Powerful strides
- Full extension
- Quick recovery
- Arms swing
- Efficient motion

## Stick Handling

### Stick Position

**Control position:**
- Top hand firm
- Bottom hand relaxed
- Blade on ice
- Puck in front
- Eyes up

### While Skating

**Moving with puck:**
- Puck protected
- Body between puck and defender
- Controlled speed
- Head up
- Aware of surroundings

## Shooting

### Wrist Shot

**Quick release:**
- Weight transfer
- Wrist snap
- Follow through
- Target focus
- Controlled power

### Slap Shot

**Power shot:**
- Wind up
- Hip rotation
- Stick flex
- Follow through
- Full body power

## Checking

### Giving Checks

**Body contact:**
- Legal positioning
- Shoulder contact
- Low center
- Controlled impact
- Safe technique

### Taking Checks

**Absorbing hits:**
- See it coming
- Brace position
- Low center
- Absorb impact
- Recovery quick

## Goaltending

### Goalie Stance

**Net protection:**
- Unique positioning
- Very low stance
- Butterfly ready
- Hands high
- Coverage maximized

Good ice hockey posture enables effective, powerful play on ice.
