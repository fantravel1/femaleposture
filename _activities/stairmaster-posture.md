# Stair Climber Posture

Stair climbing machines offer intense lower body cardio. Proper posture maximizes benefits while protecting knees and back.

## Basic Position

### Body Alignment

**Proper stance:**
- Upright torso
- Core engaged
- Shoulders back
- Light hand touch only
- Eyes forward

### Handle Use

**Hand position:**
- Light touch for balance
- Not leaning on handles
- Alternate no-hands periods
- Not death grip
- Core working

## Stepping Technique

### Step Depth

**Proper stepping:**
- Full range of motion
- Controlled pace
- Not shallow steps
- Deep, effective steps
- Sustainable speed

### Foot Position

**On the pedals:**
- Whole foot on step
- Push through heel
- Knees tracking forward
- Even pressure
- Controlled movement

## Common Errors

### Leaning Forward

**What to avoid:**
- Hunching over console
- Weight on arms
- Looking down
- Shallow quick steps
- Poor core engagement

### Corrections

**Better form:**
- Stand tall
- Light touch only
- Core engaged
- Deeper steps
- Full body upright

## Intensity Management

### Sustainable Pace

**Effective workout:**
- Challenging but form-able
- Consistent effort
- Proper breathing
- Not compensating
- Progressive challenge

Good stair climber posture keeps your core working and protects your lower back during this intense workout.
