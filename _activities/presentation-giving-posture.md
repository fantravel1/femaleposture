# Presentation Giving Posture

Giving presentations requires confident presence. Proper posture supports effective, engaging delivery.

## Standing Presence

### Speaker Stance

**At podium or stage:**
- Balanced stance
- Feet shoulder-width
- Weight distributed
- Confident bearing
- Grounded presence

### Without Podium

**Open space:**
- Mobile as appropriate
- Purposeful movement
- Return to home base
- Confident positioning
- Engage audience

## Upper Body

### Shoulder Position

**Confident shoulders:**
- Back but relaxed
- Not tense
- Open chest
- Breathe freely
- Capable appearance

### Head and Eye

**Audience connection:**
- Head up
- Eye contact with sections
- Move gaze naturally
- Connect with audience
- Engaging presence

## Gestures

### Hand Movement

**Natural gestures:**
- Purposeful gestures
- Not fidgeting
- Open hand movements
- Emphasize points
- Natural expression

### Avoid Habits

**Nervous tendencies:**
- No hand wringing
- No hair touching
- Controlled movement
- Calm presence
- Professional demeanor

## Voice and Breathing

### Posture Supports Voice

**For projection:**
- Upright stance
- Open chest
- Deep breathing
- Clear voice
- Confident sound

## Handling Nerves

### Physical Calming

**Before presenting:**
- Deep breaths
- Relaxed shoulders
- Grounded stance
- Calm center
- Confident start

Good presentation posture supports confident, effective public speaking.
