# Shopping Posture

Shopping trips involve walking, carrying, and bending. Proper posture protects your body during retail excursions.

## Walking the Store

### Extended Walking

**Store navigation:**
- Upright posture
- Comfortable pace
- Proper footwear
- Rest breaks
- Awareness of fatigue

### Pushing Cart

**Cart use:**
- Stand upright
- Don't lean on cart
- Arms at comfortable level
- Core engaged
- Relaxed pace

## Carrying Purchases

### Shopping Bags

**Bag carrying:**
- Balance both sides
- Multiple trips from car
- Reasonable weight per bag
- Core engaged
- Posture maintenance

### Heavy Items

**Large purchases:**
- Cart or dolly use
- Store assistance
- Car loading help
- Multiple trips
- Know limits

## Trying On Clothes

### Fitting Room

**Changing posture:**
- Space to move
- Proper bending
- Seated for shoes
- Mirror positioning
- Take your time

### Reaching and Bending

**Browsing:**
- Squat for low items
- Safe reaching for high
- Core engaged
- Don't overreach
- Move to item

## Shoe Shopping

### Trying On

**Seated comfortably:**
- Bench at proper height
- Squat safely if needed
- Walk to test
- Full weight bearing
- Take time

Good shopping posture makes the experience more enjoyable and prevents post-trip back and leg pain.
