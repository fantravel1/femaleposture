# Crafting Posture Guide

> **Create Without Pain:** Crafting is a joyful activity, but hours of detailed handwork can strain your body. Position yourself for comfortable creativity.

---

## The Crafting Challenge

### Physical Demands

- **Detailed focus** - Leaning in to see
- **Static positions** - Absorbed in project
- **Repetitive motions** - Same movements repeated
- **Hand intensity** - Gripping, manipulating
- **Head dropped** - Looking down at work
- **Shoulders hunched** - Common tendency

### Common Problems

- Neck pain and stiffness
- Upper back strain
- Shoulder tension
- Hand and wrist pain (RSI)
- Eye strain
- Headaches

---

## General Principles

### Work Position

**Raise your work:**
- Bring work toward eye level
- Not hunched over lap
- Less neck flexion
- Support arms

### Support Yourself

**Chair and setup:**
- Good lumbar support
- Arms supported
- Comfortable seating
- Proper height

### Take Breaks

**Every 20-30 minutes:**
- Look up and away
- Stretch neck and shoulders
- Move hands differently
- Brief rest

---

## Knitting and Crocheting

### Position

**Seated setup:**
- Back supported
- Elbows close to body
- Work raised (pillow on lap)
- Not hunched over

**Arms:**
- Supported on armrests or cushion
- Elbows at comfortable angle
- Shoulders relaxed

### Neck Position

**The problem:**
- Looking down at stitches
- Head dropped forward
- Sustained flexion

**Solutions:**
- Raise work higher
- Learn to feel stitches
- Periodic look-up breaks
- Don't crane neck

### Hands and Wrists

**Repetitive motion:**
- Light grip on needles
- Wrist position varies (continental vs. English)
- Rest periods
- Stretch hands between sessions

---

## Sewing by Hand

### Position

**Setup:**
- Good lighting essential
- Work raised toward you
- Sit at table if helpful
- Support arms

### Needle Work

**Hand care:**
- Comfortable needle grip
- Thimble to protect
- Frequent hand breaks
- Vary movements

### Detail Work

**Close focus:**
- Magnification if needed
- Proper lighting
- Eyes at comfortable distance
- 20-20-20 rule for eyes

---

## Machine Sewing

### Workstation Setup

**Table/Desk:**
- Machine at comfortable height
- Chair height adjusted
- Feet flat (or on pedal)
- Not hunching over machine

**Positioning:**
- Arms at comfortable angle
- Not reaching
- Back supported
- Good posture fundamentals

### Looking at Work

**Neck care:**
- Don't lean way in
- Bring work to you when positioning
- Look up regularly
- Adjust lighting to see better

### Foot Pedal

**Consider:**
- Both feet options
- Position that doesn't twist body
- Ergonomic pedal options

---

## Embroidery and Cross-Stitch

### Position

**Frame/hoop:**
- Use stand if possible
- Frees both hands
- Raises work
- Less arm fatigue

**Seated:**
- Back supported
- Armrests or cushion support
- Work at comfortable height

### Detail Focus

**Close work:**
- Magnifying lamp helpful
- Good directional light
- Reduce eye strain
- Take eye breaks

### Hand Work

**Needlework technique:**
- Light grip
- Comfortable needle size
- Smooth movements
- Rest hands regularly

---

## Quilting

### At the Machine

**Same as machine sewing:**
- Proper table height
- Good seating
- Arms comfortable
- Back supported

### Hand Quilting

**Extended sessions:**
- Frame or hoop on stand
- Comfortable seating
- Raise work toward you
- Breaks for back and hands

### Cutting and Prep

**Rotary cutting:**
- Standing at comfortable height
- Proper mat and ruler
- Don't hunch
- Cutting technique

---

## Painting and Drawing

### Sitting at Table

**Position:**
- Work surface at proper angle
- Not flat if possible
- Chair height adjusted
- Arms supported

### Easel Work

**Standing:**
- Proper easel height
- Work at eye level
- Weight shifting
- Not overreaching

**Sitting at easel:**
- Good stool/chair
- Work centered on you
- Comfortable distance

### Detail Work

**Close focus:**
- Adequate lighting
- Magnification if needed
- Eye breaks
- Don't hunch in

---

## Paper Crafts

### Cutting and Assembly

**At table:**
- Comfortable standing or sitting height
- Everything within reach
- Not twisted to reach
- Good lighting

### Card Making

**Similar to other table crafts:**
- Work raised slightly
- Good seating
- Arm support
- Breaks for hands

---

## Beading and Jewelry Making

### Fine Detail Work

**Challenges:**
- Very close focus
- Small manipulations
- Sustained concentration
- Static position

**Solutions:**
- Magnifying lamp essential
- Raised work surface
- Arm supports
- Frequent breaks
- Good task lighting

### Hand Intensive

**Small movements:**
- Rest hands often
- Stretch fingers
- Vary activities
- Don't work through pain

---

## Workspace Setup

### Lighting

**Critical:**
- Bright task lighting
- Positioned well (no shadows)
- Appropriate for task
- Reduce eye strain

### Seating

**Good chair:**
- Adjustable height
- Back support
- Armrests if helpful
- Comfortable for hours

### Table/Work Surface

**Height matters:**
- Depends on activity
- Adjustable helpful
- Not too high or low
- Slanted option for some activities

---

## Taking Breaks

### Within Sessions

**Every 20-30 minutes:**
- Look away from work
- Eye rest
- Shoulder rolls
- Hand stretches

### Between Sessions

**Movement:**
- Different activity
- Counter-stretches
- Walk around
- Rest hands

### Knowing Limits

**Stop signals:**
- Pain or numbness
- Eye fatigue
- Body aching
- Listen to signals

---

## Building Sustainability

### Not a Marathon

**Crafting should be:**
- Enjoyable, not painful
- Sustainable practice
- Multiple sessions
- Life-long hobby

### When Pain Occurs

- Assess setup
- Modify position
- Shorten sessions
- Seek help if persistent
