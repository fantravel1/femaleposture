# Sitting Cross-Legged Posture

Cross-legged sitting is common floor position. Proper posture supports hip and spine health.

## Basic Position

### Leg Placement

**Cross-legged setup:**
- Ankles crossed
- Knees out to sides
- Comfortable opening
- Hips above knees ideally
- Natural position

### Spine Alignment

**Upper body:**
- Upright torso
- Natural curves
- Shoulders relaxed
- Head balanced
- Core engaged lightly

## Hip Considerations

### Flexibility Matters

**Hip openness:**
- Varies by person
- Don't force
- Use props
- Gradual improvement
- Honor body

### Props Help

**Elevation:**
- Cushion under hips
- Raises pelvis
- Reduces knee strain
- More comfortable
- Recommended setup

## Comfort Tips

### Alternating Legs

**Switching:**
- Alternate which leg front
- Balance both sides
- Prevent asymmetry
- Regular switching
- Even development

### Duration Limits

**Time consideration:**
- Not too long
- Take breaks
- Stand periodically
- Circulation needs
- Comfortable duration

## When Used

### Meditation

**Sitting practice:**
- Stable base
- Grounded feeling
- Alert posture
- Sustainable
- Traditional position

### Casual Sitting

**Floor activities:**
- Playing with kids
- Informal settings
- Flexible option
- Natural position
- Comfortable choice

Good cross-legged sitting posture supports comfortable floor positioning.
