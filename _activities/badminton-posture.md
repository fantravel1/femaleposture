# Badminton Posture

Badminton demands quick reactions and precise racquet control. Proper badminton posture enables fast movement and powerful shots.

## Ready Position

### Base Stance

**Court-ready:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees bent
- Weight on balls of feet
- Racquet up
- Eyes on shuttle

### Athletic Posture

**Movement-ready:**
- Low center of gravity
- Core engaged
- Balanced stance
- Quick reactions
- Explosive potential

## Stroke Mechanics

### Overhead Shots

**Smash and clear:**
- Turn sideways
- Racquet back
- Rotate through
- Full extension
- Follow through

### Underhand Shots

**Net play:**
- Low position
- Wrist control
- Soft touch
- Quick recovery
- Balance maintained

### Backhand

**Opposite side:**
- Body rotation
- Elbow lead
- Wrist snap
- Quick execution
- Recovery position

## Footwork

### Court Movement

**Getting to shuttle:**
- Small steps
- Split step
- Explosive first step
- Balanced movement
- Quick recovery

### Net Movement

**Forward play:**
- Lunge position
- Controlled approach
- Quick recovery
- Balance maintenance
- Ready position return

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female players:**
- Flexibility advantage
- Wrist strength building
- Shoulder care
- Speed development
- Technique focus

### Injury Prevention

**Protection:**
- Proper warm-up
- Shoulder exercises
- Ankle stability
- Rest and recovery
- Footwear importance

Good badminton posture enables the speed and precision the sport demands while protecting your body.
