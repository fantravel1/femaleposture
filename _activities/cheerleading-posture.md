# Cheerleading Posture

Cheerleading combines stunting, tumbling, and dance. Proper posture ensures safety and impressive performance.

## Basic Positions

### Cheer Stance

**Standing ready:**
- Feet together or apart
- Knees straight or bent per motion
- Core engaged
- Shoulders back
- Sharp movements

### Motion Technique

**Clean positions:**
- Sharp lines
- Locked joints
- Tight muscles
- Precise angles
- Consistent execution

## Stunting

### Basing

**Supporting flyers:**
- Strong legs
- Core braced
- Arms locked
- Connected to flyer
- Stable base

### Flying

**Top position:**
- Core extremely tight
- Balance centered
- Body control
- Trust in bases
- Safe dismount

### Spotting

**Safety role:**
- Eyes on flyer
- Ready to catch
- Core engaged
- Quick reactions
- Safety priority

## Tumbling

### Floor Skills

**Flipping and twisting:**
- Same as gymnastics
- Performance quality
- Safe technique
- Progressive skill building
- Proper training

## Dance

### Performance

**Entertainment value:**
- Sharp movements
- Character and energy
- Synchronized with team
- Expression
- Athletic execution

Good cheerleading posture creates the precision and safety that define excellent cheer performance.
