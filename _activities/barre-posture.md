# Barre Posture

Barre workouts blend ballet, Pilates, and strength training. Proper barre posture protects joints while building the long, lean muscles the method promises.

## Ballet-Inspired Alignment

### Basic Posture

**Standing position:**
- Tall spine
- Core engaged
- Shoulders back and down
- Chin level
- Weight distributed

### At the Barre

**Support use:**
- Light hand hold
- Not leaning on barre
- Balance challenge
- Proper distance
- Full body engaged

## Common Positions

### First Position

**Feet and legs:**
- Heels together
- Toes turned out comfortably
- Knees over toes
- Inner thighs engaged
- Tall stance

### Second Position

**Wide stance:**
- Feet wider than hips
- Toes turned out
- Knees track over toes
- Pelvis neutral
- Core engaged

### Plie Position

**Bent knees:**
- Knees over toes
- Spine tall
- Core active
- Controlled movement
- Proper alignment

## Upper Body Work

### Arm Series

**Proper form:**
- Shoulders down
- Core stable
- Controlled movement
- Light weights if used
- Full range

### Pushup Position

**Barre pushups:**
- Aligned spine
- Core engaged
- Shoulder stability
- Controlled movement
- Proper depth

## Women-Specific Benefits

### Targeted Training

**Female-focused:**
- Core strength
- Pelvic floor awareness
- Postnatal appropriate
- Muscle lengthening
- Low impact

Good barre posture creates the elegant, efficient movement that defines this popular workout method.
