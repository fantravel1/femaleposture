# Diving Posture

Competitive diving demands precise body control. Proper posture creates clean dives and safe entries.

## Approach

### Walk/Run

**To edge:**
- Controlled approach
- Consistent steps
- Core engaged
- Arm position
- Hurdle preparation

### Hurdle

**Final step:**
- Controlled jump
- Arms swing
- Board timing
- Core tight
- Prepared for takeoff

## Takeoff

### Leaving Board

**Launch:**
- Explosive push
- Arms set
- Core engaged
- Body position
- Height and distance

## Flight Positions

### Straight

**Pike/Layout:**
- Tight body line
- Core extremely engaged
- Pointed toes
- Head position
- Controlled rotation

### Tuck

**Compact position:**
- Knees to chest
- Tight ball
- Core engaged
- Quick rotation
- Controlled open

### Pike

**L-shape:**
- Hips bent
- Legs straight
- Hands to feet
- Core tight
- Controlled movement

## Entry

### Clean Entry

**Into water:**
- Straight body
- Arms overhead
- Core tight
- Feet together
- Minimal splash

## Platform vs Springboard

### Height Considerations

**Different approach:**
- Platform higher
- More rotation time
- Entry considerations
- Safety awareness
- Progressive training

Good diving posture is the foundation of beautiful, safe dives from any height.
