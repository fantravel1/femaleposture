# Raking Leaves Posture

Raking leaves involves repetitive motion. Proper posture prevents strain during yard work.

## Rake Handling

### Grip Position

**Holding rake:**
- Hands shoulder-width
- Relaxed grip
- Switch hand positions
- Alternate sides
- Comfortable hold

### Rake Length

**Handle fit:**
- Appropriate length
- Not too short
- No hunching
- Upright stance
- Comfortable reach

## Raking Motion

### Core Engagement

**Movement pattern:**
- Core powers motion
- Not just arms
- Legs engaged
- Rotate torso
- Full body work

### Alternating Sides

**Prevent strain:**
- Switch lead hand
- Work both sides
- Balanced motion
- Prevent asymmetry
- Cross-train movement

## Bending for Bags

### Leaf Collection

**Bagging leaves:**
- Kneel or squat
- Don't bend at waist
- Bag on ground
- Fill partially
- Lift with legs

### Bag Carrying

**Moving bags:**
- Partial fill lighter
- Close to body
- Multiple trips
- Safe weight
- Protect back

## Pacing

### Work Duration

**Long sessions:**
- Take breaks
- Hydrate
- Rest periodically
- Split over days
- Sustainable pace

## Weather Consideration

### Dry Leaves Lighter

**Optimal conditions:**
- Dry leaves easier
- Wet leaves heavier
- Time work wisely
- Dress appropriately
- Comfortable conditions

Good raking posture prevents strain during fall leaf cleanup.
