# Driving Posture

Hours behind the wheel can strain the back, neck, and hips. Proper driving posture protects your body whether commuting daily or taking long road trips.

## Seat Adjustment

### Proper Setup

**Seat position:**
- Distance allows bent knees
- Slight knee bend at full pedal
- Thighs supported
- Back against seat
- Arms relaxed reaching wheel

### Seat Features

**Using adjustments:**
- Lumbar support engaged
- Seat height appropriate
- Tilt angle comfortable
- Headrest at head height
- Armrest if available

## Steering Position

### Wheel Adjustment

**Proper placement:**
- Hands at 9 and 3
- Elbows slightly bent
- Wrists clear of wheel top
- Shoulders relaxed
- No reaching

### Hand Position

**Comfortable grip:**
- Relaxed hold
- Not death grip
- Alternate positions slightly
- Use both hands
- Wrists neutral

## Long Drive Strategies

### Breaks

**Rest stops:**
- Every 1-2 hours
- Get out and move
- Stretch legs and back
- Walk around
- Reset posture

### During Drive

**Maintaining comfort:**
- Small position adjustments
- Seat heater if available
- Climate control
- Periodic stretches
- Posture awareness

## Common Driving Issues

### Lower Back Pain

**Solutions:**
- Lumbar support
- Seat adjustment
- Frequent breaks
- Core engagement
- After-drive stretching

### Neck Tension

**Relief strategies:**
- Headrest use
- Shoulder drops
- Head position check
- Mirror adjustment
- Gentle movements

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Fit Issues

**Female drivers:**
- Often closer to wheel
- Shorter leg reach
- Smaller frame fit
- Clothing considerations
- Shoe choices for driving

Good driving posture protects your body during every trip, from daily commute to cross-country adventure.
