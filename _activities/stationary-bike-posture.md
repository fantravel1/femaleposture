# Stationary Bike Posture

Stationary bikes offer accessible cardio. Proper posture varies by bike type but always protects your back and knees.

## Upright Bike

### Seat Adjustment

**Proper height:**
- Slight knee bend at bottom
- Hips stable
- Not rocking side to side
- Comfortable reach to handlebars
- Easy pedaling

### Body Position

**Riding posture:**
- Upright or slight lean
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Light grip on handles
- Eyes forward

## Recumbent Bike

### Seat Position

**Proper setup:**
- Slight knee bend at extension
- Back fully supported
- Comfortable seat position
- Arms at easy reach
- Relaxed pedaling

### Body Alignment

**Reclined riding:**
- Back against seat
- Head supported or neutral
- Shoulders relaxed
- Core engaged
- Smooth pedaling

## Pedaling Technique

### Proper Form

**Efficient pedaling:**
- Full circles
- Push and pull
- Even pressure
- Appropriate resistance
- Controlled speed

### Foot Position

**On pedals:**
- Ball of foot on pedal center
- Straps or clips if available
- Knees tracking forward
- Ankles neutral
- No excessive toe pointing

## Common Issues

### Knee Problems

**Prevention:**
- Proper seat height
- Not too much resistance too soon
- Controlled cadence
- Gradual increase
- Listen to body

Good stationary bike posture creates effective cardio with proper joint protection.
