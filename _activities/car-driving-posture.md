# Car Driving Posture

Driving requires sustained positioning. Proper posture enables comfort and safety.

## Seat Adjustment

### Distance

**From pedals:**
- Full pedal press with slight knee bend
- Not too close
- Not reaching
- Comfortable distance
- Safe control

### Height

**Seat height:**
- Clear view over wheel
- Clear dash view
- Comfortable hip angle
- Adjust if possible
- Optimal visibility

### Recline Angle

**Seat back:**
- Slightly reclined
- Shoulders touch seat
- Not too upright
- Not too reclined
- Comfortable angle

## Steering Position

### Wheel Height

**Adjust wheel:**
- Comfortable arm angle
- No shoulder strain
- Full control
- Airbag consideration
- Personal preference

### Hand Position

**On wheel:**
- 9 and 3 o'clock
- Relaxed grip
- Elbows bent
- Shoulders relaxed
- Safe control

## Lumbar Support

### Back Support

**Lower back:**
- Use built-in support
- Add cushion if needed
- Maintain curve
- Reduce fatigue
- Comfortable support

## Long Drives

### Rest Stops

**On road trips:**
- Stop every 2 hours
- Get out and stretch
- Walk around
- Rest eyes
- Reset posture

### Fatigue Prevention

**Staying alert:**
- Position changes
- Micro-adjustments
- Climate comfort
- Music or podcasts
- Stay engaged

Good driving posture enables comfortable, safe vehicle operation.
