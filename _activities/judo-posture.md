# Judo Posture

Judo uses throws and ground technique. Proper posture enables effective technique with safety.

## Standing Posture

### Shizentai

**Natural stance:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees slightly bent
- Core engaged
- Upright posture
- Ready for gripping

### Jigotai

**Defensive stance:**
- Lower center
- Wider base
- Bent knees deep
- Difficult to throw
- Defensive posture

## Gripping

### Kumi-kata

**Grip fighting:**
- Sleeve and lapel
- Various positions
- Fight for grip
- Control opportunity
- Strategic placement

### Posture Control

**Kuzushi:**
- Break balance
- Create opportunity
- Postural manipulation
- Throw setup
- Essential skill

## Throwing

### Entry

**Getting in:**
- Break balance
- Enter deeply
- Hip position
- Arm control
- Committed entry

### Execution

**Completing throw:**
- Hip drive or leg reap
- Follow through
- Control descent
- Land with partner
- Continue to ground

## Ground Work

### Newaza

**On the mat:**
- Controlling pins
- Submission attempts
- Escapes
- Transitions
- Continuous action

## Ukemi

### Falling

**Safe landing:**
- Breakfall technique
- Protect head
- Disperse force
- Roll through
- Essential skill

Good judo posture creates effective throwing and ground technique.
