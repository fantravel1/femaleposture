# Piano Posture

Piano playing demands precise finger work from a stable, aligned body. Proper piano posture prevents injury while enabling technical development.

## Bench Position

### Height

**Proper level:**
- Forearms parallel to floor
- Elbows slightly above keys
- Comfortable reach
- Bench height adjusted
- No shoulder tension

### Distance

**From keyboard:**
- Arms can extend comfortably
- Not too close
- Not reaching
- Elbows at sides
- Natural position

## Body Alignment

### Seated Posture

**Proper position:**
- Sit forward on bench
- Feet flat on floor
- Spine tall
- Core engaged
- Balanced weight

### Upper Body

**Arm freedom:**
- Shoulders relaxed
- Arms hang naturally
- Elbows free
- No tension holding
- Easy breathing

## Hand Position

### At Keys

**Proper placement:**
- Curved fingers
- Relaxed hand
- Wrist neutral
- Knuckles raised
- Natural arch

### Technique Basics

**Playing mechanics:**
- Play from arm weight
- Finger independence
- Wrist flexibility
- No excessive tension
- Efficient movement

## Common Issues

### Tension Problems

**What to avoid:**
- Raised shoulders
- Tight wrists
- Flat fingers
- Gripping keys
- Held breath

### Prevention

**Healthy playing:**
- Regular breaks
- Stretching routine
- Technique focus
- Gradual practice increase
- Teacher guidance

Good piano posture creates the physical foundation for musical expression while protecting your body through years of practice.
