# Jump Rope Posture

Jump rope provides excellent cardio in minimal space. Proper posture protects joints while enabling efficient, sustainable jumping.

## Basic Position

### Standing Posture

**Ready stance:**
- Feet together
- Knees slightly bent
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Eyes forward

### Arm Position

**Rope control:**
- Elbows at sides
- Hands at hip level
- Wrists turn rope
- Arms relaxed
- Minimal arm movement

## Jumping Technique

### The Jump

**Proper form:**
- Small jumps
- On balls of feet
- Soft landings
- Minimal height
- Consistent rhythm

### Rope Timing

**Coordination:**
- Jump as rope passes
- Steady rhythm
- Relaxed pace
- Consistent timing
- Eyes forward

## Landing Mechanics

### Soft Landing

**Joint protection:**
- Balls of feet
- Knees slightly bent
- Shock absorption
- Quiet landings
- Low impact goal

### Surface Matters

**Where to jump:**
- Shock-absorbing surface
- Even floor
- Appropriate shoes
- Indoor options
- Outdoor awareness

## Common Errors

### What to Avoid

**Problematic form:**
- Jumping too high
- Landing flat-footed
- Arms too wide
- Tense shoulders
- Double bouncing

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female jumpers:**
- Sports bra essential
- Pelvic floor awareness
- Modifications available
- Great cardio
- Anywhere workout

Good jump rope posture makes this exercise efficient, sustainable, and easy on your joints.
