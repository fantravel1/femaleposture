# Skateboarding Posture

Skateboarding demands dynamic balance and quick reactions. Understanding proper skateboarding posture improves control while reducing injury risk.

## Basic Stance

### Board Position

**Foot placement:**
- Feet over trucks
- Shoulder-width stance
- Knees bent
- Weight centered
- Stable base

### Regular vs Goofy

**Stance preference:**
- Left foot forward (regular)
- Right foot forward (goofy)
- Natural feel
- Consistent use
- Personal preference

## Riding Position

### Cruising Stance

**Comfortable riding:**
- Low center of gravity
- Knees flexed
- Arms for balance
- Eyes forward
- Relaxed body

### Pushing Stance

**Gaining speed:**
- Front foot forward
- Back foot pushes
- Weight transfers
- Balance maintained
- Smooth motion

## Tricks and Transitions

### Ollie Position

**Basic jump:**
- Crouch before
- Pop tail
- Slide front foot
- Level board
- Absorb landing

### Falling Technique

**Safety posture:**
- Roll with fall
- Protect wrists
- Tuck and roll
- Slide if possible
- Relaxed muscles

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female skaters:**
- Lower center of gravity advantage
- Flexibility use
- Core strength needs
- Confidence building
- Community support

### Safety Gear

**Protection:**
- Helmet always
- Wrist guards
- Knee pads
- Elbow pads
- Appropriate shoes

Good skateboarding posture creates control and style while protecting you when things don't go as planned.
