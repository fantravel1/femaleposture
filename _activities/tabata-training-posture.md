# Tabata Training Posture

Tabata uses 20-10 intervals for maximum efficiency. Proper posture enables safe, intense training.

## Protocol

### Classic Tabata

**20-10 structure:**
- 20 seconds work
- 10 seconds rest
- 8 rounds
- 4 minutes total
- Maximum effort

### Intensity Level

**True Tabata:**
- All-out effort
- Maximum heart rate
- Full exertion
- Brief recovery
- Repeated bursts

## Exercise Selection

### Cardio Focus

**High heart rate:**
- Sprints
- Burpees
- High knees
- Jump squats
- Skaters

### Strength Options

**Resistance work:**
- Thrusters
- Kettlebell swings
- Push-ups
- Squats
- Compound movements

## Form Under Intensity

### 20 Second Focus

**During work:**
- Maximum effort
- Form maintained
- Full range
- Explosive motion
- Count matters

### 10 Second Recovery

**Brief rest:**
- Quick breath
- Reset position
- Prepare for next
- Mental readiness
- Immediate restart

## Progression

### Building Up

**Development:**
- Single Tabatas first
- Perfect form
- Add Tabatas
- Varied exercises
- Progressive challenge

### Modifications

**When needed:**
- Reduce intensity
- Simpler movements
- Form priority
- Build capacity
- Long-term progress

Good Tabata posture maintains form during intense 20-10 interval training.
