# Treadmill Running Posture

Treadmill running offers controlled conditions. Proper posture maximizes the indoor workout.

## Basic Position

### Upright Running

**On the belt:**
- Same as outdoor
- Tall spine
- Core engaged
- Eyes forward
- Natural motion

### Belt Position

**Where to run:**
- Center of belt
- Not too far forward
- Not too far back
- Consistent position
- Safe placement

## Differences from Outdoor

### Belt Assistance

**Machine effect:**
- Belt moves beneath
- Less propulsion needed
- Slight incline helps
- 1% grade compensates
- Similar effort

### Handrails

**Use sparingly:**
- Don't hold while running
- Only for emergencies
- Affects natural gait
- Reduces workout
- Let go

## Incline Running

### Hill Simulation

**Elevated belt:**
- Lean slightly forward
- Shorter stride
- Same outdoor technique
- Core engaged
- Sustained effort

## Speed Training

### Intervals

**On treadmill:**
- Precise speed control
- Easy speed changes
- Structured intervals
- Consistent pacing
- Effective training

### Speed Adjustment

**Changing pace:**
- Prepare for changes
- Smooth transitions
- Safety first
- Know your limits
- Controlled training

## Safety

### Treadmill Safety

**Safe practice:**
- Emergency stop nearby
- Start at low speed
- Build up gradually
- Stay focused
- Avoid distractions

Good treadmill running posture mirrors outdoor form in controlled conditions.
