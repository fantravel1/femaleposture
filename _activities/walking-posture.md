# Walking Posture

Walking is the most fundamental human movement, yet few people pay attention to their walking posture. Good walking form prevents pain, builds fitness, and improves overall posture.

## Proper Walking Form

### Body Position

**Alignment basics:**
- Upright torso
- Eyes forward
- Chin level
- Shoulders back and down
- Core gently engaged

### Arm Swing

**Natural motion:**
- Opposite arm to leg
- Relaxed swing
- 90-degree bend
- From shoulder, not elbow
- Natural rhythm

### Foot Strike

**Gait mechanics:**
- Heel first
- Roll through foot
- Push off from toes
- Moderate stride length
- Quiet footfall

## Common Walking Errors

### What to Avoid

**Problematic patterns:**
- Looking down constantly
- Shuffling feet
- Over-striding
- Rigid arms
- Hunched shoulders

### Corrections

**Better habits:**
- Eyes on horizon
- Lift feet properly
- Natural stride
- Relaxed arm swing
- Open chest

## Types of Walking

### Everyday Walking

**Daily practice:**
- Awareness in routine
- Comfortable shoes
- Natural pace
- Posture attention
- Regular movement

### Fitness Walking

**Exercise walking:**
- Faster pace
- More deliberate form
- Arm involvement
- Core engagement
- Sustained effort

### Hiking

**Trail walking:**
- Adapted terrain form
- Shorter uphill steps
- Careful downhill control
- Pole technique
- Pack carrying posture

## Building Better Gait

### Practice Exercises

**Improvement work:**
- Mindful walking
- Posture check-ins
- Gait drills
- Strengthening exercises
- Flexibility work

Walking with good posture turns everyday movement into beneficial exercise while preventing pain and strain.
