# Dressing Posture

Dressing involves balance challenges and bending. Proper technique prevents the falls and strains that can occur during this daily routine.

## Standing Tasks

### Balance Challenges

**One-leg moments:**
- Support nearby
- Wall or furniture touch
- Seated option
- Core engaged
- Stable base before lifting foot

### Reaching

**Arms overhead:**
- Shoulders relaxed
- Full range of motion
- Efficient movement
- Clothing organized
- Not rushed

## Lower Body Dressing

### Pants and Underwear

**Safe technique:**
- Sit to put on
- Or wall support
- One leg at a time
- Stable before standing
- Not rushing

### Socks and Shoes

**Safe technique:**
- Sit to put on
- Foot to knee if able
- Long-handled tools
- Slip-on options
- Not balancing precariously

## Upper Body Dressing

### Shirts and Tops

**Efficient dressing:**
- Head first usually
- Arms then head sometimes
- Appropriate size openings
- Unrestricted movement
- Comfortable fit

### Jackets and Coats

**Heavy outerwear:**
- One arm at a time
- Assisted entry
- Proper weight
- Easy movement
- Comfortable fit

## Closet Organization

### Reaching Clothes

**Safe access:**
- Accessible organization
- Step stool for high
- Squat for low
- Good lighting
- Organized layout

Good dressing posture makes this daily routine safer and easier, especially as we age.
