# Interview Posture

Interview posture communicates confidence, competence, and professionalism. Proper positioning supports your candidacy from first impression to final handshake.

## Entering the Room

### First Impression

**Walking in:**
- Upright posture
- Confident stride
- Eye contact with interviewer
- Friendly expression
- Smooth approach

### Handshake

**Professional greeting:**
- Firm but not crushing
- Web to web
- Eye contact
- Brief duration
- Confident smile

## Seated Position

### In the Chair

**Professional sitting:**
- Sit fully back
- Shoulders back
- Feet flat
- Hands visible
- Alert posture

### Showing Interest

**Engaged positioning:**
- Slight forward lean
- Eye contact
- Open body language
- Appropriate gestures
- Active listening signals

## During Conversation

### While Speaking

**Answering questions:**
- Upright posture
- Eye contact
- Open gestures
- Confident voice
- Stable position

### While Listening

**Attentive posture:**
- Slight forward lean
- Nodding appropriately
- Open expression
- Focus on speaker
- Ready to respond

## Managing Nerves

### Physical Control

**Calming techniques:**
- Deep breathing before
- Grounded feet
- Relaxed shoulders
- Slow movements
- Posture confidence

### What to Avoid

**Nervous tells:**
- Fidgeting
- Crossed arms
- Slouching
- Touching face
- Looking away

Good interview posture conveys the confidence and professionalism that complement your qualifications.
