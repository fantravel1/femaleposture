# Backpack Wearing Posture

Backpacks distribute weight evenly. Proper posture maximizes comfort and prevents strain.

## Strap Adjustment

### Both Straps

**Essential:**
- Use both straps
- Not one shoulder
- Even distribution
- Balanced weight
- Proper wearing

### Strap Length

**Proper fit:**
- Snug but comfortable
- Backpack sits high
- Not hanging low
- Shoulders relaxed
- Comfortable wearing

## Weight Distribution

### Packing Strategy

**Loading backpack:**
- Heavy items near back
- Balanced left to right
- Accessible items outside
- Organized packing
- Efficient loading

### Weight Limits

**Appropriate load:**
- 10-15% body weight guideline
- Listen to body
- Lighter is better
- Multiple trips option
- Protect back

## Posture While Wearing

### Upright Position

**With backpack:**
- Stand tall
- Don't lean forward
- Core engaged
- Shoulders back
- Balanced stance

### Walking Form

**With pack:**
- Natural gait
- Upright posture
- Core support
- Comfortable pace
- Sustainable walking

## Waist Belt

### Additional Support

**Hip belt:**
- Takes weight off shoulders
- Distributes to hips
- More comfortable
- For heavier loads
- Use when available

Good backpack wearing posture distributes weight properly and protects the back.
