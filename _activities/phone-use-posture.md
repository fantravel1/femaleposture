# Phone Use Posture

Phone use is constant for many. Proper posture prevents text neck and related issues.

## Viewing Position

### Screen Height

**Raising the phone:**
- Bring phone to eye level
- Avoid looking down
- Reduce neck flexion
- Brief glances below okay
- Awareness of position

### Head Position

**Neutral neck:**
- Head over shoulders
- Chin not forward
- Minimal flexion
- Natural alignment
- Regular checks

## Holding Techniques

### Two-Handed

**Stable hold:**
- Both hands support
- Raise to view
- Reduce strain
- Comfortable grip
- Sustainable position

### One-Handed

**Single hand use:**
- Support elbow
- Raise device
- Alternate hands
- Limited duration
- Careful of strain

## Typing on Phone

### Thumb Typing

**Mobile input:**
- Light touch
- Alternate thumbs
- Brief sessions
- Awareness of tension
- Consider voice input

### Voice Options

**Reduce typing:**
- Voice-to-text
- Voice messages
- Reduce thumb strain
- Alternative input
- Modern convenience

## Usage Duration

### Time Awareness

**Limiting exposure:**
- Conscious of time
- Regular breaks
- Put phone down
- Look up and around
- Rest eyes

## Positions to Avoid

### Bad Habits

**Common problems:**
- Looking down extended
- Phone in lap
- Lying down scrolling
- Hunched position
- Unconscious use

Good phone use posture prevents text neck and related postural issues.
