# Boxing Posture

Boxing demands precise body positioning for power generation and protection. Understanding proper boxing posture improves your technique while keeping you safe.

## The Stance

### Basic Position

**Fighter's stance:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Lead foot forward
- Weight on balls of feet
- Knees slightly bent
- Hips under shoulders

### Guard Position

**Hands up:**
- Fists at cheekbones
- Elbows protecting ribs
- Chin tucked
- Shoulders relaxed
- Eyes forward

## Punching Mechanics

### The Jab

**Lead hand:**
- Shoulder rotation
- Arm extension
- Wrist straight
- Quick return
- Guard maintenance

### The Cross

**Power hand:**
- Hip rotation
- Full body power
- Rear foot pivot
- Shoulder behind punch
- Return to guard

### Hooks and Uppercuts

**Angled punches:**
- Arm angle
- Hip drive
- Core rotation
- Balance maintenance
- Guard return

## Movement

### Footwork

**Ring movement:**
- Small steps
- Never crossing feet
- Balance maintained
- Distance management
- Quick direction changes

### Defense Movement

**Evasion:**
- Slips
- Rolls
- Step backs
- Angles
- Clinching

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female boxers:**
- Technique emphasis
- Speed advantage
- Endurance building
- Hand wrapping
- Glove fitting

### Injury Prevention

**Protection:**
- Proper wrapping
- Appropriate gear
- Gradual progression
- Hand and wrist care
- Conditioning

Good boxing posture generates power from the ground up while keeping you protected and balanced.
