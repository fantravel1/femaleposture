# Public Speaking Posture

Public speaking posture communicates confidence and authority. Proper positioning supports your message and presence.

## Standing Position

### Foundation Stance

**Speaker base:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Weight balanced
- Knees soft
- Core engaged
- Grounded presence

### Upper Body

**Confident carriage:**
- Chest open
- Shoulders back
- Head balanced
- Arms free
- Relaxed but alert

## Stage Movement

### Walking with Purpose

**Moving while speaking:**
- Deliberate steps
- Meaningful movement
- Not pacing nervously
- Pause for emphasis
- Command the space

### Standing Still

**Anchored position:**
- Plant and stay
- Small gestures fine
- No swaying
- Stable presence
- Eye contact enabled

## Microphone Use

### Handheld

**Proper holding:**
- Mouth distance consistent
- Arm supported
- Switch hands if long
- Not blocking face
- Natural position

### Standing at Lectern

**Podium posture:**
- Don't grip or lean
- Stand behind, not on
- Maintain openness
- Use for notes only
- Project beyond it

## Gestures

### Hand Movement

**Purposeful gestures:**
- Natural and open
- Support message
- From core
- Varied but intentional
- Return to rest

## Nervous Energy

### Managing Physically

**Before speaking:**
- Ground through feet
- Deep breathing
- Shoulder relaxation
- Posture check
- Power pose if helpful

Good public speaking posture supports your message and projects the confidence your audience expects.
