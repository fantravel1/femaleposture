# Baby Carrying Posture

Carrying babies is constant in early parenthood. Proper posture protects parent and child.

## Arms Carrying

### Cradle Hold

**Across arms:**
- Balanced support
- Close to body
- Alternate sides
- Core engaged
- Comfortable position

### Hip Carry

**On one side:**
- One arm supports
- Hip shifts out
- Alternate sides
- Limited duration
- Common but asymmetric

### Upright Hold

**Against chest:**
- Support head/neck
- Close to body
- Both arms
- Balanced weight
- Comfortable for both

## Baby Carriers

### Front Carrier

**Structured carrier:**
- Proper fit critical
- Straps adjusted
- Weight distributed
- Baby positioned correctly
- Follow instructions

### Wrap Carrier

**Fabric wrap:**
- Learn proper technique
- Snug but comfortable
- Airway visible
- Weight on hips
- Practice needed

### Back Carrier

**For larger babies:**
- Appropriate age/weight
- Proper loading
- Waist belt on hips
- Shoulder straps fitted
- Mirror to check

## Duration

### Time Limits

**Carrying duration:**
- Rest when possible
- Set baby down
- Share carrying
- Carrier transfers weight
- Listen to body

## Common Issues

### Hip Pain

**From hip carrying:**
- Alternate sides
- Use carriers
- Strengthen core
- Limit duration
- Address early

### Back Pain

**From carrying:**
- Check posture
- Use proper carriers
- Strengthen back
- Physical therapy if needed
- Prevention focus

Good baby carrying posture protects both parent and child.
