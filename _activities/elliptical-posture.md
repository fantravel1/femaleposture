# Elliptical Posture

Elliptical trainers offer low-impact cardio. Proper posture maximizes benefits while protecting your body.

## Machine Setup

### Stride Length

**Proper settings:**
- Natural stride feels right
- Not overreaching
- Comfortable motion
- Adjustable if available
- Test before intense workout

### Handle Options

**Hand position:**
- Moving handles for full body
- Fixed handles for stability
- Varied grip positions
- Not gripping too tight
- Arms comfortable

## Body Position

### Upright Posture

**Proper form:**
- Stand tall
- Core engaged
- Shoulders back
- Not leaning on handles
- Eyes forward

### Lower Body

**Leg movement:**
- Feet flat on pedals
- Knees tracking forward
- Hips stable
- Smooth motion
- Controlled speed

## Common Errors

### Leaning Forward

**What to avoid:**
- Hunching over console
- Weight on handles
- Rounded back
- Looking down
- Poor engagement

### Corrections

**Better form:**
- Light hand touch
- Upright stance
- Core engagement
- Eyes ahead
- Full body work

## Workout Optimization

### Resistance Use

**Proper challenge:**
- Enough resistance for control
- Not too easy
- Engages muscles
- Sustainable effort
- Progressive increase

Good elliptical posture creates effective, full-body cardio workout while protecting your joints.
