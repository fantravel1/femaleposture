# Field Hockey Posture

Field hockey requires running while bent over a stick. Proper posture protects your back while enabling effective play.

## Running Position

### With Stick

**Ball control running:**
- Forward lean from hips
- Not rounded back
- Core engaged
- Eyes up when possible
- Stick work

### Without Ball

**Field running:**
- More upright
- Natural run
- Stick carried efficiently
- Ready to receive
- Aware of play

## Stick Handling

### Dribbling

**Ball control:**
- Hip hinge lean
- Core engaged
- Stick close to ball
- Quick movements
- Balance maintained

### Receiving

**Taking passes:**
- Ready position
- Soft stick reception
- Body behind ball
- Balanced stance
- Quick transition

## Striking

### Push Pass

**Accurate pass:**
- Weight transfer
- Core rotation
- Follow through
- Balanced finish
- Controlled power

### Hit

**Power shot:**
- Full swing preparation
- Hip drive
- Arm speed
- Follow through
- Recovery position

## Back Protection

### Lean Technique

**Safe posture:**
- Hinge from hips
- Core engaged
- Flat back when possible
- Frequent position changes
- Stretching routine

Good field hockey posture maintains back health despite the sport's demand for forward bending.
