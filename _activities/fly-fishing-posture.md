# Fly Fishing Posture

Fly fishing requires specific casting technique. Proper posture enables effective, enjoyable fishing.

## Casting Stance

### Basic Position

**Ready to cast:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Weight forward slightly
- Core engaged
- Arm position ready
- Eyes on target

### Body Alignment

**For casting:**
- Face target
- Shoulders square
- Balanced stance
- Relaxed but ready
- Prepared for motion

## Casting Motion

### Back Cast

**Loading rod:**
- Stop at 1 o'clock
- Pause for loop
- Arm position high
- Wrist controlled
- Line extends back

### Forward Cast

**Delivery:**
- Forward motion
- Stop at 10 o'clock
- Line unfurls
- Follow through
- Controlled delivery

### Arm Position

**Casting arm:**
- Upper arm stable
- Forearm moves
- Wrist firm mostly
- Elbow as pivot
- Efficient motion

## Wading

### In Stream

**Water positioning:**
- Secure footing
- Read current
- Belt at proper depth
- Slow movement
- Safety awareness

### Staff Use

**Wading stick:**
- Third point of contact
- Stability assistance
- Current resistance
- Highly recommended
- Safety tool

## Extended Fishing

### Long Days

**Sustainable effort:**
- Regular breaks
- Vary positions
- Rest arm
- Hydration
- Pace yourself

Good fly fishing posture enables effective casting and comfortable fishing.
