# Sitting Posture Guide

## Why Sitting Posture Matters

### The Sitting Problem

**Modern life means:**
- Hours at desks
- Time in cars
- Meals seated
- Entertainment seated
- Cumulative strain

### What Poor Sitting Does

**Over time:**
- Lower back pain
- Neck and shoulder tension
- Hip tightness
- Circulation issues
- Postural changes

---

## Basic Sitting Principles

### The Foundation

**Key elements:**
- Sit bones on seat
- Spine in natural curves
- Feet supported
- Arms relaxed
- Head balanced

### What "Good" Sitting Feels Like

**Should be:**
- Relatively effortless
- Sustainable
- Not rigid or forced
- Comfortable for reasonable duration

---

## Optimal Seated Position

### Lower Body

**Feet:**
- Flat on floor
- Or on footrest
- Not dangling
- Hip-width apart or comfortable

**Knees:**
- About 90 degrees (or slightly more)
- At or slightly below hip level
- Not pressed together or spread extremely

**Hips:**
- At back of seat
- Sit bones on surface
- Pelvis relatively neutral
- Weight distributed evenly

### Upper Body

**Spine:**
- Natural curves maintained
- Not flattened or exaggerated
- Lower back curve present (lumbar)
- Upper back gently rounded

**Shoulders:**
- Relaxed, not elevated
- Not rolled forward
- Dropped away from ears
- Arms hang naturally

**Head:**
- Balanced on spine
- Ears over shoulders
- Chin neutral (not jutting forward)
- Eyes look forward naturally

## Chair Fit

### Chair Height

**Correct height:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Thighs parallel to floor
- Knees at about 90 degrees

**Too high:**
- Feet dangle
- Pressure behind thighs
- Strain

**Too low:**
- Knees above hips
- Lower back rounds
- Hip flexor issues

### Seat Depth

**Correct depth:**
- Back touches chair back
- Space behind knees (2-3 fingers)
- Full thigh support

**Too deep:**
- Can't reach back
- Perching on edge
- No back support

### Lumbar Support

**What it does:**
- Supports lower back curve
- Reduces strain
- Maintains neutral spine
- Essential for long sitting

**If chair lacks it:**
- Add lumbar roll
- Rolled towel
- Cushion
- Position matters

## Different Sitting Scenarios

### At a Desk

**Work setup:**
- Chair adjusted properly
- Desk at right height
- Monitor positioned well
- See [Desk Work Posture](/activities/desk-work/)

### At a Table (Dining)

**Meal times:**
- Sit tall
- Chair height appropriate
- Not hunching over plate
- Brief duration usually

### On a Couch

**Relaxation reality:**
- Softer, deeper seats
- Less structural support
- Easy to slouch

**Better approach:**
- Pillow behind lower back
- Sit more upright when possible
- Duration matters
- Not for working

### In a Car

**Driving position:**
- Seat adjusted properly
- Lumbar support
- Mirrors set for good posture
- See [Driving Posture](/activities/driving/)

### On Floor

**Cross-legged, etc.:**
- Different demands
- Sit on cushion for height
- Variety of positions
- See [Floor Sitting](/exercises/floor-sitting/)

## The Movement Principle

### Best Position Is Next Position

**Key insight:**
- No single perfect position
- Variation is essential
- Static positions strain
- Movement prevents problems

### Practical Application

**Every 30 minutes:**
- Shift position
- Stand briefly
- Move somehow
- Reset

## Active Sitting

### What It Means

**Engaging while sitting:**
- Core gently active
- Not fully collapsed
- Micro-movements
- Position awareness

### Active Sitting Options

**Equipment:**
- Wobble cushion
- Kneeling chair
- Saddle stool
- Balance ball (not all day)

**Benefits:**
- Engages muscles
- Prevents static strain
- May improve attention
- Variety option

## Common Sitting Problems

### Forward Head

**The problem:**
- Head ahead of shoulders
- Neck strain
- Common from screens

**Fix:**
- Chin tucks
- Bring screen to you
- Awareness
- Upper back strengthening

### Rounded Upper Back

**The problem:**
- Thoracic kyphosis
- Shoulders forward
- Chest collapsed

**Fix:**
- Chest opening
- Shoulder blade work
- Thoracic extension
- Awareness of position

### Lower Back Flattening

**The problem:**
- Lumbar curve lost
- Pelvis tucked under
- Disc pressure increased

**Fix:**
- Lumbar support
- Sit on sit bones
- Hip flexor stretching
- Awareness of pelvic position

### Crossed Legs

**The problem:**
- Creates asymmetry
- Hip tightness
- Circulation issues
- Fine briefly, not sustained

**Better:**
- Both feet flat
- Alternate if crossing
- Awareness of duration

## Building Awareness

### Body Scans

**Regular check-ins:**
- How am I sitting?
- What's tense?
- What needs adjusting?
- Brief awareness moments

### External Cues

**Reminders help:**
- Timer for position checks
- Alarm for standing
- Visual cue in environment
- Build awareness habit

---

## Sitting Duration

### Guidelines

**Evidence suggests:**
- Break every 30 minutes ideal
- Maximum 1 hour without moving
- Total daily sitting reduced when possible
- Movement matters more than perfect position

### Reality Check

**Some sitting required:**
- Work, travel, meals
- Can't always control
- Optimize what you can
- Move when possible
