# Ironing Posture

Ironing requires standing and arm work. Proper posture prevents strain during clothes care.

## Board Setup

### Height Adjustment

**Ironing board:**
- Hip height ideal
- Adjust to person
- No hunching needed
- Comfortable reach
- Ergonomic setup

### Board Stability

**Secure surface:**
- Locked in place
- Won't wobble
- Safe ironing
- Stable surface
- Quality work

## Standing Position

### At Board

**Ironing stance:**
- Stand close
- Upright posture
- Shoulders relaxed
- Core engaged lightly
- Comfortable position

### Weight Distribution

**Extended standing:**
- Shift weight
- Small steps
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Comfortable shoes
- Sustainable standing

## Arm Motion

### Iron Handling

**Using iron:**
- Relaxed grip
- Wrist neutral
- Smooth strokes
- Not too much pressure
- Efficient motion

### Reaching

**Across board:**
- Turn item instead
- Don't overreach
- Keep close
- Efficient positioning
- Comfortable access

## Session Duration

### Batch Ironing

**Multiple items:**
- Take breaks
- Not too long
- Split sessions
- Efficient process
- Sustainable task

### Sitting Option

**When possible:**
- Seated ironing possible
- Lower board
- Lap items
- Alternative approach
- Comfort choice

Good ironing posture supports comfortable, efficient clothes pressing.
