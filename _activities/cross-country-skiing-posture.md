# Cross-Country Skiing Posture

Cross-country skiing demands endurance positioning. Proper posture enables efficient, sustainable skiing.

## Classic Technique

### Diagonal Stride

**Basic movement:**
- Alternating kick
- Glide phase
- Opposite arm/leg
- Pole plant
- Natural rhythm

### Body Position

**While skiing:**
- Slight forward lean
- Hips forward
- Core engaged
- Natural arm swing
- Efficient motion

## Skate Technique

### V-Style Skating

**Advanced technique:**
- Ski angled out
- Push off edge
- Weight transfer
- Poling variations
- Rhythmic motion

### Body Position

**Skating stance:**
- Athletic position
- Low center
- Dynamic balance
- Core engagement
- Efficient power

## Poling

### Single Pole

**Classic poling:**
- Alternating poles
- Plant and push
- Core engagement
- Arm follows through
- Natural rhythm

### Double Poling

**Both together:**
- Arms together
- Core crunch
- Full body power
- Efficient on flats
- Endurance technique

## Hills

### Climbing

**Going up:**
- Herringbone or V
- Strong poles
- Short steps
- Sustained effort
- Technique matters

### Descending

**Coming down:**
- Controlled position
- Knees bent
- Weight balanced
- Prepared for terrain
- Safe speed

## Endurance

### Long Tours

**Extended skiing:**
- Sustainable pace
- Nutrition and hydration
- Layer management
- Technique focus
- Enjoy the journey

Good cross-country skiing posture enables efficient winter touring.
