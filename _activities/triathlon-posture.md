# Triathlon Posture

Triathlon combines three disciplines. Proper posture in each enables efficient racing.

## Swim Posture

### Open Water Form

**In water:**
- Streamlined body
- Efficient stroke
- Sighting integrated
- Navigation aware
- Energy conservation

### Wetsuit Considerations

**Swim equipment:**
- Buoyancy changes
- Range of motion
- Shoulder fatigue
- Exit preparation
- Transition ready

## Bike Posture

### Aero Position

**On bike:**
- Flat back
- Narrow arms
- Hips forward
- Core engaged
- Sustainable position

### Power Output

**Efficient cycling:**
- Smooth pedal circles
- Core stable
- Energy conservation
- Nutrition access
- Run preparation

## Run Posture

### Off the Bike

**Transition running:**
- Different muscle activation
- Find running legs
- Upright gradually
- Patience needed
- Efficient form

### Race Form

**Running tired:**
- Maintain form
- Core engaged
- Efficient stride
- Arms controlled
- Mental focus

## Transitions

### T1 (Swim to Bike)

**Quick change:**
- Wetsuit removal
- Helmet first
- Efficient motion
- Focus on essentials
- Smooth exit

### T2 (Bike to Run)

**Final switch:**
- Rack bike
- Shoes on
- Quick exit
- Find rhythm
- Final push

## Endurance

### Pacing

**Race management:**
- Even effort
- Reserve for run
- Nutrition timing
- Hydration plan
- Finish strong

Good triathlon posture maintains efficiency across all three disciplines.
