# Physical Therapy Exercises Posture

Physical therapy exercises require precise form. Proper posture ensures therapeutic benefit.

## Following Instructions

### Therapist Guidance

**During sessions:**
- Listen carefully
- Watch demonstrations
- Ask questions
- Practice correctly
- Learn proper form

### Home Exercises

**Independent practice:**
- Remember instruction
- Use mirrors
- Correct form
- Consistent practice
- Track progress

## Common PT Exercises

### Range of Motion

**Joint exercises:**
- Controlled movement
- Full range as directed
- Smooth motion
- No forcing
- Gradual improvement

### Strengthening

**Building strength:**
- Proper alignment
- Controlled resistance
- Core engagement
- Breathing pattern
- Progressive loading

## Exercise Positions

### Lying Exercises

**Supine or prone:**
- Neutral spine
- Supported position
- Stable base
- Controlled movement
- Comfortable surface

### Seated Exercises

**Chair or bench:**
- Upright posture
- Feet grounded
- Core engaged
- Proper alignment
- Stable position

### Standing Exercises

**Upright work:**
- Balanced stance
- Core support
- Proper alignment
- Safe footing
- Control maintained

## Safety Considerations

### Pain Awareness

**Listening to body:**
- Therapeutic vs harmful
- Communicate with PT
- Modify as needed
- Don't push through wrong pain
- Progress appropriately

Good physical therapy exercise posture supports effective rehabilitation and recovery.
