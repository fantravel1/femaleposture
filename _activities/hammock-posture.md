# Hammock Posture

Hammocks offer unique relaxation. Proper positioning enables comfortable, spinal-healthy rest.

## Getting In

### Entry Technique

**Sitting down:**
- Center of hammock
- Lower slowly
- Hands stabilize
- Controlled entry
- Balanced motion

### Positioning

**Once in:**
- Centered in fabric
- Head toward one end
- Adjust height
- Find comfort
- Settle in

## Lying Position

### Diagonal Lay

**Best position:**
- Lay diagonally
- Head one direction
- Feet opposite
- Flattens surface
- Proper technique

### Avoiding Curve

**Preventing banana shape:**
- Diagonal position key
- Spreads fabric
- Flatter surface
- Better spinal alignment
- More comfortable

## For Sleeping

### Overnight Use

**Camping in hammock:**
- Underquilt for warmth
- Proper hang angle
- Pillow optional
- Side sleeping possible
- Practice needed

### Sleep Quality

**Getting rest:**
- Gentle sway
- Enclosed options
- Weather protection
- Comfort adjustments
- Personal preference

## Relaxation Use

### Daytime Rest

**Casual use:**
- Any position comfortable
- Reading possible
- Short naps
- Gentle motion
- Peaceful rest

### Getting Out

**Exit technique:**
- Hold edges
- Swing legs out
- Sit first
- Stand carefully
- Controlled exit

## Choosing Hammock

### For Comfort

**Selection:**
- Width matters
- Length appropriate
- Weight capacity
- Quality materials
- Intended use

Good hammock posture enables comfortable suspended relaxation.
