# Bobsled Posture

Bobsled requires explosive starts and aerodynamic positioning. Proper form enables speed and safety.

## Push Start

### Starting Blocks

**Ready position:**
- Sprint stance
- Hands on push bars
- Ready for explosion
- Team synchronized
- Maximum effort

### The Push

**Accelerating sled:**
- Explosive start
- Low driving position
- Synchronized with teammates
- Maximum power
- Quick transition

### Loading In

**Getting in sled:**
- Quick entry
- Smooth motion
- Immediate tuck
- No lost momentum
- Practiced precision

## In-Sled Position

### Pilot Position

**Steering:**
- Specific visibility
- Steering control
- Aerodynamic
- Focused attention
- Precise inputs

### Crew Position

**Riding:**
- Tucked low
- Head down
- Aerodynamic shape
- Minimize drag
- Hold position

## Aerodynamics

### Body Position

**During run:**
- Maximize streamlining
- Minimize air resistance
- Still body
- Helmet down
- Team synchronized

## Braking

### At Finish

**Stopping:**
- Brake engaged
- Smooth application
- Force management
- Safe stop
- Team coordination

## Physical Demands

### Training

**Athlete requirements:**
- Explosive power
- Core strength
- Team coordination
- Fearlessness
- Specific training

Good bobsled posture maximizes speed through optimal positioning.
