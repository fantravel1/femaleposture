# Pottery Posture

Pottery involves sustained positions at the wheel or table. Proper posture protects your back and shoulders while working with clay.

## Wheel Work

### Seated Position

**At the wheel:**
- Seat at proper height
- Feet on pedal/floor
- Back supported
- Arms comfortable
- Core engaged

### Body Position

**While throwing:**
- Lean forward from hips
- Arms supported on legs
- Shoulders relaxed
- Eyes on work
- Periodic breaks

## Hand Building

### Table Work

**Standing or sitting:**
- Work surface at proper height
- Weight balanced
- Arms at comfortable level
- Core engaged
- Position variety

### Body Mechanics

**Clay handling:**
- Use whole body for wedging
- Don't just use arms
- Safe lifting
- Core engagement
- Movement breaks

## Common Issues

### Back Strain

**From leaning:**
- Proper seat height
- Core engagement
- Regular breaks
- Stretching routine
- Position changes

### Shoulder Tension

**From arm work:**
- Arms supported
- Relaxed shoulders
- Movement breaks
- Strength building
- Massage relief

## Studio Setup

### Ergonomic Space

**Comfortable working:**
- Adjustable wheel height
- Proper seating
- Good ventilation
- Floor mat
- Tool organization

Good pottery posture allows you to focus on your creative work while protecting your body through long studio sessions.
