# Sailing Posture

Sailing demands dynamic balance on a moving vessel. Proper posture enables effective, safe sailing.

## On Deck

### Sitting Position

**When seated:**
- Low center of gravity
- Feet braced
- Secure hold
- Ready to move
- Balance focused

### Hiking Out

**Balancing boat:**
- Feet under straps
- Body weight out
- Counter boat heel
- Core engaged
- Team coordination

## Moving About

### Safe Movement

**On deck:**
- Low stance
- Hold something always
- Deliberate steps
- Watch for boom
- Secure movement

### In Heavy Weather

**Challenging conditions:**
- Stay low
- Frequent holds
- Clipped in if appropriate
- Careful motion
- Safety priority

## Working Positions

### Trimming Sails

**Sheet handling:**
- Secure stance
- Two-hand holds
- Quick adjustments
- Ready to ease
- Controlled power

### At the Helm

**Steering:**
- Secure position
- Comfortable access
- Good visibility
- Feel the boat
- Sustained attention

## Racing Positions

### Competitive Sailing

**Performance focus:**
- More extreme positions
- Hiking harder
- Quick movement
- Athletic demands
- Team synchronization

## Cruising

### Relaxed Sailing

**Leisure focus:**
- Comfortable positions
- Enjoy the sail
- Safety maintained
- Less extreme
- Sustainable pleasure

Good sailing posture balances effectiveness with safety on the water.
