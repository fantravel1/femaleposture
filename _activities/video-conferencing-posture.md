# Video Conferencing Posture

Video calls require on-camera presence. Proper posture projects professionalism through screen.

## Camera Setup

### Eye Level Camera

**Positioning:**
- Camera at eye level
- Not looking down
- Direct gaze effect
- Professional appearance
- Flattering angle

### Distance from Camera

**Framing:**
- Head and shoulders visible
- Not too close
- Not too far
- Centered in frame
- Professional shot

## Seated Posture

### Chair Position

**Video call sitting:**
- Upright posture
- Back straight
- Shoulders relaxed
- Alert appearance
- Professional look

### Off-Camera Body

**Full body position:**
- Feet on floor
- Good overall posture
- Even if not seen
- Supports upper body
- Complete positioning

## Engagement

### Active Appearance

**On camera:**
- Slight forward lean
- Engaged expression
- Nodding appropriately
- Visible attention
- Connected presence

### Speaking Posture

**When talking:**
- Upright and confident
- Look at camera
- Natural gestures
- Clear audio positioning
- Professional delivery

## Long Meetings

### Extended Calls

**Multi-hour meetings:**
- Micro-movements
- Off-camera stretches
- Mute and move
- Sustainable sitting
- Comfortable duration

### Break Utilization

**Meeting breaks:**
- Stand and stretch
- Move around
- Reset position
- Refresh posture
- Return ready

Good video conferencing posture supports professional virtual presence.
