# Horseback Riding Posture

Horseback riding demands specific body positioning for communication with the horse and rider safety. Proper riding posture creates effective riding while protecting your body.

## Basic Seat

### Position in Saddle

**Proper seat:**
- Weight on seat bones
- Tall spine
- Shoulders back
- Head balanced
- Heels down

### Leg Position

**Lower body:**
- Long leg
- Soft knee
- Ankle flexible
- Heel lower than toe
- Contact with horse

## Upper Body

### Arm Position

**Rein handling:**
- Soft elbows
- Straight line to bit
- Relaxed shoulders
- Following hands
- Quiet contact

### Core Engagement

**Torso stability:**
- Core active but not rigid
- Independent seat
- Absorbs movement
- Stable yet flexible
- Follows horse motion

## Gaits and Posture

### At Walk

**Walking position:**
- Following seat
- Relaxed position
- Slight hip movement
- Soft contact
- Balanced

### At Trot

**Rising/sitting:**
- Posting rhythm
- Sitting trot engagement
- Core absorption
- Soft knees
- Balanced rising

### At Canter

**Cantering position:**
- Following hip motion
- Deep seat
- Quiet hands
- Balanced position
- Core engaged

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female riders:**
- Hip flexibility advantage
- Core strength needs
- Leg strength
- Upper body endurance
- Equipment fit

### Injury Prevention

**Common issues:**
- Back strain
- Hip tightness
- Inner thigh issues
- Falls prevention
- Protective gear

Good riding posture creates partnership with the horse while protecting your body through long rides.
