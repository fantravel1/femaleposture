# Nordic Walking Posture

Nordic walking adds poles for full-body engagement. Proper technique increases benefits significantly.

## Basic Stance

### Walking Posture

**Upright position:**
- Tall spine
- Shoulders relaxed
- Core engaged
- Eyes forward
- Natural gait

### Arm Position

**Ready for poles:**
- Relaxed grip
- Arms swing naturally
- Opposite arm/leg
- Full range
- Coordinated motion

## Pole Technique

### Plant and Push

**Using poles:**
- Plant at angle behind
- Push through handle
- Release grip at back
- Full arm extension
- Power from push

### Hand Grip

**Holding poles:**
- Strap supports hand
- Relaxed grip
- Open hand at back
- Catch at front
- Not death grip

## Stride

### Natural Gait

**Walking rhythm:**
- Normal stride length
- Heel to toe
- Arm swing matches
- Smooth motion
- Sustainable pace

### Enhanced Movement

**With poles:**
- Slightly longer stride
- More rotation
- Upper body engaged
- Full body motion
- Increased intensity

## Upper Body

### Engagement

**Working arms:**
- Tricep push
- Shoulder rotation
- Core rotation
- Full range
- Conscious effort

## Terrain

### Hills

**Incline walking:**
- Shorter stride up
- Poles assist push
- Core engaged
- Sustainable effort
- Use poles descending

Good Nordic walking posture transforms walking into full-body exercise.
