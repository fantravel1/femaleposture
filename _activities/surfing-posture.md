# Surfing Posture

Surfing demands paddling, explosive pop-ups, and dynamic balance on moving water. Understanding proper surfing posture improves your riding while protecting your body.

## Paddling Position

### Proper Form

**On the board:**
- Centered on board
- Chest lifted
- Chin up
- Strong paddle strokes
- Legs together

### Paddling Technique

**Efficient strokes:**
- Full arm extension
- Deep catch
- Pull through
- Alternating arms
- Core engagement

## The Pop-Up

### Proper Technique

**Getting up:**
- Hands at chest level
- Push up explosively
- Bring feet under
- Land in stance
- Stay low

### Common Errors

**What to avoid:**
- Knee first
- Too slow
- Poor hand placement
- Standing too tall
- Looking down

## Riding Stance

### Proper Position

**On the wave:**
- Feet perpendicular to board
- Knees bent
- Weight centered
- Arms for balance
- Eyes forward

### Stance Types

**Position options:**
- Regular stance
- Goofy stance
- Wide or narrow
- Front or back foot weight
- Dynamic adjustment

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female surfing:**
- Upper body strength building
- Core for pop-up
- Balance and flexibility
- Board selection
- Suit considerations

### Injury Prevention

**Common issues:**
- Shoulder strain
- Low back issues
- Ankle injuries
- Impact injuries
- Prevention strategies

Good surfing posture connects you to the wave while protecting your body through the sport's dynamic demands.
