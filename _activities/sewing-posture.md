# Sewing Posture

Sewing requires sustained focus in relatively static positions. Proper sewing posture prevents the neck, shoulder, and back strain common to this craft.

## Machine Sewing

### Chair Setup

**Proper seating:**
- Chair height adjusted
- Feet flat on floor
- Thighs parallel
- Back supported
- Arms at comfortable height

### Machine Position

**Ergonomic setup:**
- Machine at appropriate height
- Good lighting
- Minimal reaching
- Foot pedal comfortable
- Work at eye level

## Hand Sewing

### Sitting Position

**Comfortable craft:**
- Supported back
- Work elevated
- Arms supported
- Good lighting
- Breaks scheduled

### Hand Position

**Needle work:**
- Relaxed grip
- Wrist neutral
- Varied positions
- Rest periods
- Hand stretches

## Common Issues

### Neck Strain

**From looking down:**
- Elevate work
- Good lighting
- Take breaks
- Neck stretches
- Proper chair height

### Shoulder Tension

**From arm position:**
- Arms supported
- Shoulders relaxed
- Regular movement
- Arm stretches
- Posture awareness

## Prevention Strategies

### Work Setup

**Ergonomic space:**
- Adjustable chair
- Proper table height
- Task lighting
- Organized workspace
- Comfort priority

### Break Routine

**Movement breaks:**
- Every 30-45 minutes
- Stand and stretch
- Walk around
- Eye rest
- Position reset

Good sewing posture protects your body while allowing you to enjoy your craft for years to come.
