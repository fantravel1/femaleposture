# Painting Posture

Painting involves sustained arm work and focused attention. Proper posture protects your body while enabling your artistic expression.

## Easel Position

### Standing Setup

**Easel height:**
- Canvas at eye level
- Comfortable reach
- Full arm movement possible
- Proper distance
- Anti-fatigue mat

### Seated Setup

**Chair painting:**
- Easel adjusted lower
- Chair supportive
- Arms at good height
- Feet grounded
- Back supported

## Body Position

### Standing Painting

**Upright posture:**
- Weight balanced
- Knees soft
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Regular weight shifts

### Arm Position

**Brush work:**
- Paint from shoulder
- Elbow flexible
- Wrist relaxed
- Varied movements
- Arm rests

## Common Issues

### Neck Strain

**From focus:**
- Step back regularly
- Varied viewing distance
- Stretching breaks
- Proper lighting
- Canvas position

### Shoulder Fatigue

**Arm held up:**
- Mahl stick use
- Arm support
- Break frequency
- Shoulder stretches
- Varied techniques

## Studio Setup

### Ergonomic Space

**Artist-friendly:**
- Good lighting
- Proper ventilation
- Floor surface
- Tool organization
- Comfortable temperature

### Break Routine

**Movement intervals:**
- Step back to view
- Walk around
- Stretch regularly
- Hydrate
- Posture reset

Good painting posture lets you focus on your art without the physical strain that can develop during long sessions.
