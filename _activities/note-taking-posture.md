# Note Taking Posture

Note taking combines writing with attention. Proper posture supports both learning and comfort.

## Seated Position

### At Desk or Table

**Writing setup:**
- Upright sitting
- Surface at comfortable height
- Paper angled slightly
- Arm supported
- Relaxed position

### In Meeting

**Conference room:**
- Table access
- Notebook positioned
- Discreet writing
- Attention maintained
- Efficient notes

## Writing Mechanics

### Hand Position

**Pen grip:**
- Relaxed hold
- Not too tight
- Wrist neutral
- Smooth movement
- Comfortable writing

### Arm Support

**During writing:**
- Forearm on surface
- Wrist floats or rests
- Hand moves freely
- Controlled strokes
- Sustainable motion

## Listening and Writing

### Dual Focus

**Attention split:**
- Eyes up for listening
- Quick glances to page
- Key points captured
- Abbreviated writing
- Efficient process

### Posture for Attention

**Alert stance:**
- Upright position
- Engaged presence
- Ready to write
- Active listening
- Balanced focus

## Extended Sessions

### Long Meetings

**Duration management:**
- Shift positions
- Brief hand rests
- Maintain posture
- Sustainable note-taking
- Comfortable duration

### Study Sessions

**Academic notes:**
- Good desk setup
- Proper lighting
- Comfortable position
- Regular breaks
- Effective studying

Good note taking posture supports effective capturing while maintaining comfort.
