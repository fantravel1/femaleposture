# Shoveling Snow Posture

Shoveling snow is strenuous work. Proper posture prevents injury during winter cleanup.

## Shovel Selection

### Ergonomic Design

**Better shovels:**
- Bent handle helps
- Lightweight material
- Appropriate size
- Comfortable grip
- Quality investment

### Push vs Lift Shovels

**Different types:**
- Push shovels easier
- Lift shovels for deep snow
- Consider both
- Match to conditions
- Right tool helps

## Lifting Technique

### Safe Shoveling

**Critical form:**
- Bend at knees
- Back straight
- Smaller loads
- Lift with legs
- Protect spine

### Throwing Motion

**Dispersing snow:**
- Turn whole body
- Don't twist spine
- Walk to dump pile
- Face throw direction
- Safe motion

## Pacing Essential

### Cardiovascular Strain

**Intense activity:**
- Work slowly
- Take frequent breaks
- Don't overexert
- Know limits
- Heart health concern

### Rest Periods

**Break frequency:**
- Every 10-15 minutes
- Catch breath
- Drink water
- Assess progress
- Sustainable pace

## Pushing Snow

### Easier Alternative

**When possible:**
- Push don't lift
- Plow motion
- Less strain
- More efficient
- Safer approach

## Safety First

### Know Your Limits

**Important:**
- Stop if pain
- Stop if breathless
- Get help
- Consider snow blower
- Professional services

Good shoveling posture prevents injury during demanding winter work.
