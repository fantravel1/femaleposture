# Snowshoeing Posture

Snowshoeing modifies normal walking. Proper posture enables comfortable winter hiking.

## Walking Technique

### Basic Stride

**Modified gait:**
- Wider stance
- Lift feet higher
- Natural swing
- Slight waddle okay
- Find rhythm

### Foot Placement

**On snow:**
- Flat placement
- Full contact
- Weight distributed
- Secure footing
- Careful steps

## Body Position

### Upright Posture

**While snowshoeing:**
- Tall spine
- Core engaged
- Arms swinging
- Natural motion
- Balanced walk

### Pole Use

**Trekking poles:**
- Recommended
- Balance assist
- Propulsion help
- Arm workout
- Common addition

## Climbing

### Uphill Technique

**Going up:**
- Toe teeth dig in
- Shorter stride
- Lean forward
- Use poles
- Kick step if steep

### Traversing

**Crossing slopes:**
- Side stepping
- Edge engagement
- Poles for balance
- Careful footwork
- Secure placement

## Descending

### Downhill Walking

**Coming down:**
- Shorter stride
- Knees bent
- Heel first
- Controlled speed
- Poles for braking

### Steep Sections

**Difficult terrain:**
- Side step down
- Heel dig
- Very controlled
- Pole assistance
- Safety first

## Endurance

### Long Tours

**Extended snowshoeing:**
- Pace yourself
- Regular breaks
- Stay warm
- Hydration
- Enjoy the snow

Good snowshoeing posture enables comfortable winter hiking.
