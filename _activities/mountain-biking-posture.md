# Mountain Biking Posture

Mountain biking demands dynamic, reactive positioning. Proper posture enables safe, controlled riding.

## Basic Position

### Neutral Stance

**Ready position:**
- Standing on pedals
- Knees and elbows bent
- Weight centered
- Core engaged
- Ready for anything

### Attack Position

**Terrain engagement:**
- Low and centered
- Bent limbs absorb
- Eyes ahead
- Quick reactions
- Aggressive ready

## Seated Climbing

### Uphill Position

**When seated:**
- Slide forward
- Light front wheel
- Sustained power
- Core engaged
- Efficient climbing

### Steeper Climbs

**More difficult:**
- More forward
- Traction focus
- Controlled power
- May stand
- Technique matters

## Descending

### Downhill Position

**Coming down:**
- Weight back
- Butt off seat
- Arms and legs bent
- Low center
- Speed control

### Steep and Technical

**Difficult descents:**
- Very low position
- Behind seat
- Brakes controlled
- Line selection
- Confidence and skill

## Technical Features

### Obstacles

**Navigating:**
- Light on bike
- Lift front wheel
- Weight shifts
- Quick reactions
- Practice needed

### Corners

**Trail turning:**
- Outside foot down
- Weight low and forward
- Look through turn
- Controlled speed
- Smooth arc

## Safety

### Protective Gear

**Essential equipment:**
- Helmet always
- Gloves recommended
- Pads for aggressive
- Appropriate bike
- Safety first

Good mountain biking posture enables confident, controlled off-road riding.
