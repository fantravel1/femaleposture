# Tap Dance Posture

Tap dance creates music through footwork. Proper posture enables clean sound production.

## Basic Stance

### Ready Position

**Foundation:**
- Weight centered
- Knees relaxed
- Core engaged
- Arms natural
- Ready for sound

### Balance

**Weight distribution:**
- Mostly ball of foot
- Quick transfers
- Light movement
- Controlled shifts
- Ready for any step

## Sound Production

### Basic Sounds

**Clean taps:**
- Ball tap
- Heel drop
- Brush
- Shuffle
- Flap

### Clarity

**Quality sound:**
- Precise strikes
- Clean articulation
- Controlled weight
- Relaxed ankle
- Clear rhythm

## Upper Body

### Arms

**Styling options:**
- Natural swing
- Character choices
- Musical accents
- Personal expression
- Period appropriate

### Core Position

**Stability center:**
- Engaged but relaxed
- Allows mobility
- Supports upper body
- Enables quick feet
- Movement foundation

## Rhythm Skills

### Timing

**Musical precision:**
- On the beat
- Off-beat accents
- Syncopation
- Break and fill
- Musical conversation

### Improvisation

**Creating:**
- Musical response
- Personal vocabulary
- Rhythmic ideas
- Spontaneous creation
- Jazz roots

## Performance

### Showmanship

**Stage presence:**
- Character expression
- Musical joy
- Audience connection
- Personal style
- Entertainment value

Good tap dance posture enables clear, musical sound production.
