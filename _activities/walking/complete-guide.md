## Why Walking Posture Matters

### Walking Is Exercise

**Even casual walking:**

- Affects spinal health
- Patterns become habitual
- Cumulative over life

### Poor Walking Creates Problems

**Issues include:**
- Lower back strain

## Good Walking Posture

### Head and Neck

**Position:**
- Head balanced on spine
- Eyes forward (not at phone)
- Chin parallel to ground
- Neck relaxed and long

**Not:**
- Looking down constantly
- Head jutting forward
- Neck tight

### Shoulders and Arms

**Position:**
- Shoulders relaxed, down
- Arms swing naturally
- Opposite arm, opposite leg
- Swing from shoulders (not elbows)

**Not:**
- Shoulders hunched up
- Arms rigid at sides
- Crossed or in pockets

### Core and Pelvis

**Position:**
- Core gently engaged
- Pelvis neutral
- Slight natural lean forward (from ankles)
- Glutes engage with each step

**Not:**
- Swayback (excessive arch)
- Slumped posture
- Core completely disengaged

### Legs and Feet

**Position:**
- Step from hip
- Knee stays slightly bent at landing
- Heel strikes first (usually)
- Push off from toes

**Not:**
- Locking knees
- Shuffling feet
- Overstriding

## Common Walking Problems

### Looking Down

**From devices or habit:**
- Head forward and down
- Neck strain
- Spinal curve changes
- Reduced awareness

**Fix:**
- Phone down while walking
- Eyes forward and up
- Awareness practice
- Scenic route focus

### Hunched Shoulders

**Often from:**
- Cold weather
- Habit
- Bag carrying
- General tension

**Fix:**
- Conscious shoulder drops
- Regular awareness checks
- Arm swings
- Upper back work

### Short, Shuffling Steps

**May indicate:**
- Weakness
- Balance concerns
- Habit
- Rushing without proper stride

**Fix:**
- Conscious longer strides
- Step from hip
- Deliberate walking practice
- Strength work if needed

### Slumped Posture

**Common in:**
- Fatigue
- Low mood
- Habit
- Sedentary life

**Fix:**
- Core engagement
- Awareness cues
- Strengthening
- Mood-movement connection

## Walking Speed and Form

### Casual Walking

**Everyday pace:**
- Relaxed but not sloppy
- Natural arm swing
- Comfortable stride
- Alert but easy

### Fitness Walking

**Faster, intentional:**
- Increased arm swing
- Longer stride
- More core engagement
- More glute work

### Hiking

**Different terrain:**
- Core very active
- Poles if helpful
- Shorter steps uphill
- Controlled descent
- See specific hiking resources

## Footwear and Walking

### Shoe Impact

**What you wear matters:**
- Support affects gait
- Heel height changes posture
- Worn shoes create problems
- Right shoe for activity

### For Regular Walking

**Choose:**
- Supportive
- Comfortable
- Appropriate for surface
- Replace when worn

## Walking in Different Environments

### Urban Walking

**Considerations:**
- Hard surfaces
- Obstacles
- Crowds
- Supportive shoes important

### Trail Walking

**Considerations:**
- Uneven surfaces
- Natural challenge
- Core more active
- Different footwear

### Treadmill Walking

**Considerations:**
- Consistent surface
- Can focus on form
- Don't hold rails (defeats purpose)
- Adjust incline for variety

---

## Walking with Load

### Carrying Bags

**Principles:**
- Balance loads
- Backpack better than one-shoulder
- Switch sides with single strap
- Don't overload

### Pushing Strollers

**Posture:**
- Stand tall, don't hunch
- Push from core
- Handle at comfortable height

### Pulling Luggage

**Technique:**
- Switch hands
- Don't twist
- Comfortable handle height

## Walking and Phone Use

### The Problem

**Walking while texting:**
- Head down
- Unaware of environment
- Posture compromised
- Unsafe

### Better Approach

**If you must:**
- Stop to text
- Raise phone to eye level briefly
- Stay aware
- Limit duration
