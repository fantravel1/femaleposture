# Dusting Posture

Dusting involves reaching various heights. Proper posture prevents strain during surface cleaning.

## High Surfaces

### Above Shoulder Level

**Upper dusting:**
- Step stool for high
- Extension dusters
- Don't overreach
- Safe access
- Comfortable height

### Overhead Work

**Very high:**
- Proper tools
- Stable platform
- Brief duration
- Neck protection
- Safe practice

## Mid-Level Surfaces

### Comfortable Heights

**Eye level to waist:**
- Natural reach
- Comfortable standing
- Efficient dusting
- Easy access
- Primary cleaning zone

## Low Surfaces

### Below Waist

**Lower dusting:**
- Kneel or squat
- Comfortable position
- Protect knees
- Easy access
- Thorough cleaning

## Arm Motion

### Wiping Technique

**Dusting action:**
- Light pressure
- Smooth strokes
- Relaxed arm
- Efficient motion
- Effective cleaning

## Extended Dusting

### Whole House

**Multiple rooms:**
- Systematic approach
- Efficient order
- Take breaks
- Alternate positions
- Complete job

Good dusting posture enables comfortable surface cleaning at all heights.
