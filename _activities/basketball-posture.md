# Basketball Posture

Basketball demands quick movement, jumping, and dynamic positioning. Understanding proper basketball posture improves performance and prevents injury.

## Basic Positions

### Athletic Stance

**Ready position:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees bent
- Hips low
- Weight on balls of feet
- Core engaged

### Defensive Stance

**Guard position:**
- Wide base
- Low center of gravity
- Arms active
- Eyes on opponent
- Ready to move

## Shooting Form

### Proper Mechanics

**Shooting posture:**
- Balanced stance
- Elbow under ball
- Shoulders square
- Core engaged
- Follow through

### Common Errors

**What to avoid:**
- Leaning back
- Elbow out
- Off-balance
- Rushing
- Poor follow through

## Movement Mechanics

### Cutting and Pivoting

**Footwork posture:**
- Low center of gravity
- Quick feet
- Balanced turns
- Pivot foot awareness
- Explosive movement

### Jumping

**Vertical leap:**
- Load properly
- Arm drive
- Core stability
- Safe landing
- Knee alignment

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Injury Prevention

**Female athlete concerns:**
- ACL awareness
- Knee alignment
- Landing mechanics
- Strength training
- Proper conditioning

### Training Focus

**Building capacity:**
- Core strength
- Lower body power
- Lateral movement
- Flexibility
- Endurance

Good basketball posture creates the foundation for effective play while protecting your body from the sport's demands.
