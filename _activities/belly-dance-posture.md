# Belly Dance Posture

Belly dance features torso isolation and hip movement. Proper posture enables expressive, controlled movement.

## Basic Stance

### Foundation

**Ready position:**
- Feet under hips
- Knees soft
- Pelvis neutral
- Core engaged
- Arms lifted

### Weight Distribution

**Balance point:**
- Even or shifted
- Enables isolation
- Stable base
- Controlled position
- Ready for movement

## Isolations

### Hip Work

**Independent movement:**
- Hip drops
- Hip lifts
- Hip circles
- Figure eights
- Sharp or flowing

### Chest Isolations

**Upper torso:**
- Chest circles
- Chest slides
- Chest lifts
- Rib cage articulation
- Independent from hips

### Shoulder Work

**Upper body:**
- Shoulder shimmy
- Shoulder circles
- Alternating motion
- Controlled speed
- Relaxed quality

## Shimmies

### Hip Shimmy

**Continuous vibration:**
- Alternating hip
- Knee drive
- Relaxed muscles
- Continuous motion
- Various speeds

### Shoulder Shimmy

**Upper shimmy:**
- Alternating shoulders
- Quick motion
- Relaxed arms
- Continuous shake
- Controlled intensity

## Arms

### Port de Bras

**Arm carriage:**
- Lifted from back
- Graceful lines
- Wrist movement
- Finger work
- Frame for body

## Expression

### Performance

**Feminine power:**
- Confidence
- Musicality
- Personal style
- Emotional connection
- Authentic expression

Good belly dance posture creates the control needed for expressive isolation.
