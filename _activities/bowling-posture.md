# Bowling Posture

Bowling combines precision and power in a repetitive motion. Proper bowling posture improves accuracy while protecting your body through many frames.

## Starting Position

### Setup Stance

**At the dots:**
- Feet together or slightly apart
- Weight centered
- Ball held comfortably
- Eyes on target
- Relaxed but focused

### Ball Hold

**Proper grip:**
- Fingers in holes
- Thumb secure
- Wrist position
- Ball weight supported
- Comfortable carry

## The Approach

### Four-Step Approach

**Movement sequence:**
- First step, pushaway
- Second step, down
- Third step, back
- Fourth step, slide and release
- Balanced finish

### Five-Step Approach

**Alternative timing:**
- Additional small step
- Same principles
- Comfortable rhythm
- Consistent timing
- Personal preference

## Release Mechanics

### Proper Release

**At the line:**
- Slide step
- Arm swing through
- Thumb exits first
- Fingers lift ball
- Follow through high

### Finish Position

**After release:**
- Balanced stance
- Arm continues up
- Eyes on target
- Watch ball path
- Stable finish

## Common Errors

### What to Avoid

**Problematic form:**
- Muscling the ball
- Dropping shoulder
- Inconsistent approach
- Poor balance
- Rushed timing

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Equipment

**Proper fit:**
- Ball weight appropriate
- Hole sizing
- Shoes fit
- Wrist support if needed
- Comfortable approach

Good bowling posture creates consistent, accurate delivery while protecting your arm and back through many games.
