# Meditation Posture

Meditation posture supports both alertness and relaxation. Finding your optimal position enables deeper practice without physical distraction.

## Seated Positions

### Cross-Legged

**Floor sitting:**
- Cushion for hip elevation
- Knees below hips
- Spine tall
- Hands on knees or lap
- Chin slightly tucked

### Kneeling

**Seiza position:**
- Knees on cushion
- Sit on bench or cushion
- Spine upright
- Hands resting
- Sustainable position

### Chair Sitting

**Accessible option:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Back away from chair back
- Spine self-supporting
- Hands on thighs
- Alert posture

## Body Alignment

### Spine Position

**Upright posture:**
- Natural curves
- Crown lifted
- Chin level or slightly tucked
- Not rigid
- Relaxed but alert

### Shoulders and Arms

**Upper body:**
- Shoulders relaxed
- Arms hang naturally
- Hands rest comfortably
- No holding tension
- Easy breathing

## Lying Position

### Savasana

**For relaxation:**
- Flat on back
- Arms at sides
- Legs extended
- Fully supported
- Alert awareness challenge

### Modifications

**If needed:**
- Knees bent
- Support under knees
- Blanket under head
- Comfortable temperature
- Wake word if drowsy

## Common Challenges

### Discomfort

**Managing pain:**
- Props and cushions
- Position changes
- Shorter sessions first
- Physical preparation
- Listening to body

Good meditation posture balances alertness and comfort, supporting deep practice without physical distraction.
