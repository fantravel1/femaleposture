# Carrying Toddler Posture

Carrying toddlers is frequent parenting task. Proper posture prevents hip and back strain.

## Hip Carrying

### Common Position

**Hip carry:**
- Toddler on hip
- One arm supports
- Alternate sides
- Temporary only
- Watch asymmetry

### Better Alternatives

**Less strain:**
- Front carry
- Both hips equally
- Carrier device
- Stroller when possible
- Protect body

## Front Carrying

### Two-Arm Hold

**Balanced carry:**
- Both arms support
- Centered on body
- Core engaged
- More symmetrical
- Better for back

## Lifting Toddler

### Picking Up

**Safe lifting:**
- Squat down
- Back straight
- Lift with legs
- Close to body
- Controlled rise

### Setting Down

**Lowering:**
- Squat again
- Controlled descent
- Back protected
- Safe placement
- Efficient motion

## Duration Limits

### Heavy Child

**Weight consideration:**
- Limited carrying time
- Use stroller
- Walking encouraged
- Arms get rest
- Sustainable parenting

### Physical Demands

**Toddler stage:**
- Very demanding
- Protect body
- Strategic carrying
- Equipment helps
- Pace yourself

## Alternative Equipment

### Carrier Options

**For larger toddlers:**
- Toddler carriers exist
- Backpack carriers
- Hip seats
- Distribute weight
- Body protection

Good toddler carrying posture protects parent body during demanding phase.
