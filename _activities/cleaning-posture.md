# Cleaning Posture

Cleaning involves bending, reaching, and repetitive motions that can strain the body. Proper cleaning posture protects your back and joints while getting the housework done.

## Vacuuming

### Proper Technique

**Vacuum posture:**
- Stand upright
- Move with vacuum, don't reach
- Switch hands periodically
- Use lunging motion
- Keep cord behind you

### Common Mistakes

**What to avoid:**
- Bending over machine
- Twisting while vacuuming
- Overreaching
- Grip tension
- Rushing

## Mopping and Sweeping

### Proper Form

**Floor care posture:**
- Stand tall
- Move body, not just arms
- Use body weight
- Keep handle at good height
- Switch sides

### Technique Tips

**Better cleaning:**
- Push rather than pull
- Overlap strokes
- Work in sections
- Take breaks
- Use appropriate tools

## Bending Tasks

### Safe Bending

**Reaching low:**
- Squat or kneel
- One knee down
- Hinge at hips
- Keep back neutral
- Use long-handled tools

### Scrubbing

**Intensive cleaning:**
- Kneel with padding
- Use arm strength
- Avoid excessive pressure
- Alternate hands
- Take breaks

## Reaching Tasks

### High Reaching

**Upper cleaning:**
- Step stool for height
- Shoulder-friendly reach
- Don't overextend
- Use extension tools
- Work in sections

### Overhead Work

**Safe approach:**
- Stable platform
- Arms below shoulder when possible
- Take frequent breaks
- Switch positions
- Use appropriate tools

## General Cleaning Tips

### Posture-Friendly Approaches

**Body-smart cleaning:**
- Divide into sessions
- Alternate task types
- Take breaks
- Use ergonomic tools
- Listen to body

Good cleaning posture means the house gets clean without you getting hurt.
