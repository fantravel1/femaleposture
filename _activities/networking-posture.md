# Networking Posture

Networking posture communicates approachability and confidence. Proper positioning supports meaningful connections at professional events.

## Standing Posture

### Approachable Stance

**Open position:**
- Face toward room
- Arms uncrossed
- Slight smile
- Available body language
- Balanced stance

### Confident Base

**Strong standing:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Weight balanced
- Core engaged
- Shoulders back
- Head high

## Conversation Position

### One-on-One

**Direct conversation:**
- Face person directly
- Appropriate distance
- Open body language
- Engaged listening posture
- Eye contact balance

### Group Positioning

**Multiple people:**
- Open stance to allow entry
- Face center of group
- Include new joiners
- Balanced attention
- Dynamic positioning

## Extended Events

### Standing for Hours

**Event endurance:**
- Comfortable shoes essential
- Weight shifting
- Posture awareness
- Brief sits when available
- Movement between conversations

### Holding Items

**Drinks and plates:**
- One hand free
- Manageable load
- Not blocking body
- Put down to shake hands
- Strategic timing

## Non-Verbal Communication

### Body Language

**Messages you send:**
- Open chest = approachable
- Crossed arms = closed
- Good posture = confident
- Fidgeting = nervous
- Relaxed = comfortable

Good networking posture creates the first impression that opens doors to valuable connections.
