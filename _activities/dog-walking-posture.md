# Dog Walking Posture

Dog walking combines exercise with pet care. Proper posture prevents strain from pulling.

## Leash Handling

### Grip Technique

**Holding leash:**
- Relaxed grip
- Wrist through loop
- Not too tight
- Controlled length
- Comfortable hold

### Arm Position

**Walking stance:**
- Arm at side
- Relaxed shoulder
- Not elevated
- Natural swing limited
- Control maintained

## Pulling Dogs

### Managing Pullers

**Strong dogs:**
- Core engaged
- Low center gravity
- Short leash control
- Training approach
- Consider harness

### Body Mechanics

**Resisting pulls:**
- Don't lean back
- Brace with legs
- Core stability
- Lower body strength
- Balanced resistance

## Walking Pace

### Matching Dog

**Speed coordination:**
- Steady pace
- Accommodate sniffing
- Consistent rhythm
- Good exercise
- Enjoyable walk

### Your Stride

**Walking form:**
- Normal gait
- Upright posture
- Core engaged
- Arms natural
- Comfortable pace

## Multi-Dog Walking

### Multiple Leashes

**Handling several:**
- Organized grip
- Untangled leashes
- Equal attention
- Balanced sides
- Controlled pack

## Stopping and Standing

### Waiting Moments

**When dog stops:**
- Stand balanced
- Patient waiting
- Not hunched
- Relaxed posture
- Allow sniffing

Good dog walking posture provides exercise while preventing strain from pulling.
