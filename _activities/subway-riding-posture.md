# Subway Riding Posture

Subway riding involves standing or sitting. Proper posture supports comfortable, safe transit.

## Standing on Subway

### Holding On

**Safety first:**
- Always hold rail
- Pole or overhead strap
- Secure grip
- Ready for motion
- Safe standing

### Balanced Stance

**On moving train:**
- Wide stance
- Knees soft
- Core engaged
- Absorb motion
- Stable position

## Seated Position

### Train Seating

**Sitting on transit:**
- Upright posture
- Feet on floor
- Back against seat
- Comfortable position
- Alert sitting

### Getting Seat

**When available:**
- Take seats near door
- Sit efficiently
- Ready to give up
- Comfortable choice
- Practical seating

## Motion Management

### Starts and Stops

**Train movement:**
- Anticipate motion
- Brace appropriately
- Hold secure
- Maintain balance
- Safe riding

### Curves and Turns

**Track movement:**
- Lean appropriately
- Hold firmly
- Core engaged
- Balanced stance
- Smooth adaptation

## Crowded Transit

### Rush Hour

**Packed cars:**
- Minimize space
- Hold secure
- Stable stance
- Patient riding
- Efficient travel

Good subway riding posture supports safe, comfortable public transit.
