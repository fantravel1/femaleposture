# CrossFit Posture

CrossFit combines Olympic lifting, gymnastics, and high-intensity conditioning. Maintaining proper posture under fatigue is crucial for both performance and injury prevention.

## Foundational Positions

### Athletic Base

**Starting stance:**
- Feet hip to shoulder-width
- Weight in midfoot
- Knees tracking over toes
- Core braced
- Shoulders engaged

### Overhead Position

**Lockout posture:**
- Arms locked
- Active shoulders
- Stacked joints
- Neutral spine
- Core engaged

## Olympic Lifting Posture

### Clean Position

**Catch posture:**
- Elbows high
- Front rack position
- Torso upright
- Deep squat if needed
- Stable base

### Snatch Position

**Overhead squat:**
- Wide grip locked overhead
- Active shoulders
- Deep squat
- Upright torso
- Stable position

## Gymnastics Movements

### Kipping Positions

**Dynamic movements:**
- Hollow body
- Arch position
- Shoulder engagement
- Core control
- Efficient swing

### Handstand Position

**Inverted posture:**
- Stacked joints
- Core tight
- Active shoulders
- Straight line
- Balance maintained

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female CrossFitters:**
- Flexibility advantages
- Core strength focus
- Shoulder stability
- Recovery attention
- Scaling appropriately

### Injury Prevention

**Protection strategies:**
- Form before intensity
- Proper scaling
- Listen to body
- Adequate rest
- Professional coaching
