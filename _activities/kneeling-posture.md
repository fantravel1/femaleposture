# Kneeling Posture

Kneeling serves many purposes. Proper posture protects knees and supports comfortable positioning.

## Basic Kneeling

### On Both Knees

**Tall kneeling:**
- Both knees down
- Upright torso
- Hips over knees
- Core engaged
- Balanced position

### On One Knee

**Half kneeling:**
- One knee down
- One foot forward
- Stable base
- Transitional position
- Practical pose

## Knee Protection

### Padding Essential

**Protecting knees:**
- Knee pad
- Folded towel
- Soft surface
- Cushioning critical
- Comfort maintained

### Weight Distribution

**Pressure management:**
- Not all on knees
- Some on shins
- Balanced weight
- Comfortable position
- Sustainable kneeling

## Upper Body

### Torso Position

**While kneeling:**
- Upright preferred
- Core engaged
- Shoulders back
- Head balanced
- Good alignment

## Transitioning

### Rising from Kneel

**Standing up:**
- One foot forward
- Push from that leg
- Rise controlled
- Use support if needed
- Careful transition

### Lowering to Kneel

**Going down:**
- Controlled descent
- One knee first
- Hand support okay
- Gradual lowering
- Safe positioning

## Work Activities

### Floor Tasks

**While kneeling:**
- Close to work
- Alternate positions
- Take breaks
- Pad adequately
- Sustainable work

Good kneeling posture protects knees while enabling floor-level work.
