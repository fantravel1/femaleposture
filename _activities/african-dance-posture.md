# African Dance Posture

African dance encompasses many traditions. Common elements include grounded movement and polyrhythm.

## Basic Stance

### Grounded Position

**Earth connection:**
- Feet planted
- Knees bent deep
- Weight low
- Core engaged
- Connected to ground

### Ready Position

**For movement:**
- Athletic stance
- Mobile hips
- Responsive body
- Ready for drums
- Full attention

## Movement Quality

### Grounded Movement

**Low center:**
- Weight in earth
- Strong legs
- Stable base
- Power from ground
- Controlled motion

### Polyrhythm

**Multiple rhythms:**
- Body parts independent
- Different rhythms simultaneously
- Responsive to drums
- Complex patterns
- Musical conversation

## Body Parts

### Hips and Pelvis

**Movement center:**
- Isolation possible
- Rhythmic emphasis
- Cultural expression
- Powerful motion
- Natural movement

### Torso

**Upper body:**
- Core engaged
- Chest movement
- Back articulation
- Connected to hips
- Expressive range

### Arms

**Upper limbs:**
- Powerful gestures
- Cultural meaning
- Rhythmic motion
- Full range
- Expressive movement

## Cultural Context

### Respect and Learning

**Authentic practice:**
- Learn origins
- Understand meaning
- Respect tradition
- Appropriate practice
- Community context

### Musical Response

**Living with drums:**
- Call and response
