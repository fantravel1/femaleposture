# Jet Skiing Posture

Jet skiing requires active balance and core engagement. Proper posture enables safe, controlled riding.

## Seated Position

### Basic Riding

**On the seat:**
- Upright posture
- Knees grip sides
- Feet on footwells
- Core engaged
- Hands on handles

### Stable Base

**Maintaining control:**
- Lower body secure
- Upper body relaxed
- Weight centered
- Ready for movement
- Eyes forward

## Standing Position

### Active Riding

**On feet:**
- Knees bent
- Athletic stance
- Core very engaged
- Arms with give
- Absorb motion

### Rough Water

**Choppy conditions:**
- More knee bend
- Active legs
- Weight balanced
- Core stability
- Shock absorption

## Turning

### Basic Turn

**Direction change:**
- Look where going
- Lean into turn
- Weight shift
- Throttle management
- Smooth arc

### Sharp Turn

**Quick direction:**
- More body lean
- Inside knee pressure
- Core engagement
- Controlled speed
- Safe execution

## Throttle Control

### Speed Management

**Power control:**
- Smooth inputs
- Progressive acceleration
- Anticipate conditions
- Controlled stops
- Safe speed for conditions

## Safety

### Awareness

**Safe riding:**
- Constant scanning
- Right-of-way knowledge
- Distance from others
- Weather awareness
- Wear PFD

Good jet skiing posture creates the control needed for safe, enjoyable riding.
