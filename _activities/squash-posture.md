# Squash Posture

Squash combines fitness and skill in a confined space. Proper squash posture enables powerful shots and quick movement in the court.

## Ready Position

### T-Position Stance

**Central court:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees bent
- Weight balanced
- Racquet ready
- Eyes on ball and opponent

### Movement Ready

**Quick reactions:**
- Athletic stance
- Low center of gravity
- Core engaged
- Explosive potential
- Balanced position

## Stroke Mechanics

### Forehand Drive

**Main shot:**
- Side-on position
- Racquet preparation
- Hip rotation
- Follow through
- Balance maintained

### Backhand Drive

**Opposite side:**
- Shoulder turn
- Elbow lead
- Racquet face control
- Full extension
- Recovery position

### Volleys

**Mid-court play:**
- Compact swing
- Quick reactions
- Racquet in front
- Touch control
- Balance maintained

## Court Movement

### Ghosting Pattern

**Movement drills:**
- T-position return
- Efficient movement
- Explosive first step
- Balanced recovery
- Pattern recognition

### Lunging

**Reaching shots:**
- Deep lunge
- Racquet extended
- Balance maintained
- Push back recovery
- Leg strength

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Physical Factors

**Female players:**
- Excellent fitness
- Court speed
- Technique development
- Injury prevention
- Competition opportunities

Good squash posture creates the foundation for powerful shots and court dominance.
