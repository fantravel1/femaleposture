# Massage Receiving Posture

Receiving massage requires comfortable positioning. Proper posture enhances therapeutic benefit.

## Face-Down Position

### Prone Setup

**On table:**
- Face in cradle
- Neck neutral
- Arms at sides or overhead
- Shoulders relaxed
- Comfortable breathing

### Face Cradle

**Head position:**
- Centered in cradle
- Neck not twisted
- Breathing clear
- Comfortable pressure
- Adjustable height

## Face-Up Position

### Supine Setup

**On back:**
- Head supported
- Neck comfortable
- Arms relaxed
- Legs extended or bolstered
- Full relaxation

### Bolster Use

**Support props:**
- Under knees
- Under ankles
- Neck support
- Comfort enhanced
- Muscle relaxation

## Side-Lying

### Lateral Position

**When used:**
- Pregnancy massage
- Specific work
- Comfort preference
- Pillow support
- Stable position

## During Massage

### Relaxation

**Mental state:**
- Let go of tension
- Deep breathing
- Don't help therapist
- Passive receiving
- Full relaxation

### Communication

**With therapist:**
- Pressure feedback
- Temperature comfort
- Area sensitivity
- Comfort needs
- Open dialogue

## After Massage

### Rising Carefully

**Getting up:**
- Move slowly
- Rise gradually
- Allow adjustment
- Hydrate well
- Enjoy benefits

Good massage receiving posture maximizes relaxation and therapeutic benefit.
