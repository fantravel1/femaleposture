# Squatting Posture

Deep squatting is natural resting position. Proper posture supports hip and ankle mobility.

## Resting Squat

### Deep Squat Position

**Low squat:**
- Feet flat ideally
- Knees track over toes
- Hips below knees
- Upright torso
- Balanced position

### Heel Position

**Foot placement:**
- Flat feet ideal
- Heels down
- Raised heels okay initially
- Gradual flexibility
- Work toward flat

## Body Alignment

### Torso Position

**Upper body:**
- Upright as possible
- Core engaged
- Balanced over feet
- Arms for balance
- Stable position

### Knee Safety

**Joint alignment:**
- Knees over toes
- Track straight
- Don't collapse inward
- Comfortable range
- Respect limits

## Mobility Work

### Building Flexibility

**Improving squat:**
- Ankle flexibility needed
- Hip mobility important
- Gradual improvement
- Regular practice
- Patient progress

### Props Help

**Assistance:**
- Hold support
- Elevated heels initially
- Wall support
- Gradual reduction
- Natural progression

## Functional Use

### Floor Access

**Low reaching:**
- Squat to floor
- Pick up items
- Work at floor level
- Protect back
- Functional movement

### With Children

**Playing low:**
- Meet their level
- Comfortable position
- Easy transitions
- Natural movement
- Practical parenting

Good squatting posture enables comfortable floor access and resting position.
