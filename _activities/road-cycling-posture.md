# Road Cycling Posture

Road cycling balances aerodynamics with comfort. Proper posture enables efficient, sustainable riding.

## Bike Fit

### Saddle Position

**Height and position:**
- Slight knee bend at bottom
- No hip rocking
- Fore/aft position
- Individual fit
- Professional fitting helpful

### Handlebar Reach

**Upper body:**
- Comfortable reach
- Slight elbow bend
- Various hand positions
- No overreaching
- Personal fit

## Riding Position

### Hoods Position

**Common position:**
- Hands on hoods
- Slight bend in elbows
- Back at comfortable angle
- Eyes ahead
- Relaxed riding

### Drops Position

**Aerodynamic:**
- Hands in drops
- Lower position
- More aero
- For speed or headwind
- Practice needed

### Tops Position

**Relaxed option:**
- Hands on bar tops
- Upright riding
- Easier breathing
- Climbing position
- Rest position

## Core Engagement

### Supporting Position

**Core work:**
- Supports upper body
- Reduces hand pressure
- Prevents back fatigue
- Essential engagement
- Sustainable riding

## Pedaling

### Efficient Stroke

**Power delivery:**
- Circles not stomping
- Pull up phase
- Push down phase
- Ankle flex
- Smooth power

## Long Rides

### Endurance Position

**Extended distances:**
- Sustainable position
- Regular position changes
- Hand position variety
- Stretch on bike
- Comfortable effort

Good road cycling posture enables efficient, comfortable long-distance riding.
