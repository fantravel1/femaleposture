# Irish Dance Posture

Irish dance demands rigid upper body with intricate footwork. Proper posture enables authentic style.

## Upper Body

### Traditional Carriage

**Characteristic position:**
- Arms at sides
- Shoulders back
- Chin level
- Core very engaged
- Minimal arm movement

### Torso Position

**Rigid frame:**
- Perfectly vertical
- No lean or sway
- Core extremely engaged
- Upper body still
- Lower body contrast

## Footwork

### Soft Shoe

**Reel and jig:**
- Pointed toes
- Quick feet
- Light landing
- Precise placement
- Musical timing

### Hard Shoe

**Hornpipe and treble:**
- Percussive sounds
- Heel and toe clicks
- Rhythmic patterns
- Clean strikes
- Power from legs

## Leg Position

### Turnout

**Classical position:**
- Legs crossed
- Toes pointed
- Knees straight
- Clean lines
- Precise angles

### Kicks and Hops

**Elevation:**
- High kicks
- Controlled jumps
- Pointed extension
- Core stability
- Clean landing

## Performance

### Stage Presence

**Competition form:**
- Perfect posture
- Eye contact with judges
- Confidence
- Precision
- Endurance

## Training

### Building Skill

**Development:**
- Flexibility work
- Strength training
- Rhythm practice
- Endurance building
- Technical refinement

Good Irish dance posture creates the characteristic contrast of still upper body with quick feet.
