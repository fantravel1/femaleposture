# Makeup Application Posture

Makeup application requires sustained arm position and close mirror work. Proper posture prevents the neck and shoulder strain common to this routine.

## Setup Position

### Mirror Height

**Proper placement:**
- Eye level best
- Not looking down
- Not straining up
- Adjustable if possible
- Good lighting

### Seating

**Comfortable chair:**
- Proper height
- Back support
- Arms at comfortable level
- Feet grounded
- Stable surface

## Body Position

### During Application

**Proper posture:**
- Upright torso
- Mirror at eye level
- Elbows supported if possible
- Shoulders relaxed
- Core engaged

### Arm Position

**Reducing fatigue:**
- Elbow on table when possible
- Alternate hands
- Rest between steps
- Relaxed grip on tools
- Efficient movements

## Eye Makeup

### Close Work

**Detail application:**
- Magnifying mirror if needed
- Good lighting critical
- Stable hand position
- Regular breaks
- Eye rest

### Head Position

**Chin and neck:**
- Neutral neck
- Not tilted back
- Chin level
- Relaxed muscles
- Comfortable viewing

## Quick Makeup

### Standing Application

**Brief touch-ups:**
- Mirror at proper height
- Stable stance
- Arms supported
- Quick and efficient
- Proper lighting

Good makeup posture makes your routine more comfortable and prevents the neck and shoulder pain of poor positioning.
