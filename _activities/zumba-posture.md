# Zumba Posture

Zumba combines dance and fitness in high-energy workouts. Proper posture protects joints while you enjoy the music.

## Basic Stance

### Dance Position

**Starting point:**
- Feet hip-width
- Knees soft
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Ready to move

### Movement Ready

**Athletic posture:**
- Weight balanced
- Quick direction changes
- Arms free
- Eyes on instructor
- Energy present

## Dance Movements

### Latin Steps

**Core movements:**
- Hip movements from core
- Feet patterns
- Arm coordination
- Rhythm following
- Full body engagement

### High Impact Moves

**Jumping movements:**
- Soft landings
- Knee alignment
- Core engagement
- Arms help momentum
- Controlled height

## Technique Principles

### Form Over Speed

**Quality priority:**
- Learn moves correctly
- Don't sacrifice form for speed
- Modify if needed
- Progress gradually
- Safety first

### Following Instructor

**Class participation:**
- Watch and learn
- Do what you can
- Modify as needed
- Have fun
- Your version is fine

## Common Issues

### Knee Strain

**Prevention:**
- Proper footwear
- Track knees over toes
- Soft landings
- Modifications available
- Listen to body

### Overexertion

**Pace yourself:**
- Your fitness level
- Breaks when needed
- Hydration
- Cool down properly
- Progressive challenge

Good Zumba posture lets you enjoy the workout while protecting your body through high-energy movement.
