# Volleyball Posture

Volleyball requires specific body positions for each skillâ€”setting, spiking, digging, serving. Understanding proper volleyball posture improves performance and prevents injury.

## Ready Position

### Base Stance

**Athletic ready:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Knees bent
- Weight forward
- Arms ready
- Core engaged

### Passing Ready

**Platform position:**
- Lower stance
- Arms together
- Shoulders forward
- Eyes on ball
- Ready to move

## Skill-Specific Posture

### Setting

**Hand position:**
- Hands above forehead
- Elbows out
- Balanced stance
- Eyes on ball
- Quick footwork

### Spiking

**Approach and hit:**
- Explosive approach
- Arm swing
- Core rotation
- Jump timing
- Follow through

### Digging

**Low position:**
- Very low stance
- Platform ready
- Quick movement
- Body behind ball
- Controlled contact

### Serving

**Serving form:**
- Balanced stance
- Ball toss consistency
- Arm swing
- Follow through
- Core engagement

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Shoulder Health

**Overhead concerns:**
- Rotator cuff care
- Proper warm-up
- Strength training
- Rest when needed
- Technique focus

### Injury Prevention

**Common issues:**
- Shoulder injuries
- Knee problems
- Ankle sprains
- Finger injuries
- Prevention strategies

Good volleyball posture creates efficient, powerful play while protecting your body from injury.
