# Strength Training Posture

Proper form in strength training isn't optionalâ€”it's essential for results and injury prevention. Understanding alignment principles helps women lift safely and effectively.

## Foundational Positions

### Standing Exercises

**Base position:**
- Feet hip-width
- Knees tracking over toes
- Core engaged
- Spine neutral
- Shoulders set

### Seated Exercises

**Sitting form:**
- Sit bones grounded
- Spine tall
- Core active
- Shoulders back
- Feet flat or braced

## Major Lifts Form

### Squat

**Proper technique:**
- Feet shoulder-width or wider
- Toes slightly out
- Hips back then down
- Knees track over toes
- Chest lifted

### Deadlift

**Key positions:**
- Feet under hips
- Bar close to body
- Neutral spine
- Hinge at hips
- Core braced

### Pressing Movements

**Form essentials:**
- Shoulder blade retraction
- Core stability
- Wrist alignment
- Controlled path
- Full range of motion

### Pulling Movements

**Technique focus:**
- Shoulder blade engagement
- Core active
- Controlled motion
- Full contraction
- Proper grip

## Women-Specific Considerations

### Anatomical Factors

**Female lifting:**
- Wider hips affect stance
- Elbow carrying angle
- Core and pelvic floor
- Shoulder mobility
- Recovery needs

### Common Issues

**Typical corrections:**
- Knee valgus prevention
- Core breathing
- Shoulder position
- Grip variations
- Load progression

## Safe Training Practices

### Form First

**Priority approach:**
- Master technique before load
- Use mirrors and video
- Get qualified coaching
- Progress gradually
- Listen to body

Proper strength training form builds muscle safely while improving overall posture and body mechanics.
