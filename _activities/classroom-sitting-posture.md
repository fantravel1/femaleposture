# Classroom Sitting Posture

Classroom sitting involves extended periods. Proper posture supports learning and comfort.

## Desk Seating

### Chair Position

**At desk:**
- Sit back in chair
- Feet on floor
- Back supported
- Shoulders relaxed
- Alert position

### Desk Height

**Work surface:**
- Comfortable height
- Writing position
- Arms relaxed
- Not too high or low
- Accessible surface

## Lecture Halls

### Auditorium Seating

**Lecture setup:**
- Use full seat
- Back against chair
- Feet grounded
- Upright posture
- Attentive position

### Note Taking

**Writing position:**
- Stable surface
- Comfortable reach
- Good visibility
- Efficient writing
- Alert posture

## Long Classes

### Extended Periods

**Hour+ sessions:**
- Shift positions subtly
- Core engagement
- Micro-movements
- Stay alert
- Comfortable duration

### Break Utilization

**Class breaks:**
- Stand up
- Move around
- Stretch briefly
- Refresh position
- Reset posture

## Technology Use

### Laptop in Class

**Computer use:**
- Screen at good height
- Keyboard accessible
- Eyes comfortable
- Typing position
- Digital note-taking

## Engagement Posture

### Active Learning

**Attentive position:**
- Upright posture
- Forward lean appropriate
- Open to material
- Engaged presence
- Ready to participate

Good classroom sitting posture supports effective learning and physical comfort.
