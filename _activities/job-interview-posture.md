# Job Interview Posture

Job interviews require confident presence. Proper posture projects professionalism and capability.

## First Impressions

### Entering Room

**Initial impression:**
- Walk confidently
- Shoulders back
- Head up
- Smile naturally
- Strong entrance

### Handshake

**Greeting:**
- Firm but not crushing
- Eye contact
- Confident grip
- Professional warmth
- Positive start

## Seated Interview

### Chair Position

**Sitting setup:**
- Sit upright
- Slight forward lean shows engagement
- Feet on floor
- Hands visible
- Open posture

### Body Language

**Non-verbal communication:**
- Open stance
- No crossed arms
- Engaged lean
- Nodding appropriately
- Active listening posture

## Hands and Gestures

### Hand Position

**During interview:**
- Hands visible
- Natural gestures
- Not fidgeting
- Calm confidence
- Professional presence

### Note Taking

**If appropriate:**
- Brief notes acceptable
- Maintain eye contact
- Not constant writing
- Show engagement
- Professional approach

## Eye Contact

### Balanced Contact

**Visual connection:**
- Natural eye contact
- Not staring
- Connect with all interviewers
- Confident gaze
- Genuine engagement

## Video Interviews

### Camera Presence

**Remote interviews:**
- Look at camera
- Good posture on screen
- Proper framing
- Background professional
- Technology prepared

Good interview posture projects confidence and professional capability.
