# Longboarding Posture

Longboarding offers stable cruising and carving. Proper posture enables smooth, controlled riding.

## Basic Stance

### Foot Position

**On board:**
- Feet shoulder-width plus
- Centered on board
- Knees bent
- Weight distributed
- Relaxed upper body

### Cruising Stance

**Comfortable riding:**
- Wider stance for stability
- Lower center of gravity
- Flexible knees
- Core engaged
- Eyes forward

## Pushing

### Efficient Push

**Getting moving:**
- Weight on front foot
- Long push strokes
- Smooth motion
- Balanced recovery
- Sustainable effort

### Switch Pushing

**Alternating feet:**
- Reduces fatigue
- Balanced development
- Longer distances
- Either foot leads
- Practice both

## Carving

### Smooth Turns

**Flowing movement:**
- Weight shift
- Full body lean
- Controlled arcs
- Rhythm development
- Relaxed motion

### Deep Carving

**More aggressive turns:**
- Greater lean
- Edge pressure
- Core stability
- Speed control
- Flowing lines

## Downhill

### Speed Position

**Going fast:**
- Lower stance
- Tucked position
- Weight forward
- Stable core
- Controlled descent

### Foot Braking

**Speed control:**
- Drag foot
- Controlled pressure
- Balance maintained
- Gradual slowing
- Safe technique

Good longboarding posture creates stable, flowing movement on the board.
