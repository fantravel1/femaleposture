# Commuting Posture

Commuting can strain the body, especially when crowded or prolonged. Proper posture during transit protects your body daily.

## Public Transit

### Standing on Train/Bus

**Transit standing:**
- Hold bar at comfortable height
- Feet wide for stability
- Knees soft
- Core engaged
- Balanced stance

### Seated Transit

**If you get a seat:**
- Sit fully back
- Bag on lap or at feet
- Feet grounded
- Core engaged
- Not slumped

## Walking Commute

### With Bag

**Carrying load:**
- Backpack best
- Cross-body second
- Balance loads
- Proper bag weight
- Posture awareness

### Walking Form

**Commute walking:**
- Upright posture
- Natural stride
- Appropriate pace
- Comfortable shoes
- Weather awareness

## Phone Use

### Standing Transit

**Avoiding text neck:**
- Bring phone up
- Not looking down
- Short interactions
- Awareness maintained
- Posture priority

### Seated Transit

**Phone position:**
- Elevated view
- Not hunched
- Arms supported
- Breaks from screen
- Look up regularly

## Heavy Bags

### Laptop Bags

**Work bag carrying:**
- Rolling if heavy
- Backpack if carrying
- Alternate shoulders
- Lighten load
- Core engaged

### Proper Packing

**Bag management:**
- Only necessities
- Weight distributed
- Comfortable straps
- Organized access
- Evaluate regularly

Good commuting posture protects your body through the daily travel that bookends your workday.
