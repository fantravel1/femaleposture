# Getting Dressed Posture

Getting dressed involves balance and bending. Proper posture prevents morning strain.

## Balance for Legs

### One-Leg Standing

**Putting on pants:**
- Hold something
- Wall or chair support
- Alternate legs
- Seated option
- Safe balance

### Seated Dressing

**Safer option:**
- Sit on bed or chair
- Both feet through
- Stand to pull up
- No balance risk
- Easier for some

## Upper Body

### Shirts and Tops

**Overhead dressing:**
- Arms through sleeves
- Head through neck
- Pull down
- Natural motion
- Easy process

### Buttoning

**Fine motor:**
- Stand or sit
- See buttons
- Relaxed hands
- Efficient motion
- Mirror if needed

## Shoes and Socks

### Sock Technique

**Feet access:**
- Sit down preferred
- Cross leg over knee
- Or foot to seat
- Comfortable access
- No strain

### Shoe Tying

**Bending options:**
- Seated is easiest
- Knee up if standing
- Foot on surface
- Comfortable reach
- Avoid back strain

## Bras and Undergarments

### Clasp Technique

**Various methods:**
- Front clasp and turn
- Behind back clasp
- Personal preference
- Shoulder comfort
- Practiced motion

## Efficient Morning

### Streamlined Dressing

**Time management:**
- Clothes prepared
- Efficient order
- Comfortable space
- Good lighting
- Smooth routine

Good dressing posture enables comfortable, efficient morning routines.
