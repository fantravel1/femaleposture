# Prayer Kneeling Posture

Prayer kneeling is spiritual practice. Proper posture supports comfortable, focused devotion.

## Kneeling Position

### Basic Kneeling

**Prayer position:**
- Knees on surface
- Torso upright
- Hands folded or open
- Head bowed or forward
- Centered presence

### Surface Consideration

**Knee comfort:**
- Cushion or kneeler
- Prayer pad
- Carpet softer
- Protect knees
- Comfortable duration

## Hand Positions

### Folded Hands

**Traditional:**
- Palms together
- Fingers pointed up
- Relaxed grip
- Classic position
- Focused prayer

### Open Hands

**Receptive posture:**
- Palms up
- Open receiving
- Relaxed position
- Alternative form
- Personal preference

## Body Alignment

### Upright Torso

**During prayer:**
- Spine straight
- Shoulders relaxed
- Core engaged
- Breathing easy
- Stable position

### Head Position

**Various options:**
- Bowed down
- Eyes closed
- Forward gaze
- Personal practice
- Reverent position

## Extended Prayer

### Long Kneeling

**Duration management:**
- Shift weight subtly
- Rise when needed
- Alternate positions
- Seated prayer option
- Listen to body

## Standing Transition

### Rising from Knees

**After prayer:**
- One foot up first
- Push to standing
- Controlled rise
- Use support if needed
- Careful transition

Good prayer kneeling posture supports meaningful, comfortable devotional practice.
