# Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Posture

Brazilian jiu-jitsu emphasizes ground technique. Proper posture enables effective control and submission.

## Guard Positions

### Closed Guard

**Bottom position:**
- Legs wrapped
- Control posture
- Hips active
- Arm control
- Attack opportunity

### Open Guards

**Variable positions:**
- Spider guard
- Lasso guard
- De la Riva
- Butterfly
- Technical guards

## Passing Guard

### Posture Up

**In guard:**
- Base established
- Posture up tall
- Control grips
- Avoid sweeps
- Passing pressure

### Pressure Passing

**Heavy style:**
- Weight on opponent
- Control hips
- Systematic advance
- Patient progress
- Secure position

## Mount Positions

### Full Mount

**Top control:**
- Hips low
- Weight distributed
- Maintain position
- Attack opportunity
- Control resistance

### Side Control

**Lateral control:**
- Pressure applied
- Control movement
- Transition options
- Submission setup
- Dominant position

## Submissions

### Chokes

**Finishing:**
- Blood or air
- Proper position
- Leverage application
- Controlled finish
- Safety awareness

### Joint Locks

**Submission:**
- Proper angle
- Control limb
- Isolate joint
- Controlled pressure
- Tap respected

## Escapes

### Bottom Work

**Getting out:**
- Bridge and roll
- Hip escape (shrimp)
- Frame creation
- Technical escape
- Never quit

Good BJJ posture enables effective ground control and technique.
