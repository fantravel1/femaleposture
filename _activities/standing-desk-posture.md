# Standing Desk Posture

Standing desks offer seated alternatives. Proper posture ensures benefits without new problems.

## Standing Position

### Feet and Legs

**Base position:**
- Feet hip-width apart
- Weight balanced
- Knees soft
- Not locked
- Slight shift okay

### Spine and Core

**Upright posture:**
- Tall spine
- Core lightly engaged
- Neutral pelvis
- No swayback
- Natural curves

## Desk Setup

### Height Adjustment

**Proper level:**
- Elbows at 90 degrees
- Wrists neutral
- Screen at eye level
- No reaching up or down
- Comfortable position

### Monitor Position

**Screen placement:**
- Eye level
- Arms length distance
- Centered
- Reduce neck strain
- Clear viewing

## Foot Comfort

### Mat and Footwear

**Standing support:**
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Supportive shoes
- No high heels
- Consider footrest
- Comfortable base

### Weight Shifting

**Movement variety:**
- Shift weight
- One foot raised
- Gentle movement
- Not locked position
- Natural motion

## Sit-Stand Balance

### Alternating

**Best practice:**
- Not all standing
- Alternate sitting
- Listen to body
- Variety of positions
- Find your rhythm

## Fatigue Management

### Recognizing Limits

**When to sit:**
- Lower body fatigue
- Back discomfort
- Before overuse
- Honor your body
- Sustainable practice

Good standing desk posture enables comfortable, productive stand-up work.
