# Pet Care Posture Guide

> **Loving Your Pets, Protecting Your Back:** Pet care involves constant bending, lifting, and walking. Good technique keeps you healthy to keep caring.

---

## The Pet Care Challenge

### Daily Physical Demands

- **Feeding** - Bending to floor level bowls
- **Walking** - Leash handling, pulling
- **Grooming** - Bathing, brushing, nail care
- **Lifting** - Into cars, onto tables
- **Cleaning** - Litter boxes, cages, yards
- **Playing** - Active engagement

### Common Problems

- Lower back pain from bending
- Shoulder strain from leash pulling
- Wrist issues from grooming
- Knee strain from kneeling

---

## Feeding

### Floor-Level Bowls

**The bending trap:**
- Multiple times daily
- Quick bends add up
- Easy to do poorly

**Better approach:**
- Squat down properly
- Or lunge to one knee
- Keep back straight
- Don't just round over

### Elevated Feeders

**Consider:**
- Raised bowl stands
- Reduces bending
- Good for large dogs anyway
- Worth the investment

---

## Dog Walking

### Leash Handling

**Common issues:**
- Arm pulled forward
- Shoulder strain
- Twisted posture
- Constant tension

**Better approach:**
- Core engaged
- Arm close to body
- Switch hands periodically
- Train dog to not pull (helps everyone)

### Pulling Dogs

**When dog pulls:**
- Don't lean back fighting
- Stop walking until slack
- Use proper training
- Consider no-pull harness

### Multiple Dogs

**Added complexity:**
- Balance forces
- Both hands engaged
- Consider couplers
- Training essential

---

## Grooming

### Bathing

**Challenging positions:**
- Leaning over tub
- Kneeling on hard floor
- Reaching into tub
- Wet, squirming pet

**Better approach:**
- Raised tub if possible
- Kneeling pad essential
- Get pet close before scrubbing
- Handheld sprayer helps

### Brushing

**Sustained activity:**
- Arm fatigue
- Leaning over pet
- Repetitive motion

**Better approach:**
- Pet at comfortable height (table for small, floor with cushion for large)
- Switch hands
- Take breaks
- Comfortable position for you

### Nail Trimming

**Difficult task:**
- Close work
- Fine motor control
- Squirming pet
- Tense positions

**Better approach:**
- Good lighting
- Pet secured (helper or restraint)
- Comfortable position before starting
- Professional grooming option

---

## Lifting Pets

### Into Cars

**Common strain point:**
- Heavy or wiggly pet
- Awkward angle
- Repeated motion

**Better approach:**
- Pet close to body
- Lift with legs
- Ramp for large dogs
- Teach pet to jump if able

### Onto Tables

**Vet visits, grooming:**
- Get close
- Squat down first
- Lift in stages
- Don't extend arms

### General Lifting

**Principles:**
- Close to body
- Legs do work
- Know your limits
- Get help for large dogs

---

## Cleaning

### Litter Boxes

**Bending and scooping:**
- Daily task
- Repeated bending
- Awkward positions

**Better approach:**
- Squat or kneel
- Raised litter box option
- Long-handled scoop
- Self-cleaning boxes reduce frequency

### Cage Cleaning

**Reaching into cages:**
- Awkward access
- Sustained positions
- Small spaces

**Approach:**
- Remove what you can to clean
- Kneel appropriately
- Take breaks
- Proper tools

### Yard Cleanup

**Bending for waste:**
- Repeated bending
- Can add up

**Approach:**
- Long-handled scooper
- Squat when picking up
- Break into sessions

---

## Playing

### Active Play

**Physical demands:**
- Quick movements
- Bending for toys
- Chasing
- Varied positions

**Approach:**
- Warm up before active play
- Good footwear outside
- Proper throw technique
- Know your limits

### Floor Play

**Cats especially:**
- Time on floor
- Reaching under furniture
- Varied positions

**Approach:**
- Cushioned surface
- Position variety
- Don't overdo uncomfortable positions
- Toys on strings reduce reaching

---

## Small Animal Care

### Cages and Aquariums

**Maintenance:**
- Reaching in
- Lifting tanks/habitats
- Water changes
- Cleaning

**Approach:**
- Comfortable height for cage
- Proper support for lifting
- Break tasks into steps
- Appropriate equipment

---

## Senior Pets

### Increased Care Needs

**May require:**
- More lifting
- Mobility assistance
- Medical care
- Extended floor time

**Protecting yourself:**
- Harnesses for mobility support
- Ramps and stairs
- Raising pet's items
- Professional help when needed

---

## Quick Tips

1. **Squat, don't bend** for floor-level tasks
2. **Raise feeding bowls** when possible
3. **Train walking manners** to reduce pulling
4. **Use ramps** for car access with large dogs
5. **Knee pads** for floor grooming
6. **Take breaks** during extended care
7. **Know your lifting limits**
