# Aikido Posture

Aikido uses circular movement to redirect force. Proper posture enables effective technique without strength.

## Basic Stance

### Hanmi

**Half-facing stance:**
- Triangular foot position
- Knees soft
- Weight centered
- Core engaged
- Ready to move

### Centered Posture

**Ki alignment:**
- Spine aligned
- One point (center)
- Relaxed power
- Weight underside
- Stable yet mobile

## Movement Principles

### Tai Sabaki

**Body movement:**
- Pivoting motion
- Off the line
- Circular path
- Blending with energy
- Never opposing force

### Irimi and Tenkan

**Entry and turn:**
- Enter directly (irimi)
- Turn and redirect (tenkan)
- Blend with attack
- Control encounter
- Lead to throw

## Techniques

### Pins

**Controlling:**
- Joint manipulation
- Prone control
- Submission holds
- Control without harm
- Ending position

### Throws

**Projection:**
- Redirect momentum
- Break balance
- Project outward
- Controlled fall
- Partner safety

## Ukemi

### Receiving Technique

**Taking falls:**
- Roll safely
- Forward and backward
- High falls
- Protect body
- Essential skill

## Philosophy

### Non-Resistance

**Principle:**
- Don't oppose force
- Blend and redirect
- Protect attacker too
- Harmonious resolution
- Martial peace

Good aikido posture enables effective technique through centeredness and movement.
