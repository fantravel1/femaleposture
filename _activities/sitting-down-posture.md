# Sitting Down Posture

Sitting down requires controlled lowering. Proper posture supports safe, graceful descent.

## To Chair

### Controlled Descent

**Sitting technique:**
- Feel chair behind
- Hips back
- Lower controlled
- Core engaged
- Smooth landing

### Not Plopping

**Avoid:**
- Falling into seat
- Uncontrolled drop
- Back impact
- Jarring motion
- Poor technique

## To Low Surfaces

### Lower Seating

**Couch or floor:**
- More control needed
- Legs do work
- Gradual lowering
- Hand support okay
- Safe descent

### To Floor

**Ground level:**
- Controlled squat
- Or kneeling first
- Gradual lowering
- Safe technique
- Comfortable positioning

## Muscle Use

### Leg Engagement

**During descent:**
- Quadriceps work
- Core engaged
- Controlled motion
- Good exercise
- Functional strength

## Graceful Movement

### Confident Sitting

**Poised descent:**
- Smooth motion
- Confident control
- Elegant transition
- Practiced technique
- Natural movement

## Safety

### Secure Seating

**Before sitting:**
- Know chair is there
- Stable seat
- Proper position
- Safe descent
- Confident sitting

Good sitting down posture supports graceful, controlled transitions to seated.
