# Paddle Tennis Posture

Paddle tennis (padel) combines tennis with wall play. Proper posture enables effective play.

## Ready Position

### Court Position

**Basic stance:**
- Net or back court
- Knees bent
- Paddle ready
- Balanced position
- Movement ready

### Doubles Positioning

**Partner play:**
- Side by side
- Court coverage
- Communication
- Team positioning
- Coordinated play

## Strokes

### Groundstrokes

**Basic shots:**
- Compact swing
- Underspin common
- Control focus
- Solid contact
- Consistent play

### Volleys

**Net play:**
- Paddle out front
- Punch motion
- Quick exchanges
- Reflexes
- Net control

## Wall Play

### Using Walls

**Unique aspect:**
- Ball off walls
- Play continues
- Strategic use
- Angles matter
- Court awareness

### Defense from Walls

**Retrieving:**
- Reading bounces
- Positioning
- Wall experience
- Extended rallies
- Unique skill

## Serving

### Underhand Serve

**Required:**
- Bounce first
- Underhand motion
- Consistent placement
- Strategic variety
- Start each point

## Movement

### Court Coverage

**Partner coordination:**
- Shift together
- Cover gaps
- Quick movements
- Wall retrieval
- Team movement

Good paddle tennis posture enables effective wall sport play.
