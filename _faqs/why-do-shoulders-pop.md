# Why Do My Shoulders Pop and Crack?

**Quick Answer:** Shoulder popping is usually caused by gas bubbles releasing in joint fluid, tendons snapping over bones, or ligaments/cartilage moving. It's often harmless unless accompanied by pain or dysfunction.

## Common Causes

**Cavitation (gas release):**
- Synovial fluid contains dissolved gas
- Movement creates pop
- Harmless
- Most common cause

**Tendon movement:**
- Tendons snap over bony structures
- Creates clicking sound
- Common with tight shoulders
- Usually harmless

**Labrum issues:**
- Cartilage in socket
- Can catch and click
- May indicate problem
- See professional if painful

## When It's Normal

**Harmless popping:**
- Occasional occurrence
- No pain
- No swelling
- Full movement preserved
- No weakness

## When to Worry

**See a doctor if:**
- Pain with popping
- Grinding sensation
- Swelling present
- Weakness
- Catching or locking
- Getting worse

## Reducing Popping

**May help:**
- Shoulder mobility exercises
- Strengthening rotator cuff
- Stretching chest and shoulders
- Improving posture
- Warming up before activity
