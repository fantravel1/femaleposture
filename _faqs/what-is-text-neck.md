# What Is Text Neck?

**Quick Answer:** Text neck is a modern term for neck pain and postural damage caused by repeatedly looking down at phones, tablets, and other devices. It affects posture, causes pain, and can lead to long-term problems.

## Symptoms of Text Neck

**Common signs:**
- Neck pain and stiffness
- Shoulder pain
- Headaches (especially at skull base)
- Upper back pain
- Reduced neck mobility
- Forward head posture

## Why It Happens

**The problem:**
- Average person looks at phone 2-4 hours daily
- Each look tilts head forward
- 60+ lbs of force on neck at 60Â° angle
- Cumulative strain damages structures

## Who Gets It

- Teens and young adults (highest)
- Office workers
- Anyone with heavy phone use
- Increasing across all ages

## How to Prevent Text Neck

**Immediate fixes:**
- Raise phone to eye level
- Take breaks every 20-30 minutes
- Set reminders to check posture

**Exercises:**
- Chin tucks
- Neck stretches
- Upper back strengthening

**Lifestyle:**
- Reduce unnecessary phone time
- Use voice commands
- Make calls instead of texting

Prevention is easier than treatment.
