# Can Posture Affect Pelvic Floor Function?

**Quick Answer:** Yes, posture significantly affects pelvic floor function. Your alignment determines how well these muscles can engage, support, and relax. Poor posture can contribute to pelvic floor weakness or tension.

## The Connection

**How posture affects pelvic floor:**
- Pelvic position changes muscle length
- Diaphragm and pelvic floor work together
- Slouching increases abdominal pressure
- Alignment affects engagement ability
- Breathing patterns interconnected

## Problematic Posture Patterns

**Issues that affect pelvic floor:**
- Chronic tailbone tucking
- Constant ab gripping
- Slouched sitting
- Breath holding patterns
- Rib thrust posture

## Signs of Pelvic Floor Issues

**May indicate problems:**
- Leaking with cough/sneeze/jump
- Urgency or frequency
- Pelvic pain
- Difficulty fully emptying bladder
- Pelvic heaviness

## Posture for Pelvic Floor Health

**Optimal alignment:**
- Neutral pelvis (not tucked or tilted)
- Stacked ribs over pelvis
- Relaxed abdomen
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Avoiding constant gripping

## Professional Help

Pelvic floor issues often need specialized assessment. A pelvic floor physical therapist can evaluate both your alignment and pelvic floor function together.
