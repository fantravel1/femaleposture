# How Can I Improve My Child's Posture?

**Quick Answer:** Encourage regular movement and active play, set up proper homework stations, limit screen time with tech breaks, and model good posture yourself. Avoid naggingâ€”make it fun and focus on strengthening through activity.

## Key Strategies

**What works:**
- Active play (climbing, sports, dance)
- Proper desk/table height
- Screen time limits
- Backpack weight under 10-15% body weight
- Parent modeling good posture

## Screen Time Setup

**For homework/devices:**
- Screen at eye level
- Feet touching floor or stool
- Back supported
- Regular breaks (every 20-30 min)
- Timer reminders

## Backpack Guidelines

**To protect spine:**
- Both straps always
- Weight under 15% of body weight
- Heaviest items close to back
- Padded straps
- Waist belt for heavy loads

## Red Flags

**See a doctor if:**
- Visible spinal curve
- Uneven shoulders/hips
- Pain with sitting or standing
- Refuses activities due to discomfort
- Posture dramatically worse than peers

## What Doesn't Work

- Constantly nagging
- Rigid posture rules
- Fear-based messaging
- Restricting all devices
- Expecting perfect posture

Focus on overall activity levels and make healthy habits normal, not special.
