# How Should I Sit at a Computer Ergonomically?

**Quick Answer:** Sit with your monitor at eye level and arm's length away, feet flat on the floor, back supported, and elbows at 90 degrees when typing. Take breaks every 30-60 minutes.

## Monitor Position

**Optimal setup:**
- Top of screen at eye level
- Arm's length distance (20-26 inches)
- Directly in front (not angled)
- No glare on screen
- Laptop users: use a stand + external keyboard

## Chair Adjustment

**Key settings:**
- Feet flat on floor or footrest
- Thighs parallel to floor
- Lumbar support in lower back curve
- Armrests at elbow height
- Seat depth allows 2-3 fingers behind knee

## Keyboard and Mouse

**Positioning:**
- Elbows at 90 degrees
- Wrists neutral (not bent)
- Keyboard and mouse at same level
- Keep frequently used items close
- Consider ergonomic keyboard/mouse

## Common Mistakes

**Avoid:**
- Screen too low (looking down)
- Keyboard too high (shoulders raised)
- Sitting on edge of chair
- Crossing legs
- Leaning to one side

## Additional Tips

- Take breaks every 30-60 minutes
- Stand and stretch regularly
- Consider sit-stand desk
- Use 20-20-20 rule for eyes
- Move throughout the day
