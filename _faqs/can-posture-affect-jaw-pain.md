# Can Poor Posture Cause Jaw Pain (TMJ)?

**Quick Answer:** Yes, forward head posture affects the jaw. It changes how your jaw closes, creates muscle tension in the face and neck, and can contribute to TMJ pain, clicking, and dysfunction.

## The Posture-Jaw Connection

**How posture affects TMJ:**
- Forward head changes jaw resting position
- Altered bite alignment
- Increased muscle tension
- Neck muscles connect to jaw
- Compensatory patterns develop

## Posture Issues That Affect Jaw

**Common patterns:**
- Forward head posture
- Rounded shoulders
- Neck tension
- Upper crossed syndrome

## TMJ Symptoms Linked to Posture

**You may notice:**
- Jaw pain or tenderness
- Clicking or popping
- Headaches
- Earaches
- Neck pain alongside jaw symptoms
- Facial muscle tension

## Improvement Approach

**What helps:**
- Correct forward head posture
- Neck and shoulder exercises
- Stress management
- Jaw muscle awareness
- Ergonomic improvements

## Important Considerations

TMJ has multiple causes including teeth grinding, stress, arthritis, and injury. Posture is one factor. If you have significant TMJ symptoms, see a dentist or TMJ specialist for comprehensive evaluation.
