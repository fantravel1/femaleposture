# Is Lying on the Floor Good for Your Back?

**Quick Answer:** Lying flat on the floor can help decompress your spine, relieve muscle tension, and reduce back pain for many people. The firm, flat surface provides different support than mattresses. However, it's not ideal for everyone.

## Benefits

**Why it can help:**
- Firm support for spine
- Decompression of vertebrae
- Muscle relaxation
- Neutral spine position
- Reduces excess curves

## How to Do It

**Best approach:**
- Lie on your back
- Knees bent, feet flat (or legs extended)
- Arms at sides or overhead
- Small pillow under head if needed
- 5-15 minutes

## Who It Helps

**Good for:**
- Those with tight lower backs
- After prolonged sitting
- Muscle tension relief
- Gentle stretching
- Resetting posture

## Who Should Avoid

**Not recommended if:**
- Severe disc issues
- Osteoporosis
- Difficulty getting up/down
- Causes pain
- Sacroiliac joint problems

## Alternatives

**If floor is too hard:**
- Yoga mat or carpet
- Thin mattress pad
- Firm mattress
- Gradual progression to floor
