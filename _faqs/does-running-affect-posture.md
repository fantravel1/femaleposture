# Does Running Affect Your Posture?

**Quick Answer:** Running can improve posture by strengthening core and back musclesâ€”but only with good form. Poor running posture (hunching, looking down) can reinforce bad habits and cause pain.

## Good Running Posture

**Proper form:**
- Tall spine, slight forward lean from ankles
- Eyes looking ahead (not down)
- Shoulders relaxed and back
- Arms swinging naturally
- Core engaged

## How Running Helps Posture

**Benefits:**
- Strengthens core muscles
- Builds hip and glute strength
- Improves body awareness
- Increases overall fitness
- Promotes upright carriage

## How Running Can Hurt Posture

**Problems from poor form:**
- Hunched shoulders from fatigue
- Forward head from looking at ground
- Overstriding causing hip tightness
- Muscle imbalances
- Compensating for weaknesses

## Tips for Better Form

**During runs:**
- Check in with posture periodically
- Run tall, imagine string pulling head up
- Relax shoulders down from ears
- Short, quick strides
- Land midfoot under body

## Complement Running With

- Core strengthening
- Hip flexor stretching
- Upper back exercises
- Foam rolling
- Cross-training variety
