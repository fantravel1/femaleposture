# At-Home Posture Exercises FAQ

> **Your Home Gym:** You don't need a gym or expensive equipment for effective posture work. Here's how to set up successful home practice.

---

## Getting Started

### Can I really improve posture at home?

**Yes:**
- Most exercises need no equipment
- Consistency matters more than location
- Home practice is accessible and sustainable

### What equipment do I need?

**Essential:** Nothing (many bodyweight exercises)

**Helpful:**
- Yoga mat
- Resistance band
- Foam roller

**Nice to have:**
- Light weights
- Stability ball
- Pull-up bar

### How much space do I need?

**Minimal:**
- Body-length space for lying down
- Arm's reach in all directions
- Clear wall for wall exercises

---

## Exercise Questions

### Best no-equipment exercises?

1. Chin tucks
2. Wall angels
3. Cat-cow
4. Bird-dog
5. Glute bridges
6. Planks
7. Doorway stretch

### What if I have no wall space?

**Alternatives:**
- Skip wall exercises temporarily
- Use door frame for stretches
- Modify positions
- Still plenty of floor exercises

### Can I use household items?

**Creative substitutions:**
- Towel = resistance band (some exercises)
- Books = weights
- Couch = bench for bridges
- Door frame = stretch station

---

## Routine Questions

### How do I structure home practice?

**Simple approach:**
- Morning: 5-10 min mobility/stretch
- Throughout day: Posture check-ins
- Evening: 5-10 min stretching
- 2-3x week: Strengthening (15-20 min)

### How do I stay motivated at home?

- Designated space for practice
- Set specific times
- Track your practice
- Notice how you feel
- Make it routine

---

## Space and Setup

### Setting up your area:

- Keep mat accessible (visible)
- Space clear and ready
- Timer or phone for timing
- Comfortable temperature

---

## Common Challenges

### I get distracted at home

- Designate practice time
- Remove distractions
- Keep it short
- Same time daily

### I don't know if I'm doing exercises right

- Use mirrors
- Record yourself
- Start with simple exercises
- Consider online classes or videos
