# How Does Posture Affect Breathing?

**Quick Answer:** Posture significantly affects breathing capacity. Slouched posture can reduce lung function by up to 30% by compressing the chest cavity and restricting diaphragm movement. Upright posture allows full, efficient breathing.

## The Connection

**How posture affects breath:**
- Rib cage expansion limited when hunched
- Diaphragm can't descend fully
- Abdominal compression restricts movement
- Accessory muscles compensate (inefficient)
- Shallow chest breathing results

## Effects of Poor Posture

**Breathing problems:**
- Reduced oxygen intake
- Faster breathing rate
- Accessory muscle overuse
- Increased CO2 retention
- Fatigue from inefficiency

## Effects of Good Posture

**Breathing benefits:**
- Full lung expansion
- Diaphragm moves freely
- Efficient oxygen exchange
- Slower, deeper breaths
- More energy, less effort

## Who Is Most Affected

**Especially important for:**
- Those with asthma
- Athletes
- Singers/wind musicians
- Anxiety sufferers
- Sleep apnea patients
- COPD patients

## Improvement Tips

**To breathe better:**
- Sit and stand upright
- Open chest, shoulders back
- Practice diaphragmatic breathing
- Take movement breaks
- Strengthen postural muscles
