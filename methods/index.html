Posture Treatment Methods

Evidence-based approaches to improve your posture. From hands-on therapies to mind-body practices, find the method that fits your needs, lifestyle, and goals.

Medical & Rehabilitation Approaches

Professional treatments for posture correction and pain management.

Physical Therapy

Evidence-based rehabilitation with exercise, manual therapy, and education. The gold standard for posture correction.

Pelvic Floor PT

Specialized therapy addressing the core-posture-pelvic floor connection. Essential for pregnancy, postpartum, and beyond.

Chiropractic Care

Spinal manipulation and adjustment for posture-related pain. What women should know about choosing a chiropractor.

Osteopathy

Whole-body manual medicine viewing structure and function as interconnected. A holistic approach to posture.

Movement Re-Education

Somatic practices that improve posture through awareness and movement quality.

Alexander Technique

125-year-old method for releasing habitual tension. Learn to "allow" good posture rather than force it.

Feldenkrais Method

Neuroplasticity-based movement lessons. Gentle, effective approach for chronic pain and complex posture issues.

Yoga Therapy

Individualized therapeutic yoga for posture correction. Beyond group classes—customized to your needs.

Clinical Pilates

Rehabilitative Pilates for posture conditions. Equipment and mat approaches with therapeutic progression.

How to Choose a Method

For Pain and Injury

Start with Physical Therapy for evidence-based rehabilitation. Consider chiropractic or osteopathy for additional manual care.

For Pregnancy and Postpartum

Pelvic Floor PT is essential. Yoga therapy and Feldenkrais offer gentle, adaptable options.

For Chronic Issues That Haven't Responded to Exercise

Try Alexander Technique or Feldenkrais for a different approach. Mind-body integration may address root causes.

For Deep Structural Change

Consider Rolfing for comprehensive fascia work. Combine with movement education for lasting results.

For Maintenance and Prevention

Massage, yoga therapy, and clinical Pilates support ongoing posture health.

Combining Methods

Many women get best results from combining approaches:

  • PT + Pilates: Rehabilitation plus ongoing strength
  • Chiropractic + PT: Manual care plus active exercise
  • Massage + Feldenkrais: Tissue release plus awareness
  • Pelvic Floor PT + Yoga Therapy: Core restoration plus whole-body approach

Work with your practitioners to coordinate care for best outcomes.
