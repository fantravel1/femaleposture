# Trust and the Body

Trust is felt in the body before it's thought in the mind. The relaxation that comes with safety, the tension that signals dangerâ€”your body is constantly assessing trustworthiness. Learning to read and build physical trust strengthens this essential quality.

## Physical Experience of Trust

### When You Trust

**What it feels like:**
- Relaxed muscles
- Open posture
- Easy breathing
- Settled nervous system
- Calm internal state

### When Trust Is Absent

**What it feels like:**
- Muscle tension
- Guarded posture
- Shallow breathing
- Alert nervous system
- Vigilant state

## Body's Trust Assessment

### Intuitive Knowing

**Physical signals:**
- Gut feelings about people
- Comfort or discomfort
- Attraction or repulsion
- Ease or tension
- Body wisdom

### Reading Others

**Physical cues:**
- Congruent body language
- Relaxed presence
- Open posture
- Consistent signals
- Trustworthy appearance

## Building Physical Trust

### With Self

**Self-trust practices:**
- Honoring body signals
- Following intuition
- Keeping promises to self
- Body kindness
- Self-reliability

### With Others

**Interpersonal trust:**
- Gradual exposure
- Small tests first
- Physical proximity
- Shared experiences
- Building over time

## Trust After Betrayal

### Physical Recovery

**Healing:**
- Nervous system regulation
- Safety establishment
- Gradual re-opening
- Protective boundaries maintained
- Slow trust rebuilding

### When to Trust Again

**Body readiness:**
- Nervous system settling
- Curiosity returning
- Openness emerging
- Willingness to risk
- Body signals safety

## Maintaining Trust

### Daily Practices

**Supporting trust:**
- Self-trust cultivation
- Healthy relationship boundaries
- Intuition honoring
- Safety prioritizing
- Honest communication

### Trust Environment

**Physical support:**
- Safe spaces
- Reliable routines
- Consistent relationships
- Predictable environments
- Security foundations

Trust is the body's assessment of safety. By learning to read physical signals accurately and build trustworthiness gradually, you develop more secure and satisfying relationships.
