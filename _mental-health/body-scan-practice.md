# Body Scan Practice

The body scan is a foundational practice for developing body awareness. By systematically moving attention through your body, you build the capacity to notice sensations, release tension, and connect with your physical self.

## What Is a Body Scan?

### The Practice

**Basic approach:**
- Systematic attention through body
- Noticing sensations without judgment
- Moving from part to part
- Neither changing nor ignoring
- Present-moment physical awareness

### Benefits

**What body scanning provides:**
- Increased body awareness
- Tension identification and release
- Improved mind-body connection
- Relaxation response
- Emotional awareness through body

## How to Practice

### Getting Started

**Preparation:**
- Find comfortable position
- Minimize interruptions
- Allow adequate time
- Set gentle intention
- Begin when settled

### The Sequence

**Moving through body:**
- Start with feet or head
- Move systematically
- Spend time with each area
- Notice without changing
- Progress through whole body

## Detailed Body Scan Guide

### Lower Body

**Feet and legs:**
- Toes, soles, tops of feet
- Ankles, calves, shins
- Knees, thighs, hips
- Notice temperature, pressure, sensation
- Accept whatever you find

### Torso

**Core areas:**
- Lower back and belly
- Middle back and chest
- Upper back and shoulders
- Notice breath, tension, feeling
- Allow without changing

### Upper Body

**Arms and head:**
- Hands, wrists, forearms
- Upper arms, shoulders
- Neck, jaw, face
- Scalp and crown
- Complete with whole body awareness

## Variations

### Quick Body Scan

**Brief check-in:**
- 2-3 minute version
- Hit major areas only
- Good for regular practice
- Useful before activities
- Building habit

### Deep Body Scan

**Extended practice:**
- 30-45 minutes
- Very detailed attention
- Subtle sensation awareness
- Deep relaxation
- Occasional practice

## Working with Difficulty

### When It's Hard

**Common challenges:**
- Mind wandering (normal, return)
- Falling asleep (adjust position)
- Uncomfortable areas (approach gently)
- Impatience (practice patience)
- No sensation (that's information)

Regular body scan practice transforms your relationship with your body, building awareness that carries into daily life.
