# Menstrual Cycle and Posture

> **Your Body Changes Week to Week:** Hormonal fluctuations affect everything—including how your body feels, moves, and holds itself. Understanding these changes helps you adapt.

---

## The Cycle Overview

### Four Phases

**Menstrual (Days 1-5):**
- Period bleeding
- Hormone levels low
- Often lowest energy

**Follicular (Days 6-14):**
- Building toward ovulation
- Estrogen rising
- Often higher energy

**Ovulatory (Around Day 14):**
- Egg release
- Estrogen peaks
- May feel strongest

**Luteal (Days 15-28):**
- After ovulation
- Progesterone rises
- PMS symptoms possible

*Individual cycles vary—this is approximate*

---

## Menstrual Phase

### What Happens

**Physical experience:**
- Bleeding
- Often fatigue
- Possible cramps
- Lower back pain common
- May want to curl up

### Posture Impacts

**Common patterns:**
- Protective curling
- Lower back discomfort
- Less energy for good posture
- Want to rest

### What Helps

**For cramps:**
- Heat on lower back/abdomen
- Gentle stretching
- Child's pose
- Cat-cow for back

**For fatigue:**
- Gentle expectations
- Restorative positions
- Support where needed
- Rest when possible

**Movement:**
- Light activity if it feels good
- Nothing too demanding
- Walking, gentle yoga
- Listen to your body

---

## Follicular Phase

### What Happens

**Physical experience:**
- Energy often returns
- Feeling stronger
- Estrogen building
- May feel good

### Posture Impacts

**Common patterns:**
- More energy for posture awareness
- Can handle more challenge
- Body feels more responsive

### What Helps

**Take advantage:**
- Strength training
- Challenging workouts
- Building good habits
- Posture work

**Still important:**
- Proper form
- Not overdoing it
- Balance

---

## Ovulatory Phase

### What Happens

**Physical experience:**
- Hormone peak
- Often highest energy
- May feel strongest
- Mid-cycle

### Posture Impacts

**Common patterns:**
- Peak performance potential
- Body feels capable
- Higher tolerance

### What Helps

**Opportunities:**
- More demanding exercise
- Pushing limits safely
- Peak training
- Feeling confident

**Considerations:**
- Some report joint laxity
- Stay mindful
- Good form still matters

---

## Luteal Phase

### What Happens

**Physical experience:**
- Progesterone rises
- May feel changes in later days
- PMS possible
- Varies greatly by individual

**Early luteal (Days 15-21):**
- Often still feeling okay
- Gradual shifts

**Late luteal (Days 22-28):**
- PMS symptoms possible
- Bloating, mood changes
- Fatigue may increase
- Breast tenderness

### Posture Impacts

**Common patterns:**
- Bloating affects comfort
- Breast tenderness affects position
- Fatigue reduces tolerance
- Mood affects body

### What Helps

**For bloating:**
- Comfortable clothes
- Adjusted positioning
- Gentle movement

**For breast tenderness:**
- Supportive bra
- Position adjustments
- Careful with certain exercises

**For fatigue:**
- Adjust expectations
- Gentler movement
- Self-compassion

**For mood:**
- Movement can help
- Don't force intensity
- Stress management

---

## Common Cycle-Related Pain

### Lower Back Pain

**Many experience:**
- Before period
- During period
- Related to uterine position
- Muscle tension

**What helps:**
- Heat
- Gentle stretching
- Cat-cow
- Child's pose
- Supported positions

### Pelvic Pain

**May experience:**
- Cramping
- General discomfort
- Varies by person

**What helps:**
- Supported rest
- Gentle movement
- Heat
- If severe, see provider

## Adapting Exercise

### Phase-Based Training

**Menstrual:**
- Lower intensity
- Yoga, walking
- Rest if needed
- No guilt

**Follicular:**
- Building intensity
- Strength training
- Higher energy work

**Ovulatory:**
- Peak performance
- High intensity okay
- Challenging workouts

**Luteal:**
- Early: Still strong
- Late: Scale back as needed
- Listen to body

### Flexibility

**Be adaptable:**
- Not rigid programming
- Tune into body
- Adjust daily
- Cycles vary

## Tracking Your Patterns

### What to Notice

**Throughout month:**
- Energy levels
- Pain or discomfort
- Sleep quality
- Mood
- Posture ease/difficulty

### Using Information

**Pattern recognition:**
- When are you most challenged?
- When can you do more?
- What helps during low points?
- Adapt proactively

---

## When It's More Than Normal

### See Provider If

- Severe pain
- Pain that prevents function
- Major energy issues
- Irregular cycles
- Symptoms worsening

### Conditions to Rule Out

- Endometriosis
- PCOS
- Fibroids
- Other hormonal issues

---

## Self-Compassion

### Your Body Is Not a Machine

**Remember:**
- Fluctuation is normal
- Not weak for adapting
- Working with your body, not against
- Each phase has value

### Guilt-Free Adaptation

- Scaling back when needed isn't failure
- Listening to your body is wisdom
- Cycles are natural
- Not every day is peak performance
