# Stillness and the Body

Stillness is not absence of movementâ€”it's a quality of presence. True physical stillness is alive, alert, settled. In a world of constant motion, learning to be still in your body is a profound practice.

## Physical Experience of Stillness

### What Stillness Feels Like

**In the body:**
- Complete settling
- Quiet alertness
- Relaxed attention
- Deep peace
- Present awareness

### Stillness Posture

**Physical expression:**
- Settled, stable position
- Relaxed muscles
- Easy breathing
- Balanced weight
- Contained energy

## Cultivating Physical Stillness

### Finding Stillness

**Basic practice:**
- Comfortable position
- Permission to be still
- Body settling
- Breath awareness
- Present-moment focus

### Deepening Stillness

**Going deeper:**
- Longer duration
- Subtler awareness
- Less movement
- Greater settling
- Profound quiet

## Stillness Practices

### Seated Stillness

**Sitting practice:**
- Comfortable seat
- Aligned spine
- Relaxed limbs
- Natural breath
- Sustained stillness

### Lying Stillness

**Horizontal practice:**
- Comfortable surface
- Full body support
- Complete relaxation
- Awake awareness
- Deep settling

### Standing Stillness

**Upright practice:**
- Grounded feet
- Balanced stance
- Relaxed muscles
- Alert presence
- Still standing

## Challenges to Stillness

### Physical Restlessness

**Working with agitation:**
- Acknowledge restlessness
- Don't fight it
- Breathe through it
- Allow gradual settling
- Patience with process

### Discomfort

**When body protests:**
- Adjust mindfully
- Find sustainable position
- Use supports
- Distinguish necessary from avoidance
- Care for body needs

## Benefits of Stillness

### Physical Benefits

**Body effects:**
- Nervous system regulation
- Muscle relaxation
- Blood pressure reduction
- Stress hormone decrease
- Physical restoration

### Mental-Physical

**Integrated benefits:**
- Mental clarity
- Emotional settling
- Presence deepening
- Awareness expanding
- Peace increasing

Stillness practiced regularly becomes a resource available anytime. Your body learns to settle quickly, providing refuge from life's constant motion.
