# Centering and Posture

Centering is the practice of coming home to yourself physicallyâ€”finding your core, your stability, your grounded presence. When life pulls you in many directions, centering returns you to your own axis.

## What Is Physical Centering?

### Finding Your Center

**Physical centering means:**
- Awareness of your body's core
- Weight distributed evenly
- Connected to ground below
- Breathing from center
- Present in your body

### When to Center

**Helpful situations:**
- Before challenging situations
- When feeling scattered
- After emotional upset
- During stress
- For decision-making clarity

## Centering Practices

### Standing Centering

**Basic practice:**
- Stand with feet hip-width apart
- Feel weight equally distributed
- Sense your physical center
- Breathe into belly
- Relax shoulders, lengthen spine

### Seated Centering

**In a chair:**
- Sit with both feet on floor
- Feel sitting bones on seat
- Stack spine upright
- Breathe into core
- Soften face and jaw

### Movement Centering

**Dynamic centering:**
- Move from center of body
- Let limbs follow core
- Stay connected to ground
- Maintain breath awareness
- Flow with stability

## Deepening Center Connection

### Core Awareness

**Developing felt sense:**
- Place hand on belly
- Breathe into hand
- Sense internal space
- Feel core as home
- Return attention here

### Expansion from Center

**Radiating outward:**
- Feel center first
- Extend awareness outward
- Stay connected to core
- Move from this place
- Speak from center

## Centering in Daily Life

### Morning Centering

**Starting centered:**
- Brief centering before rising
- Establish connection to core
- Set centered intention
- Carry awareness forward
- Return when lost

### Throughout Day

**Maintaining center:**
- Regular check-ins
- Brief centering pauses
- Returning when scattered
- Centering before transitions
- Evening reflection

## Centering in Challenges

### When Pulled Off Center

**Recovery practice:**
- Notice you've lost center
- Pause without judgment
- Ground through feet
- Breathe into core
- Reestablish center

Centering becomes more accessible with practice. What initially requires effort becomes natural as your body learns to return to its core.
