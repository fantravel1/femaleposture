# Pelvic Floor Health During Menopause

> **Hidden but Essential:** Your pelvic floor supports your organs, controls continence, and affects posture. Menopause brings changes that need attention.

---

## How Menopause Affects Pelvic Floor

### Hormone Effects

**Estrogen decline:**
- Affects muscle tone
- Tissues become thinner
- Elasticity decreases
- Support weakens

### Common Changes

**You may notice:**
- Stress incontinence (leaking with cough/sneeze)
- Urge incontinence
- Pelvic pressure
- Vaginal symptoms
- Prolapse symptoms

---

## Understanding the Pelvic Floor

### What It Does

**Functions:**
- Supports bladder, uterus, bowel
- Controls continence
- Sexual function
- Core stability
- Posture support

### Connection to Core and Posture

**Integrated system:**
- Works with core muscles
- Affects spinal support
- Part of stability system
- Influences posture

---

## Common Issues

### Stress Incontinence

**What it is:**
- Leaking with pressure
- Cough, sneeze, laugh, exercise
- Weakened closure

**Contributing factors:**
- Childbirth history
- Estrogen decline
- Muscle weakness
- Weight factors

### Urge Incontinence

**What it is:**
- Sudden strong urge
- May not reach bathroom
- Overactive bladder

**Related factors:**
- Hormonal changes
- Bladder sensitivity
- Neurological factors

### Prolapse

**What it is:**
- Organs dropping down
- Due to weakened support
- Various degrees

**Symptoms:**
- Pressure or heaviness
- Feeling of something falling
- Visible bulge
- Bowel/bladder symptoms

---

## Prevention and Management

### Pelvic Floor Exercises

**Kegels:**
- Identify correct muscles
- Contract (lift and squeeze)
- Hold 10 seconds
- Relax completely
- Repeat 10 times, 3x daily

**Important:**
- Correct technique matters
- Don't hold breath
- Don't bear down
- Relax fully between

### Finding the Muscles

**How to identify:**
- Like stopping urine flow
- Squeeze around vagina
- Lift up inside
- If unsure, see PT

### Advanced Options

**Beyond basic Kegels:**
- Quick flicks
- Endurance holds
- Functional integration
- With movement

---

## Exercise Considerations

### High-Impact Activity

**May need modification:**
- Running (if leaking)
- Jumping
- Heavy lifting
- High-intensity activity

**Not stopping entirely:**
- But may need progression
- Pelvic floor training first
- Build support
- Gradual return

### What Helps

**Beneficial exercise:**
- Core strengthening
- Pelvic floor exercises
- Moderate activity
- Yoga/Pilates

### What to Be Careful With

**Monitor:**
- Heavy lifting
- High impact
- Straining exercises
- Anything causing symptoms

---

## Lifestyle Factors

### Weight Management

**Connection:**
- Extra weight = more pressure
- Weight loss can help symptoms
- Part of overall approach

### Constipation

**Important:**
- Straining worsens pelvic floor
- Avoid constipation
- Fiber, fluids, movement
- Don't strain on toilet

### Chronic Cough

**If present:**
- Treat cough
- Repeated pressure damages floor
- Address underlying cause

---

## Posture and Pelvic Floor

### Connection

**How posture affects:**
- Slouching compresses
- Alignment affects function
- Core integration matters
- Posture supports pelvic floor

### What Helps

**Good posture:**
- Tall spine
- Core engagement
- Proper alignment
- Reduces pressure

---

## When to Seek Help

### Signs to Act On

**See provider if:**
- Leaking affecting life
- Avoiding activities
- Prolapse symptoms
- Not improving with exercises
- Any concerning symptoms

### Treatment Options

**May include:**
- Pelvic floor physiotherapy
- Vaginal estrogen (for some)
- Pessary (for prolapse)
- Surgical options (if needed)
- Various approaches
