# Menopause and Bone Health

> **Protect Your Skeleton:** Estrogen decline during menopause accelerates bone loss. What you do now impacts your bones for decades.

---

## Why Menopause Affects Bones

### The Estrogen Connection

**How it works:**
- Estrogen helps maintain bone density
- Menopause = significant estrogen drop
- Bone breakdown accelerates
- Bone building slows

### The Numbers

**What happens:**
- Women lose 2-3% bone per year in first years after menopause
- Loss continues at slower rate
- Without intervention, significant loss possible
- Prevention is key

---

## Who's at Higher Risk

### Risk Factors

**Greater concern with:**
- Family history of osteoporosis
- Small, thin frame
- Smoking
- Excessive alcohol
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Certain medications
- Poor nutrition

### Getting Assessed

**Consider:**
- Bone density testing (DEXA scan)
- Discuss with your doctor
- Know your baseline
- Monitor over time

---

## Posture and Bone Health

### Why Posture Matters

**Connection:**
- Good posture protects spine
- Reduces fracture risk
- Better load distribution
- Maintains function

### Spinal Curves

**Concern:**
- Vertebral fractures can change posture
- Lead to kyphosis (forward curve)
- Prevention essential
- Maintain good alignment

---

## Exercise for Bones

### Weight-Bearing Exercise

**Essential types:**
- Walking
- Jogging (if appropriate)
- Dancing
- Stair climbing
- Tennis, etc.

**Why it works:**
- Stress on bones stimulates building
- Gravity-loaded activity
- Regular practice matters

### Resistance Training

**Highly effective:**
- Builds muscle and bone
- Can slow bone loss
- Improves strength
- Protects from falls

**Approach:**
- 2-3 times per week
- Major muscle groups
- Progressive challenge
- See [Strength Training Guide](/tools/strength-training-fundamentals/)

### Balance Training

**Prevents falls:**
- Falls cause fractures
- Balance work essential
- Tai chi helpful
- Simple balance exercises

---

## Safe Exercise with Bone Concerns

### If Osteoporosis Present

**Caution with:**
- Forward bending under load
- Twisting with weight
- High-impact if severe
- Certain yoga poses

**Safe choices:**
- Walking
- Gentle strength training
- Balance exercises
- Swimming (for overall fitness)

### Consult Professionals

**If concerns:**
- Physical therapist
- Exercise physiologist
- Doctor guidance
- Safe exercise prescription

---

## Nutrition for Bones

### Calcium

**Essential:**
- Dairy products
- Fortified foods
- Leafy greens
- Supplements if needed
- 1200mg/day typically recommended

### Vitamin D

**Critical for absorption:**
- Sunlight exposure
- Fortified foods
- Supplements often needed
- Blood test can check levels

### Protein

**For bone and muscle:**
- Adequate intake important
- Each meal
- Supports bone matrix
- Helps muscle maintenance

### What to Limit

**May affect bones:**
- Excessive alcohol
- Too much caffeine
- Very high sodium
- Smoking (quit if you do)

---

## Medical Considerations

### Bone Density Testing

**When to consider:**
- At menopause
- Risk factors present
- After 65 generally recommended
- To monitor treatment

### Treatment Options

**If indicated:**
- Hormone therapy
- Bisphosphonates
- Other medications
- Discuss with doctor

### Ongoing Monitoring

**Regular assessment:**
- Follow-up bone density tests
- Fracture risk assessment
- Treatment adjustment
- Lifestyle review

## Falls Prevention

### Why It Matters

**Critical because:**
- Falls cause most fractures
- Bones are more fragile
- Prevention is key
- See [Falls Prevention](/life-stages/senior/falls-prevention/)

### Home Safety

**Reduce risk:**
- Remove trip hazards
- Good lighting
- Grab bars if needed
- Non-slip surfaces

---

## Posture Protection

### Daily Habits

**Protect your spine:**
- Lift properly
- Avoid heavy loads
- Maintain good posture
- Strengthen core and back

### What to Avoid

**If bone concerns:**
- Heavy lifting
- Sudden movements
- Forward bending with weight
- High-risk activities

---

## Long-Term Approach

### Lifetime Practice

**Bone health is ongoing:**
- Not just during menopause
- Exercise continues
- Nutrition matters always
- Regular monitoring

### It's Never Too Late

**Starting now helps:**
- Can slow further loss
- Improve strength and balance
- Reduce fracture risk
- Maintain independence
