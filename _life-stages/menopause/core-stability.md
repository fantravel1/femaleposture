# Core Stability During Menopause

> **Core strength becomes increasingly important during menopause—supporting posture, protecting your back, and maintaining function as muscle mass naturally declines.**

---

## Why Core Matters Now

Core strength is critical at this stage.

### What's Changing

**Age and hormones:**
- Muscle mass declining
- Strength decreasing
- Core stability affected
- Support reduced
- Attention needed

### Why Core Is Foundation

**What core does:**
- Supports spine
- Enables movement
- Protects back
- Maintains posture
- Essential function

## Core Exercises

### Deep Core Activation

**Foundation:**
- Draw belly in gently
- Activate transversus
- Hold and breathe
- Foundational skill
- Learn first

### Bird-Dog

**Excellent exercise:**
- On hands and knees
- Extend opposite arm and leg
- Keep back stable
- Core engaged
- Balance and stability

### Modified Plank

**Core challenge:**
- On forearms and knees
- Body in line
- Hold position
- Progress to full plank
- Core strengthening

### Side Plank

**Side core:**
- Modified on knee
- Side of body engaged
- Hold steady
- Both sides
- Oblique strength

### Dead Bug

**Safe core work:**
- On back
- Opposite arm and leg lower
- Back stays flat
- Core engaged
- Controlled movement
