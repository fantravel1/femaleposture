# Pelvic Floor Changes in Menopause

> **Estrogen decline during menopause affects pelvic floor tissues, leading to changes that many women experience but few discuss. Understanding these changes and available solutions empowers you to maintain pelvic health.**

---

## What Happens to the Pelvic Floor

Menopause brings changes to pelvic floor muscles and tissues.

### Estrogen's Role

**What estrogen did:**
- Maintained tissue elasticity
- Supported blood flow to area
- Kept muscles responsive
- Maintained tissue thickness
- Declining changes everything

### Common Changes

**What many experience:**
- Weakened pelvic floor muscles
- Tissue thinning
- Reduced muscle tone
- Decreased sensation
- Changed bladder function

---

## Common Symptoms

### Urinary Changes

**Bladder issues:**
- Stress incontinence (leaking with cough, sneeze, exercise)
- Urgency incontinence (sudden need)
- Increased frequency
- Nighttime urination
- Difficulty emptying fully

### Pelvic Organ Prolapse

**When support weakens:**
- Feeling of heaviness
- Something "falling out" sensation
- Pressure in pelvis
- Worsens with standing
- Various degrees of severity

### Other Symptoms

**Also possible:**
- Pain with intimacy
- Vaginal dryness
- Recurring infections
- Decreased sensation
- General discomfort

## Pelvic Floor Exercises

### Kegels Still Matter

**Why they help:**
- Strengthen pelvic muscles
- Improve muscle control
- Reduce symptoms
- Prevention and treatment
- Never too late to start

### Proper Technique

**Doing them right:**
- Identify correct muscles
- Squeeze and lift
- Don't hold breath
- Relax completely between
- Quality over quantity

### Daily Practice

**Building routine:**
- 3 sets daily
- 10 repetitions each
- Mix quick and sustained holds
- Consistency is key
- Progress gradually

---

## Beyond Kegels

### Full Pelvic Floor Fitness

**Comprehensive approach:**
- Not just squeezing
- Relaxation equally important
- Coordination matters
- Functional training
- Movement integration

### Exercises That Support

**Helpful movements:**
- Squats (light)
- Bridge pose
- Core engagement
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Whole-body approach

### What to Avoid Initially

**May worsen symptoms:**
- Heavy lifting
- High-impact exercise
- Deep squats (at first)
- Sit-ups/crunches
- Until function improves

## Lifestyle Factors

### Weight Management

**Body weight effects:**
- Extra weight stresses pelvic floor
- Weight loss can improve symptoms
- Healthy weight goal
- Part of comprehensive approach
- Even modest loss helps

### Constipation

**Straining matters:**
- Chronic straining weakens floor
- Address constipation
- Adequate fiber
- Hydration
- Good toilet habits

### Chronic Cough

**Repeated pressure:**
- Smoking-related cough problematic
- Address allergies
- Treat respiratory issues
- Each cough stresses floor
- Prevention helps

## Professional Help

### When to Seek Care

**Get evaluated for:**
- Significant incontinence
- Prolapse symptoms
- Pain
- Impact on quality of life
- Symptoms not improving

### Pelvic Floor Physical Therapy

**Specialized treatment:**
- Assessment of function
- Personalized exercises
- Manual therapy
- Biofeedback if needed
- Very effective

### Medical Options

**Available treatments:**
- Vaginal estrogen (localized)
- Pessaries for prolapse
- Medications for urgency
- Surgical options if needed
- Discuss with provider
