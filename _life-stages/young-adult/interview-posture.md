# Job Interview Posture

> **Your posture during a job interview communicates confidence, competence, and professionalism before you even speak. Managing body language helps you make the impression you want.**

---

## Why Posture Matters in Interviews

First impressions are largely nonverbal.

### What Interviewers See

**Beyond your words:**
- Confidence level
- Energy and engagement
- Professionalism
- Nervousness or ease
- Overall presence

### Impact on Perception

**Posture communicates:**
- Self-assurance
- Competence
- Interest in position
- Readiness to work
- Cultural fit

---

## Arrival and Entrance

### Walking In

**First impression:**
- Head up
- Shoulders back
- Confident stride
- Eye contact
- Energy visible

### The Greeting

**Handshake moment:**
- Stand fully upright
- Firm handshake
- Eye contact
- Confident smile
- Clear greeting

---

## Seated Interview Posture

### Basic Position

**Foundation:**
- Sit toward front of chair
- Feet flat on floor
- Back straight but not rigid
- Shoulders relaxed
- Settled and present

### What to Avoid

**Common mistakes:**
- Slouching
- Crossing arms
- Fidgeting
- Leaning too far back
- Looking uncomfortable

### Optimal Position

**Best approach:**
- Slight forward lean (shows interest)
- Open body position
- Hands visible
- Relaxed but alert
- Engaged presence

---

## Hand Positioning

### Where to Put Hands

**Options:**
- Lightly clasped on table
- On armrests if available
- In lap loosely
- Gesturing naturally
- Visible and calm

### Avoid

**Problematic:**
- Hidden hands
- Constant fidgeting
- Nervous habits
- Clenched fists
- Over-gesturing

### When Speaking

**Natural gestures:**
- Emphasize points naturally
- Not too much
- Not too little
- Match your personality
- Genuine expression

---

## Eye Contact

### During Interview

**Best practice:**
- Maintain regular eye contact
- Not staring constantly
- Natural breaks
- With all interviewers
- Engaged and present

### If Nervous

**Managing:**
- Look at eyebrows or forehead
- Brief breaks natural
- Practice beforehand
- Gets easier
- Confidence builds

---

## Managing Nervousness

### Physical Symptoms

**Common issues:**
- Shaking
- Sweating
- Voice trembling
- Fidgeting
- Tension visible

### Before Interview

**Calming techniques:**
- Deep breathing
- Power poses (in private)
- Shake out tension
- Calm yourself
- Center

### During Interview

**Managing:**
- Slow, deep breaths
- Ground feet on floor
- Relax shoulders consciously
- Speak slowly
- Breathe

## Virtual Interview Posture

### Camera Positioning

**Setup:**
- Camera at eye level
- Good lighting
- Clean background
- Appropriate distance
- Professional setup

### On-Screen Presence

**What to do:**
- Sit up straight
- Look at camera (not screen)
- Engaged expression
- Upper body visible
- Good frame

### Unique Challenges

**Virtual considerations:**
- More deliberate eye contact
- Gestures visible but not too much
- Energy comes across differently
- Practice on camera
- Test setup

---

## Body Language Reading

### Reading Interviewers

**Notice:**
- Their engagement
- Positive responses
- Questions of interest
- Energy shifts
- Adjust accordingly

### Mirroring

**Subtle technique:**
- Match their energy level
- Similar posture
- Natural mirroring
- Builds rapport
- Not imitation

---

## Panel Interviews

### Multiple Interviewers

**Managing:**
- Include everyone
- Shift eye contact
- Turn body toward speaker
- Engage whole group
- Don't favor one

### Physical Logistics

**Positioning:**
- Can you see everyone
- Position chair if possible
- Body language to all
- Equal attention
- Group awareness

---

## Pre-Interview Preparation

### Practice

**Before the day:**
- Practice posture
- Mock interviews
- Video yourself
- Get feedback
- Build muscle memory

### Morning of Interview

**Getting ready:**
- Posture check
- Power pose practice
- Calm breathing
- Confident mindset
- Ready to go

### Just Before

**Final moments:**
- Bathroom posture check
- Deep breaths
- Shoulder release
- Stand tall
- Enter ready

---

## After the Interview

### Exit

**Leaving well:**
- Stand smoothly
- Confident handshake
- Thank you with eye contact
- Exit with good posture
- Strong finish

### Follow-Up

**Impression continues:**
- Written thank you
- Continued professionalism
- Consistent presentation
- Complete package
- Follow through
