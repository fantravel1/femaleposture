# Networking Body Language

> **Networking events can feel awkward, but your body language communicates more than words. Good posture and open body language help you make connections and project approachability.**

---

## Why Body Language Matters

Nonverbal communication dominates first impressions.

### First Impressions

**What people notice:**
- How you carry yourself
- Openness or closure
- Energy level
- Confidence
- Approachability

### Lasting Effects

**Impact:**
- Sets the tone
- Affects how others respond
- Creates opportunities
- Influences connections
- Memory of you

---

## Arrival and Entry

### Walking In

**Enter with confidence:**
- Head up
- Shoulders back
- Comfortable pace
- Eyes surveying room
- Present and ready

### First Moments

**Setting up:**
- Find your bearings
- Get a drink if helpful
- Scan for opportunities
- Open body position
- Ready to engage

---

## Open vs. Closed Posture

### Approachable Body Language

**Open signals:**
- Arms uncrossed
- Facing the room
- Relaxed shoulders
- Open stance
- Available expression

### What to Avoid

**Closed signals:**
- Arms crossed
- Turned away
- Phone as barrier
- Tense posture
- Unavailable look

### Being Approachable

**Position yourself:**
- Visible in the room
- Open to approach
- Welcoming expression
- Not in corner
- Accessible

---

## Conversation Posture

### Engaging with Others

**When talking:**
- Face them fully
- Slight forward lean
- Eye contact (not staring)
- Nodding when listening
- Engaged presence

### Group Conversations

**Multiple people:**
- Include everyone
- Shift attention
- Open body to group
- Don't exclude
- Inclusive positioning

### Listening Actively

**Show you're engaged:**
- Attentive posture
- Eye contact
- Nodding
- Reactions
- Present attention

---

## Managing Nervousness

### Common Physical Signs

**Nervous tells:**
- Fidgeting
- Crossed arms
- Avoiding eye contact
- Rushed movements
- Tension visible

### Calming Strategies

**Before engaging:**
- Deep breaths
- Power pose in bathroom
- Slow down
- Ground yourself
- Relax shoulders

### During Conversations

**Stay calm:**
- Breathe
- Slow your pace
- Feet grounded
- Hands relaxed
- Easy does it

---

## Standing Comfortably

### Long Events

**For duration:**
- Comfortable shoes essential
- Shift weight
- Move around
- Find sitting breaks
- Pace yourself

### Standing Posture

**Throughout:**
- Weight balanced
- Knees soft
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Natural stance

---

## Hand Positioning

### What to Do with Hands

**Good options:**
- Holding drink (one hand)
- Gesturing naturally
- At sides
- Visible and relaxed
- Natural movements

### Handshakes

**Greeting:**
- Firm but not crushing
- Web to web
- Eye contact
- Brief and confident
- Professional greeting

### Avoid

**Hand problems:**
- Constant fidgeting
- Hiding hands
- Gripping drink with both
- Nervous habits
- Distracting movements

---

## Moving Through the Room

### Circulating

**Working the room:**
- Move with purpose
- Pause at groups
- Join conversations appropriately
- Exit gracefully
- Continue circulating

### Joining Groups

**Entry:**
- Wait for natural opening
- Approach open groups
- Acknowledge everyone
- Join at edge
- Work in naturally

### Exiting Conversations

**Leaving gracefully:**
- Natural conclusion
- Exchange cards if appropriate
- Positive exit
- Move on
- Don't linger awkwardly

---

## Specific Situations

### Meeting Important People

**VIP encounters:**
- Same principles
- Confident but not overeager
- Genuine interest
- Professional presence
- Don't overthink

### When Feeling Out of Place

**If uncomfortable:**
- Breathe and ground
- Remember everyone feels this
- Start with friendly faces
- Take breaks if needed
- Persist

### Long Events

**Sustaining energy:**
- Pace yourself
- Food and water
- Breaks when needed
- Leave when finished
- Quality over quantity

---

## Follow-Up

### End of Event

**Leaving:**
- Thank hosts if appropriate
- Final good impressions
- Exit with energy still
- Leave wanting more
- Positive conclusion

### Next Steps

**After event:**
- Send follow-up messages
- Continue connections
- Build relationships
- Use contacts made
- Investment pays off
