# Commuter Posture

## Why Commute Posture Matters

Travel time affects your body.

### Accumulated Time

**Hours add up:**
- 30 minutes each way = 5 hours/week
- Longer commutes even more
- Years of daily travel
- Significant body time
- Worth attention

### Impact on Day

**How it affects you:**
- Arrive tense vs. relaxed
- Start day stiff
- End day exhausted
- Accumulated strain
- Sets the tone

---

## Driving Posture

### Seat Setup

**Optimal positioning:**
- Seat distance allows bent elbows
- Back against seat fully
- Headrest at right height
- Knees slightly bent
- Proper adjustments matter

### While Driving

**Maintain posture:**
- Shoulders relaxed
- Hands comfortable on wheel
- Core gently engaged
- Head not forward
- Check periodically

### Traffic Tension

**When stressed:**
- Grip loosening
- Shoulder drops
- Jaw unclenching
- Deep breaths
- Don't hold tension

### Long Commutes

**Extended driving:**
- Lumbar support
- Micro-adjustments
- Stop if possible
- Stretch when arriving
- Take care

---

## Public Transit

### Standing

**On bus or train:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Soft knees
- Core engaged
- Hold support
- Balance practice

### Sitting

**When seats available:**
- Sit fully back
- Feet on floor
- Don't slump
- Bag off shoulders
- Use the time well

### Crowded Conditions

**When packed:**
- Protect your space
- Bag in front
- Hold on securely
- Best posture possible
- Balance is key

### Reading/Phone Use

**Common activity:**
- Bring device up
- Don't hunch over
- Neck awareness
- Alternative hand holding
- Limit duration

## Bike Commuting

### Bike Fit

**Critical setup:**
- Proper frame size
- Saddle height
- Handlebar position
- Comfortable reach
- Professional fitting helpful

### Riding Position

**While cycling:**
- Relaxed shoulders
- Soft elbows
- Core engaged
- Neutral spine
- Not hunched

### Arriving at Work

**Transition:**
- Cool-down stretch
- Change positions
- Shake out tension
- Ready for day
- Body reset

## Walking Commute

### Posture While Walking

**Good form:**
- Head up
- Shoulders back
- Arms swinging naturally
- Core engaged
- Efficient gait

### Bag Carrying

**If carrying things:**
- Backpack preferred
- Both shoulders
- Not too heavy
- Weight distributed
- Alternate if one-shoulder

### Weather Challenges

**Various conditions:**
- Don't hunch against cold
- Walk tall in rain
- Heat awareness
- Adapt but maintain posture
- All weather requires attention

## Bags and Gear

### Backpack Best

**Ideal option:**
- Two straps
- Weight distributed
- Close to body

### Shoulder Bags

### Work Bags
