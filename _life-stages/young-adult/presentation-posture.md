# Presentation and Public Speaking Posture

> **Your body language during presentations affects how your message is received. Good posture projects confidence and credibility, helping you deliver your content effectively.**

---

## Why Posture Matters

Audiences read your body language.

### Nonverbal Communication

**What they see:**
- Confidence or nervousness
- Authority or uncertainty
- Engagement or discomfort
- Energy level
- Credibility

### Impact on Message

**Posture affects:**
- How message is received
- Believability
- Attention retention
- Overall impression
- Presentation success

---

## Standing Posture

### Foundation

**Basic stance:**
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Weight evenly distributed
- Knees slightly soft
- Shoulders back and down
- Head balanced

### Presence

**Projecting:**
- Take up space
- Grounded stance
- Confident bearing
- Don't shrink
- Own the space

### Common Mistakes

**Avoid:**
- Swaying
- Weight on one leg
- Locked knees
- Hunched shoulders
- Fidgeting

---

## Movement and Gestures

### Purposeful Movement

**How to move:**
- Move with intention
- Don't pace nervously
- Move to emphasize points
- Engage all areas of room
- Controlled movement

### Hand Gestures

**Using hands:**
- Natural gestures
- Emphasize points
- Not too much
- Not too little
- Support your words

### What to Avoid

**Distracting habits:**
- Playing with hair/clothes
- Fidgeting with objects
- Hands in pockets always
- Clasped in front constantly
- Distracting patterns

---

## Eye Contact

### With Audience

**Making connection:**
- Scan the room
- Brief eye contact with individuals
- Include everyone
- Not staring
- Natural connection

### When Nervous

**Strategies:**
- Look at foreheads
- Section the room
- Practice helps
- Gets easier
- Builds connection

---

## Managing Nervousness

### Physical Symptoms

**What happens:**
- Shaking
- Voice trembling
- Sweating
- Tension
- Normal response

### Before Speaking

**Preparation:**
- Deep breathing
- Power pose (private)
- Shake out tension
- Calming techniques
- Ground yourself

### During Presentation

**Managing:**
- Breathe
- Slow down
- Ground feet
- Pause if needed
- You've got this

---

## Voice and Breath

### Breathing for Speaking

**Support voice:**
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Supports projection
- Calms nerves
- Steady voice
- Foundation

### Projection

**Being heard:**
- Speak from diaphragm
- Good posture supports voice
- Eye contact with back row
- Adequate volume
- Clear delivery

### Pace

**Speed:**
- Slow down
- Nervous speakers rush
- Pauses are powerful
- Let points land
- Deliberate pace

---

## Seated Presentations

### Virtual Meetings

**Camera presence:**
- Sit up straight
- Camera at eye level
- Good lighting
- Engaged posture
- Virtual presence

### In-Person Seated

**At tables:**
- Sit forward slightly
- Engaged posture
- Gesture naturally
- Eye contact
- Active presence

---

## Handling Q&A

### Body Language

**During questions:**
- Open posture
- Engaged listening
- Face the questioner
- Thoughtful response
- Confident answers

### When You Don't Know

**Still confident:**
- Honest response
- Good posture maintained
- Not deflated
- Professional handling
- Confidence continues

## Practice Strategies

### Preparation

**Before the day:**
- Practice out loud
- Record yourself
- Mirror practice
- Get feedback
- Know your content

### Posture Practice

**Body rehearsal:**
- Practice standing
- Practice gestures
- Movement patterns
- Build muscle memory
- Physical preparation

## Different Settings

### Large Audiences

**Bigger room:**
- Bigger gestures
- More projection
- Use the space
- Eye contact sections
- Scale up

### Small Groups

**Intimate settings:**
- More conversational
- Less dramatic
- More personal connection
- Adapted approach
- Scale down
