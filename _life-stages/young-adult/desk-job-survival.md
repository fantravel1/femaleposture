# Desk Job Survival Guide

> **Your first desk job is excitingâ€”but suddenly sitting 8+ hours a day when you've been active in school is a shock to the system. Setting up good habits from day one prevents years of problems.**

---

## The Desk Job Reality Check

Office work presents physical challenges you might not expect.

### What You're Facing

**New demands:**
- 8+ hours of sitting
- Fixed position for long periods
- Screen staring
- Repetitive motions
- Reduced daily movement

### Why It Hits Hard

**The transition:**
- School had variety and movement
- Now chained to one spot
- Body not used to this
- Effects accumulate quickly
- Habits form fast

---

## Setting Up Your Workspace

### Chair Adjustment

**Getting it right:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Knees at 90 degrees
- Lower back supported
- Armrests at elbow height
- Seat pan appropriate depth

### Desk and Monitor

**Proper positioning:**
- Monitor at arm's length
- Top of screen at eye level
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Mouse near keyboard
- Avoid reaching

### What to Request

**Don't be shy:**
- Chair adjustment help
- Monitor riser if needed
- External keyboard
- Ergonomic assessment
- What you need to work well

---

## Movement Strategies

### Break Frequency

**Building habits:**
- Every 30-60 minutes minimum
- Stand and stretch
- Walk to water or bathroom
- Brief movement breaks
- Set reminders initially

### Meeting Movement

**Incorporating activity:**
- Walk to in-person meetings
- Stand during calls if possible
- Walking meetings when appropriate
- Movement between meetings
- Don't stay glued to chair

### Lunch Break Activity

**Midday reset:**
- Walk outside if possible
- Move away from desk
- Stretch or exercise
- Don't eat at desk daily
- Real break from sitting

## Common Early Career Mistakes

### Desk Eating

**Why it's a problem:**
- No break from position
- Mindless eating
- No mental reset
- Social isolation

### Over-Dedication

**The trap:**
- Never taking breaks
- Eating at desk to work

- Proving yourself vs. health
- Sustainable pace matters

### Ignoring Discomfort

**Early warnings:**
- Pain is a signal
- Don't push through
- Adjust setup
- Address early
- Prevention easier than treatment

## Dealing with Problems

### Neck Pain

**Common issue:**
- Often from screen position
- Forward head posture
- Taking work home (laptop)
- Adjust setup
- Stretch regularly

### Back Pain

**Address quickly:**
- Check chair support
- Standing breaks
- Core engagement
- Adjust positioning
- Don't ignore

### Wrist and Hand Issues

**Repetitive strain:**
- Keyboard and mouse position
- Take micro-breaks
- Stretch hands
- Ergonomic equipment
- Early intervention
