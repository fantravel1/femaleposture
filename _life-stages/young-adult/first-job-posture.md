# First Job Posture: Building Workplace Habits

> **Start Right:** The habits you build in your first job can affect your body for your entire career. Set yourself up for decades of healthy work.

---

## Why Early Career Matters

### Habits Form Now

**Your first years:**
- Establish patterns
- Create defaults
- Build or damage body
- Set trajectory

### The Opportunity

**Advantage:**
- No bad habits yet (maybe)
- Young body still adaptable
- Can build right from start
- Prevention is easiest now

---

## Your Workstation

### Set It Up Right

**From day one:**
- Don't accept default setup
- Adjust everything
- Ask for what you need
- It matters

### Basic Setup

**Essential adjustments:**
- Screen at eye level
- Chair height correct
- Keyboard at elbow height
- Feet flat or supported

### Asking for Equipment

**It's okay to ask:**
- Monitor riser/stand
- Keyboard tray
- Proper chair
- What you need

### If You Get Pushback

**Advocate for yourself:**
- Explain ergonomics
- Frame as productivity
- Most employers will accommodate
- Start with low-cost options

---

## Common First Job Pitfalls

### "Paying Your Dues"

**The trap:**
- Taking worst desk
- Not speaking up
- Suffering in silence
- Damaging your body

**The reality:**
- Reasonable requests are fine
- Your health matters
- Set boundaries early
- Professional and appropriate

### Proving Yourself

**Temptation:**
- Working constantly
- Skipping breaks
- No lunch away from desk
- Burning out

**Better approach:**
- Sustainable from start
- Breaks actually help productivity
- Model healthy behavior
- Set tone for career

---

## Professional Presence

### Posture and Perception

**How you're seen:**
- Posture affects impression
- Confidence matters
- First impressions count
- Body language speaks

### In Meetings

**Present well:**
- Sit attentively
- Engaged posture
- Don't slouch
- Look interested

### With Clients/Customers

**Project competence:**
- Stand tall
- Confident handshake
- Open body language
- Professional presence

---

## Movement at Work

### Building the Habit

**From the start:**
- Regular breaks
- Walking meetings when possible
- Not sitting all day
- Movement throughout

### Break Culture

**Even if not common:**
- Take your breaks
- Walk at lunch
- Stand and stretch
- It's okay to prioritize health

## Different Work Environments

### Office

**Traditional setup:**
- Desk ergonomics essential
- Break routine
- See [Office Posture](/populations/office-workers-posture/)

### Open Plan

**Challenges:**
- May have less control
- Hot desking issues
- Make the best of it
- Portable setup helps

### Remote/Hybrid

**Home setup matters:**
- Don't work from couch
- Proper home office
- See [Work From Home](/activities/working-from-home/)
- Invest in yourself

### Retail/Service

**Standing jobs:**
- Proper footwear
- Posture awareness
- Movement variety
- See [Standing Work](/populations/retail-workers-posture/)

---

## Physical Demands

### If Your Job Is Physical

**Early attention:**
- Learn proper lifting
- Use equipment correctly
- Don't show off
- Prevent injury early

### If Sedentary

**Counter the sitting:**
- Exercise outside work
- Movement during day
- Strength training helps
- Active lifestyle

---

## Long Hours

### When Required

**If long hours expected:**
- Set up well (spend more time there)
- Take micro-breaks
- Stretch at desk
- Sustainable approach

### When Voluntary

**Choosing to work more:**
- Question if necessary
- Health > extra credit
- Diminishing returns
- Work smart

---

## Work-Life Balance for Body

### After Work

**Physical balance:**
- Exercise routine
- Not collapsing on couch
- Active recovery
- Sleep priority

### Weekends

**Restoration:**
- Movement
- Different activities
- Not more sitting
- Recharge physically

## Building Career-Long Habits

### Start Now

**Establish:**
- Ergonomic awareness
- Break routine
- Movement habit
- Self-advocacy

### Long-Term View

**Think ahead:**
- 40+ year career ahead
- Protect your body now
- Habits compound
- Prevention is easier
