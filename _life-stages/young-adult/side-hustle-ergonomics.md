# Side Hustle Ergonomics

> **When you're working extra hours to meet your goals, protecting your body becomes even more critical. Smart ergonomics help you hustle sustainably.**

---

## The Side Hustle Physical Challenge

Extra work means extra demands on your body.

### Compounding Strain

**What happens:**
- Main job already takes toll
- Side work adds more hours
- Less recovery time
- Often different physical demands
- Cumulative stress on body

### Common Side Hustle Types

**Various work categories:**
- Computer-based (freelance, online)
- Delivery and driving
- Retail and food service
- Physical labor and gigs
- Creative and making

---

## Computer-Based Side Work

### Home Laptop Setup

**When freelancing at home:**
- Proper desk setup if possible
- Laptop stand essential
- External keyboard and mouse
- Good lighting
- Comfortable chair

### Working in Evenings

**After main job:**
- Take break before starting
- Stretch transition
- Fresh water and snacks
- Time limit to protect sleep
- Quality over quantity

### Caf√© and Library Work

**Public space hustling:**
- Portable ergonomic kit
- Choose better seating
- Limited duration sessions
- Movement between locations
- Don't compromise posture

---

## Delivery and Driving

### In-Car Posture

**Long hours driving:**
- Proper seat adjustment
- Mirror check equals posture check
- Lumbar support
- Breaks for stretching
- Watch for tension

### Loading and Unloading

**Package handling:**
- Lift with legs
- Don't rush
- Use carts when available
- Avoid twisting
- Protect your back

### Walking Deliveries

**On foot delivery:**
- Comfortable, supportive shoes
- Bag worn properly
- Balanced loads
- Stretch after shifts
- Hydration and breaks

---

## Retail and Food Service

### Standing for Hours

**When on feet:**
- Supportive shoes essential
- Anti-fatigue mat if possible
- Shift weight regularly
- Brief seated breaks
- Stretching on breaks

### Repetitive Tasks

**Register, counter work:**
- Vary position when possible
- Alternate tasks
- Core engagement
- Wrist awareness
- Movement between customers

### Food Prep and Serving

**Kitchen and restaurant:**
- Proper lifting technique
- Reaching safely
- Managing heavy trays
- Floor surface awareness
- Watch for wet areas

---

## Physical Labor Gigs

### Construction and Moving

**Heavy work:**
- Proper safety training
- Use equipment correctly
- Team lifting
- Pace yourself
- Know your limits

### Cleaning and Organizing

**Service work:**
- Proper cleaning postures
- Long-handled tools
- Kneel don't bend repeatedly
- Alternate activities
- Time management

### Yard and Outdoor Work

**Landscaping gigs:**
- Vary tasks frequently
- Proper lifting
- Hydration in heat
- Sun protection
- Stretching routine

---

## Creative Side Hustles

### Crafting and Making

**Handwork:**
- Good work surface height
- Lighting for detail work
- Posture during hand work
- Frequent breaks
- Stretch hands and arms

### Art and Design

**Creative work:**
- Easel or tablet positioning
- Drawing posture
- Eye strain prevention
- Neck position for looking down
- Movement between sessions

---

## Managing Total Hours

### Realistic Limits

**Protecting yourself:**
- Track total work hours
- Notice fatigue signals
- Burnout prevention
- Sleep protection
- Body before money sometimes

### Rest Days

**Recovery time:**
- At least one full rest day
- Gentle movement only
- Sleep priority
- Active recovery
- Prepare for next week

### Signs of Overworking

**Warning signals:**
- Chronic pain developing
- Fatigue not resolving
- Increasing injuries
- Sleep problems
- Mental exhaustion

---

## Transition Between Jobs

### Between Main and Side Job

**Managing transitions:**
- Stretch between roles
- Change clothes if helpful
- Mental reset
- Physical reset
- Eat and hydrate

### Different Physical Demands

**Contrasting work:**
- Desk job + physical side hustle
- Standing job + computer side work
- Variety can be good
- Or compound certain strains
- Balance intentionally

---

## Self-Care Essentials

### Non-Negotiables

**Must-dos:**
- Adequate sleep
- Proper nutrition
- Hydration
- Some movement
- Rest when truly needed

### Investment in Body

**Worth spending on:**
- Good shoes for your work
- Ergonomic equipment
- Quality mattress
- Physical therapy if needed
- Prevention over treatment

---

## Long-Term Thinking

### Sustainable Hustle

**Making it last:**
- Body care enables earning
- Injury stops income
- Build sustainable habits
- Invest in ergonomics
- Think long-term

### When to Scale Back

**Recognizing limits:**
- Physical symptoms persisting
- Quality of life suffering
- Main job affected
- Health declining
- Money isn't worth breakdown
