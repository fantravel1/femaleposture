# Baby Wearing Posture: Carrier and Wrap Ergonomics

> **Close and Safe:** Baby wearing keeps your hands free and baby close. Proper technique protects both of you.

---

## Benefits of Baby Wearing

### For Baby

**Benefits:**
- Closeness and bonding
- Calming effect
- Developmental support
- Temperature regulation

### For Parent

**Benefits:**
- Hands free
- Mobility
- Bonding hormone release
- Can be easier than carrying

---

## Choosing the Right Carrier

### Types of Carriers

**Options:**
- Soft structured carriers
- Ring slings
- Wraps
- Meh dai
- Each has pros and cons

### Fit Matters

**Important factors:**
- Fits your body
- Adjusts properly
- Comfortable for duration
- Supports baby correctly

### Getting Help

**Consider:**
- Professional fitting
- Babywearing consultant
- Learn proper technique
- Practice safely

---

## Proper Baby Position

### Key Points

**Baby should be:**
- High enough to kiss
- Visible at all times
- Chin off chest (breathing clear)
- Close to your body
- Supported knees to knees

### T.I.C.K.S. Rules

**Remember:**
- **T**ight (carrier snug)
- **I**n view at all times
- **C**lose enough to kiss
- **K**eep chin off chest
- **S**upported back

### Hip Position

**For hip development:**
- Knees higher than bottom
- "M" position
- Legs supported widely
- Ergonomic position

---

## Your Posture While Wearing

### Standing Tall

**How to:**
- Don't lean backward
- Keep core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Stand normally

### Common Mistakes

**Avoid:**
- Leaning back to balance
- Hunching shoulders
- Letting baby drag down
- Tensing up

### Why It Matters

**Poor posture leads to:**
- Back pain
- Shoulder strain
- Neck tension
- Fatigue

---

## Front Carry

### When Appropriate

**Best for:**
- Newborns
- Younger babies
- Face-in position
- Bonding

### Posture Tips

**For front carrying:**
- Baby centered
- High on chest
- Don't lean back
- Core engaged

### As Baby Grows

**Heavier babies:**
- Consider hip or back carry
- May need different carrier
- Adjust technique

---

## Hip Carry

### When Appropriate

**Good for:**
- Older babies
- Quick ups and downs
- Some activities

### Posture Considerations

**Be careful of:**
- One-sided loading
- Hip popping out
- Leaning away
- Alternate sides

---

## Back Carry

### When Appropriate

**For:**
- Older babies with head control
- Heavier babies
- Longer wearing
- Activities

### Posture Benefits

**May be:**
- More natural weight distribution
- Better for heavier babies
- More like a backpack

### Getting Baby on Back

**Safely:**
- Practice with spotter
- Learn proper technique
- Start over soft surface
- Build confidence

---

## Duration and Breaks

### How Long

**Listen to body:**
- Take breaks as needed
- Build up duration
- Don't overdo it
- Regular position changes

### Signs to Stop

**Watch for:**
- Pain
- Fatigue
- Baby uncomfortable
- Need a break

---

## Exercises for Baby Wearing

### Before Wearing

**Preparation:**
- Core engagement practice
- Back strengthening
- Shoulder mobility
- Good baseline strength

### While Wearing

**Gentle options:**
- Walking
- Light housework
- Gentle movement
- Not strenuous exercise

### Recovery

**After wearing:**
- Stretch shoulders
- Thoracic mobility
- Rest if fatigued
- Counter-stretches

---

## Common Problems

### Lower Back Pain

**May indicate:**
- Carrying too low
- Leaning backward
- Weak core
- Need different carrier

**Solutions:**
- Carry higher
- Engage core
- Check fit
- Strengthen

### Shoulder Pain

**May indicate:**
- Straps too narrow
- Wrong adjustment
- Too much weight on shoulders
- Poor posture

**Solutions:**
- Adjust straps
- Check fit
- Consider different carrier
- Take breaks

### Neck Pain

**May indicate:**
- Looking down at baby
- Tension in shoulders
- Poor head position

**Solutions:**
- Trust baby is okay
- Relax shoulders
- Good posture
- Neck stretches

## Special Considerations

### Postpartum Body

- Core still recovering

- Build up gradually
- Listen to your body

### Multiple Children

- May need hands
