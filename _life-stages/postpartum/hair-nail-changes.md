# Postpartum Hair and Nail Changes

> **Your Whole Body Is Adjusting:** Hormone shifts after birth affect more than you might expect, including hair and nails.

---

## Why Changes Happen

### Hormonal Shifts

**After delivery:**
- Dramatic hormone changes
- Estrogen drops significantly
- Affects hair growth cycle
- Affects nail health

### Normal Process

**These changes:**
- Are very common
- Usually temporary
- Part of recovery
- Resolve over time

---

## Hair Changes

### Postpartum Hair Loss

**What happens:**
- Hair may fall out significantly
- Usually 2-4 months postpartum
- Can be alarming
- Actually normal

### Why It Happens

**During pregnancy:**
- Less hair fell out (estrogen effect)
- Hair seemed thicker
- After birth, it catches up
- Shedding accumulated hair

### What to Expect

**Timeline:**
- Peaks around 3-4 months postpartum
- Can last several months
- Usually resolves by 6-12 months
- Hair returns to normal

### What Helps

**Management:**
- Gentle handling
- Loose hairstyles
- Good nutrition
- Patience

---

## Nail Changes

### What You Might Notice

**Postpartum nails may:**
- Be brittle
- Break easily
- Have ridges
- Change texture

### Why It Happens

**Contributing factors:**
- Hormone changes
- Nutritional demands
- Stress on body
- Breastfeeding needs

### Improvement

**Over time:**
- Usually improves
- As hormones stabilize
- With good nutrition
- Patience needed

---

## Self-Care During Changes

### Be Gentle

**With hair:**
- Avoid harsh treatments
- Skip tight styles
- Gentle brushing
- Nourishing products

**With nails:**
- Keep trimmed
- Moisturize
- Protect from chemicals
- Gentle care

### Nutrition Matters

**Support recovery with:**
- Adequate protein
- Iron (especially if bled significantly)
- Vitamins
- Good overall nutrition

---

## Body Image

### These Changes Can Be Hard

**Emotionally:**
- Another thing changing
- May affect self-image
- Visible change
- Feels like loss

### Perspective

**Remember:**
- It's temporary
- It's normal
- Recovery takes time
- You're not alone

---

## When to Worry

### Normal vs. Concerning

**Normal:**
- Gradual hair loss
- Returns over time
- No bald patches

**See provider if:**
- Extreme loss
- Bald spots
- Not improving by 12 months
- Other symptoms
