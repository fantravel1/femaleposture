# Solo Parenting Posture: Managing Alone

> **When It's All on You:** Solo parenting means no one to hand the baby to when you're exhausted. Protecting your body is essential.

---

## The Solo Parent Challenge

### Physical Demands

**What's different:**
- No relief for tired muscles
- Every lift is yours
- Every night waking
- Constant physical demands
- No backup

### Why Posture Matters More

**When alone:**
- Injury is bigger problem
- Can't rest and recover
- Must keep functioning
- Prevention essential

---

## Managing Lifting

### Car Seat Challenges

**Every time:**
- Lifting in and out
- Awkward angles
- Heavy and growing
- No one to help

**Solutions:**
- Convertible seat stays in car
- Leave baby in seat, slide out
- Squat low, lift with legs
- Consider seat weight

### Crib and Bed

**Without help:**
- Lowering into crib
- Picking up at night
- Multiple times daily
- Strains back

**Strategies:**
- Crib at right height
- Bedside bassinet early
- Lower mattress as needed
- Proper form every time

### Bath Time

**Solo:**
- Lifting in and out
- Awkward positioning
- Wet, slippery baby
- Challenging

**Approaches:**
- Infant tub on counter height
- Kneel by regular tub
- Kitchen sink for newborns
- Supportive setup

---

## Night Duty Alone

### Every Waking Is Yours

**Reality:**
- No shifts
- No backup
- Cumulative exhaustion
- Affects everything

### Protecting Yourself

**Strategies:**
- Side-car bassinet (less lifting)
- Side-lying nursing if breastfeeding
- Safe co-sleeping options (research carefully)
- Minimize strain

### Morning After

**When exhausted:**
- Be extra careful with lifting
- Use support when sitting
- Slow down
- Forgive yourself

---

## All-Day Demands

### Wearing Baby

**Helpful for solo parents:**
- Hands free
- Baby close
- Can do tasks
- Distribute weight properly

### When Arms Are Tired

**Solutions:**
- Carrier or wrap
- Supportive seating
- Bouncer or swing (safely)
- Give yourself breaks

### Doing Everything Else

**While caring for baby:**
- Laundry, cooking, cleaning
- Multi-tasking constant
- Watch posture during tasks
- Take shortcuts that help

---

## Building in Breaks

### No Natural Breaks

**Without partner:**
- No "here, take baby"
- No natural relief
- Must create breaks

### Creating Rest

**Options:**
- Baby in safe space while you stretch
- Nap when baby naps
- Accepting help when offered
- Asking for help

### Accepting Help

**When offered:**
- Say yes
- Let others help
- No guilt
- Essential for survival

### Building Support

**Create network:**
- Family if available
- Friends
- Other parents
- Community resources

### Practical Help

**What helps most:**
- Someone to hold baby
- Help with tasks
- Meals
- Actual relief

### Finding Time

**Challenge:**
- When?
- How?
- With baby?
- Seems impossible

### Options

**Realistic:**
- Baby-wearing walks
- Stretch while baby plays
- Short bursts of movement
- Workout videos with baby
- Any movement counts

### Priority

**Essential:**
- Your health matters
- Prevent injury
- Stay functioning
- Model self-care

## Equipment That Helps

### Worth Investing

**For solo parents:**
- Good carrier
- Safe containment options
- Ergonomic setup
- Tools that reduce strain

### Saving Your Body

**Consider:**
- Items at proper height
- Equipment that helps
- Setup that reduces lifting
- Anything that saves your back

## When Injured

### Bigger Problem Alone

**If you hurt yourself:**
- Still have to function
- No one else takes over
- Healing harder
- Prevention critical

### Getting Help

**If injured:**
- Ask for emergency support
- Seek treatment
- Don't push through
- Allow healing
