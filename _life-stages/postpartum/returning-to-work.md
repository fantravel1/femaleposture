# Returning to Work After Baby: Posture Considerations

> **A New Challenge:** Returning to work postpartum brings unique physical demands. Your body is still recovering while managing new responsibilities.

---

## The Return Challenge

### What's Different

**After baby:**
- Body still recovering
- Sleep-deprived
- New routines
- Pumping if breastfeeding
- Emotional adjustment

### Physical Demands

**Compounding factors:**
- Work posture needs
- Baby care mornings/evenings
- Less time for self-care
- Exhaustion affects posture

---

## Workstation Review

### Before Returning

**Prepare:**
- Review your setup
- Request adjustments if needed
- Pumping space arranged
- Ergonomic needs addressed

### May Need Changes

**Consider:**
- Different chair if body changed
- Footrest if height shifted
- Lumbar support
- Updated setup

### Core Considerations

**Postpartum specific:**
- Core may still be weak
- Back needs support
- Comfort matters more
- See [Core Recovery](/life-stages/postpartum/core-recovery/)

---

## Pumping at Work

### The Physical Demands

**Pumping affects:**
- Break schedule
- Posture during pumping
- Energy levels
- Time management

### Pumping Posture

**While pumping:**
- Sit upright
- Good back support
- Relaxed shoulders
- Comfortable position

### Setup

**For comfort:**
- Chair that works
- Table height appropriate
- Hands-free option helpful
- Not hunched over

---

## Managing Exhaustion

### Sleep Deprivation Effects

**On posture:**
- Slouching increases
- Muscle fatigue
- Less awareness
- Everything harder

### Strategies

**To cope:**
- Support when sitting
- Regular breaks
- Movement helps alertness
- Be gentle with yourself

### Movement for Energy

**Quick boosts:**
- Short walks
- Stretching
- Stand when you can
- Any movement helps

---

## Morning and Evening

### Morning Rush

**Watch for:**
- Rushing affects posture
- Lifting car seat
- Hurrying
- Start day well

### Evening Tasks

**After work:**
- Carrying baby
- Childcare activities
- Exhaustion peak
- Still need awareness

---

## Desk Work Adaptations

### Frequent Breaks

**More important now:**
- Need more breaks
- Pumping creates structure
- Use for movement too
- Don't skip them

### Position Variety

**When possible:**
- Standing intervals
- Different seating
- Walking meetings
- Variety helps

### Core Engagement

**Throughout day:**
- Gentle activation
- Support while sitting
- Building back strength
- Sustainable approach

---

## Physical Activity

### Fitting It In

**Challenging but important:**
- Lunch walks
- Active commute if possible
- Evening exercise
- Any movement counts

### Benefits

**Exercise helps:**
- Energy
- Mood
- Physical recovery
- Stress management

### Realistic Expectations

**This phase:**
- Shorter workouts okay
- Consistency matters
- Something beats nothing
- Build gradually
