# Postpartum Pelvic Pain Recovery

> **Your Pelvis Worked Hard:** Childbirth is demanding on your pelvis. Pain after delivery is common but treatable.

---

## Why Pelvic Pain Occurs Postpartum

### Physical Causes

**After birth:**
- Pelvis separated for delivery
- Ligaments stretched
- Muscles strained
- Potential injury

### Types of Pain

**Common sources:**
- Pubic symphysis (front)
- Sacroiliac joints (back)
- Coccyx (tailbone)
- General pelvic floor

---

## Symphysis Pubis Dysfunction (SPD)

### What It Is

**Pubic joint pain:**
- Front of pelvis
- Where two sides meet
- May have separated
- Can be significant

### Symptoms

**You may notice:**
- Pain at pubic bone
- Difficulty walking
- Pain with leg movements
- Clicking or grinding

### Recovery

**Healing:**
- Usually improves weeks to months
- May need support
- Physical therapy helpful
- Gradual return to activity

---

## Sacroiliac Joint Pain

### What It Is

**Back of pelvis:**
- One or both SI joints
- Common postpartum
- Related to joint laxity
- May have been issue during pregnancy

### Symptoms

**Typical:**
- Lower back/buttock pain
- One side usually worse
- Pain with movement
- May radiate to thigh

### Treatment

**Approaches:**
- Physical therapy
- Support belt
- Gentle exercises
- Gradual improvement

---

## Tailbone Pain (Coccydynia)

### Causes

**After birth:**
- Bruising from delivery
- Fracture possible
- Pressure during birth
- Can be severe

### Symptoms

**Pain:**
- At tailbone
- Worse with sitting
- May be constant
- Very uncomfortable

### Relief

**What helps:**
- Donut/coccyx cushion
- Side-lying
- Avoid direct pressure
- Usually heals with time

---

## General Recovery Strategies

### Rest and Support

**Early weeks:**
- Rest when possible
- Use support aids
- Don't push too hard
- Allow healing

### Movement

**Appropriate activity:**
- Gentle walking as able
- Not bed rest entirely
- Balance rest and movement
- Progress gradually

### Positioning

**For comfort:**
- Sit on cushion if needed
- Keep legs together when turning in bed
- Support when nursing
- Mindful movement

---

## Physical Therapy

### When to Seek

**Consider PT if:**
- Pain significant
- Not improving
- Affecting function
- Need guidance

### What They Offer

**Treatment includes:**
- Assessment
- Targeted exercises
- Manual therapy
- Recovery plan

---

## Support Garments

### Pelvic Belts

**May help:**
- SI joint support
- Compression for stability
- Temporary use
- Professional fitting ideal

### When to Use

**Appropriate:**
- During activity
- Not 24/7
- As aid, not solution
- With exercises

---

## Exercises for Recovery

### Early Postpartum

**Gentle start:**
- Pelvic floor activation
- Gentle walking
- Basic movements
- Nothing painful

### As You Heal

**Progression:**
- Core rehabilitation
- Stability exercises
- Strength building
- See [Core Recovery](/life-stages/postpartum/core-recovery/)

### What to Avoid

**Initially:**
- Wide leg positions
- Heavy lifting
- High impact
- Asymmetrical loading

---

## Daily Life Modifications

### Getting Up

**Protect pelvis:**
- Keep legs together
- Roll to side
- Use arms
- Don't straddle

### Stairs

**If painful:**
- One step at a time
- Hold rail
- Lead with stronger leg
- Go slowly

### Lifting Baby

**Safe technique:**
- Feet together or close
- Bend knees
- Keep baby close
- Core engaged

---

## Timeline Expectations

### When Improvement Happens

**Typical:**
- Some improvement in weeks
- Significant improvement by 3-6 months
- Full recovery may take longer
- Individual variation

### When to Worry

**Seek help if:**
- No improvement by 6 weeks
- Severe ongoing pain
- Can't function
- Getting worse

---

## Long-Term Considerations

### Subsequent Pregnancies

**If history of pelvic pain:**
- May recur
- Early support helpful
- Physical therapy prevention
- Plan ahead
