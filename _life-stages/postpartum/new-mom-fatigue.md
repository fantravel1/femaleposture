# New Mom Fatigue and Posture

> **Exhaustion Is Real:** The fatigue of new motherhood affects everything, including how you carry your body. Protecting yourself matters.

---

## Why New Moms Are So Tired

### The Reality

**Contributing factors:**
- Broken sleep
- Healing from birth
- Hormonal changes
- Feeding demands
- Constant caregiving
- Emotional intensity

### It's Not Weakness

**Normal:**
- This level of tired is normal
- Your body is doing a lot
- It gets better
- Be gentle with yourself

---

## How Fatigue Affects Your Body

### Posture

**When exhausted:**
- Slouching increases
- Core engagement decreases
- Head forward
- Shoulders rounded
- Everything sags

### Pain

**Tiredness increases:**
- Pain perception
- Muscle fatigue
- Tension
- Discomfort

### Injury Risk

**More likely when tired:**
- Poor lifting mechanics
- Accidents
- Strain
- Not paying attention

---

## Protecting Yourself

### Support When Sitting

**Essential:**
- Use back support
- Don't rely on muscles
- Pillows help
- Let furniture work

### When Feeding Baby

**Nursing/bottle feeding:**
- Full support
- Bring baby to you
- Don't hunch over
- See [Breastfeeding Posture](/life-stages/postpartum/breastfeeding-posture/)

### When Holding Baby

**Carrying:**
- Carriers help
- Switch sides
- Support yourself
- See [Baby Carrying](/life-stages/postpartum/baby-carrying/)

---

## Safe Movement When Tired

### Slow Down

**When exhausted:**
- Move more slowly
- Think before lifting
- Be careful
- Don't rush

### Mindful Lifting

**Every time:**
- Bend knees
- Keep close
- Core engaged (as able)
- Never lift when really depleted

### Asking for Help

**Essential:**
- Accept help
- Ask for help
- Not weakness
- Necessary

---

## Rest Strategies

### Sleep When Baby Sleeps

**Classic advice:**
- Actually try it
- Even lying down helps
- Let housework wait
- Rest matters

### Night Feeding Posture

**During night feeds:**
- Comfortable chair
- Support everywhere
- Don't hunch
- Safe positioning

### Lying Down to Feed

**If safe for you:**
- Side-lying nursing
- Reduces energy output
- Must be done safely
- Learn proper technique

---

## Minimal Movement

### Gentle Movement

**Even when exhausted:**
- Short walks
- Gentle stretches
- Not exercise, just movement
- Helps energy and mood

### What to Skip

**Don't push:**
- No intense exercise
- No pushing through
- Listen to body
- This is temporary

---

## Posture Reminders

### When You Remember

**Quick check-ins:**
- Roll shoulders back
- Stand taller
- Breathe
- Brief awareness

### Don't Beat Yourself Up

**Reality:**
- Posture will suffer sometimes
- Do what you can
- Not permanent
- Survive this phase

---

## Getting Through

### This Phase Ends

**Remember:**
- Sleep will improve
- Energy will return
- Body will recover
- Not forever

### Timeline

**Typically:**
- Worst is first weeks/months
- Gradual improvement
- Variable by baby
- Be patient

---

## Warning Signs

### When to Seek Help

**Beyond normal fatigue:**
- Can't function at all
- Not improving
- Depression symptoms
- Concerning physical symptoms

### Postpartum Depression

**Fatigue can be sign:**
- Along with other symptoms
- Seek help if struggling
- Treatable
- See [Postpartum Mental Health](/mental-health/postpartum-mental-health/)

---

## Support Systems

### Accept Help

**What others can do:**
- Hold baby while you rest
- Do household tasks
- Bring food
- Give you breaks

### Partner/Family

**If available:**
- Share night duty
- Take shifts
- Teamwork
- Communicate needs
