# Partner Support Postpartum

> **Recovery from birth and adjusting to new parenthood is a team effort. Partners who understand the physical demands on new mothers can provide crucial support that helps prevent injury and promotes healing.**

---

## Understanding Her Physical State

Partners benefit from understanding what new mothers experience physically.

### What Her Body Has Been Through

**The reality:**
- Major physical event (birth)
- Sleep deprivation
- Hormonal shifts
- Healing from delivery
- New physical demands

### Ongoing Physical Demands

**Daily challenges:**
- Frequent feeding (breast or bottle)
- Constant lifting and carrying
- Disrupted sleep
- Limited recovery time
- New repetitive motions

---

## Sharing Physical Duties

### Carrying and Lifting

**How partners help:**
- Take turns carrying baby
- Handle heavy items
- Car seat lifting
- Stroller management
- Baby wearing sometimes

### Night Duties

**Supporting rest:**
- Share night wakings
- Bring baby for feeding
- Handle non-feeding needs
- Let her sleep when possible
- Recognize sleep's healing role

### Household Tasks

**Reducing her load:**
- Tasks requiring bending/lifting
- Grocery shopping and carrying
- Cleaning that strains body
- Errands and driving
- Whatever helps most

---

## Supporting Her Posture

### Observing and Mentioning

**Gentle awareness:**
- Notice hunching during feeding
- Observe tension patterns
- Gently suggest position changes
- Don't nag or criticize
- Supportive observations

### Creating Better Setups

**Environmental help:**
- Arrange nursing stations
- Place pillows strategically
- Set up changing table properly
- Organize diaper supplies
- Think about her body

### Encouraging Rest

**Recovery support:**
- Take baby for walks
- Cover duties so she can rest
- Encourage lying flat
- Support nap time
- Protect recovery time

---

## Feeding Support

### Breastfeeding Help

**Non-feeding support:**
- Bring water and snacks
- Position pillows
- Burp baby after
- Handle diaper changes
- Support her position

### Bottle Feeding

**Shared responsibility:**
- Take feeding turns
- Night bottle duties
- Practice good posture yourself
- Model proper positioning
- Reduce her repetitions

### Pumping Support

**If she pumps:**
- Help with equipment
- Handle storage tasks
- Feed pumped milk
- Understand the time it takes
- Appreciate the effort

---

## Physical Activity Support

### Encouraging Movement

**Gentle support:**
- Encourage walks together
- Take baby for her exercise time
- Support her return to activity
- Don't pressure or push
- Celebrate her efforts

### Exercising Together

**Shared activity:**
- Family walks
- Stroller walks together
- Gentle yoga together
- Active recovery
- Bonding through movement

### Respecting Limitations

**Understanding:**
- Recovery takes time
- Energy is limited
- Some days are harder
- Progress isn't linear
- Patience required

---

## Mental Load Awareness

### Reducing Decision Fatigue

**Taking initiative:**
- Handle meal planning
- Manage appointments
- Deal with household decisions
- Don't ask "what should I do?"
- Just do things

### Proactive Help

**Without being asked:**
- Notice what needs doing
- Take initiative
- Handle tasks completely
- Don't create more work
- Be observant

---

## Recovery Time

### Protecting Her Rest

**Creating opportunities:**
- Take baby for walks
- Handle evening duties
- Weekend morning shifts
- Visitors managed
- Rest prioritized

### Self-Care Support

**Enabling recovery:**
- Time for shower uninterrupted
- Opportunity to stretch or exercise
- Moments for herself
- Meal coverage
- True breaks

---

## Communication

### Checking In

**Asking about:**
- How is your body feeling?
- What hurts or is stiff?
- How can I help right now?
- What's most tiring?
- Open dialogue

### Listening

**Without fixing:**
- Let her express
- Validate experience
- Don't minimize
- Problem-solve together
- Support first

### Adjusting Together

**Ongoing conversation:**
- What's working
- What needs to change
- How to redistribute
- Evolving needs
- Partnership approach

---

## Partner Self-Care

### Your Own Posture

**Leading by example:**
- Model good form
- Take care of yourself
- You also have new demands
- Prevent your own injury
- Both parents matter

### Managing Your Energy

**Sustainable support:**
- You need rest too
- Take turns with breaks
- Ask for help from others
- Can't pour from empty cup
- Team effort

---

## When to Seek Help

### Signs She Needs More

**Watch for:**
- Persistent pain
- Unable to function
- Emotional struggles
- Overwhelming fatigue
- Not improving

### Professional Support

**Consider:**
- Physical therapy referral
- Lactation consultant
- Mental health support
- Postpartum doula
- Additional hands
