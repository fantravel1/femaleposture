# C-Section Recovery Posture

> **Recovering from a cesarean requires modified movement patterns to protect your incision while caring for your newborn. Understanding how to move safely speeds healing and prevents complications.**

---

## Early Recovery

The first days are critical.

### Hospital Phase

**Initial movement:**
- Nurse-assisted
- Very slow and careful
- Getting up challenging
- Incision protection
- Pain management

### First Movements

**What to expect:**
- Sitting up difficult
- Standing requires help
- Walking encouraged but slow
- Everything takes effort
- Gradual progress

---

## Protecting Your Incision

### Movement Modifications

**How to move:**
- Log-roll to sit up
- No abdominal crunching
- Splint when coughing
- Gentle movements
- Incision awareness

### Splinting Technique

**When needed:**
- Hold pillow against incision
- For coughing, sneezing, laughing
- Reduces strain
- Supports area
- Pain reduction

### What to Avoid

**Don't:**
- Sit straight up from lying
- Lift heavy items
- Strain abdominals
- Twist at waist
- Overexert

---

## Getting Out of Bed

### Log-Roll Technique

**Proper method:**
- Roll to side as unit
- Don't twist
- Bend knees
- Push up with arms
- Feet to floor together

### Step by Step

**Process:**
1. Bend knees while lying
2. Roll to side keeping body aligned
3. Use arms to push up
4. Lower feet to floor
5. Pause before standing

### Standing

**Rising:**
- Use support
- Stand slowly
- Pause for dizziness
- Hold onto something
- Steady yourself

---

## Sitting Posture

### Comfortable Sitting

**Best positions:**
- Supported back
- Feet on floor or elevated
- Pillow over incision for nursing
- Good posture when possible
- Whatever feels okay

### Nursing Positions

**C-section friendly:**
- Side-lying nursing
- Football hold
- Pillow support
- Protect incision
- Comfortable for both

---

## Walking

### Early Walking

**Why important:**
- Prevents blood clots
- Speeds healing
- Bowel function
- Essential recovery
- Encouraged early

### How to Walk

**Posture:**
- As upright as possible
- Don't hunch over incision
- Short shuffling okay initially
- Improves daily
- Gradual progress

### Building Distance

**Progression:**
- Start with room
- Hallway next
- Around ward
- Increase gradually
- Don't rush

---

## Lifting Restrictions

### What You Can Lift

**Guidelines (ask your provider):**
- Baby only initially
- Nothing heavier than baby
- Weeks before more
- Individual instructions
- Follow guidance

### Lifting Baby

**Safe technique:**
- Bend at knees
- Hold close to body
- Don't lift from arms extended
- Bring baby to you
- Minimize lifting

### Getting Help

**Essential:**
- Accept assistance
- Partner support
- Family help
- Not weakness
- Recovery need

---

## Stair Navigation

### When to Start

**Timing:**
- When provider approves
- May be before discharge
- Very carefully
- One at a time
- With support

### Technique

**How to:**
- One step at a time
- Use railing
- Go slowly
- Limit trips
- Conserve energy

---

## Core Recovery

### Timeline

**Progression:**
- No core work initially
- Provider clearance needed
- Gradual reactivation
- Months for full recovery
- Patience required

### Gentle Activation

**When approved:**
- Breathing exercises
- Gentle pelvic floor
- Very gentle core
- Progressive
- Professional guidance helpful

### What to Avoid

**Not yet:**
- Crunches or sit-ups
- Planks
- Heavy lifting
- Intense exercise
- Until cleared

---

## Pain Management

### How Pain Affects Posture

**Connection:**
- Pain causes guarding
- Hunching over
- Tension patterns
- Affects movement
- Manage for better posture

### Medication

**Importance:**
- Take as prescribed
- Don't try to tough it out
- Movement better with management
- Healing helped
- Follow instructions

---

## Week by Week

### Week 1

**Focus:**
- Basic movement
- Incision protection
- Accept help
- Rest
- Survival mode

### Weeks 2-4

**Progression:**
- More movement
- Less pain
- Building activity
- Still careful
- Improving daily

### Weeks 4-6

**Advancing:**
- More normal movement
- Walking longer
- Less protection needed
- Await clearance
- Healing well

### After 6 Weeks

**With clearance:**
- Gradual return to activity
- Exercise progression
- Still rebuilding
- Months to full recovery
- Patient approach

---

## When to Worry

### Warning Signs

**Seek help if:**
- Incision problems
- Fever
- Severe pain
- Bleeding
- Anything concerning

### Normal Discomfort

**Expected:**
- Soreness
- Tightness
- Gradual improvement
- Good and bad days
- Healing normal
