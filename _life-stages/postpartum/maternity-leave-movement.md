# Maternity Leave Movement

> **Maternity leave offers time to recover and bond, but gentle movement helps both physical and mental health. Finding ways to incorporate activity while caring for a newborn supports your recovery.**

---

## Movement During Recovery

Activity helps postpartum healing.

### Benefits of Movement

**Why move:**
- Improves mood
- Supports recovery
- Increases energy
- Prevents stiffness
- Mental health

### Starting Gently

**Early weeks:**
- Walking when ready
- Gentle stretching
- Light activity
- Not exercise yet
- Gradual return

---

## Walking with Baby

### Getting Started

**First walks:**
- Short distances
- Flat terrain
- Weather permitting
- When you feel ready
- Listen to body

### Building Up

**Progression:**
- Longer walks gradually
- Different routes
- Regular routine
- Stroller practice
- Increasing stamina

### Benefits

**What walking provides:**
- Fresh air
- Light exercise
- Change of scenery
- Baby sleeping often
- Mental break

---

## Movement at Home

### With Baby

**Together activity:**
- Gentle floor play
- Tummy time (you can stretch)
- Dancing with baby
- Baby-wearing walking
- Connected movement

### During Naps

**When baby sleeps:**
- Gentle stretching
- Light exercise
- Movement you need
- Or rest instead
- Your choice

### Throughout Day

**Integrated:**
- Movement while holding
- Stretches between feeds
- Walking around house
- Not sedentary all day
- Built-in activity

## Classes and Groups

### Mom and Baby Options

**Community:**
- Stroller fit classes
- Mom and baby yoga
- Walking groups
- Social and active
- Support system

### What to Look For

**Good classes:**
- Postpartum appropriate
- Baby-friendly environment
- Qualified instructors
- Safe progression
- Welcoming

### Benefits Beyond Exercise

**Social aspect:**
- Meet other moms
- Support network
- Getting out
- Adult interaction
- Community building

---

## When to Start What

### Early Weeks (1-2)

**Focus:**
- Basic walking
- Gentle stretching
- Very light
- Recovery priority
- Don't push

### Weeks 3-6

**Increasing:**
- Longer walks
- More stretching
- Light activity
- Still careful
- Building gradually

### After Clearance (6+ weeks)

**With approval:**
- Gradual exercise return
- Classes possible
- More variety
- Progressive loading
- Full activity eventually

---

## Practical Challenges

### Time

**Finding it:**
- Brief sessions okay
- During naps maybe
- Baby-wearing counts
- Integrated activity
- Creative scheduling

### Fatigue

**When exhausted:**
- Rest if needed
- Short walks still help
- Don't force
- Listen to body
- Some days rest

### Weather

**Indoor options:**
- Home stretching
- Online videos
- Mall walking
- Indoor movement
- Options exist

## Baby-Wearing Benefits

### Movement with Baby

**Wearing baby:**
- Hands-free walking
- Baby often content
- Activity together
- Bonding bonus
- Practical solution

### Posture Considerations

**While wearing:**
- Proper carrier fit
- Good posture maintained
- Not too long initially
- Build tolerance
- Body mechanics

---

## Energy Management

### Realistic Expectations

**Understand:**
- Recovery takes energy
- Breastfeeding takes energy
- Sleep deprivation real
- Not full fitness yet
- Patience

### Fueling Activity

**Support movement:**
- Eat adequately
- Stay hydrated
- Not restricting
- Energy needs high
- Fuel the body

## Mental Health Benefits

### Why Movement Helps

**Psychological:**
- Mood improvement
- Anxiety reduction
- Sense of accomplishment
- Time for self
- Stress relief

### When Struggling

**If mood very low:**
- Gentle movement may help
- Seek professional support
- Don't force intense activity
- Mental health priority
- Get help if needed

- Bag ready
- Stroller accessible

- Route planned
- Minimal barriers

### Flexible Approach

**Reality of baby:**
- Plans change
- Flexibility needed
