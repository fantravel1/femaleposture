# Pelvic Organ Prolapse Postpartum

> **Pelvic organ prolapse affects many women after childbirth, yet it's often not discussed. Understanding this condition, its prevention and treatment, empowers you to seek help and recover.**

---

## What Is Pelvic Organ Prolapse

Prolapse occurs when pelvic organs descend due to weakened support.

### What Happens

**Anatomically:**
- Pelvic floor muscles stretched/weakened
- Ligaments damaged
- Organs descend from normal position
- Various degrees of severity
- One or more organs affected

### Types

**Different prolapses:**
- Bladder (cystocele)
- Rectum (rectocele)
- Uterus (uterine prolapse)
- Vaginal vault (after hysterectomy)
- May have more than one

---

## Risk Factors

### Birth-Related

**Delivery factors:**
- Vaginal delivery
- Large baby
- Prolonged pushing
- Forceps or vacuum
- Multiple vaginal births

### Other Factors

**Contributing:**
- Chronic coughing
- Heavy lifting
- Constipation/straining
- Obesity
- Genetics

---

## Symptoms

### What You Might Feel

**Common symptoms:**
- Heaviness or pressure in pelvis
- Something "falling out" sensation
- Bulge you can see or feel
- Dragging sensation
- Worsens with standing

### Related Symptoms

**Associated:**
- Urinary symptoms
- Bowel difficulties
- Discomfort with intimacy
- Low back pain
- Improves when lying down

### When to Seek Help

**Don't wait if:**
- Any of above symptoms
- Visible bulge
- Significant discomfort
- Affecting quality of life
- Sooner is better

---

## Posture and Prolapse

### How Posture Affects It

**Connection:**
- Posture affects pressure on floor
- Poor posture increases downward force
- Alignment matters
- Standing posture significant
- Daily positioning important

### Protective Posture

**What helps:**
- Tall, aligned standing
- Avoid slumping
- Proper lifting technique
- Core engagement (gentle)
- Reducing downward pressure

---

## Prevention

### During Pregnancy

**Protective steps:**
- Pelvic floor exercises
- Avoid excessive straining
- Manage constipation
- Know risk factors
- Prepare body

### During Birth

**When possible:**
- Discuss with provider
- Positioning options
- Controlled pushing if possible
- Provider skill matters
- Not all controllable

### Postpartum

**Prevention focus:**
- Pelvic floor rehabilitation
- Avoid heavy lifting early
- Gradual return to activity
- Address constipation
- Proper recovery

---

## Early Postpartum

### What's Normal

**Initial period:**
- Some heaviness common
- May improve with healing
- Pelvic floor recovering
- Give it time
- Monitor symptoms

### When It's Prolapse

**Persistent symptoms:**
- Not improving with time
- Worsening
- Significant symptoms
- Seek evaluation
- Early intervention helps

---

## Treatment Options

### Conservative

**Non-surgical:**
- Pelvic floor physical therapy
- Pessary (support device)
- Lifestyle modifications
- Often very effective
- First-line treatment

### Physical Therapy

**What it involves:**
- Pelvic floor assessment
- Muscle training
- Coordination work
- Lifestyle education
- Highly effective

### Pessary

**Support device:**
- Inserted to support organs
- Various types
- Fitted by provider
- Managed over time
- Can be long-term solution

### Surgery

**When needed:**
- If conservative fails
- Significant symptoms
- Various procedures
- Usually not immediate postpartum
- Waiting often recommended

---

## Living with Prolapse

### Daily Management

**Strategies:**
- Supportive posture
- Avoid heavy lifting
- Manage constipation
- Regular pelvic floor exercises
- Activity modification

### Exercise

**What's safe:**
- Walking usually fine
- Low-impact activities
- Pelvic floor-friendly exercise
- May need modifications
- Physical therapy guidance

### What to Avoid

**Problematic activities:**
- Heavy lifting
- High impact
- Straining
- Activities that worsen symptoms
- Until improved
