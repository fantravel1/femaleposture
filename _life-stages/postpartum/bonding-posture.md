# Bonding Activities and Posture

> **The precious time spent bonding with your new baby involves lots of close contact holding, wearing, and gazing. Doing this comfortably protects your recovering body while nurturing your connection.**

---

## Why Bonding Positions Matter

Early bonding involves extensive holding and contact.

### The Physical Reality

**What bonding involves:**
- Hours of holding
- Skin-to-skin contact
- Gazing at baby
- Soothing and rocking
- Repetitive positions

### Protecting Yourself

**Balance needed:**
- Bond without strain
- Sustainable positions
- Comfort for both
- Long-term body health
- Enjoy this time

---

## Skin-to-Skin Contact

### Benefits

**Why it's important:**
- Temperature regulation
- Bonding hormones
- Breastfeeding support
- Baby calm and secure
- Parent benefits too

### Comfortable Positions

**Doing it right:**
- Reclined position supported
- Baby on chest
- Pillows for arms
- Not hunched over
- Relaxed posture

### Extended Skin-to-Skin

**Longer sessions:**
- Comfortable seating essential
- Full back support
- Arms not holding weight unsupported
- Change positions periodically
- Still magical when comfortable

---

## Holding and Gazing

### The Newborn Gaze

**Spending time:**
- Looking at baby's face
- Baby gazes back
- Essential bonding
- Easy to hunch over
- Watch your neck

### Supported Gazing

**Better positions:**
- Bring baby to you
- Pillow in lap
- Back supported
- Neck neutral
- Eye level when possible

### Rocking and Soothing

**When baby needs comfort:**
- Rock from hips, not back
- Core engaged gently
- Vary holding positions
- Sit when possible
- Sustainable movements

---

## Babywearing for Bonding

### Benefits of Wearing

**Why wear:**
- Hands-free bonding
- Continued closeness
- Baby content
- Can do light tasks
- Transitions easier

### Carrier Choice

**What matters:**
- Proper fit for you
- Baby positioned well
- Weight distributed
- Comfortable for extended wear
- Learn proper use

### Wearing Posture

**While babywearing:**
- Stand tall
- Don't lean back
- Core engaged
- Shoulders relaxed
- Adjust as needed

---

## Nighttime Closeness

### Bedside Care

**Night feeding and soothing:**
- Good posture even at night
- Support when holding
- Don't slump in exhaustion
- Feeding station set up
- Make it sustainable

### Co-Sleeping If Chosen

**If safely co-sleeping:**
- Side-lying nursing
- Proper bed setup
- Back support
- Safe sleep guidelines followed
- Position for comfort

## Multiple Children Bonding

### When There Are Siblings

**Including older children:**
- Holding both sometimes
- Managing positions
- Reading with baby
- Floor time together
- Balancing attention

### Sibling Holding Help

**When siblings hold baby:**
- Supervised always
- Supported positions
- Your posture while supervising
- Comfortable for all
- Shared bonding

## Partner Bonding Support

### Sharing the Holding

**Trading off:**
- Partner takes baby
- Your body gets break
- Bonding for both
- Different holding styles
- Team approach

### Supporting Each Other

**Physical help:**
- Adjusting pillows
- Bringing baby to you
- Taking over when you're tired
- Noticing discomfort
- Active support

## Bath Time Bonding

### Bathing Baby

**Physical demands:**
- Bending over tub
- Holding slippery baby
- Kneeling on floor
- Supporting in sink
- Protect your back

### Better Positions

**Bath time setup:**
- Appropriate height tub
- Kneeling pad
- Counter-height basins
- Sit when possible
- Brief activity

### Joint Bathing

**Bathing with baby:**
- Tub entry and exit safely
- Holding baby securely
- Partner handoff
- Careful movements
- Special bonding time

---

## Physical Recovery and Bonding

### Balancing Needs

**Both matter:**
- Bond extensively
- But recover too
- Comfort enables bonding
- Pain interferes
- Take care of yourself

### When to Put Baby Down

**It's okay:**
- Safe sleep space
- Brief breaks
- Stretch and move
- Rest your body
- Baby is fine

### Signs of Strain

**Watch for:**
- Neck pain
- Back ache
- Shoulder tension
- Arm fatigue
- Adjust when noticed

## Special Circumstances

### NICU Bonding

**In hospital:**
- Chair positions
- Kangaroo care positions
- Limited control over setup
- Ask for support
- Do what you can

### Twins or Multiples

**Double the bonding:**
- Tandem holds
- Taking turns
- Support essential
- Physical demands higher
- Accept all help
