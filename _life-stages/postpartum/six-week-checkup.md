# Six-Week Postpartum Checkup

> **The six-week checkup marks an important milestone in your postpartum recovery. Knowing what to expect and what to ask helps you get the most from this appointment.**

---

## What Happens at This Visit

The six-week checkup assesses your physical recovery from birth.

### Standard Examination

**What provider checks:**
- Healing of any tears or incisions
- Uterus returning to normal size
- Bleeding has stopped or minimal
- Blood pressure check
- Overall physical assessment

### What You'll Discuss

**Common topics:**
- Contraception plans
- Resuming intimacy
- Exercise clearance
- Breastfeeding if applicable
- Emotional wellbeing check

---

## Questions to Ask About Your Body

### Exercise and Activity

**Ask your provider:**
- "Am I cleared for exercise?"
- "What activities should I start with?"
- "Are there movements I should avoid?"
- "How do I know if I'm doing too much?"
- "When can I return to running/lifting/etc?"

### Posture and Core

**Physical concerns to raise:**
- "How is my diastasis recti?" (ask them to check)
- "Should I see a pelvic floor therapist?"
- "Is my posture concern related to birth?"
- "When can I resume core exercises?"
- "Are my lingering pains normal?"

### Pelvic Floor

**Important questions:**
- "How is my pelvic floor healing?"
- "When can I safely do Kegels?"
- "Is incontinence I'm experiencing normal?"
- "Should I see a specialist?"
- "When should symptoms improve?"

## Preparing for the Appointment

### Track Your Symptoms

**What to note beforehand:**
- Any pain and its location
- Bladder or bowel issues
- Bleeding or discharge status
- Energy levels
- Mood concerns

### Bringing Your Concerns

**Make a list:**
- Write down all questions
- Note physical symptoms
- Record any concerns
- Include baby-related feeding issues
- Don't rely on memory

### Practical Preparations

**Appointment logistics:**
- Arrange baby care or bring baby
- Wear comfortable clothes
- Bring pad (may have internal exam)
- Allow time for questions
- Have support person if wanted

---

## What "Cleared for Exercise" Really Means

### Understanding Clearance

**What it does and doesn't mean:**
- General healing has occurred
- Starting point, not finish line
- Begin gradually
- Listen to your body
- Not a green light for everything

### Starting Exercise After Clearance

**Smart progression:**
- Walking first
- Gentle pelvic floor exercises
- Core rehabilitation exercises
- Build slowly over weeks
- Not back to pre-pregnancy intensity yet

### Warning Signs to Watch

**Stop and reassess if:**
- Pain during or after exercise
- Bleeding resumes or increases
- Feeling of heaviness or pressure
- Leaking urine significantly
- Anything feels wrong

---

## Advocating for Yourself

### If Something Feels Wrong

**Trust your instincts:**
- Speak up about concerns
- Ask for referrals if needed
- Request physical therapy evaluation
- Don't accept "just do Kegels"
- Your symptoms matter

### Requesting Specialist Referrals

**When to ask for more:**
- Ongoing pelvic floor issues
- Diastasis recti concerns
- Significant incontinence
- Pain with movement
- Any lingering problems

### Follow-Up Appointments

**Beyond six weeks:**
- Schedule follow-up if concerns
- Pelvic floor PT if referred
- Mental health support if needed
- Ongoing care matters
- Six weeks is just the start

---

## Mental Health Screening

### What to Expect

**Emotional wellness check:**
- Questionnaire often provided
- Discussion of mood
- Sleep and functioning
- Screening for PPD/PPA
- Resources available

### Being Honest

**Speak openly about:**
- How you're really feeling
- Difficulty bonding if present
- Intrusive thoughts
- Anxiety symptoms
- Any concerns

---

## After the Appointment

### Processing Information

**Following up:**
- Review any instructions
- Fill prescriptions if given
- Schedule referrals
- Start approved exercises
- Note any remaining questions

### Creating Your Plan

**Moving forward:**
- Gradual return to activity
- Addressing any issues identified
- Self-care continuation
- Follow-up appointments as needed
- Patience with ongoing recovery

---

## If This Appointment Feels Rushed

### Common Experience

**Reality of postpartum care:**
- Appointments can be quick
- Many women feel dismissed
- You deserve thorough care
- Advocate for yourself
- Seek additional support if needed

### Getting the Care You Need

**Options available:**
- Request longer appointment
- Schedule follow-up for remaining questions
- Seek second opinion if needed
- Find pelvic floor PT independently
- Postpartum doula support
