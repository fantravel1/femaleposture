# Postpartum Back Pain

> **After the Birth, the Ache Continues:** Many new mothers are surprised when back pain persists after delivery. Understanding why—and what helps—gets you back to feeling yourself.

---

## Why Postpartum Back Pain Happens

### Pregnancy Effects Linger

**What changed:**
- Ligaments stretched (relaxin still present)
- Abdominal muscles weakened/separated
- Core stability reduced
- Spinal curves changed
- These don't resolve immediately

### New Physical Demands

**Caring for baby:**
- Constant lifting and holding
- Feeding positions
- Carrying car seat
- Bending over cribs and changing tables
- Sleep deprivation affecting everything

### Birth Recovery

**Delivery effects:**
- Epidural site soreness (temporary)
- C-section recovery affects core
- Pushing strain
- Overall physical exhaustion

---

## Types of Postpartum Back Pain

### Lower Back Pain

**Most common:**
- Related to weakened core
- Postural changes from pregnancy
- New mom activities
- May worsen with standing or sitting

### SI Joint Pain

**Pelvis-related:**
- Joints loosened by relaxin
- Asymmetrical activities (baby on hip)
- May have been present in pregnancy
- Often improves as hormones normalize

### Upper Back Pain

**Related to:**
- Breastfeeding postures
- Hunching over baby
- Carrying and holding
- Sleep positioning

---

## Timeline

### First Weeks

**Immediately postpartum:**
- Some pain normal as body recovers
- Weakness is expected
- Focus on rest and healing
- Gentle movement when ready

### Weeks 2-6

**Early weeks:**
- Gradual improvement expected
- Assess for diastasis recti
- Begin gentle core work (when cleared)
- Posture awareness helps

### 6+ Weeks

**Longer term:**
- Should be improving
- If not, seek assessment
- PT can help
- Core rebuilding focus

---

## Immediate Relief Strategies

### Positioning

**What helps:**
- Lumbar support when sitting
- Avoid prolonged positions
- Change positions frequently
- Side-lying when resting

### Ice and Heat

**Application:**
- Ice for acute pain/inflammation
- Heat for muscle tension
- 15-20 minutes at a time
- Whatever feels better

### Pain Management

**Options:**
- Over-the-counter pain relievers (check if breastfeeding)
- Rest when possible
- Gentle movement
- Discuss with provider if severe

## Baby Care Modifications

### Feeding

**Posture protection:**
- Proper breastfeeding setup (pillows)
- Bring baby to you
- Support your back
- See [Breastfeeding Posture](/life-stages/postpartum/breastfeeding-posture/)

### Lifting Baby

**Every time:**
- Get close first
- Lift with legs
- Don't twist
- Alternate hip carrying

### Crib and Changing Table

**Reduce bending:**
- Proper changing table height
- Kneel for floor-level access
- Use drop-side techniques safely
- Don't lean way over crib

### Car Seat

**The heavy culprit:**
- Don't carry in car seat far
- Take baby out, then carry
- Alternate arms
- Leave base in car

## Rebuilding Core

### Why It Matters

**Core weakness contributes to:**
- Lower back pain
- Poor posture
- Continued problems
- Delayed recovery

### Safe Starting Points

**Early postpartum (when cleared):**
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Gentle pelvic floor activation
- Core connection exercises
- Progress gradually

### Diastasis Recti

**Check for:**
- Abdominal separation
- May require specific approach
- See [Diastasis Recti](/life-stages/postpartum/diastasis-recti/)
- PT assessment helpful

---

## When to Seek Help

### Signs to See Provider

**Get assessed if:**
- Pain severe or worsening
- Numbness or tingling
- Weakness in legs
- Bladder/bowel issues
- Not improving as expected
- Significantly affecting function

### Physical Therapy

**PT can help:**
- Assess what's causing pain
- Specific exercise prescription
- Manual therapy
- Postural guidance
- Diastasis assessment
- Return to activity planning

---

## Movement and Exercise

### Return to Exercise

**Progression:**
- Walking often first activity
- Core restoration before intensity
- Gradual return to exercise
- See [Postpartum Exercise Timeline](/life-stages/postpartum/exercise-timeline/)

### What to Avoid Initially

**Wait until ready:**
- High-impact exercise
- Heavy lifting
- Intense core work
- Running (usually 12+ weeks)

### Helpful Activities

**Once cleared:**
- Walking
- Gentle stretching
- Swimming (after bleeding stops)
- Postpartum yoga
- Gradual strength building

---

## Posture Throughout the Day

### Feeding

**Whether breast or bottle:**
- Good support setup
- Bring baby to you
- Don't hunch for extended periods
- See specific guides

### Baby Wearing

**Can help or hurt:**
- Proper carrier use essential
- Baby positioned correctly
- Don't wear when pain increases
- Good option if done right

### Sleep

**Challenging with newborn:**
- Best position when you can
- Support when side-lying
- Move carefully getting in/out
- Prioritize any sleep you can get

---

## Self-Care Strategies

### When Baby Sleeps

**Sometimes you should:**
- Rest instead of chores
- Stretch gently
- Practice core exercises
- Prioritize your recovery

### Partner Support

**Ask for help with:**
- Night feeds if possible
- Heavy lifting
- Household tasks
- Getting rest

---

## Long-Term Prevention

### Address Now, Benefit Later

**Investment in recovery:**
- Core restoration
- Posture attention
- Proper body mechanics
- Sets foundation for future

### Future Pregnancies

**If planning more:**
- Better core = better pregnancy
- Resolved pain won't carry over as much
- Foundation for next time
- Recovery matters
