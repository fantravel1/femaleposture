# Physical Aspects of Weaning

> **Weaning brings physical changes as your body transitions from breastfeeding. Understanding what to expect and how to manage discomfort supports this transition.**

---

## Physical Changes

Weaning affects your body.

### Breast Changes

**What happens:**
- Engorgement possible
- Gradual milk reduction
- Size changes
- Tissue changes
- Adjustment period

### Hormonal Shifts

**Significant:**
- Prolactin decreases
- Hormones shifting
- Mood effects possible
- Menstruation may return
- Body recalibrating

---

## Managing Engorgement

### If Weaning Gradually

**Usually minimal:**
- Gradual reduction
- Body adjusts
- Less engorgement
- Preferred approach
- Easier transition

### If Weaning Quickly

**More challenging:**
- Engorgement likely
- Discomfort significant
- Careful management
- Medical guidance helpful
- Harder physically

### Relief Strategies

**What helps:**
- Cold compresses
- Supportive bra
- Express only for comfort
- Cabbage leaves (folk remedy)
- Pain relief if needed

### Nighttime Shifts

**When night feeds end:**
- May sleep longer
- Body adjusting
- Initial discomfort possible
- Eventual improvement
- Better rest

- Comfortable fit

- Support helps

### After Weaning

- Size may change

## Timeline

### Gradual Weaning

**What to expect:**
- Weeks to months
- Slow reduction
- Body adjusts gradually
- Less dramatic
- Easier physically

### Quick Weaning

**Different experience:**
- More intense symptoms
- Quicker resolution
- More discomfort
- Medical support helpful
- Harder but faster

### Milk Drying Up

**Duration:**
- Varies widely
- Weeks typically
- Sometimes longer
- Individual variation
- Patience needed

## When to Seek Help

### Medical Consultation

**Seek help if:**
- Signs of mastitis
- Fever
- Severe pain
- Depression symptoms
- Concerning symptoms

### Normal vs. Concerning

**Typical:**
- Mild engorgement
- Gradual changes
- Manageable discomfort
- Improving over time
- Normal transition
