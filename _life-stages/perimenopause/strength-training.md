# Strength Training During Perimenopause

> **As hormones shift during perimenopause, strength training becomes more important than ever for maintaining muscle, supporting bone health, managing weight, and preserving posture.**

---

## Why Strength Training Matters Now

Perimenopause brings changes that make resistance training essential.

### What Happens Without It

**Age-related changes:**
- Muscle mass declines (sarcopenia begins)
- Bone density decreases
- Metabolism slows
- Posture weakens
- Functional strength diminishes

### Benefits of Strength Training

**What you gain:**
- Preserve and build muscle
- Support bone health
- Boost metabolism
- Improve posture
- Maintain independence
- Better mood and sleep

---

## Getting Started

### If You're New to Weights

**Beginning safely:**
- Start with bodyweight exercises
- Learn proper form first
- Progress gradually
- Consider working with trainer
- No shame in starting light

### If You've Been Strength Training

**Adapting for perimenopause:**
- May need recovery adjustments
- Prioritize certain exercises
- Listen to body more
- Hormonal fluctuations affect performance
- Consistency matters most

---

## Key Exercises for Posture

### Back Strengthening

**Counter forward rounding:**
- Rows (bent over, cable, machine)
- Lat pulldowns
- Reverse flyes
- Face pulls
- Back extensions

### Core Strengthening

**Support your spine:**
- Planks and variations
- Dead bugs
- Bird dogs
- Pallof press
- Anti-rotation exercises

### Lower Body

**Foundation of posture:**
- Squats (goblet, back, front)
- Lunges
- Deadlifts (Romanian, conventional)
- Glute bridges
- Step-ups

### Upper Body

**Balanced strength:**
- Chest press and push-ups
- Shoulder press
- Tricep work
- Bicep curls
- Balanced development

---

## Bone-Building Focus

### Why Bones Need Loading

**Bone responds to stress:**
- Weight-bearing exercise stimulates bone
- Resistance creates healthy stress
- Prevention of osteoporosis
- Critical during perimenopause
- Investment in future

### Best for Bones

**Bone-loading exercises:**
- Squats and lunges
- Deadlifts
- Step-ups
- Standing exercises
- Impact (jumping) if appropriate

### Areas to Focus

**Common bone loss areas:**
- Spine (spinal loading exercises)
- Hips (hip hinging movements)
- Wrists (gripping, weight-bearing)
- Targeted strengthening

---

## Programming Considerations

### Frequency

**How often:**
- 2-4 sessions per week
- Allow recovery between sessions
- Consistency over perfection
- Adjust based on energy
- Something is better than nothing

### Intensity

**How hard:**
- Challenging but sustainable
- Progressive overload over time
- Not to failure every set
- Moderate to heavy weights
- Quality repetitions

### Recovery

**More important now:**
- May need longer between sessions
- Sleep affects recovery significantly
- Nutrition supports rebuilding
- Listen to body signals
- Adjust as symptoms fluctuate

---

## Adapting to Symptoms

### Fatigue Days

**When energy is low:**
- Shorter session
- Lighter weights acceptable
- Something is better than skipping
- Modify but don't abandon
- Energy often increases after starting

### Joint Stiffness

**Working with stiff joints:**
- Longer warm-up
- Full range of motion when possible
- May need exercise modifications
- Strength builds support around joints
- Address underlying issues

### Hot Flashes

**During symptoms:**
- Cool environment if possible
- Layer clothing
- Water accessible
- Modify but continue
- Symptoms often improve with exercise

### Brain Fog

**When focus is difficult:**
- Familiar routine helps
- Written workout
- Simpler exercises
- Autopilot workout
- Show up anyway

---

## Form and Safety

### Learning Proper Form

**Foundation matters:**
- Study technique for each exercise
- Video resources available
- Personal trainer beneficial
- Mirror or recording for feedback
- Form before weight

### Common Form Issues

**Watch for:**
- Rounding lower back
- Knee tracking incorrectly
- Neck strain during exercises
- Holding breath
- Rushing repetitions

### Injury Prevention

**Stay safe:**
- Warm up before lifting
- Progress gradually
- Don't ego lift
- Stop if pain occurs
- Address issues early
