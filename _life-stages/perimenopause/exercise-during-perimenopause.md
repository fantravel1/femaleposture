# Exercise During Perimenopause

> **Your Body Is Changingâ€”So Should Your Approach:** Exercise remains essential during perimenopause, but how you exercise may need to adapt.

---

## Why Exercise Matters Now

### Essential Benefits

**During perimenopause:**
- Manages symptoms
- Protects bone density
- Maintains muscle mass
- Supports mood
- Helps sleep
- Controls weight distribution

### The Challenge

**What's different:**
- Energy fluctuates
- Recovery may be slower
- Some activities harder
- Motivation varies

---

## Changes You Might Notice

### Energy Variability

**Common experience:**
- Good days and bad days
- Unpredictable energy
- May need to modify intensity
- Flexibility required

### Recovery Differences

**You may find:**
- Need more rest between sessions
- Soreness lasts longer
- Harder to bounce back
- Adjust accordingly

### Body Composition

**Shifts happening:**
- Muscle harder to maintain
- Weight distribution changing
- Metabolism slowing
- Exercise helps counter this

---

## Priority: Strength Training

### Why It's Essential

**Most important now:**
- Preserves muscle mass
- Supports metabolism
- Protects bone density
- Maintains function

### How Much

**Recommendations:**
- 2-3 times per week
- All major muscle groups
- Progressive challenge
- Consistency matters

### Getting Started

**If new to it:**
- Start lighter
- Learn proper form
- Progress gradually
- Consider instruction

---

## Cardiovascular Exercise

### Still Important

**Benefits:**
- Heart health
- Mood support
- Energy
- Weight management

### Adjustments

**Consider:**
- Moderate intensity often
- High intensity in moderation
- Listen to your body
- Recovery matters

### Options

**Good choices:**
- Walking (always beneficial)
- Swimming
- Cycling
- Dancing
- What you enjoy

---

## Flexibility and Balance

### Increased Importance

**Why now:**
- Joints may stiffen
- Balance can decline
- Injury prevention
- Maintains function

### What to Include

**Regular practice:**
- Daily stretching
- Yoga or Pilates
- Balance exercises
- Mobility work

---

## Managing Symptoms with Exercise

### Hot Flashes

**What helps:**
- Regular exercise reduces frequency
- Avoid overheating
- Cool environment
- Stay hydrated

### Sleep Issues

**Exercise can help:**
- Better sleep quality
- Not too close to bedtime
- Consistent timing
- Regular practice

### Mood Changes

**Physical activity:**
- Reduces anxiety
- Improves depression
- Stress relief
- Endorphin boost

---

## Adapting to Fluctuations

### Variable Energy Days

**Strategy:**
- Listen to your body
- Modify as needed
- Something is better than nothing
- Don't give up on hard days

### When to Push, When to Rest

**Learning to gauge:**
- Gentle movement often helps
- But don't ignore true fatigue
- Rest is productive too
- Find balance

### Menstrual Changes

**During perimenopause:**
- Periods may be unpredictable
- May affect exercise
- Adapt as needed
- See [Period and Posture](/life-stages/teen/period-posture/)

---

## Common Mistakes

### Overdoing It

**Watch for:**
- Trying to do what you used to
- Not allowing recovery
- Ignoring signals
- Burnout

### Underdoing It

**Also a problem:**
- Giving up when hard
- Using symptoms as excuse
- Becoming sedentary
- Losing fitness

### Finding Balance

**Goal:**
- Consistent practice
- Appropriate intensity
- Recovery included
- Sustainable approach

---

## Building Your Routine

### Weekly Structure

**Example:**
- 2-3 strength sessions
- 2-3 cardio sessions
- Daily movement
- Flexibility work
- Rest as needed

### Making It Work

**Practical tips:**
- Schedule like appointment
- Have backup plans
- Shorter sessions okay
- Consistency over perfection

- Bone loss accelerates

- Resistance training crucial

### Best for Bones

- Strength training
