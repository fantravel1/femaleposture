# Study Posture for Teens

> **Hours of Homework:** You spend a lot of time studying. The way you sit matters more than you think.

---

## Why Study Posture Matters

### The Reality

**You're spending:**
- Hours on homework
- More hours on screens
- Time studying for tests
- Years of this pattern

### What's at Stake

**Poor study posture leads to:**
- Neck pain
- Back ache
- Headaches
- Fatigue
- Difficulty concentrating

---

## The Ideal Setup

### Desk and Chair

**Basic requirements:**
- Chair that supports your back
- Desk at right height
- Feet flat on floor (or footrest)
- Room to move

### Screen Position

**If using computer:**
- Screen at eye level
- About arm's length away
- Not looking down
- Reduces neck strain

### Lighting

**For studying:**
- Good overall light
- Task light for papers
- No glare on screen
- Reduces eye strain

---

## Common Mistakes

### The Bed Study

**Why it's bad:**
- No support
- Terrible posture
- Makes you sleepy
- Affects sleep later

### The Couch Slump

**Problems:**
- Slouching
- Neck forward
- Legs up = back curves
- Not sustainable

### The Floor Hunch

**Issues:**
- No back support
- Neck bent down
- Legs uncomfortable
- Hard surfaces

---

## If You Don't Have a Desk

### Making It Work

**Alternatives:**
- Kitchen table
- Dining table
- Lap desk improvements
- Library (has desks)

### Improving Any Spot

**Basic fixes:**
- Back support (pillow)
- Screen/book raised
- Good lighting
- Frequent position changes

---

## Taking Breaks

### Why Breaks Matter

**Your body needs:**
- Movement
- Position change
- Eye rest
- Mental reset

### The 25/5 Rule

**Every 25 minutes:**
- Stand up
- Stretch
- Move around
- 5 minute break

### What to Do on Breaks

**Options:**
- Walk around
- Stretch
- Get a snack
- Look at distance (rest eyes)

---

## Stretches for Studying

### Neck Rolls

**Quick relief:**
- Drop chin to chest
- Roll ear toward shoulder
- Roll around
- Slowly, gently

### Shoulder Shrugs

**For tension:**
- Raise shoulders to ears
- Hold 5 seconds
- Drop and relax
- Repeat 5 times

### Chest Opener

**Counter-stretch:**
- Hands behind head
- Elbows back
- Open chest
- Breathe deeply

### Wrist Circles

**For typing fatigue:**
- Circle wrists
- Both directions
- Flex and extend
- Shake out

---

## Long Study Sessions

### Test Prep

**Extended studying:**
- Set timers for breaks
- Change positions periodically
- Don't skip breaks
- Move more, not less

### Papers and Projects

**For long work:**
- Break into chunks
- Vary position/location
- Regular stretch breaks
- Stay hydrated

---

## Group Study

### Different Setup

**With friends:**
- Still need good posture
- May sit differently
- Don't forget basics
- Keep moving

### Library Study

**Benefits:**
- Proper desks
- Good lighting
- Designed for study
- Take advantage

---

## Tablets and Laptops

### The Problem

**Portable devices:**
- Screen too low
- Neck bends down
- Keyboard too high or low
- Compromise position

### Solutions

**Better approach:**
- External keyboard
- Prop up screen
- Stand or case
- Proper height

---

## Textbooks and Papers

### Reading Position

**For books:**
- Book propped up (not flat)
- Book stand helps
- Don't hunch over
- Raise material

### Writing Position

**For paper work:**
- Desk at right height
- Paper angled slightly
- Don't hunch
- Arm support

---

## Eyes and Posture

### The Connection

**Eye strain causes:**
- Leaning forward
- Squinting
- Neck strain
- Headaches

### Prevention

**For eyes:**
- Proper lighting
- Right distance
- Regular breaks
- 20-20-20 rule (every 20 min, look 20 feet, for 20 sec)

---

## Building Good Habits

### Start Now

**Why it matters:**
- You're still growing
- Habits are forming
- Prevention is easier
- Lifetime impact

### Make It Automatic

**Practice until:**
- Good posture feels normal
- Breaks are routine
- Setup is automatic
- Body knows what's right
