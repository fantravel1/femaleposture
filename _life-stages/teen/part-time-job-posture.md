# Part-Time Job Posture for Teens

> **Your first job is an exciting milestone, but work can be physically demanding. Protecting your posture now builds habits for your entire career.**

---

## Why Work Posture Matters Early

Good habits formed now protect you for decades of working life.

### Building Career-Long Habits

**Starting right:**
- Patterns you set now stick
- Easier to prevent than fix
- Young bodies recover but still at risk
- Foundation for adult work life
- Investment in your future

### Common Teen Jobs

**Where teens often work:**
- Retail and clothing stores
- Fast food and restaurants
- Grocery stores
- Babysitting and childcare
- Tutoring and desk work
- Lifeguarding
- Outdoor work

---

## Retail and Store Work

### Standing for Long Shifts

**When on your feet:**
- Comfortable, supportive shoes
- Shift weight side to side
- Engage core gently
- Take allowed breaks
- Move when possible

### Cash Register Posture

**At checkout:**
- Stand tall, don't lean on counter
- Alternate standing positions
- Anti-fatigue mat if available
- Adjust register height if possible
- Scan items without twisting

### Stocking and Merchandising

**Moving products:**
- Lift with legs, not back
- Get close before lifting
- Ask for help with heavy items
- Use ladders properly
- Don't overreach

---

## Food Service Jobs

### Standing on Kitchen Floors

**Restaurant work:**
- Very supportive shoes essential
- Mat at your station if allowed
- Move and stretch when able
- Standing for hours is hard
- Break time is sacred

### Carrying Trays and Plates

**Serving:**
- Balance trays properly
- Keep heavy loads close
- Make multiple trips
- Watch for wet floors
- Core engaged when carrying

### Bending and Reaching

**Kitchen work:**
- Squat to low shelves
- Use step stool for high items
- Avoid repeated deep bending
- Move your feet, don't just reach
- Protect your back

### Cleaning Tasks

**End of shift duties:**
- Mop with proper technique
- No twisting and bending
- Wipe at comfortable heights
- Taking out trash carefully
- Finish strong

---

## Grocery Store Jobs

### Bagging and Checkout

**At register:**
- Rotate bagging and other tasks
- Lift bags properly
- Standing mat helps
- Stay engaged, don't slump
- Move items efficiently

### Stocking Shelves

**Loading product:**
- Use carts for heavy items
- Proper lifting always
- Low shelves = squat
- High shelves = ladder
- Inventory work varies position

### Cart Collection

**Outside work:**
- Push, don't pull heavy carts
- Engage core when pushing
- Line up multiple carts carefully
- Weather affects comfort
- Wear appropriate shoes

---

## Childcare and Babysitting

### Lifting Children

**Safe kid handling:**
- Get close before lifting
- Lift with legs
- Young kids are heavier than they look
- Put down when arms tire
- Floor play reduces holding

### Floor Play

**Playing at kid level:**
- Sit in various positions
- Get up safely
- Don't stay in one position too long
- Kneeling okay for short periods
- Change positions frequently

### Carrying and Holding

**When kids need holding:**
- Switch hips
- Carrier if available
- Sit down to hold
- Arms close to body
- Put down to rest

---

## Tutoring and Desk Jobs

### Sitting for Work

**Office-type work:**
- Proper desk setup
- Good chair if possible
- Take standing breaks
- Eye level screen
- Same rules as studying

### One-on-One Tutoring

**Working with students:**
- Side by side positioning
- Don't hover and hunch
- Seated at same level
- Move and stretch between sessions
- Mind your neck looking at their work

## Lifeguarding

### Sitting in Guard Chair

**Elevated watching:**
- Maintain alert posture
- Rotate positions if possible
- Stay engaged, don't slump
- Sun protection
- Hydration important

### Rescue Preparation

**Ready for action:**
- Core engaged for quick movement
- Athletic ready stance
- Physical training matters
- Recovery after rescues
- Body matters for safety

## General Work Tips

### Shoes Matter Most

**Invest in footwear:**
- Comfortable, supportive shoes
- Right for your job type
- Replace when worn

### Taking Breaks Properly

**When you get breaks:**
- Actually sit down
- Stretch briefly
- Hydrate and eat
- Rest your feet
- Prepare for return

### Knowing Your Rights

**Work safety:**
- You can ask about safety
- Report unsafe conditions
- Don't lift beyond ability
- Break requirements
- Age-appropriate tasks
