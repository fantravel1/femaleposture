# Music Performance Posture

> **When you step on stage, your posture tells the audience you're ready to share something special. Confident physical presence enhances your musical performance.**

---

## Performance vs Practice Posture

Performing requires elevated awareness beyond daily practice.

### What Changes on Stage

**Performance differences:**
- Audience watching affects you
- Heightened physical tension
- Adrenaline affecting body
- Formal attire considerations
- Need to project presence

### Building Stage Presence

**Physical communication:**
- Posture conveys confidence
- Audience sees before they hear
- Body language sets expectations
- Energy projects outward
- Presence enhances music

---

## Walking On Stage

### The Entrance

**Make an impression:**
- Walk at moderate pace
- Head up, eyes forward
- Shoulders relaxed and back
- Arms swinging naturally
- Confident but not arrogant

### Getting to Position

**Navigate with grace:**
- Know your path beforehand
- Avoid bumping equipment
- Move with purpose
- Don't rush or dawdle
- Reach your spot smoothly

### The Bow/Acknowledgment

**Greeting the audience:**
- Face audience fully
- Brief, confident bow or nod
- Make eye contact if comfortable
- Smile genuinely
- Take a breath before starting

---

## Seated Performance

### Piano/Keyboard

**At the piano:**
- Sit toward front of bench
- Feet positioned on pedals
- Back straight but not rigid
- Shoulders down and relaxed
- Appropriate distance from keys

### Orchestra/Band Seated

**In ensemble seating:**
- Sit forward, don't use back of chair
- Feet flat on floor
- Instrument in correct position
- Ready posture throughout
- Stay engaged even when not playing

### Maintaining Presence

**Throughout the piece:**
- Don't collapse between sections
- Stay physically engaged
- Breathe visibly if appropriate
- Recover from mistakes gracefully
- Performance face stays on

---

## Standing Performance

### Solo Standing

**Vocalist or instrumentalist:**
- Weight evenly on both feet
- Slight bounce in knees
- Core engaged but relaxed
- Shoulders open, not hunched
- Head balanced, not jutting

### Choir Standing

**Group positioning:**
- Match height with neighbors if possible
- Stand at consistent attention
- Hold music or folder properly
- Eyes on conductor
- Breathe as a group

### Ensemble Standing

**Band or orchestra:**
- Instrument-ready position
- Alert and focused
- Respond to conductor
- Part of visual whole
- Individual presence within group

---

## Instrument-Specific Considerations

### String Instruments

**Violin, viola, cello, bass:**
- Instrument position affects posture
- Support from whole body
- Avoid excessive tension
- Movement appropriate to piece
- Bow arm freedom

### Wind and Brass

**Flute, clarinet, trumpet, etc.:**
- Breath support requires good posture
- Instrument angle and position
- Embouchure and body connection
- Standing vs seated differences
- Air flow optimization

### Percussion

**Drums, mallet instruments:**
- Balance and stance
- Movement between instruments
- Height appropriate to setup
- Energy and control
- Visual performance aspect

## Managing Performance Anxiety

### Physical Symptoms

**What nerves do to body:**
- Shoulders rise up
- Breathing becomes shallow
- Muscles tense
- Shaking may occur
- Stomach butterflies

### Calming the Body

**Techniques that help:**
- Deep diaphragmatic breaths
- Shoulder drops
- Shake out tension
- Ground through feet
- Focus on preparation

### Using Posture to Reduce Anxiety

**Body affects mind:**
- Power pose backstage
- Standing tall reduces cortisol
- Confident posture feels better
- Fake it till you make it works
- Physical preparation helps mental

## Audition Settings

### Entering the Room

**Make strong first impression:**
- Knock and enter confidently
- Greet adjudicators briefly
- Walk to performance spot
- Set up efficiently
- Ready yourself visibly

### During Audition

**Maintain presence:**
- Introduce yourself clearly
- Stay confident between pieces
- Recover gracefully from errors
- Maintain posture throughout
- End pieces definitively

### Leaving the Room

**Exit with same energy:**
- Thank adjudicators
- Gather belongings calmly
- Walk out confidently
- Close door gently
- Don't deflate until outside

---

## Recital Preparation

### Practice Performance

**Run-throughs that help:**
- Practice walking on stage
- Rehearse bowing/greeting
- Perform for family or friends
- Video yourself performing
- Practice in performance clothes

### Day of Preparation

**Physical preparation:**
- Warm up body, not just instrument
- Stretch neck and shoulders
- Check posture in mirror
- Practice deep breathing
- Visualization of success

### Performance Clothing

**Dress considerations:**
- Clothes that allow movement
- Nothing that restricts breathing
- Shoes you can walk in confidently
- Appropriate for venue
- Practice performing in outfit

---

## During the Performance

### Starting

**Begin with intention:**
- Settle into position
- Take a centering breath
- Feel your feet on floor
- Mental preparation moment
- Begin when truly ready

### Staying Present

**Throughout performance:**
- Focus on music, not audience
- Let training take over
- Breathe consistently
- Maintain posture awareness
- Be in the moment

### Handling Mistakes

**When things go wrong:**
- Don't break posture
- Don't make faces
- Keep going
- Physical composure helps recovery
- Audience usually doesn't notice

---

## After Performance

### The Bow

**Acknowledge applause:**
- Wait for music to end fully
- Stand or rise gracefully
- Face audience
- Genuine bow or acknowledgment
- Accept applause graciously

### Leaving Stage

**Exit with confidence:**
- Same quality as entrance
- Don't rush off
- Exit direction rehearsed
- Keep presence until offstage
- Then celebrate!

---

## Building Long-Term Habits

### Regular Practice

**Make it automatic:**
- Practice posture daily
- Warm up before practice sessions
- Record yourself regularly
- Watch recordings for posture
- Correct habits early

### Physical Fitness

**Body support for performance:**
- Core strengthening
- Upper back strength
- Flexibility work
- Endurance for long performances
- Overall fitness helps
