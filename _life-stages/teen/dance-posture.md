# Dance Posture for Teens

> **Dance Shapes Your Body:** The posture you develop in dance carries into daily life. Good technique now protects you for years.

---

## Dance and Posture Connection

### How Dance Helps

**Benefits:**
- Body awareness
- Core strength
- Alignment training
- Flexibility
- Coordination

### Potential Issues

**Watch for:**
- Overarching lower back
- Forced turnout
- Hypermobility strain
- Repetitive strain

---

## Basic Dance Alignment

### Head and Neck

**Proper position:**
- Head balanced over spine
- Not forward or back
- Chin level
- Neck long

### Shoulders

**Good alignment:**
- Down and back (slightly)
- Not hunched
- Not forced back
- Relaxed but placed

### Spine

**Natural curves:**
- Maintain natural curves
- Don't flatten or exaggerate
- Lengthened feeling
- Supported by core

### Pelvis

**Neutral position:**
- Not tucked under
- Not tilted forward
- Level
- Stable base

## Dance Style Considerations

### Ballet

**Posture demands:**
- Very upright carriage
- Strong core
- Turnout from hips
- Lifted chest

**Watch for:**
- Forcing turnout
- Swayback
- Hyperextended knees
- Ribs popping

### Contemporary/Modern

**Characteristics:**
- More natural alignment
- Fluid movement
- Floor work
- Varied positions

**Considerations:**
- Core strength for floor work
- Spine mobility
- Safe release techniques

### Hip Hop

**Style:**
- Grounded stance
- Isolations
- Power moves
- Various positions

**Watch for:**
- Impact on joints
- Repetitive movements
- Core engagement

### Jazz

**Demands:**
- Extended lines
- Flexibility
- Varied tempos
- Performance quality

**Consider:**
- Controlled flexibility
- Core strength
- Proper warm-up

## Common Dance Posture Issues

### Swayback

**What it is:**
- Pelvis forward
- Ribs back
- Exaggerated curves

**Why common:**
- Trying to look "lifted"
- Weak core
- Habit

**Fix:**
- Core strengthening
- Pelvic awareness
- Teacher feedback

### Forced Turnout

**The problem:**
- Turning out from knees/ankles
- Instead of hips
- Causes injury

**Correct approach:**
- Only use what you have
- Turn out from hips
- Don't force
- Build flexibility safely

### Hyperextension

**Risk:**
- Very flexible dancers
- Locking joints
- Strain and injury

**Management:**
- Strengthen around joints
- Don't lock
- Control movement
- Micro-bend

## Cross-Training for Dancers

### Why It Helps

**Benefits:**
- Prevents overuse
- Builds balanced strength
- Addresses weak areas
- Supports dance

### What to Include

**Good options:**
- Core work (Pilates)
- Strength training
- Cardio
- Yoga (carefully)

### Balance Development

**For dancers:**
- Anterior muscles often strong
- Posterior may be weak
- Balance front and back
- Functional strength

## Growing Dancer Bodies

### Growth Spurts

**During spurts:**
- Coordination may decrease
- Flexibility changes
- Strength-to-weight shifts
- Be patient

### Body Changes

**During adolescence:**
- Center of gravity shifts
- May affect balance
- Technique may need adjustment
- Normal process

### Nutrition

**For dancers:**
- Fuel your body
- Support growth
- Support activity
- Don't restrict

## Injury Prevention

### Common Dance Injuries

**Watch for:**
- Hip issues
- Knee problems
- Ankle injuries
- Back pain

### Prevention

**Strategies:**
- Proper warm-up
- Good technique
- Rest and recovery
- Listen to body

### When to Rest

**Stop dancing if:**
- Sharp pain
- Pain that worsens
- Can't do movement correctly
- Injury occurring
