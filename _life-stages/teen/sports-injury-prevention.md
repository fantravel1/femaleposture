# Sports Injury Prevention for Teens

> **Your athletic career depends on staying healthy. Understanding how posture and body mechanics protect you can keep you in the game and performing your best.**

---

## Why Teen Athletes Face Unique Risks

Adolescent bodies have specific vulnerabilities that require attention.

### Growth-Related Factors

**What makes teens different:**
- Bones growing faster than muscles
- Growth plates are vulnerable
- Coordination still developing
- Muscle imbalances common
- Bodies changing rapidly

### Training Load Concerns

**Risk factors:**
- Year-round sports participation
- Single-sport specialization
- Intensive training schedules
- Not enough recovery time
- Pressure to perform

---

## Posture's Role in Injury Prevention

### Sport-Ready Posture

**Foundation for performance:**
- Athletic stance requires alignment
- Balanced muscles prevent strain
- Good posture enables power
- Core stability protects spine
- Proper mechanics prevent injury

### Common Postural Issues in Athletes

**Problems that cause injury:**
- Forward head position
- Rounded shoulders
- Excessive low back arch
- Knee alignment problems
- Ankle instability

## Pre-Activity Preparation

### Dynamic Warm-Up Essentials

**Get body ready:**
- Light cardio to raise temperature
- Joint circles to lubricate joints
- Dynamic stretches (not static)
- Sport-specific movements
- Gradual intensity increase

### Sample Warm-Up Routine

**15-minute sequence:**
1. Light jogging (2 minutes)
2. Leg swings (30 seconds each leg)
3. Arm circles (30 seconds)
4. Walking lunges (1 minute)
5. High knees (30 seconds)
6. Butt kicks (30 seconds)
7. Lateral shuffles (1 minute)
8. Hip circles (30 seconds each)
9. Sport-specific drills (5 minutes)

### Mental Preparation

**Mind-body readiness:**
- Focus on body sensations
- Visualize proper movements
- Set intentions for practice
- Notice any tightness or pain
- Be present and ready

## Core Strength and Stability

### Why Core Matters

**Injury prevention role:**
- Protects spine during movement
- Transfers power efficiently
- Provides stable base for limbs
- Prevents compensatory movements
- Essential for all sports

### Core Exercises for Athletes

**Build stability:**
- Planks (front and side)
- Dead bugs
- Bird dogs
- Anti-rotation exercises
- Pallof press

### Activating Core During Play

**In-sport application:**
- Engage before impact
- Stabilize during direction changes
- Support spine during lifting
- Maintain during endurance efforts
- Automatic with practice

## Sport-Specific Considerations

### Running Sports

**Posture for runners:**
- Forward lean from ankles
- Head up, eyes ahead
- Arms relaxed, driving straight
- Core engaged for stability
- Proper footstrike for your body

### Throwing Sports

**Protect arm and shoulder:**
- Full body participation in throw
- Hip and trunk rotation
- Shoulder blade stability
- Proper follow-through
- Rest between sessions

### Jumping and Landing

**Reduce knee/ankle injury:**
- Land with soft knees
- Knee tracks over toe
- Hip engagement in landing
- Absorb impact through whole body
- Avoid knee caving inward

### Contact Sports

**Impact preparation:**
- Core braced before contact
- Neck strength for head protection
- Proper tackling/falling technique
- Recovery positioning
- Awareness of surroundings

## Recovery and Rest

### Why Recovery Matters

**Essential for prevention:**
- Muscles repair during rest
- Growth happens in recovery
- Fatigue increases injury risk
- Mental freshness prevents mistakes
- Overtraining leads to breakdown

### Active Recovery

**Light movement helps:**
- Walking
- Swimming
- Gentle stretching
- Yoga
- Foam rolling

### Sleep for Athletes

**Non-negotiable:**
- 8-10 hours for teens
- Growth hormone during sleep
- Muscle repair overnight
- Mental recovery
- Performance depends on rest

## Strength Training for Injury Prevention

### When to Start

**Age-appropriate training:**
- Body weight exercises first
- Proper form before adding weight
- Supervision from qualified coach
- Progress gradually
- Focus on movement quality

### Key Exercises

**Build protective strength:**
- Squats for leg strength
- Hip hinges for posterior chain
- Rows for back strength
- Push-ups for upper body
- Planks for core

### Balance and Proportion

**Prevent imbalances:**
- Work opposing muscle groups
- Address weaknesses
- Don't just train sport-specific
- Full body development
- Flexibility with strength

## Recognizing Warning Signs

### Pain Signals

**Listen to your body:**
- Sharp pain = stop immediately
- Aching after activity = monitor
- Pain during activity = concerning
- Pain that worsens = get evaluated
- Never push through sharp pain

### Signs of Overtraining

**Needing rest:**
- Persistent fatigue
- Declining performance
- Frequent minor injuries
- Mood changes
- Sleep problems
- Losing motivation

### When to Sit Out

**Take yourself out if:**
- Significant pain
- Unable to move normally
- Swelling present
- Feeling unwell
- Something feels wrong

### Advocating for Yourself

- Off-season training
- Sport variety when possible
- Mental breaks

## When Injuries Happen

### Immediate Response

**Right away:**
- Stop activity
- Assess what happened
- Tell coach or adult
- Apply RICE if appropriate
- Don't return until evaluated

### Getting Back Safely

**Return to play:**
- Full medical clearance
- Completed rehabilitation
- No pain during activity
- Regained strength and mobility
- Gradually return to full intensity
