# Social Media, Body Image, and Posture for Teens

> **What You See Isn't Real:** Social media shows edited, filtered, posed images. Your body is living in the real world, and that's perfectly okay.

---

## The Social Media Effect

### What's Happening

**On your feeds:**
- Filtered photos
- Perfect angles
- Edited bodies
- Highlight reels

### The Impact

**Can lead to:**
- Body comparison
- Negative self-image
- Posture affected by insecurity
- Never feeling "good enough"

---

## The Truth About Images

### Editing Is Everywhere

**What you don't see:**
- Photo editing
- Filters
- Lighting tricks
- Many attempts for one photo
- Professional help

### Even Influencers

**They also:**
- Edit photos
- Use filters
- Choose best angles
- Don't actually look like that

### Real Bodies

**Truth:**
- Have different shapes
- Change throughout life
- Are for living, not posing
- Are worthy as they are

---

## Body Image and Posture

### The Connection

**Poor body image leads to:**
- Trying to hide
- Slouching
- Hunching
- Making yourself smaller

**Confident posture:**
- Takes up space
- Stands tall
- Open and relaxed
- Present in your body

### The Cycle

**Negative cycle:**
- Feel bad about body → poor posture
- Poor posture → feel worse
- Affects how others perceive you

**Positive cycle:**
- Stand tall → feel more confident
- Confidence → better body image
- Better image → natural posture

---

## Building Body Confidence

### Focus on Function

**Your body can:**
- Move
- Learn
- Create
- Experience life
- Be amazing

### Practice Acceptance

**Steps:**
- Notice negative thoughts
- Challenge unrealistic standards
- Appreciate what your body does
- Treat yourself with kindness

### Positive Self-Talk

**Replace:**
- "I hate my..." with "I appreciate that my body..."
- Criticism with neutrality
- Comparison with individuality

---

## Posture as Power

### Standing Tall

**Benefits:**
- Feel more confident
- Others perceive confidence
- Breathe better
- Take up space you deserve

### Body Language

**Open posture signals:**
- Confidence
- Approachability
- Self-assurance
- That you belong

### Practice

**Try:**
- Standing tall deliberately
- Notice how it feels
- Use in situations you find hard
- Build the habit

---

## Social Media Strategies

### Curate Your Feed

**Follow accounts that:**
- Show real bodies
- Focus on function
- Promote positivity
- Make you feel good

### Unfollow/Mute

**Accounts that:**
- Make you feel bad
- Promote unrealistic standards
- Focus on appearance only
- Affect your mood

### Limit Time

**Reduce exposure:**
- Screen time limits
- Phone-free times
- Other activities
- Real-life connection

---

## The Comparison Trap

### Why We Compare

**Natural but:**
- Social media amplifies it
- Comparisons are unfair
- You're seeing edited versions
- Not helpful

### Breaking the Habit

**Strategies:**
- Notice when comparing
- Remind yourself of filters
- Focus on your own journey
- Appreciate your uniqueness

---

## Real Life vs. Online

### In Person

**People are:**
- Not filtered
- Not perfectly posed
- Real humans
- All imperfect

### Difference Matters

**Remember:**
- Online isn't reality
- Everyone looks different in real life
- You only see your unfiltered self
- That's normal

## Getting Help

### When to Talk to Someone

**If you:**
- Can't stop negative thoughts
- Avoid things because of body
- Feel distressed about appearance
- Have disordered eating

### Who to Talk To

**Resources:**
- Parent or trusted adult
- School counselor
- Doctor
- Mental health professional
