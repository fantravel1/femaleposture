# Social Anxiety and Your Body

> **Anxiety Lives in Your Body:** Social anxiety isn't just thoughts—it shows up physically. Understanding this connection gives you tools.

---

## How Social Anxiety Feels Physically

### Common Physical Symptoms

**In social situations:**
- Racing heart
- Sweating
- Shakiness
- Stomach upset
- Blushing
- Muscle tension

### Postural Effects

**Anxiety changes posture:**
- Shrinking into yourself
- Crossed arms
- Head down
- Making yourself smaller
- Protective stance

---

## The Anxiety-Posture Loop

### How It Works

**The cycle:**
- Feel anxious → body tenses
- Tense, small posture → feel more anxious
- Look anxious → others may respond differently
- Reinforces anxiety
- Self-perpetuating

### The Opposite Also Works

**Breaking the cycle:**
- Change posture → feel different
- Look confident → treated as confident
- Body affects mind
- Entry point for change

---

## Physical Signs of Anxiety

### What Others See

**May notice:**
- Avoiding eye contact
- Fidgeting
- Crossed arms
- Hunched shoulders
- Looking uncomfortable

### What You Feel

**Internal experience:**
- Heart pounding
- Shallow breathing
- Muscle tension
- Overwhelming sensations

---

## Body Strategies Before Social Situations

### Preparation

**Before you enter:**
- Deep breaths
- Power pose (privately)
- Roll shoulders back
- Relax jaw
- Check posture

### Calming the Nervous System

**Physical reset:**
- Slow breathing
- Exhale longer than inhale
- Ground feet on floor
- Release tension

---

## In the Moment Strategies

### Quick Resets

**When anxiety spikes:**
- Feet flat on ground
- Deep breath
- Relax shoulders
- Unclench hands
- Quick scan and release

### Posture Adjustment

**Deliberate change:**
- Stand/sit taller
- Uncross arms
- Shoulders back slightly
- Open body language

### Breathing

**For calm:**
- Slow down breath
- Belly breathing
- Exhale fully
- Several breaths

---

## Challenging Situations

### Speaking in Class

**Physical prep:**
- Feet grounded
- Shoulders back
- Breath before speaking
- Voice from grounded place

### Meeting New People

**Body language:**
- Open posture
- Eye contact (as able)
- Relaxed stance
- Approachable body

### Parties/Social Events

**Survival:**
- Relaxed posture
- Deep breaths as needed
- Bathroom break to reset
- Not hiding in corner

---

## What Confident Posture Looks Like

### The Basics

**Confident body:**
- Standing tall
- Open chest
- Relaxed shoulders
- Taking up space

### Not Over-Doing It

**Balance:**
- Natural, not forced
- Relaxed, not stiff
- Confident, not aggressive
- Authentic

## Fake It Till You Make It

### The Science

**Real effects:**
- Posture affects hormones
- Body affects mind
- Not just looking confident
- Actually feeling different

### How to Use This

**Practice:**
- Adopt confident posture
- Even when anxious
- Notice the shift
- It gets more natural
