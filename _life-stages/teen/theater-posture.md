# Theater and Drama Posture

> **In theater, your body is your instrument. Learning to control and use your physical presence is as important as remembering your lines.**

---

## Why Body Matters in Theater

Stage performance requires deliberate physical awareness.

### Body as Communication

**What posture conveys:**
- Character personality
- Emotional state
- Status and power
- Time period authenticity
- Story information

### Physical Training

**What theater develops:**
- Body awareness
- Deliberate movement
- Physical stamina
- Presence and projection
- Mind-body integration

---

## Neutral Position

### Finding Your Baseline

**Neutral stance:**
- Weight evenly on both feet
- Knees soft, not locked
- Pelvis neutral
- Spine stacked
- Shoulders relaxed
- Head balanced

### Why Neutral Matters

**Starting point for:**
- All character work
- Movement preparation
- Quick transformations
- Physical reset
- Stage awareness

### Practicing Neutral

**Building awareness:**
- Daily neutral check
- Mirror work
- Partner feedback
- Contrast with character
- Return frequently

## Character Physicality

### Building Character from Body

**Physical choices:**
- How does character stand?
- How do they walk?
- What are their gestures?
- Where is their center?
- What's their physical signature?

### Status and Posture

**Communicating power:**
- High status: expanded, still, centered
- Low status: contracted, fidgeting, uncentered
- Status can shift in scene
- Physical transformation
- Contrast between characters

### Period Movement

**Historical accuracy:**
- Different eras, different bodies
- Costume affects movement
- Social norms of time
- Research and practice
- Authentic physical life

## Stage Presence

### Projecting Energy

**Filling the space:**
- Energy radiates outward
- Not just about size
- Intention reaches audience
- Physical commitment
- Presence before speaking

### Stillness and Movement

**Deliberate choices:**
- When to be still
- When to move
- Stillness can be powerful
- Movement means something

### Eyes and Focus

**Where you look:**
- Audience connection when appropriate

## Vocal Posture

### Body Support for Voice

**Physical foundation:**
- Good posture enables projection
- Breath support from alignment
- Tension blocks voice
- Open throat and chest
- Grounded stance

### Breath and Body

**Breathing for stage:**
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Rib expansion
- Capacity for long phrases
- Breath supports emotion
- Posture enables breath

### Projection Without Strain

**Healthy vocal use:**
- Power from body, not throat
- Good posture reduces strain
- Resonance from open space
- Sustainable for long runs
- Voice care and posture connection

### Warm-Up Importance

**Physical preparation:**
- Body warm-up before rehearsal
- Stretching and movement
- Breath work

### Long Rehearsals

- Take care of your body

- Notice fatigue
