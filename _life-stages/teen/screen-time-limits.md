# Screen Time and Your Growing Body

> **Your Body Is Developing Now:** The hours you spend on screens during teenage years shape your posture, eyes, and physical development.

---

## The Screen Time Reality

### Average Teen Screen Time

**Statistics:**
- 7+ hours daily average
- Often much more
- Doesn't include school use
- Significant portion of day

### What It Means Physically

**Your body:**
- Still developing
- Forming patterns
- Affected by position
- Building habits for life

---

## Physical Effects

### Posture

**Extended screen use causes:**
- Forward head position
- Rounded shoulders
- Hunched back
- Tech neck
- Poor posture patterns

### Eyes

**Screen effects:**
- Eye strain
- Dry eyes
- Possible vision impacts
- Blue light effects

### Activity Replacement

**Less time for:**
- Physical activity
- Movement
- Sports
- Active play

### Sleep

**Screens affect:**
- Melatonin production
- Sleep quality
- Sleep duration
- Next-day function

## Why Limits Help

### Physical Benefits

**Less screen time means:**
- More movement
- Better posture opportunities
- Eye rest
- Healthier patterns

### What to Replace It With

**Instead of screens:**
- Physical activity
- Social time in person
- Hobbies
- Being outside

## Setting Limits

### Realistic Goals

**Start where you are:**
- Track current use
- Set small reduction goals
- Progress gradually
- Be realistic

### Strategies

**What works:**
- Screen-free times
- Phone-free zones
- Scheduled breaks
- Alternative activities

### Apps Can Help

**Tools:**
- Screen time trackers
- Usage limits
- App blockers
- Awareness tools

## Quality Over Quantity

### Not All Screen Time Equal

**Consider:**
- Passive vs. active use
- Creating vs. consuming
- Connecting vs. scrolling
- Learning vs. mindless

### Mindful Use

**Being intentional:**
- Purpose for use
- Time awareness
- Not just habit
- Conscious choice

## Posture During Screen Time

### Making It Less Harmful

**When on screens:**
- Raise device higher
- Good setup
- Regular breaks
- Movement between

### Break Strategies

**Build in:**
- 20-20-20 rule for eyes
- Movement breaks
- Position changes
- Stand and stretch

## Sleep Protection

### Before Bed

**Screen boundaries:**
- Stop screens 1+ hour before bed
- Night mode settings
- Device out of bedroom
- Better sleep quality

### Morning Habits

**Start well:**
- Not phone first thing
- Movement before screens
- Set tone for day
