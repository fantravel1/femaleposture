# Prom Night Posture

> **Prom is a night of photos, dancing, and looking your best. Good posture helps you look confident in pictures and feel comfortable throughout the evening in formal wear.**

---

## Why Posture Matters at Prom

This night is heavily documented.

### Photo Reality

**You'll have:**
- Professional photos
- Candid shots
- Group pictures
- Dance floor shots
- Memories forever

### Feeling Confident

**Good posture:**
- Looks confident
- Photographs well
- Makes you feel better
- Projects poise
- Enhances everything

---

## Dress Considerations

### How Dress Affects Posture

**Structure matters:**
- Bodice construction
- Weight of fabric
- Movement restrictions
- Backless considerations
- Style impacts posture

### Working with Your Dress

**Practice:**
- Wear dress before prom
- Practice sitting
- Practice standing
- Know how to move
- No surprises

---

## Photo Posture Basics

### Standing

**Best positioning:**
- Weight on back foot
- Front foot angled
- Shoulders back and down
- Chin slightly up
- Elongated neck

### Avoiding Common Mistakes

**Don't:**
- Slouch
- Stick chin out
- Lock knees
- Tense up
- Look uncomfortable

### Partner Photos

**With date:**
- Angle toward each other
- Natural positioning
- Both with good posture
- Comfortable closeness
- Genuine expressions

---

## Heels Survival

### If Wearing High Heels

**Reality:**
- Can affect posture
- Balance different
- Feet will hurt
- Long night ahead
- Plan accordingly

### Walking in Heels

**Technique:**
- Shorter steps
- Heel to toe
- Core engaged
- Practice beforehand
- Confident stride

### When Feet Hurt

**What to do:**
- Sit when possible
- Bring backup flats
- Stretch feet
- Know your limits
- Change if needed

## Throughout the Night

### During Dinner

**Seated posture:**
- Sit tall
- Napkin in lap
- Feet on floor
- Engage with table
- Elegant dining

### Dancing

**On the floor:**
- Natural movement
- Good posture in motion
- Have fun
- Core engaged
- Enjoy yourself

### Standing Around

**Between activities:**
- Don't slump
- Comfortable stance
- Mingle with confidence
- Position yourself well
- Stay poised

## Managing Fatigue

### Long Night

**Hours of event:**
- Pace yourself
- Sit when possible
- Don't lock knees
- Move around
- Energy management

### When Tired

**Posture suffers:**
- Be aware
- Take sitting breaks
- Breathe deeply
- Reset posture
- Rally
