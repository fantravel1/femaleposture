# Building Confidence Through Posture for Teens

> **Your Body Speaks First:** Before you say a word, your posture sends a message. The good news? You can use this to build real confidence.

---

## The Posture-Confidence Connection

### It Works Both Ways

**Two directions:**
- Confident → stand tall
- Stand tall → feel more confident

### The Science

**Research shows:**
- Power poses affect hormones
- Posture changes how you feel
- Body language affects self-perception
- Not just external perception

### Why This Matters for Teens

**During adolescence:**
- Confidence often shaky
- Body awareness changing
- Social situations challenging
- Tools to help matter

---

## What Confident Posture Looks Like

### The Basics

**Standing tall:**
- Head balanced over spine
- Shoulders back (relaxed, not forced)
- Chest open
- Weight balanced

### Taking Up Space

**Confident presence:**
- Not shrinking
- Appropriate space
- Arms uncrossed (usually)
- Grounded stance

### Eye Contact

**Connected:**
- Can look at people
- Not staring down
- Engaged presence
- Approachable

---

## What Insecure Posture Looks Like

### Shrinking

**Trying to hide:**
- Rounded shoulders
- Head down
- Crossed arms
- Making yourself smaller

### Why It Happens

**Common reasons:**
- Self-consciousness
- Wanting to hide
- Social anxiety
- Not wanting attention

### The Problem

**Self-fulfilling:**
- Feel insecure → look insecure
- Others treat you as insecure
- Reinforces feeling
- Cycle continues

---

## Using Posture to Build Confidence

### Before Challenging Situations

**Preparation:**
- Stand tall for a few minutes
- Power pose in private
- Set your shoulders
- Enter with good posture

### During Situations

**In the moment:**
- Notice your posture
- Adjust if shrinking
- Keep shoulders open
- Breathe

### Fake It Till You Make It

**The strategy:**
- Act confident first
- Body sends signals to brain
- Feelings follow behavior
- Becomes genuine over time

---

## Specific Situations

### Presentations

**When speaking:**
- Stand grounded
- Open posture
- Project confidence
- You've got this

### Social Events

**At parties:**
- Don't hide in corner
- Open body language
- Approachable stance
- Take up your space

### Meeting New People

**First impressions:**
- Stand/sit tall
- Eye contact
- Open posture
- Confident handshake

### Classroom

**In school:**
- Sit toward front
- Engage
- Speak up
- Good posture shows attention

## Common Teen Challenges

### Wanting to Fit In

**Balance:**
- Confidence doesn't mean arrogance
- You can be confident and belong
- Stand out in good ways
- Be yourself

### Fear of Judgment

**Reality:**
- Everyone is self-conscious
- Most people are focused on themselves
- Confidence is attractive
- Fake it till you feel it

### Physical Insecurities

**About your body:**
- Every body deserves good posture
- Standing tall helps at any size
- Confidence is inside
- Posture reflects internal state

## Building Over Time

### Daily Practice

**Habits:**
- Notice your posture
- Correct when slouching
- Practice regularly
- Gets more natural

### Physical Strength

**Helps confidence:**
- Exercise builds confidence
- Strength supports posture
- Physical capability feels good
- Body and mind connect

### Small Wins

**Build on success:**
- Try in small situations first
- Notice when it works
- Build gradually
- Celebrate progress
