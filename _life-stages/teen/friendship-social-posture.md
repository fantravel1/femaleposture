# Social Dynamics and Body Language for Teens

> **Your body language affects how others perceive you and how you navigate friendships. Understanding the connection between posture and social dynamics helps you feel more confident.**

---

## Body Language in Friendships

How you hold yourself matters socially.

### First Impressions

**What people notice:**
- Confidence level
- Openness
- Approachability
- Energy
- Before you speak

### Ongoing Signals

**In friendships:**
- Interest when listening
- Engagement in conversation
- Comfort level
- Connection signals
- Nonverbal communication

---

## Approachable vs. Closed

### Looking Approachable

**Open signals:**
- Relaxed posture
- Uncrossed arms
- Head up
- Eye contact available
- Space for others

### When Closed Off

**Signals isolation:**
- Hunched over phone
- Arms crossed
- Head down
- No eye contact
- Barrier creation

---

## Group Dynamics

### In Social Groups

**Body language:**
- Position in group
- Engagement level
- Inclusion signals
- Taking up space
- Being present

### Feeling Left Out

**What happens:**
- Body may shrink
- Protective posture
- Looking uncomfortable
- Physical response to exclusion
- Normal reaction

### Rejoining

**How to:**
- Open body language
- Move into group
- Face members
- Engage physically
- Show you're there

---

## Confidence and Posture

### Fake It Till You Feel It

**Can work:**
- Confident posture affects mood
- Stand tall first
- Feelings can follow
- Research supports this
- Worth trying

### Authentic Confidence

**Building real confidence:**
- From within
- Practice helps
- Social skills grow
- Body language supports
- Develops over time

---

## When Feeling Shy

### Physical Manifestation

**Shy often looks like:**
- Making yourself small
- Avoiding eye contact
- Physical tension
- Protective posture
- Visible discomfort

### Managing

**What can help:**
- Breathe deeply
- Stand a bit taller
- Eye contact briefly
- Small changes
- Gradual exposure

---

## Listening Body Language

### Showing You Care

**Good listening looks like:**
- Facing the person
- Eye contact
- Nodding
- Open posture
- Present attention

### Being a Good Friend

**Physically:**
- Leaning in
- Attentive posture
- Not distracted
- Physical presence
- Real attention

---

## Conflict and Body Language

### During Arguments

**Be aware:**
- Defensive postures
- Aggressive stances
- Tension showing
- Body positioning
- De-escalation possible

### Calming Situations

**What helps:**
- Open body language
- Relaxed posture
- Non-threatening position
- Soft voice
- Physical calm

## Social Media vs. Real Life

### Different Skills

**In person:**
- Body language matters
- Can't edit
- Real-time interaction
- Different skills needed
- Practice required

### Transferring Skills

**Learning:**
- Practice in person
- Video calls help
- Build real-world skills
- Balance online and offline
- Both matter
