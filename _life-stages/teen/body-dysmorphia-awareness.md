# Body Dysmorphia Awareness for Teens

> **Body dysmorphia goes beyond normal body image concernsâ€”it's a condition where perceived flaws feel overwhelming even when others don't see them. Understanding this helps with recognition and seeking help.**

---

## What Is Body Dysmorphia

It's more than disliking your appearance.

### Definition

**Body Dysmorphic Disorder (BDD):**
- Intense focus on perceived flaws
- Others often don't see the flaw
- Preoccupation is distressing
- Affects daily life
- Mental health condition

### How It Differs from Normal

**Beyond typical concerns:**
- Hours spent thinking about it
- Interferes with life
- Distress is significant
- Hard to dismiss thoughts
- Compulsive behaviors

---

## Warning Signs

### In Yourself

**Notice if:**
- Can't stop thinking about appearance
- Checking mirrors constantly (or avoiding)
- Comparing to others obsessively
- Feeling others notice your "flaw"
- Significant distress

### Behavioral Signs

**Actions that indicate:**
- Excessive grooming or covering
- Seeking reassurance repeatedly
- Avoiding photos or mirrors
- Avoiding social situations
- Significant time consumed

---

## How It Affects Posture

### Hiding and Protecting

**Body language:**
- Hunching to hide
- Crossing arms over body
- Avoiding being seen
- Protective posturing
- Physical manifestation

### Physical Health Effects

**Consequences:**
- Poor posture from hiding
- Avoiding exercise
- Physical neglect
- Tension from distress
- Body affected

---

## Common Focus Areas

### What People Fixate On

**Typical areas:**
- Skin
- Hair
- Nose or other features
- Weight/body shape
- Any body part possible

### The Reality

**Important to know:**
- Perceived flaw often not visible to others
- Or vastly exaggerated
- Perception distorted
- Brain playing tricks
- Not how others see you

---

## Getting Help

### When to Seek Support

**Important if:**
- Interfering with school
- Affecting friendships
- Causing significant distress
- Avoiding activities
- Feeling hopeless

### Who Can Help

**Resources:**
- Therapist or counselor
- Doctor or pediatrician
- School counselor
- Trusted adult
- Mental health professional

### Treatment Works

**Good news:**
- CBT is effective
- Sometimes medication helps
- Recovery is possible
- Treatment available
- People get better

---

## If a Friend Is Struggling

### How to Help

**What you can do:**
- Listen without judgment
- Don't dismiss their feelings
- Encourage professional help
- Be supportive
- Stay connected

### What Not to Do

**Avoid:**
- Constantly reassuring (can reinforce)
- Getting frustrated
- Trying to prove them wrong
- Ignoring the problem
- Providing excessive reassurance

## Healthy Body Image

### Building Positive Relationship

**What helps:**
- Focus on what body does
- Limit comparison
- Media literacy
- Diverse representation
- Function over form

### Body Neutrality

**Alternative approach:**
- Don't have to love appearance
- Accepting and neutral
- Reducing focus on looks
- Body is vessel
- Different perspective

## Posture and Confidence

### Positive Connection

**Good posture can:**
- Help you feel better
- Project confidence
- Not about hiding
- About inhabiting body
- Connected to self-acceptance

### Not About Perfection

**Important:**
- Posture isn't appearance-based
- It's about function
- Health not aesthetics
- Self-care not self-criticism
- Healthy motivation

---

## Social Media and BDD

### The Connection

**Risk factors:**
- Filtered images
- Comparison culture
- Idealized bodies
- Constant exposure
- Can worsen symptoms

### Protective Measures

**What helps:**
- Curate your feed
- Limit screen time
- Follow diverse bodies
- Remember filters exist
- Reality check
