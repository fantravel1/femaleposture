# Cheerleading Posture and Safety

> **Cheerleading demands exceptional posture, core strength, and body control. Understanding proper alignment for stunts, jumps, and tumbling helps you perform safely and excel in the sport.**

---

## Why Posture Matters in Cheer

Cheerleading is a precision sport.

### Performance Impact

**Posture affects:**
- Stunt stability
- Jump height and form
- Tumbling technique
- Overall appearance
- Team synchronization

### Safety Connection

**Alignment and safety:**
- Proper technique prevents injury
- Body control essential
- Core stability protects
- Good form is safe form
- Critical connection

---

## Core Strength Foundation

### Essential for Everything

**Why core matters:**
- Stunt stability
- Jump power
- Tumbling control
- Injury prevention
- Everything in cheer

### Building Core

**What to do:**
- Consistent core training
- Planks and variations
- Hollow body holds
- Cheer-specific work
- Regular practice

---

## Stunting Posture

### Bases

**Base positioning:**
- Strong stance
- Core tight
- Back straight
- Arms and legs engaged
- Stable platform

### Flyers

**Flying posture:**
- Tight body position
- Core engaged throughout
- Lock positions
- Balance from center
- Controlled and stable

### Back Spots

**Support role:**
- Strong stance
- Ready position
- Good body mechanics
- Protective positioning
- Alert and prepared

---

## Jumping Technique

### Alignment for Height

**Jump mechanics:**
- Prep position proper
- Straight up drive
- Core engaged
- Arms tight
- Controlled landing

### Landing Safely

**Critical:**
- Soft landings
- Absorb through legs
- Knee alignment
- Full foot contact
- Protect joints

---

## Tumbling Posture

### Through the Movement

**Body position:**
- Tight throughout
- Core engaged
- Proper head position
- Arms by ears
- Controlled form

### Common Problems

**What to avoid:**
- Loose body
- Arched back
- Head forward or back
- Poor arm position
- Form breakdown

---

## Flexibility and Posture

### Flexibility Demands

**Cheer requirements:**
- High kicks
- Splits
- Backbends
- Scorpions and scales
- Extreme flexibility

### Safe Flexibility Training

**Best approach:**
- Proper warm-up first
- Gradual progression
- Not forcing
- Strength with flexibility
- Sustainable gains

### Hypermobility Caution

**Watch for:**
- Too flexible without strength
- Joint instability
- Injury risk
- Need strength balance
- Control over flexibility
