# Anxiety and Physical Symptoms

> **Anxiety isn't just in your headâ€”it shows up in your body too. Understanding the physical side of anxiety and how it affects posture helps you manage both the feelings and the physical symptoms.**

---

## The Body-Mind Connection

Anxiety creates real physical sensations.

### How Anxiety Affects Your Body

**Physical responses:**
- Muscle tension
- Shallow breathing
- Increased heart rate
- Stomach upset
- Sweating

### Why This Happens

**The stress response:**
- Fight or flight activation
- Prepares body for danger
- Even without real threat
- Body reacts to thoughts
- Normal but uncomfortable

---

## Common Physical Symptoms

### Muscle Tension

**Where it shows:**
- Shoulders creeping up
- Neck tightness
- Jaw clenching
- Back tension
- Fists clenching

### Breathing Changes

**What happens:**
- Shallow breathing
- Holding breath
- Chest breathing only
- Rapid breaths
- Feeling short of breath

### Other Symptoms

**Also common:**
- Headaches
- Stomachaches
- Shaky or trembling
- Feeling lightheaded
- Tingling sensations

---

## Anxiety Posture

### How It Looks

**Physical patterns:**
- Hunched shoulders
- Curled in posture
- Making self smaller
- Protective stance
- Closed body language

### The Feedback Loop

**How it works:**
- Anxiety causes tense posture
- Tense posture increases anxiety
- Cycle reinforces itself
- Breaking cycle helps
- Body affects mind too

---

## Breaking the Physical Pattern

### Posture Adjustment

**Simple shifts:**
- Roll shoulders back
- Drop shoulders down
- Open chest
- Stand or sit taller
- Uncross arms

### Why It Helps

**Changing the signal:**
- Open posture signals safety
- Brain receives calm message
- Reduces anxiety feelings
- Simple intervention
- Works in the moment

---

## Breathing Techniques

### Basic Deep Breathing

**How to do it:**
- Breathe into belly, not chest
- Inhale through nose
- Exhale through mouth
- Slow and steady
- Practice when calm first

### When Anxious

**In the moment:**
- Notice your breathing
- Slow it down
- Longer exhale than inhale
- 4 counts in, 6 out
- Keep practicing

### Regular Practice

**Building skill:**
- Practice when not anxious
- Morning and evening
- Before stressful events
- Becomes automatic
- Powerful tool

---

## Muscle Relaxation

### Releasing Tension

**Progressive relaxation:**
- Tense muscle group briefly
- Then release completely
- Notice the difference
- Move through body
- Creates relaxation

### Quick Releases

**Fast tension relief:**
- Shoulder shrugs then drop
- Make fist, then open
- Scrunch face, then relax
- Works anywhere
- Immediate relief

## Movement for Anxiety

### Physical Activity

**Exercise helps:**
- Burns off stress hormones
- Releases endorphins
- Gets out of head
- Muscle tension release
- Mood improvement

### Types That Help

**Options:**
- Walking
- Running
- Dancing
- Sports
- Yoga

### In the Moment

**When anxious:**
- Walk around
- Jump up and down
- Shake out limbs
- Any movement helps
- Gets body moving

## When Anxiety Happens

### At School

**Quick strategies:**
- Bathroom break
- Deep breaths at desk
- Grounding techniques
- Ask to step out
- Prepared strategies

### Before Tests or Events

**Preparation:**
- Deep breathing before
- Posture check
- Quick stretches
- Positive self-talk
- Have a plan

### Social Situations

**Managing:**
- Open posture helps confidence
- Slow breathing
- Focus outside yourself
- Grounding techniques
- Know you can handle it

## Grounding Techniques

### 5-4-3-2-1

**Sensory grounding:**
- 5 things you see
- 4 things you hear
- 3 things you feel
- 2 things you smell
- 1 thing you taste

### Physical Grounding

**Body awareness:**
- Feet on floor
- Hands on desk
- Feel your breath
- Notice your body
- Come back to now
