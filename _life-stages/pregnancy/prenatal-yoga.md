# Prenatal Yoga Guide

> **Moving with Your Changing Body:** Prenatal yoga offers strength, flexibility, and relaxation—all adapted for pregnancy. Learn what's safe and beneficial.

---

## Why Prenatal Yoga

### Benefits During Pregnancy

**Physical benefits:**
- Maintains flexibility
- Builds strength for labor
- Improves posture
- Reduces back pain
- Prepares body for birth
- Helps with sleep

**Mental benefits:**
- Stress reduction
- Body awareness
- Breath work for labor
- Community with other pregnant women
- Mind-body connection

### Posture Benefits Specifically

**Helps with:**
- Counteracts pregnancy posture changes
- Strengthens postural muscles
- Opens chest and shoulders
- Maintains spinal mobility
- Prepares for postpartum recovery

---

## General Safety Guidelines

### Essential Rules

**Always:**
- Inform instructor you're pregnant
- Modify as needed for YOUR body
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid overheating
- Listen to your body above all

**Never:**
- Push through pain
- Hold breath (except specific techniques)
- Continue if something feels wrong
- Compare to pre-pregnancy practice
- Ignore warning signs

### When to Stop

**Seek care if:**
- Bleeding or fluid leaking
- Dizziness or faintness
- Chest pain
- Contractions
- Significant pain
- Decreased baby movement

## Poses by Category

### Safe Throughout Pregnancy

**Generally safe:**
- Cat-Cow (all fours)
- Modified Child's Pose (knees wide)
- Standing poses (with balance support)
- Seated stretches
- Hip openers (gentle)
- Supported breathing poses

### Needs Modification

**Modify or avoid in later pregnancy:**
- Any lying on back (after first trimester)
- Deep twists (twist from shoulders, not waist)
- Belly-down poses
- Full forward folds (space for belly)
- Single-leg balancing (use wall)

### Avoid

**Not recommended during pregnancy:**
- Hot yoga / Bikram
- Deep backbends
- Inversions
- Core work like crunches
- Poses requiring lying on belly
- Extreme stretching

---

## First Trimester

### Approach

**Early pregnancy:**
- May continue regular practice with modifications
- Fatigue may limit practice
- Nausea may affect what's comfortable
- Start modifications early

### Modifications to Start

**Begin now:**
- Reduce intensity
- Skip hot yoga permanently
- Start avoiding belly-down poses
- Prepare for continued changes

---

## Second Trimester

### Approach

**Mid-pregnancy:**
- Often feel best for practice
- Belly requires accommodation
- Balance may change
- Good time to establish prenatal routine

### Key Modifications

**Adjust:**
- No lying flat on back
- Wide-kneed poses for belly
- Wall support for balance
- Props for support

---

## Third Trimester

### Approach

**Late pregnancy:**
- Most modification needed
- Gentle practice recommended
- Focus on breath and relaxation
- Preparation for birth

### Focus Areas

**Emphasize:**
- Hip opening (for labor)
- Breath work
- Relaxation
- Gentle movement
- Pelvic floor awareness

## Specific Poses and Modifications

### Cat-Cow

**Excellent throughout:**
- On hands and knees
- Arch and round spine gently
- Relieves back pain
- Safe entire pregnancy

### Child's Pose

**Modification:**
- Knees wide apart for belly
- May use props under forehead
- Comfortable stretch for back
- Resting pose

### Downward Dog

**Modification:**
- Shorten stance as belly grows
- Wall version later in pregnancy
- May become uncomfortable—skip if so
- Alternative: hands on chair

### Warrior Poses

**Safe with modifications:**
- Shorten stance for balance
- Wall nearby if needed
- Don't hold too long
- Strengthen legs for labor

### Tree Pose

**Balance challenge:**
- Hand on wall for support
- Foot lower (ankle or calf)
- Don't risk falling
- Build balance gradually

### Pigeon Pose

**Hip opener:**
- Use props generously
- Supported version safest
- Don't force depth
- Excellent for tight hips

### Squat (Malasana)

**Birth preparation:**
- Great for hip opening
- May use wall or props
- Not if SPD/pelvic pain
- Good for labor prep

## Breath Work (Pranayama)

### Safe Techniques

**Appropriate during pregnancy:**
- Deep belly breathing
- Extended exhale
- Gentle ujjayi (ocean breath)
- Counting breaths

### Avoid

**Not recommended:**
- Breath retention (holding)
- Rapid breathing (kapalabhati)
- Forceful breathing
- Anything causing dizziness

### Benefits

**Breath work helps:**
- Relaxation
- Labor preparation
- Stress management
- Body awareness

## Finding Classes

### Prenatal-Specific Classes

**Recommended:**
- Designed for pregnancy
- Instructor trained in prenatal
- Community of pregnant women
- Appropriate modifications built in

### Regular Yoga Classes

**If attending:**
- Inform instructor immediately
- Know your modifications
- Don't hesitate to modify
- Skip what doesn't feel right

### Online Prenatal Yoga

**Options:**
- Many available
- Choose reputable sources
- Still modify for your body
- Can practice at home

---

## Home Practice

### Setting Up

**What you need:**
- Yoga mat
- Props: bolster/pillows, blocks, blankets
- Clear wall space for balance
- Comfortable temperature

### Building a Practice

**Gentle routine:**
- 15-30 minutes is beneficial
- Consistency over intensity
- Include breath work
- End with relaxation

---

## Props for Pregnancy

### Essential Props

**Helpful items:**
- Bolster or firm pillows
- Yoga blocks
- Blankets
- Wall space
- Strap

### How They Help

**Uses:**
- Support for side-lying
- Height for sitting
- Stability for balance
- Comfort in poses
