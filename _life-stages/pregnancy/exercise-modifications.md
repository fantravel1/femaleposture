# Exercise Modifications During Pregnancy

> **Staying Active Safely:** Exercise during pregnancy is beneficial for both you and baby. The key is knowing how to modify as your body changes.

---

## General Pregnancy Exercise Guidelines

### Benefits of Prenatal Exercise

- Better posture support as body changes
- Reduced back pain
- Improved mood and energy
- Easier labor and recovery
- Reduced risk of gestational diabetes
- Better sleep

### When to Stop and Consult Provider

Stop exercising and seek advice if you experience:
- Vaginal bleeding
- Contractions
- Dizziness or faintness
- Chest pain
- Severe headache
- Calf swelling or pain
- Fluid leaking from vagina
- Decreased fetal movement

### General Modifications

**Avoid:**
- Lying flat on back after first trimester
- Exercises with fall risk
- Overheating
- Holding breath (Valsalva maneuver)
- Deep twisting or crunching
- High-impact jumping
- Contact sports

**Embrace:**
- Listening to your body
- Staying hydrated
- Keeping cool
- Modifying as needed
- Breathing freely

## First Trimester Modifications

### What's Happening

- Fatigue common
- Nausea possible
- Body may feel normal externally
- Relaxin hormone increasing
- Joint laxity beginning

### Exercise Approach

**Most exercises can continue as normal if:**
- You exercised before pregnancy
- No complications
- Energy allows

**Begin modifying:**
- Reduce intensity if fatigued
- Stay hydrated
- Don't overheat
- Listen to body signals

### Specific Modifications

**Stretching:**
- Don't overstretch—joints are loosening
- Hold stretches for less time
- Avoid extreme ranges

**Core Work:**
- Planks still usually fine
- Crunches can continue (for now)
- Focus on breathing patterns

**Strength Training:**
- Continue with modifications as needed
- Reduce weight if fatigued
- Skip exercises that feel wrong

## Second Trimester Modifications

### What's Happening

- Belly growing
- Center of gravity shifting
- Back curve increasing (lordosis)
- Round ligament discomfort possible
- Often feel more energetic

### Major Changes to Make

**1. No More Lying on Back (Supine)**

After 16-20 weeks:
- Uterus can compress major blood vessels
- May cause dizziness, nausea, shortness of breath
- Modify all back-lying exercises

**2. Avoid Deep Core Exercises**

- No crunches or sit-ups
- No intense twisting
- Focus on gentle core engagement

### Exercise Modifications

**Glute Bridges:**
- Original: Lying on back
- Modified: Inclined position (shoulders elevated on cushions) or standing hip thrusts against wall

**Bird-Dog:**
- Usually still works well
- Great core exercise during pregnancy
- Keep movements controlled

**Dead Bug:**
- Original: Lying on back
- Modified: Incline or switch to bird-dog

**Planks:**
- May continue if no diastasis recti concerns
- Modified: Incline plank (hands on bench)
- Wall plank for later pregnancy

**Stretching:**
- Continue gentle stretching
- Avoid extreme hip opener stretches
- Support belly during floor stretches

## Third Trimester Modifications

### What's Happening

- Significant belly size
- Balance more challenging
- Fatigue may return
- Braxton Hicks possible
- Preparing for labor

### Exercise Approach

**Focus on:**
- Gentle movement
- Breathing exercises
- Pelvic floor awareness
- Labor preparation
- Maintaining mobility

**Reduce:**
- Duration of workouts
- Intensity
- Complex movements
- Anything that feels wrong

### Third Trimester Exercise Options

**Standing Exercises:**
- Wall push-ups
- Wall squats (partial range)
- Standing pelvic tilts
- Standing cat-cow
- Arm exercises with light weights

**Seated Exercises:**
- Seated posture work
- Arm exercises
- Gentle stretches
- Breathing exercises

**Supported Side-Lying:**
- Side-lying leg lifts
- Clamshells (modified)
- Side-lying stretches

**Hands and Knees:**
- Cat-cow (very gentle)
- Bird-dog (modified range)
- Pelvic circles
- Rocking

## Specific Exercise Modifications

### For Back Pain Prevention

**Original: Regular Dead Bug**
→ Modified: Bird-dog on hands and knees

**Original: Lying Hip Flexor Stretch**
→ Modified: Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch

**Original: Cobra Pose**
→ Modified: Supported sphinx or cat-cow

### For Core Support

**Original: Planks**
→ Modified: Wall planks or incline planks

**Original: Ab Exercises**
→ Modified: Gentle pelvic floor engagement, diaphragmatic breathing

**Original: Twisting Exercises**
→ Modified: Open rotation without compression

### For Hip Strength

**Original: Clamshells (on floor)**
→ Modified: Standing clamshell against wall, or side-lying with pillow between knees

**Original: Squats (full depth)**
→ Modified: Partial squats, wall squats, or chair squats

**Original: Lunges**
→ Modified: Supported lunges holding onto something stable

### For Upper Back

**Original: Rows on bench**
→ Modified: Standing rows with band or cable

**Original: Prone exercises**
→ Modified: Standing or seated alternatives

## Sample Pregnancy-Safe Routine

### First Trimester (15-20 minutes)

1. Warm-up: Walking or gentle movement (3 min)
2. Cat-cow: 10 reps
3. Bird-dog: 8 each side
4. Squats: 12 reps
5. Wall push-ups: 10 reps
6. Rows with band: 12 reps
7. Hip flexor stretch: 30 sec each
8. Upper trap stretch: 30 sec each
9. Cool-down and breathing

### Second Trimester (15 minutes)

1. Walking warm-up (3 min)
2. Cat-cow: 8 reps
3. Bird-dog: 6 each side
4. Wall squats: 10 reps
5. Incline plank: 20 seconds × 2
6. Standing rows: 10 reps
7. Clamshells (side-lying with support): 10 each
8. Gentle stretching and breathing

### Third Trimester (10-15 minutes)

1. Gentle walking or movement
2. Pelvic circles on hands and knees
3. Cat-cow: 6 reps
4. Wall push-ups: 8 reps
5. Supported squats: 8 reps
6. Standing arm exercises with light weight
7. Gentle stretching
8. Deep breathing and relaxation

## Pelvic Floor During Pregnancy

### Importance

Your pelvic floor supports your growing baby. Proper exercise helps:
- Maintain strength
- Prepare for birth
- Support recovery

### Safe Pelvic Floor Exercise

**Kegels:**
- Still appropriate during pregnancy
- Focus on both contraction AND relaxation
- 10 contractions, holding 5 seconds each
- Practice full release

**Breathing:**
- Coordinate breath with pelvic floor
- Inhale: pelvic floor relaxes
- Exhale: gentle engagement

## Warning Signs During Exercise

### Stop Immediately If:

- Any pain
- Bleeding
- Leaking fluid
- Contractions
- Dizziness
- Chest pain
- Calf pain or swelling
- Decreased fetal movement

### Modify If:

- Uncomfortable
- Out of breath (can't talk)
- Feeling too hot
- Fatigued
- Any exercise feels wrong

---

## Questions to Ask Your Provider

Before starting or continuing exercise:
- Are there any restrictions for me?
- What activities should I avoid?
- How will I know if I'm doing too much?
- Any specific modifications for my situation?
