# Computer Work During Pregnancy

> **Your Body Is Changing—Your Setup Should Too:** As pregnancy progresses, your workstation needs to adapt to your changing body and needs.

---

## Changes That Affect Desk Work

### Physical Changes

**What happens:**
- Belly grows (obviously)
- Center of gravity shifts
- Breasts may enlarge
- Swelling may occur
- Fatigue increases

### Comfort Challenges

**Common issues:**
- Can't sit as close to desk
- Back pain
- Neck strain
- Swelling in legs/feet
- Carpal tunnel symptoms

---

## Chair Adjustments

### As Pregnancy Progresses

**Adapt by:**
- Adding lumbar support
- Adjusting seat height
- Moving closer or further as needed
- Using footrest

### Lumbar Support

**Crucial during pregnancy:**
- Rolled towel works
- Lumbar pillow
- Built-in support adjusted
- Low back needs support

### Seat Depth

**Consider:**
- May need shallower seat
- Belly clearance
- Edge of seat if needed
- Proper thigh support

---

## Desk Setup

### Distance Issues

**With growing belly:**
- Can't pull as close
- May need to adjust monitor
- Keyboard position changes
- Adapt throughout trimesters

### Monitor Position

**Adjust for:**
- Proper eye level
- Appropriate distance
- No neck strain
- May need to move further

### Keyboard and Mouse

**Considerations:**
- Elbows still at 90°
- Wrist neutral
- May need different position
- Watch for carpal tunnel symptoms

---

## Taking Breaks

### More Frequent Breaks

**During pregnancy:**
- Get up more often
- Every 30 minutes ideal
- Movement crucial
- Bathroom trips count

### What to Do on Breaks

**Helpful activities:**
- Walk briefly
- Stretch
- Drink water
- Rest eyes
- Change position

### Movement at Desk

**Between breaks:**
- Ankle circles
- Shoulder rolls
- Neck stretches
- Posture resets

---

## Managing Specific Issues

### Back Pain

**At computer:**
- Good lumbar support
- Feet elevated slightly
- Standing intervals
- Regular movement

### Swelling

**Reduce with:**
- Feet elevated
- Movement
- Compression stockings (ask provider)
- Avoid crossing legs

### Carpal Tunnel

**If developing:**
- Wrist splints
- Neutral wrist position
- Take breaks
- Ice if helpful
- See [Pregnancy Carpal Tunnel](/life-stages/pregnancy/carpal-tunnel/)

### Fatigue

**Managing:**
- Take breaks
- Stay hydrated
- Healthy snacks
- Pace yourself

---

## Standing Desk Options

### Benefits

**During pregnancy:**
- Reduces sitting pressure
- May help back pain
- Encourages movement
- Variety helps

### Cautions

**Watch for:**
- Fatigue
- Swelling
- Don't stand too long
- Alternate positions

### If You Have One

**Use wisely:**
- Alternate sit and stand
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Good shoes
- Listen to your body

---

## By Trimester

### First Trimester

**Challenges:**
- Fatigue
- Nausea
- May not need physical modifications
- Rest as needed

**Adjustments:**
- Keep snacks nearby
- Stay hydrated
- Take breaks for fatigue
- Listen to body

### Second Trimester

**Changes:**
- Belly growing
- Energy often returns
- Starting to need adjustments
- May be most comfortable

**Modifications:**
- Begin chair adjustments
- Add support as needed
- Increase break frequency

### Third Trimester

**Challenges:**
- Large belly
- Fatigue returns
- Discomfort increases
- Most modifications needed

**Major adjustments:**
- Full workstation review
- Frequent breaks essential
- Consider reduced hours if possible
- Comfort priority

---

## Working from Home

### Advantages

**Pregnant benefits:**
- Control environment
- Comfortable clothes
- Easy bathroom access
- Rest when needed

### Setup Still Matters

**Don't neglect:**
- Proper workstation
- Good chair
- Ergonomic setup
- Not couch working

## Talking to Your Employer

### Reasonable Accommodations

**May include:**
- Ergonomic equipment
- Break allowances
- Modified schedule
- Workstation adjustments

### How to Ask

**Approach:**
- Document needs
- Specific requests
- Doctor's note if needed
- Know your rights
