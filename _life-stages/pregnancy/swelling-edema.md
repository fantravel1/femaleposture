# Pregnancy Swelling: Managing Edema

> **Puffiness Is Common:** Most pregnant women experience some swelling. Positioning and movement can help manage it.

---

## Why Swelling Occurs

### Normal Pregnancy Changes

**Contributing factors:**
- Increased blood volume
- Fluid retention
- Pressure on blood vessels
- Hormonal changes
- Gravity effects

### When It's Worst

**Typically worse:**
- Third trimester
- End of day
- Hot weather
- After standing
- After sitting long periods

---

## Common Areas

### Feet and Ankles

**Most common:**
- Can be significant
- Shoes may not fit
- Uncomfortable
- Affects walking

### Hands

**Often affected:**
- Rings may not fit
- Can affect grip
- Morning stiffness
- May have numbness

### Legs

**Can extend:**
- Calves
- Thighs
- General puffiness
- Heaviness feeling

### Face

**Mild swelling:**
- Some is normal
- Sudden facial swelling concerning
- Monitor carefully

---

## Position Strategies

### Elevate When Possible

**For relief:**
- Feet up when sitting
- Legs elevated at rest
- Above heart level ideal
- Multiple times daily

### Lying Down

**Best positions:**
- Left side preferred
- Improves circulation
- Legs slightly elevated
- Pillow support

### Avoid Problematic Positions

**Reduce swelling by avoiding:**
- Sitting with legs crossed
- Standing still for long periods
- Legs dangling
- Constricted positions

---

## Movement Helps

### Why Movement Matters

**Activity:**
- Promotes circulation
- Reduces pooling
- Muscle pump action
- Prevents stagnation

### What to Do

**Helpful activities:**
- Walking regularly
- Ankle circles and pumps
- Swimming (excellent)
- Any movement

### At Desk or Seated

**Keep moving:**
- Ankle circles
- Foot pumps
- Regular standing
- Position changes

---

## Other Strategies

### Stay Hydrated

**Counterintuitive but true:**
- Dehydration worsens swelling
- Drink adequate water
- Reduce sodium intake
- Balance is key

### Compression

**May help:**
- Compression stockings
- Put on before swelling starts
- Ask provider about appropriate type
- Can reduce discomfort

### Cool Water

**For relief:**
- Cool foot soak
- Swimming
- Cooling gel
- Avoid extreme cold

### Comfortable Clothing

**Avoid:**
- Tight socks
- Constrictive bands
- Tight shoes
- Anything that cuts off circulation

---

## When to Worry

### Normal Swelling

**Typical:**
- Gradual onset
- Both sides equal
- Worse at end of day
- Improves with elevation

### Seek Immediate Care If

**Warning signs:**
- Sudden severe swelling
- Face swelling suddenly
- One leg much more than other
- Swelling with headache
- Swelling with vision changes
- High blood pressure

**These could indicate preeclampsia or blood clot - urgent evaluation needed.**

---

## Footwear

### What Helps

**Good choices:**
- Comfortable, roomy shoes
- Supportive
- Easy to put on
- Accommodate swelling

### What to Avoid

**Skip:**
- Tight shoes
- High heels
- Constrictive straps
- Anything uncomfortable
