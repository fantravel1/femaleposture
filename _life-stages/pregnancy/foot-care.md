# Foot Care During Pregnancy

> **The Foundation Changes:** Your feet carry extra weight and change during pregnancy. Good foot care affects your posture and comfort.

---

## How Pregnancy Affects Feet

### Common Changes

**What happens:**
- Swelling (edema)
- Arch flattening
- Foot size increase
- Balance shifts
- Pain and discomfort

### Why It Occurs

**Contributing factors:**
- Weight gain
- Fluid retention
- Hormone effects (relaxin)
- Center of gravity shift
- Increased load on feet

---

## Swelling

### Why Feet Swell

**During pregnancy:**
- Increased blood volume
- Fluid retention
- Pressure on blood vessels
- Normal but uncomfortable

### When It's Worst

**Typical pattern:**
- End of day
- Hot weather
- Standing for long periods
- Third trimester often worse

### Relief Strategies

**What helps:**
- Elevate feet regularly
- Rotate ankles
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid tight footwear
- Compression stockings (ask provider)
- Don't stand still

---

## Arch and Foot Size Changes

### Arch Flattening

**What happens:**
- Relaxin loosens ligaments
- Weight pushes arches down
- May be permanent for some
- Affects posture

### Size Changes

**Common experience:**
- Feet may grow 1/2 size or more
- May be permanent
- Width may increase too
- Need new shoes

---

## Footwear During Pregnancy

### What to Look For

**Good pregnancy shoes:**
- Supportive arch
- Wide toe box
- Low, stable heel
- Comfortable fit
- Easy to put on

### What to Avoid

**Skip:**
- High heels
- Flat shoes without support
- Tight, narrow shoes
- Anything uncomfortable

### Practical Tips

**Adjustments:**
- Shop later in day (when feet larger)
- May need bigger size
- Slip-ons easier as belly grows
- Supportive sandals okay

---

## Plantar Fasciitis

### Why It Occurs

**During pregnancy:**
- Extra weight
- Arch changes
- Foot stress increased
- Can develop or worsen

### Symptoms

**What you feel:**
- Heel pain (especially morning)
- Pain with first steps
- Improves then worsens
- May be severe

### Management

**Relief:**
- Supportive footwear
- Stretching
- Ice
- Rest when possible
- Orthotics may help

---

## Standing and Walking

### Daily Strategies

**Reduce foot stress:**
- Take sitting breaks
- Don't stand in one place
- Shift weight frequently
- Walk to keep circulation moving

### Anti-Fatigue Mats

**If standing:**
- At work
- In kitchen
- Cushions impact
- Reduces fatigue

---

## Exercises and Stretches

### Calf Stretches

**Helps feet too:**
- Against wall
- Hold 30 seconds
- Both legs
- Several times daily

### Ankle Circles

**Easy relief:**
- Rotate ankles
- Both directions
- Improves circulation
- Do sitting or lying

### Toe Exercises

**Strengthen feet:**
- Pick up towel with toes
- Toe curls
- Spread and squeeze
- When comfortable

### Rolling

**With ball:**
- Roll foot over tennis ball
- Gentle pressure
- Massage arch
- If comfortable reaching

---

## Nail and Skin Care

### As Belly Grows

**Challenges:**
- Hard to reach feet
- Can't see well
- Nail care difficult
- Skin needs attention

### Solutions

**Options:**
- Ask for help
- Professional pedicure
- Long-handled tools
- Moisturize regularly

---

## When to Worry

### Normal Swelling

**Typical:**
- Both feet
- End of day
- Reduces with elevation
- Not sudden

### Call Provider If

**Concerning:**
- Sudden severe swelling
- One leg more than other
- Swelling with headache/vision changes
- Doesn't improve with rest

---

## Postpartum Recovery

### After Birth

**What to expect:**
- Swelling continues briefly then improves
- Foot size may or may not return
- Arches may remain flatter
- Recovery varies
