# Pregnancy Rib Pain and Breathing

> **Baby Takes Up Space:** As your baby grows, your ribs and lungs have less room. This common discomfort has solutions.

---

## Why Ribs Hurt During Pregnancy

### Physical Changes

**What happens:**
- Uterus pushes upward
- Ribs expand outward
- Baby may kick ribs
- Diaphragm compressed

### When It's Worst

**Typically:**
- Third trimester
- When sitting
- After eating
- Baby in certain positions

---

## Types of Rib Discomfort

### Pressure Pain

**From expansion:**
- Aching along ribs
- Feeling of pressure
- Both sides or one
- Constant or intermittent

### Sharp Pain

**From baby movement:**
- Kicks to ribs
- Sudden jabs
- Usually one side
- Comes and goes

### Muscle Strain

**From stretching:**
- Intercostal muscles strained
- Achiness
- Worse with movement
- Can be significant

---

## Breathing Challenges

### Why Breathing Changes

**Physical causes:**
- Less room for lungs
- Diaphragm pushed up
- Increased oxygen demand
- Feels like not enough air

### Normal vs. Concerning

**Normal:**
- Some shortness of breath
- Especially with activity
- Later pregnancy
- Improves with position change

**Seek help if:**
- Severe difficulty
- Chest pain
- Blue lips or fingers
- Sudden onset
- With rapid heart rate

---

## Positions for Relief

### Sitting

**What helps:**
- Sit tall, not slumped
- Arms raised overhead briefly
- Lean slightly back
- Give ribs room

### Standing

**Relief strategies:**
- Reach arms up
- Side stretches
- Good posture
- Don't compress

### Lying Down

**At rest:**
- Prop up on pillows
- Side-lying with arm support
- Semi-reclined
- Not flat if breathing difficult

---

## Stretches for Relief

### Side Stretch

**Opens rib cage:**
- Raise one arm overhead
- Lean to opposite side
- Gentle stretch
- Both sides

### Chest Opener

**Expands breathing:**
- Arms behind back
- Open chest
- Shoulders back
- Breathe deeply

### Cat-Cow

**Spine mobility:**
- On hands and knees
- Arch and round
- Creates space
- Gentle movement

### Doorway Stretch

**Chest expansion:**
- Arms on doorframe
- Lean through gently
- Opens chest
- Improves breathing

---

## When Baby Is in Ribs

### Encouraging Movement

**May help:**
- Change your position
- Gentle pressure
- Movement
- Ice pack (baby may move away)

### Acceptance

**Sometimes:**
- Baby stays put
- Nothing works
- Temporary discomfort
- Baby will drop eventually

---

## Eating and Breathing

### Smaller Meals

**When stomach compressed:**
- Large meals worsen discomfort
- Smaller, frequent meals
- Don't overeat
- Easier breathing

### Timing

**Consider:**
- Eat earlier in evening
- Space between eating and lying down
- Avoid overly full feeling

---

## Bra Considerations

### Support Without Constriction

**For rib comfort:**
- Proper fitting bra
- Not too tight
- Extenders if needed
- Soft, stretchy options

### Going Without

**If helpful:**
- At home especially
- Less constriction
- Comfort priority
- Whatever helps

---

## When Baby Drops

### Relief Coming

**Late pregnancy:**
- Baby descends into pelvis
- Ribs get relief
- Breathing easier
- Different pressure (pelvic)

### Timing

**Varies:**
- First pregnancy: may drop weeks before labor
- Subsequent: may not drop until labor
- Individual variation

---

## Exercises for Breathing

### Diaphragmatic Breathing

**Practice:**
- Hand on belly
- Breathe into belly
- Expand ribs sideways
- Slow, controlled

### Pursed Lip Breathing

**For breathlessness:**
- Inhale through nose
- Exhale slowly through pursed lips
- Slows breathing
- Calms sensation
