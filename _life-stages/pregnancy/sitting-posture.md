# Sitting Posture During Pregnancy

> **Sitting Gets Complicated:** As your belly grows, sitting comfortably requires more thought. Good positioning reduces discomfort.

---

## How Pregnancy Affects Sitting

### Physical Changes

**What happens:**
- Belly takes up space
- Center of gravity shifts
- Lower back curve increases
- Hips loosen

### Common Challenges

**Sitting can cause:**
- Back pain
- Pelvic discomfort
- Difficulty breathing
- Circulation issues
- Heartburn

---

## Basic Sitting Position

### Alignment

**Try to:**
- Sit tall (as possible)
- Feet flat on floor
- Weight on sit bones
- Back supported

### Support

**Use:**
- Lumbar pillow or roll
- Seat cushion if helpful
- Footrest if feet don't reach
- Armrests when available

### Avoid

**Don't:**
- Slouch into soft furniture
- Cross legs (affects circulation)
- Sit for too long without moving
- Perch on edge unsupported

---

## By Trimester

### First Trimester

**Generally:**
- Most positions still work
- Fatigue may affect posture
- Start good habits
- May not need modifications yet

### Second Trimester

**Changes needed:**
- Lumbar support becomes important
- More frequent position changes
- Belly starting to affect position
- Find what works

### Third Trimester

**Significant adaptations:**
- Limited comfortable positions
- May need legs more open
- Support essential
- Frequent breaks crucial

## Different Sitting Surfaces

### Office Chair

**Adjustments:**
- Lower back support
- Proper height
- Room for belly
- See [Computer Work](/life-stages/pregnancy/computer-work/)

### Couch

**Challenges:**
- Often too soft
- Hard to get up
- Poor support
- Easy to slouch

**Solutions:**
- Sit toward edge
- Add back support
- Legs supported
- Avoid sinking in

### Car

**Considerations:**
- Seatbelt positioning
- Lumbar support
- Seat position for belly
- See [Driving Guide](/activities/driving/)

### Floor

**If sitting on floor:**
- Support behind you
- Pillow under hips
- Change positions frequently
- May not be comfortable late pregnancy

---

## Addressing Common Issues

### Lower Back Pain

**While sitting:**
- Good lumbar support
- Feet elevated slightly
- Don't sit too long
- Regular movement

### Pelvic Pain

**May help:**
- Sit on exercise ball (if stable for you)
- Equal weight on sit bones
- Legs slightly apart
- Avoid asymmetrical positions

### Circulation

**Prevent issues:**
- Don't cross legs
- Feet supported
- Regular movement
- Elevate legs when possible

### Heartburn

**Position matters:**
- More upright helps
- Don't slouch after eating
- See [Heartburn Positions](/life-stages/pregnancy/heartburn-positions/)

### Breathing Difficulty

**As baby grows:**
- Upright position helps
- Don't compress diaphragm
- Good posture opens chest
- May need to sit taller

---

## Movement While Sitting

### Regular Changes

**Every 30 minutes:**
- Shift position
- Stand briefly if possible
- Walk if able
- Prevent stiffness

### Seated Movement

**Can do:**
- Ankle circles
- Shoulder rolls
- Pelvic tilts
- Gentle stretches

### Getting Up

**Safely:**
- Move to edge of seat
- Feet flat
- Use arms
- Rise slowly

---

## Work Considerations

### Desk Job

**Long sitting:**
- Ergonomic setup essential
- Frequent breaks
- Movement throughout day
- Standing intervals if possible

### Meetings

**Extended sitting:**
- Choose good chair if possible
- Bring lumbar support
- Shift position
- Stand if appropriate

### Accommodations

**May need:**
- Ergonomic equipment
- Break allowances
- Chair upgrade
- Discuss with employer

---

## Home Sitting

### Create Comfortable Spaces

**Have ready:**
- Supportive chair option
- Pillows for support
- Footrest
- Good lighting

### Evening Rest

**Relaxation:**
- Feet up
- Supported position
- Comfortable but not too slouched
- Restorative

---

## Alternative Options

### Exercise Ball

**Some find helpful:**
- Promotes movement
- Can be comfortable
- Active sitting
- Caution: stability

### Standing

**Mix it up:**
- Standing desk options
- Alternate positions
- Don't stand too long either
- Balance between positions
