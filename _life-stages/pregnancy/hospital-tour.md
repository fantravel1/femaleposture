# Hospital Birth Tour During Pregnancy

Most pregnant women tour their planned birth facility before delivery. These tours, typically offered in the third trimester, involve walking, standing, and paying attention while your pregnant body is at its most cumbersome. Here's how to manage the tour comfortably while noting important details about your upcoming birth experience.

## Before the Tour

### Timing Selection
Choose a tour time when you typically have more energy.

### Comfortable Clothing
Wear comfortable shoes and clothing that accommodates your belly and temperature fluctuations.

### Snack and Water
Bring water and a snack in case the tour is longer than expected.

### Companion
Bring your birth partner so they know the facility too.

## Physical Tour Demands

### Walking Duration
Tours involve significant walking through hospital corridors.

### Standing Periods
Groups often stand while guides explain features.

### Pace Adjustment
Don't be afraid to ask for slower pacing or breaks.

### Elevator Access
Note elevator locations for actual labor day.

## What to Notice

### Entrance and Parking
Where do you enter in labor? Where does your support person park?

### Check-In Process
What happens when you arrive in labor?

### Labor Room Features
What equipment is available? Is there a tub, birthing ball, squat bar?

### Room Movement Space
Is there space to walk, change positions, and move during labor?

### Bathroom Access
Where is the bathroom relative to the bed?

## Comfort Observations

### Bed Quality
What does the labor bed look like? Can it adjust for different positions?

### Seating for Partners
Where will your support person sit or rest?

### Temperature Control
Is there individual room temperature control?

### Lighting Options
Can lights be dimmed for comfort?

## Movement and Positioning

### Wireless Monitoring
Is wireless fetal monitoring available for movement during labor?

### Walking Areas
Can you walk the halls during labor? Where?

### Alternative Positions
What equipment supports non-bed positions (balls, bars, stools)?

### Shower Access
Is there a shower you can use during labor?

## Physical Questions to Ask

### Movement Policies
What are the policies about movement during labor?

### Eating and Drinking
Can you eat or drink during labor?

### IV Requirements
Are IVs required, or can you have a hep-lock for mobility?

### Pushing Positions
What positions are supported for pushing?

## Postpartum Observations

### Recovery Room
Will you stay in the same room or move after delivery?

### Bed and Seating
What are the bed and seating options for recovery?

### Bathroom Distance
How far is the bathroom from the bed?

### Baby Equipment
Where will baby sleep? What equipment is provided?

## Tour Discomfort Management

### Speak Up
If you need to sit, say so. Tours should accommodate pregnant women.

### Lean When Standing
Use walls or partner support during standing explanations.

### Bathroom Breaks
Ask where bathrooms are and use them as needed.

### Leave Early if Needed
If you're too uncomfortable, it's okay to cut the tour short.

## Post-Tour Planning

### Route Rehearsal
Practice the route from home to hospital, including parking.

### Entrance Familiarity
Know exactly where to go when labor begins.

### Partner Preparation
Ensure your support person understands the layout.

### Comfort Item Planning
Based on what you saw, what might you want to bring for comfort?
