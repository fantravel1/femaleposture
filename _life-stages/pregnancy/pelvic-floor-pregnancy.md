# Pelvic Floor During Pregnancy

> **Your pelvic floor supports your growing baby and plays a crucial role in birth. Understanding and caring for these muscles helps prevent problems during pregnancy and supports recovery after.**

---

## What Is the Pelvic Floor?

A group of muscles that form the base of your core.

### Anatomy Basics

**Understanding the muscles:**
- Bowl-shaped muscle group
- Spans from pubic bone to tailbone
- Supports bladder, uterus, bowel
- Controls continence
- Works with diaphragm and abs

### Function During Pregnancy

**What pelvic floor does:**
- Supports growing uterus
- Maintains continence
- Stabilizes pelvis
- Will stretch during birth
- Needs care throughout

---

## Pregnancy Effects on Pelvic Floor

### Physical Changes

**What happens:**
- Weight of uterus increases pressure
- Hormones relax muscles
- Blood volume increases
- Baby's weight bears down
- Demand on muscles increases

### Common Symptoms

**You might experience:**
- Leaking when coughing or sneezing
- Increased urgency to urinate
- Heaviness feeling
- Difficulty controlling gas
- Pressure in pelvic area

## Pelvic Floor Exercises

### Finding the Muscles

**How to locate:**
- Imagine stopping urine flow
- Or holding back gas
- Feel lifting and squeezing
- Don't actually stop urine (just for learning)
- Internal sensation

### Basic Kegel Technique

**How to do Kegels:**
- Squeeze pelvic floor muscles
- Lift up and in
- Hold 3-5 seconds
- Release completely
- Rest between repetitions

### Exercise Routine

**Daily practice:**
- 3 sets of 10 repetitions
- Hold for 3-5 seconds
- Build to 10-second holds
- Include quick contractions too
- Twice daily minimum

### When to Do Them

**Integrate into life:**
- While brushing teeth
- At red lights
- During TV commercials
- Before getting out of bed
- Any waiting time

---

## Beyond Kegels

### Connection to Breathing

**Breath and pelvic floor:**
- Inhale, pelvic floor relaxes
- Exhale, pelvic floor lifts
- Natural coordination
- Practice this connection
- Useful for birth

### Core Integration

**Whole-body approach:**
- Pelvic floor works with core
- Transverse abdominis connection
- Diaphragm coordination
- Deep core system
- Not isolated exercise

### Relaxation Is Important

**Learning to release:**
- Not just about strength
- Need to relax for birth
- Balance tension and release
- Perineal massage practice
- Prepare for stretching

---

## Trimester Considerations

### First Trimester

**Early pregnancy:**
- Begin or continue exercises
- Establish habit
- Fatigue may affect consistency
- Foundation building
- Check with provider if concerns

### Second Trimester

**Mid-pregnancy:**
- Often feel best
- Continue consistent practice
- May notice more pressure
- Adjust if uncomfortable
- Build strength for later

### Third Trimester

**Late pregnancy:**
- Emphasize relaxation too
- Prepare for birth
- Pressure increases
- Continue gentle exercises
- Perineal massage begins

---

## Preparing for Birth

### Perineal Massage

**After 34 weeks:**
- Helps tissue stretch
- May reduce tearing
- Partner can help
- Technique matters
- Ask provider or midwife

### Birth Positions

**Pelvic floor in labor:**
- Upright positions helpful
- Squatting opens pelvis
- Side-lying gentle
- Water labor supportive
- Movement during labor

### During Pushing

**Pelvic floor in delivery:**
- Controlled pushing when able
- Exhale while pushing
- Allow stretching
- Listen to body
- Follow provider guidance

---

## Common Concerns

### Leaking Urine

**Stress incontinence:**
- Common in pregnancy
- Exercises help
- Not something to accept
- Address early
- Usually improves postpartum

### Pelvic Pressure

**Heaviness feelings:**
- Growing uterus weight
- Baby's position
- Supporting with exercise
- Support garment may help
- Discuss with provider if severe

### When to Seek Help

**Consult provider if:**
- Significant incontinence
- Pain with exercises
- Bulging sensation
- Unable to identify muscles
- Symptoms worsening

## Professional Support

### Pelvic Floor Physical Therapy

**What PT offers:**
- Assessment of function
- Personalized exercise program
- Internal evaluation if appropriate
- Techniques for birth
- Preparation for postpartum

### When to See Specialist

**Consider PT for:**
- Difficulty finding muscles
- Symptoms not improving
- Previous pelvic floor issues
- Wanting preparation guidance
- Any concerns

## Equipment and Props

### Biofeedback Devices

**Learning aids:**
- Home devices available
- Visual feedback of contraction
- Can help with awareness
- Not required
- May be useful for some

### Support Garments

**Physical support:**
- Maternity support belts
- May reduce pelvic pressure
- Not replacement for exercise
- Comfort measure
- Fitting important

## Postpartum Preview

### After Birth

**What to expect:**
- Pelvic floor will be stretched
- Recovery takes time
- Gentle exercises resume
- PT often helpful
- See related postpartum articles

### Building Foundation Now

**Preparation matters:**
- Strength before birth helps recovery
- Knowing the exercises
- Mind-body connection established
- Habits in place
- Investment in future
