# Prenatal Physical Therapy

> **Physical therapy during pregnancy can address pain, support pelvic floor function, improve posture, and prepare your body for birth. It's an underutilized resource that can significantly improve pregnancy comfort.**

---

## What Prenatal PT Offers

Physical therapists specializing in pregnancy address unique needs.

### Areas of Focus

**What PTs address:**
- Back and pelvic pain
- Pelvic floor function
- Diastasis recti prevention
- Posture optimization
- Birth preparation

### Specialized Training

**Prenatal expertise:**
- Understanding pregnancy changes
- Safe exercise modification
- Pelvic floor specialization
- Labor positioning knowledge
- Pregnancy-specific skills

---

## When to Seek PT

### For Pain

**Common complaints:**
- Low back pain
- Pelvic girdle pain
- Sciatica
- Round ligament pain
- Hip pain

### For Prevention

**Proactive reasons:**
- Pelvic floor education
- Posture guidance
- Exercise modification
- Preparing for demands
- Before problems develop

### For Preparation

**Birth readiness:**
- Labor positions
- Pushing preparation
- Pelvic floor function
- Physical readiness
- Recovery preparation

## Pelvic Floor Physical Therapy

### Why It Matters

**Pelvic floor focus:**
- Major pregnancy changes
- Affects birth and recovery
- Incontinence prevention
- Prolapse prevention
- Often overlooked

### What It Involves

**Pelvic floor PT:**
- Internal and external assessment
- Muscle awareness training
- Strengthening AND relaxing
- Coordination work
- Education

### When to Start

**Timing:**
- Any trimester
- Earlier for history of issues
- Prevention-focused
- Treatment if problems
- Sooner better than later
