# Third Trimester Posture Guide

> **The final stretch of pregnancy brings maximum physical challenges. Smart posture strategies help you stay as comfortable as possible while preparing for birth.**

---

## Third Trimester Body Changes

Your body undergoes dramatic changes that significantly impact posture and movement.

### Physical Realities

**What you're experiencing:**
- Maximum belly size
- Significant weight gain
- Shifted center of gravity
- Loosened ligaments at peak
- Baby's position affecting body

### Postural Challenges

**Common issues:**
- Increased low back curve
- Shoulders rounding forward
- Head pushing forward
- Waddling gait
- Balance challenges

---

## Standing Posture Strategies

### Finding Your Alignment

**Best standing position:**
- Feet wider than hips
- Knees slightly bent
- Pelvis gently tucked under
- Ribs stacked over pelvis
- Shoulders relaxed back

### Avoiding the Sway

**Prevent excessive back arch:**
- Gentle core engagement
- Tuck tailbone slightly
- Think of lengthening spine
- Don't lock knees
- Distribute weight evenly

### Managing Stand Time

**When you must stand:**
- Shift weight side to side
- Use one foot on low stool
- Limit standing duration
- Lean against support when possible
- Rest frequently

---

## Sitting in Late Pregnancy

### Chair Selection

**What works now:**
- Firm, supportive seats
- Arms for pushing up
- Proper height for your body
- Avoid deep, soft cushions
- Easy to get out of

### Positioning

**Comfortable seated posture:**
- Sit back fully in chair
- Both feet on floor (or footrest)
- Knees at or below hip level
- Lumbar support behind you
- Shoulders relaxed, not hunched

### Getting Up Safely

**Rising from seated:**
- Scoot to edge of chair
- Feet flat, shoulder-width apart
- Lean forward, nose over toes
- Push with arms and legs
- Rise slowly, don't rush

## Sleep Positions

### Left Side Lying

**Optimal sleep position:**
- Left side improves blood flow
- Pillow between knees essential
- Support under belly
- Arm positioned comfortably
- Head supported at neutral

### Pillow Fortress

**Create support system:**
- Body pillow or multiple pillows
- Under belly support
- Between knees for hips
- Behind back for stability
- Whatever keeps you comfortable

### When You Can't Get Comfortable

**Alternatives:**
- Try slight forward tilt (not full stomach)
- Reclined position in chair
- Propped in bed at angle
- Switch sides throughout night
- Accept frequent position changes

---

## Daily Movement Adjustments

### Walking

**Maintain mobility:**
- Shorter, more frequent walks
- Supportive shoes essential
- Slow, deliberate pace
- Watch for uneven surfaces
- Take breaks when needed

### Stairs

**Navigate carefully:**
- Use handrail always
- One step at a time
- Lead with stronger leg up
- Take your time
- Reduce trips when possible

### Bending and Lifting

**Protect your back:**
- Bend knees, keep back straight
- Avoid bending at waist
- Ask for help with heavy items
- Bring things to your level
- Use long-handled tools

## Common Discomforts and Solutions

### Lower Back Pain

**Relief strategies:**
- Pelvic tilts
- Cat-cow stretches
- Warm (not hot) compress
- Prenatal massage
- Support belt if recommended

### Pubic Symphysis Pain

**Managing SPD/PGP:**
- Keep legs together for movements
- Avoid wide stances
- Get in/out of car carefully
- Physical therapy may help
- Support belt for stability

### Rib Pain and Breathing

**When baby is up high:**
- Practice rib-expanding breaths
- Arm stretches overhead
- Seated forward lean (slightly)
- Baby may drop soon
- Short frequent breaths

### Leg and Foot Swelling

**Managing edema:**
- Elevate feet when sitting
- Avoid standing too long
- Stay hydrated (counterintuitive but helps)
- Comfortable, wide shoes
- Compression socks if recommended

## Work Modifications

### Desk Work

**Ergonomic essentials now:**
- Footrest important
- Lumbar support crucial
- Frequent standing breaks
- Monitor at eye level
- Keyboard at comfortable height

### On Your Feet Jobs

**Managing active work:**
- Discuss modifications with employer
- More frequent breaks
- Stool to sit when possible
- Compression stockings
- Reduce hours if possible

### Working from Home

**Create comfortable setup:**
- Move around freely
- Change positions often
- No commute stress
- Comfortable clothing
- Rest when needed

## Preparing for Labor

### Positions to Practice

**Birth-ready postures:**
- Squatting with support
- Hands and knees position
- Leaning forward on birth ball
- Standing with support
- Side-lying

### Pelvic Mobility

**Keep pelvis moving:**
- Hip circles daily
- Figure eight movements
- Gentle lunges (with support)
- Pelvic rocks
- Butterfly stretches

### Building Stamina

**Energy for labor:**
- Continue gentle walking
- Conserve energy when tired
- Sleep as much as possible
- Stay active but not exhausted
- Balance rest and movement

---

## Exercises for Third Trimester

### Safe Movement

**What's still appropriate:**
- Walking
- Swimming or water exercise
- Prenatal yoga (modified)
- Gentle stretching
- Pelvic floor exercises

### Core Awareness

**Gentle engagement:**
- No crunches or planks
- Transverse abdominis breathing
- Pelvic tilts
- Cat-cow stretches
- Awareness, not intensity

### Stretches That Help

**Daily relief:**
- Cat-cow for back
- Chest opener for shoulders
- Hip flexor stretch (gentle)
- Calf stretches
- Neck releases

## Preparing Your Space

### Home Setup

**Baby preparation and body care:**
- Items at accessible heights
- Minimize stairs if possible
- Comfortable rest spots
- Support items positioned
- Clear walking paths

### Hospital Bag

**Comfort items for labor:**
- Birth ball if facility allows
- Comfortable clothes
- Support tools
- Snacks and drinks
- Comfort from home

## When to Seek Help

### Warning Signs

**Contact provider for:**
- Severe pain
- Significant swelling
- Difficulty breathing at rest
- Decreased fetal movement
- Signs of preterm labor

### Physical Therapy

**May help with:**
- Severe back or pelvic pain
- Balance concerns
- Specific postural issues
- Birth preparation
- Specialized guidance

---

## Final Weeks

### Slowing Down

**Honor your body:**
- Rest more
- Nap when possible
- Accept limitations
- Conserve energy
- Almost there

### Baby Drops

**When baby engages:**
- Breathing may ease
- More pressure below
- More frequent bathroom trips
- Waddling may increase
- Sign of progress

### Staying Comfortable

**Day by day:**
- Each day brings changes
- Do what you can
- Rest when you need
- You're growing a human
- Be kind to yourself
