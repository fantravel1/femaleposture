# Labor Positions for Comfort and Progress

> **Movement Is Your Friend:** Staying mobile and changing positions during labor can help with comfort and baby's progress.

---

## Why Positions Matter

### Benefits of Movement

**During labor:**
- Helps baby descend
- Uses gravity
- Reduces pain
- May speed labor
- Gives you control

### Upright and Active

**Research shows:**
- Upright positions may shorten labor
- Gravity assists baby
- More comfort for many
- Better outcomes often

---

## Early Labor Positions

### Walking

**Benefits:**
- Uses gravity
- Keeps you moving
- Distracting
- Comfortable for many

### Standing and Swaying

**Technique:**
- Stand and sway hips
- Lean on partner/support
- Dance-like movement
- Rhythmic motion

### Hands and Knees

**Good for:**
- Back labor relief
- Rotating baby
- Resting
- Any time

---

## Active Labor Positions

### Sitting

**Options:**
- Birth ball
- Chair backwards
- Toilet (seriously)
- Opens pelvis

### Birth Ball

**Uses:**
- Sitting and bouncing
- Leaning over ball
- Rocking
- Very versatile

### Squatting

**Benefits:**
- Opens pelvis maximally
- Uses gravity fully
- Supported squat helpful
- Bar or partner support

### Kneeling

**Variations:**
- Leaning on bed
- On all fours
- Supported positions
- Good for back labor

### Side-Lying

**When needed:**
- When tired
- For fetal monitoring
- Still allows movement
- Better than flat on back

---

## For Back Labor

### What Is Back Labor

**When baby is:**
- Posterior position (back to back)
- Causes back pain
- Can slow labor
- Positions can help

### Helpful Positions

**Try:**
- Hands and knees
- Leaning forward
- Pelvic rocking
- Avoid lying on back

### Movement for Rotation

**Encouraging baby to turn:**
- Hip circles
- Asymmetrical positions
- Movement helps
- Patience needed

---

## With an Epidural

### Limited Mobility

**When you can't feel:**
- Movement still possible
- Position changes still help
- Staff can help move
- Not stuck on back

### Options

**Positions with epidural:**
- Side-lying (alternate sides)
- Supported sitting
- Peanut ball between legs
- Hands and knees (with help)

---

## Pushing Positions

### Various Options

**Consider:**
- Semi-reclined
- Squatting (supported)
- Side-lying
- Hands and knees
- Depends on situation

### What May Work

**Benefits:**
- Upright uses gravity
- Side-lying good for slow delivery
- Hands and knees for posterior baby
- Provider will guide

---

## Partner Support

### How Partners Help

**In labor:**
- Physical support
- Counter pressure
- Encouragement
- Position assistance

### Counter Pressure

**For back labor:**
- Pressure on sacrum
- Hip squeeze
- Very relieving
- Partner learns technique

---

## Hospital vs. Home/Birth Center

### Movement Options

**May vary by location:**
- Hospital: may have restrictions
- Birth center: usually mobile
- Home: maximum flexibility
- Know your options

### Advocating

**If in hospital:**
- Ask about movement
- Wireless monitoring if available
- Express preferences
- Work with staff

---

## Preparing

### Practice Before

**During pregnancy:**
- Try different positions
- Get comfortable with options
- Practice with partner
- Know what feels good

### Birth Plan

**Consider:**
- Your position preferences
- Flexibility for what helps
- Communication with team
- Be open to adaptation
