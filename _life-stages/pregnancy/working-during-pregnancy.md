# Working During Pregnancy: Posture Guide

> **Nine Months at the Office:** Your body is changing dramatically while you're still meeting deadlines. Smart adaptations keep you comfortable and productive.

---

## How Pregnancy Changes Work Posture

### Physical Changes Affecting Work

**First Trimester:**
- Fatigue affects sitting stamina
- Nausea may limit positions
- Breast tenderness changes comfort
- Subtle center of gravity shifts begin

**Second Trimester:**
- Belly growth starts affecting desk distance
- Lower back curve increases
- Ligament loosening begins
- Energy often improves

**Third Trimester:**
- Significant belly accommodation needed
- Increased lower back strain
- Swelling in legs and feet
- Frequent position changes essential
- Bathroom breaks more frequent

---

## Desk Setup Modifications

### Chair Adjustments

**As pregnancy progresses:**
- May need lower lumbar support position
- Seat depth may feel different
- More frequent position changes needed
- Consider pregnancy pillow or wedge

**Key adjustments:**
- Lumbar support repositioned for changing curve
- Seat height adjusted as belly grows
- Armrests may need widening or removal
- Footrest becomes more important

### Desk Distance

**Accommodating belly:**
- Pull chair closer initially
- Eventually need desk farther away
- Keyboard tray helpful
- Consider standing desk options

### Monitor Position

**As posture changes:**
- May need height adjustment
- Distance may need to change
- Avoid neck strain from compensating

---

## Sitting Strategies

### Position Variety

**Essential during pregnancy:**
- Change positions every 30 minutes minimum
- Alternate sitting and standing
- Brief walks regularly
- Don't stay static

### Supportive Sitting

**What helps:**
- Firm cushion or pregnancy wedge
- Lumbar support (adjustable)
- Feet flat on floor or footrest
- Knees at or below hip level

### What to Avoid

**Problematic positions:**
- Crossing legs (circulation issues)
- Leaning back without support
- Perching on chair edge
- Sitting for hours without breaks

---

## Standing Work

### Standing Desk Benefits

**During pregnancy:**
- Reduces sitting pressure
- Allows position variety
- Can ease lower back strain
- Promotes circulation

### Standing Desk Challenges

**Later in pregnancy:**
- Fatigue increases
- Foot swelling worsens
- May need more sitting breaks
- Balance considerations

### Best Approach

**Alternating:**
- Mix sitting and standing
- Anti-fatigue mat essential
- Supportive footwear
- Frequent changes

---

## Managing Specific Discomforts

### Lower Back Pain

**At work strategies:**
- Lumbar support in chair
- Frequent position changes
- Standing breaks
- Gentle stretching
- Heat or ice as appropriate

### Sciatica

**If experiencing:**
- Avoid prolonged sitting
- Cushion under affected side
- Standing may help
- See [Pregnancy Sciatica](/life-stages/pregnancy/sciatica/)

### Pelvic Pain

**SPD/PGP at work:**
- Avoid asymmetrical positions
- Keep knees together when turning
- Supportive seating
- May need accommodations

### Swelling

**Legs and feet:**
- Elevate when possible
- Compression stockings
- Regular walking
- Reduce salt intake
- Stay hydrated

---

## Breaks and Movement

### Essential Breaks

**Minimum schedule:**
- Stand/move every 30 minutes
- Walk every 1-2 hours
- Bathroom as needed (don't hold it)
- Snack breaks for blood sugar

### Office Movement

**Easy options:**
- Walk to colleague instead of email
- Take stairs (if comfortable)
- Walking meetings
- Stretch at desk

### Stretches at Work

**Safe pregnancy stretches:**
- Shoulder rolls
- Neck stretches
- Cat-cow at desk edge
- Standing hip stretches
- Calf raises

## First Trimester Specific

### Hiding Pregnancy at Work

**If not yet sharing:**
- Fatigue may be noticeable
- Nausea may require breaks
- Posture changes subtle
- Privacy is your choice

### Managing Symptoms

**At the office:**
- Snacks for nausea
- Rest when possible
- Hydration
- Work from home if available

## Second Trimester Specific

### The "Good" Trimester

**Often easier:**
- Energy improves
- Nausea typically subsides
- Belly not yet limiting
- Good time to optimize setup

### Growing Belly Adjustments

**Starting to show:**
- Begin desk adjustments
- Wardrobe changes for comfort
- Belly starting to affect posture

---

## Third Trimester Specific

### Maximum Accommodation

**Final stretch:**
- Belly significantly impacts positioning
- Fatigue returns
- Bathroom frequency increases
- May need modified schedule

### Work Modifications

**May need:**
- Reduced hours
- More work from home
- Modified duties
- Earlier maternity leave
