# Arthritis and Posture for Seniors

> **Movement Is Medicine:** Arthritis makes movement difficult, but movement is essential for managing arthritis. Finding the balance is key.

---

## Understanding Arthritis and Posture

### How Arthritis Affects Posture

**Direct effects:**
- Joint stiffness limits range
- Pain alters movement patterns
- Inflammation affects function
- Structural changes over time

**Compensations:**
- Avoiding painful positions
- Favoring one side
- Reduced activity
- Postural adaptations

### Common Types

**Most common in seniors:**
- Osteoarthritis (wear and tear)
- Rheumatoid arthritis (autoimmune)
- Each affects body differently
- Both impact posture

---

## Arthritis by Joint

### Spine

**Effects:**
- Stiffness
- Limited mobility
- Changed posture
- Pain with movement

**Considerations:**
- Gentle movement important
- Avoid extremes
- Support when sitting
- See [Spinal Stenosis](/conditions/spinal-stenosis/)

### Hips

**Effects:**
- Changed gait
- Compensatory posture
- Difficulty standing
- Limited range

**Approaches:**
- Hip-friendly movement
- Strengthening around joint
- Assistive devices if needed

### Knees

**Effects:**
- Difficulty with stairs
- Changed walking pattern
- Standing challenges
- May affect back

**Management:**
- Appropriate footwear
- Strengthening exercises
- Weight management
- Assistive devices

### Hands

**Effects:**
- Grip difficulties
- Task limitations
- May affect assistive device use

**Approaches:**
- Ergonomic aids
- Gentle exercises
- Adaptive equipment

---

## Movement Strategies

### Keep Moving

**Why essential:**
- Movement lubricates joints
- Inactivity worsens stiffness
- Muscle strength supports joints
- Use it or lose it principle

### Finding the Balance

**The challenge:**
- Enough activity to help
- Not so much it flares
- Rest when needed
- Move when able

### Morning Stiffness

**Managing:**
- Gentle stretching before rising
- Warm shower
- Movement loosens joints
- Don't rush

---

## Exercise for Arthritis

### What Helps

**Beneficial activities:**
- Walking (joint-friendly)
- Swimming/water exercise
- Tai chi
- Chair exercises
- Gentle stretching

### What to Modify

**Adapt:**
- High-impact activities
- Heavy weights
- Extreme ranges of motion
- Whatever flares symptoms

### Strengthening

**Important because:**
- Strong muscles protect joints
- Improves function
- Maintains independence
- Better posture

---

## Sitting with Arthritis

### Chair Selection

**Look for:**
- Appropriate height
- Armrests (help rising)
- Firm support
- Easy to get up from

### Positioning

**For comfort:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Support lower back
- Change positions often
- Don't stay still too long

### Rising from Seated

**Technique:**
- Scoot to edge
- Use armrests
- Lean forward
- Push up with legs

---

## Standing and Walking

### Posture

**Despite arthritis:**
- Stand as tall as comfortable
- Don't overcorrect
- Avoid pain positions
- Use support if needed

### Assistive Devices

**If helpful:**
- Cane (properly sized)
- Walker
- No shame in using
- Enables activity

### Footwear

**Important:**
- Supportive shoes
- Good cushioning
- Easy to put on
- Stable base

---

## Managing Flares

### When Joints Worsen

**During flares:**
- Rest more
- Gentle movement still helpful
- Ice or heat as advised
- Medication as prescribed

### Posture During Flares

**Be gentle:**
- Don't push through pain
- Modify activities
- Use support
- This is temporary

---

## Environmental Adaptations

### Home Setup

**Helpful changes:**
- Raised toilet seat
- Grab bars
- Appropriate seating
- Reduced reaching/bending

### Tools and Aids

**Consider:**
- Long-handled reachers
- Ergonomic tools
- Jar openers
- Adaptive equipment

---

## Heat and Cold

### For Relief

**May help:**
- Warm shower for stiffness
- Heating pad
- Ice for inflammation
- Find what works for you

### Before Activity

**Often helpful:**
- Warm up joints first
- Gentle movement
- Then more activity
- Reduces stiffness

---

## Working with Healthcare

### Team Approach

**May involve:**
- Rheumatologist
- Physical therapist
- Occupational therapist
- Primary care doctor

### Treatment Options

**May include:**
- Medications
- Physical therapy
- Joint injections
- Surgery for severe cases
