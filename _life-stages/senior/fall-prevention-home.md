# Fall Prevention at Home for Seniors

> **Falls are not an inevitable part of aging. With smart home modifications and body awareness, you can significantly reduce your risk and maintain your independence.**

---

## Why Falls Happen

Understanding fall risk helps you prevent them.

### Physical Factors

**Body-related risks:**
- Muscle weakness
- Balance problems
- Vision changes
- Medication side effects
- Chronic conditions

### Environmental Factors

**Home hazards:**
- Clutter and obstacles
- Poor lighting
- Slippery surfaces
- Unstable furniture
- Challenging stairs

### Behavioral Factors

**Actions that increase risk:**
- Rushing
- Not using assistive devices
- Wearing inappropriate footwear
- Taking risks
- Ignoring warning signs

---

## Room-by-Room Safety

### Bathroom

**Highest risk area:**
- Install grab bars by toilet and tub
- Use non-slip mat in tub/shower
- Consider shower chair or bench
- Raised toilet seat if helpful
- Good lighting, especially night light

### Bedroom

**Where falls often occur:**
- Clear path from bed to bathroom
- Night light or bedside lamp
- Firm mattress edge for sitting
- Bedside phone or call device
- Avoid slippery rugs

### Kitchen

**Daily use area:**
- Non-slip mats at sink
- Step stool with handrail for reaching
- Keep frequently used items accessible
- Wipe spills immediately
- Good task lighting

### Living Areas

**Common spaces:**
- Secure or remove throw rugs
- Clear walking paths
- Stable furniture arrangement
- Electrical cords tucked away
- Adequate lighting throughout

### Stairs

**High-risk transitions:**
- Handrails on both sides
- Even, visible stair edges
- No storage on stairs
- Good lighting top and bottom
- Consider stair lift if needed

---

## Lighting Improvements

### General Lighting

**See where you're going:**
- Bright enough to see clearly
- Light switches at entrances
- Night lights in hallways
- Reduce glare
- Replace bulbs promptly

### Task Lighting

**For specific activities:**
- Reading areas well lit
- Kitchen work surfaces
- Bathroom mirror lighting
- Stairway lighting
- Outdoor path lights

### Night Safety

**Darkness navigation:**
- Night lights throughout
- Motion-activated lights helpful
- Flashlight by bed
- Glow-in-dark light switches
- Path to bathroom lit

---

## Flooring Considerations

### Rug Safety

**What to do about rugs:**
- Remove throw rugs if possible
- Secure edges if keeping
- Non-slip backing
- No curled corners
- Nothing to trip over

### Floor Surfaces

**Safe flooring:**
- Non-slip surfaces
- No loose tiles or boards
- Consistent surface level
- Clean but not slippery
- Threshold strips secure

### Wet Areas

**Managing moisture:**
- Non-slip mats in bathrooms
- Wipe up spills immediately
- Non-slip shoe covers
- Mats outside entrances
- Careful in rain

---

## Furniture Arrangement

### Creating Clear Paths

**Easy navigation:**
- Wide walkways
- Furniture pushed back
- No obstacles in paths
- Consistent arrangement
- Know your space

### Stable Furniture

**What to trust for support:**
- Solid, heavy pieces
- No wobbly tables
- Chairs with armrests
- Appropriate heights
- Nothing that slides

### Strategic Resting Spots

**Support along routes:**
- Chair or bench in hallways
- Place to pause on long walks
- Something to hold if needed
- Plan routes with support
- Don't rush

---

## Movement and Posture Habits

### Moving Safely

**Body mechanics:**
- Get up slowly
- Use furniture for support
- Don't rush
- Look where you're going
- Carry loads carefully

### Posture for Stability

**Balance foundations:**
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Weight evenly distributed
- Core engaged
- Head up, eyes forward
- Knees slightly bent

### Transitional Movements

**Getting up and down:**
- Pause at edge of bed
- Use armrests to rise from chairs
- Hold handrails on stairs
- Be careful on ladders (or don't use)
- One movement at a time

---

## Footwear Matters

### Indoor Shoes

**What to wear at home:**
- Supportive shoes, not slippers
- Non-slip soles
- Secure fit
- Low heel or flat
- Not barefoot or socks alone

### Outdoor Footwear

**For going out:**
- Good tread for traction
- Appropriate for weather
- Comfortable for walking
- Supportive structure
- Replace when worn

## Assistive Devices

### When to Consider

**Signs you might benefit:**
- Unsteadiness
- Near-falls
- Fear of falling
- Doctor recommendation
- Want extra security

### Options Available

**Types of devices:**
- Cane
- Walker
- Rollator
- Grab bars
- Shower chair

### Using Devices Properly

**Effectiveness depends on use:**
- Correct size and adjustment
- Proper technique
- Consistent use
- Maintenance
- Professional fitting

## Physical Preparation

### Strength Training

**Build fall resistance:**
- Leg strength especially
- Core stability
- Upper body for catching
- Balance exercises
- Regular practice

### Balance Exercises

**Improve stability:**
- Single leg standing
- Heel-to-toe walking
- Tai Chi
- Weight shifts
- Challenge safely

### Flexibility

**Maintain mobility:**
- Daily stretching
- Joint range of motion
- Prevent stiffness
- Enable reactions
- Overall suppleness

---

## Vision and Medication

### Regular Eye Exams

**See clearly:**
- Annual eye exams
- Updated prescriptions
- Appropriate glasses worn
- Be careful with bifocals on stairs
- Treat eye conditions

### Medication Review

**Know your effects:**
- Discuss with doctor
- Side effects that affect balance
- Interaction effects
- Timing of medications
- Don't skip doses

---

## Emergency Preparation

### If You Fall

**What to do:**
- Stay calm
- Assess for injury
- If able, get to phone
- Call for help if needed
- Roll to side to get up

### Getting Up Safely

**After a fall:**
- Roll to hands and knees
- Crawl to sturdy furniture
- Use furniture to push up
- Rest before walking
- Seek medical attention if hurt

### Communication Plans

**Be prepared:**
- Phone accessible
- Medical alert device option
- Regular check-in with someone
- Emergency contacts posted
- Know when to call 911
