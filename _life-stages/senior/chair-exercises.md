# Chair Exercises for Senior Women

> **Strength from Your Seat:** A sturdy chair is all the equipment you need. These exercises build strength, maintain flexibility, and support good postureâ€”all from a seated position.

---

## Why Chair Exercises Work

### Benefits

**Chair exercises offer:**
- Safe, stable base
- Reduced fall risk
- Accessible to most abilities
- Effective strength building
- Posture improvement
- Can progress to standing

### Who Benefits

**Helpful for:**
- Anyone wanting safe exercise
- Limited mobility
- Balance concerns
- Recovering from injury
- Starting or returning to exercise

---

## Getting Started

### What You Need

**Equipment:**
- Sturdy chair (no wheels, arms optional)
- Comfortable clothing
- Water nearby
- Optional: light weights, resistance band

### Safety Guidelines

**Important:**
- Sit back in chair firmly
- Feet flat on floor
- Breathe throughout
- Never hold breath
- Stop if painful
- Quality over quantity

## Warm-Up (5 Minutes)

### Seated March

**Movement:**
- Sit tall
- Lift one knee, then other
- Alternate for 1-2 minutes
- Arms can swing opposite

### Shoulder Rolls

**Movement:**
- Roll shoulders forward, up, back, down
- 10 circles forward
- 10 circles backward
- Smooth, continuous motion

### Ankle Circles

**Movement:**
- Lift one foot slightly
- Circle ankle 10 times each direction
- Repeat other foot

### Head Turns

**Movement:**
- Slowly turn head right
- Return to center
- Turn left
- 5 times each side
- Keep shoulders still

---

## Upper Body Exercises

### Seated Row (Posture)

**Excellent for posture:**
- Sit tall
- Extend arms forward
- Pull elbows back, squeeze shoulder blades
- Return slowly
- 10-15 repetitions

### Arm Raises

**Front raise:**
- Arms at sides
- Lift arms forward to shoulder height
- Lower slowly
- 10-15 repetitions

**Side raise:**
- Arms at sides
- Lift arms out to sides to shoulder height
- Lower slowly
- 10-15 repetitions

### Bicep Curls

**With or without weights:**
- Arms at sides, palms forward
- Bend elbows, bring hands to shoulders
- Lower slowly
- 10-15 repetitions

### Chest Press

**Movement:**
- Arms bent, hands at shoulder height
- Press arms forward
- Return to start
- 10-15 repetitions

### Overhead Press

**Movement:**
- Hands at shoulders
- Press up toward ceiling
- Lower slowly
- 10-15 repetitions
- Can do one arm at a time

---

## Core Exercises

### Seated Core Engagement

**Foundation:**
- Sit tall
- Gently pull belly button toward spine
- Hold 5-10 seconds
- Release
- 10 repetitions

### Seated Twist

**Movement:**
- Sit tall, hands on shoulders or across chest
- Rotate upper body right
- Return to center
- Rotate left
- 10 times each side
- Keep hips facing forward

### Seated Side Bend

**Movement:**
- Sit tall, hands at sides
- Reach right hand toward floor
- Return to center
- Reach left side
- 10 times each side

### Seated Crunch (Gentle)

**Movement:**
- Sit tall
- Lean back slightly (keeping back straight)
- Return to upright
- 10 repetitions
- Control the movement

---

## Lower Body Exercises

### Seated Leg Extensions

**Movement:**
- Sit tall
- Extend one leg straight out
- Hold 2-3 seconds
- Lower slowly
- 10-15 each leg

### Knee Lifts

**Movement:**
- Sit tall
- Lift one knee toward chest
- Lower slowly
- 10-15 each leg
- Can add hand assistance

### Heel Raises

**Movement:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Lift heels, staying on toes
- Lower slowly
- 15-20 repetitions

### Toe Raises

**Movement:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Lift toes while heels stay down
- Lower
- 15-20 repetitions

### Seated Hip Abduction

**Movement:**
- Sit at edge of chair
- Feet together
- Open knees apart
- Bring back together
- 10-15 repetitions
- Can add resistance band

---

## Balance Challenges (From Chair)

### Seated Single Leg Lift

**Movement:**
- Sit tall
- Lift one foot off floor
- Hold 10-30 seconds
- Repeat other side
- Builds balance confidence

### Arms Overhead Balance

**Movement:**
- Sit tall
- Raise both arms overhead
- Hold 10-30 seconds
- Challenges core stability

### Reduced Base

**Movement:**
- Sit without touching chair back
- Engage core for balance
- Hold as long as comfortable
- Builds core stability

---

## Flexibility/Stretching

### Chest Opener

**Stretch:**
- Clasp hands behind back
- Lift chest, squeeze shoulder blades
- Hold 15-30 seconds
- Great for posture

### Neck Stretch

**Stretch:**
- Tilt ear toward shoulder
- Hold 15-30 seconds
- Repeat other side
- Don't force

### Seated Cat-Cow

**Movement:**
- Hands on knees
- Round back, drop head (cat)
- Arch back, lift chest (cow)
- 10 repetitions
- Gentle spinal movement

### Seated Hamstring Stretch

**Stretch:**
- Extend one leg straight
- Lean forward from hips (back straight)
- Hold 15-30 seconds
- Repeat other leg

### Hip Flexor Stretch

**Stretch:**
- Sit at edge of chair
- One foot forward, one foot back
- Gently shift forward
- Hold 15-30 seconds each side

## Sample Workout Routine

### Quick Session (10 Minutes)

1. Seated march (1 minute)
2. Shoulder rolls (30 seconds)
3. Seated rows (10 reps)
4. Leg extensions (10 each)
5. Heel raises (15 reps)
6. Core engagement (5 holds)
7. Stretches (2-3)

### Full Session (20-30 Minutes)

1. Complete warm-up (5 minutes)
2. All upper body exercises (5-10 minutes)
3. All lower body exercises (5-10 minutes)
4. Core exercises (3-5 minutes)
5. Stretching (5 minutes)

---

## Progressing

### Making It Harder

**When ready:**
- Add light weights
- Use resistance bands
- Increase repetitions
- Progress to standing versions
- Hold positions longer

### Moving to Standing

**Progression:**
- Standing behind chair (holding for balance)
- One hand on chair
- Standing near chair
- Fully independent
