# Stair Safety for Seniors

> **Stairs are a leading location for falls in older adults. Understanding safe navigation techniques, making modifications, and knowing your limits helps you use stairs safely or decide on alternatives.**

---

## Why Stairs Are Risky

Stairs present significant challenges.

### Physical Demands

**What stairs require:**
- Strength
- Balance
- Coordination
- Vision
- Concentration

### Age-Related Changes

**What may decline:**
- Leg strength
- Balance
- Depth perception
- Reaction time
- Joint mobility

---

## Safe Climbing Techniques

### Going Up

**Best approach:**
- Use handrail always
- One step at a time
- Full foot on step
- Steady pace
- Don't rush

### Going Down

**Even more careful:**
- Railing essential
- Watch each step
- Slower is safer
- No carrying items in both hands
- Extra caution

### General Principles

**Always:**
- Hold handrail
- Look at steps
- Well-lit conditions
- No rushing
- Full attention

---

## Handrail Use

### Always Use Them

**Critical:**
- Both directions
- Not optional
- Proper grip
- Full length of stairs
- Essential safety

### Grip Strength

**Maintaining:**
- Hand exercises
- Grip strength matters
- Practice holding firmly
- Strong grip protects
- Build and maintain

### When No Railing

**Extra caution:**
- Consider not using those stairs
- Request installation
- Extreme care
- Find alternatives
- Advocate for safety

---

## Environmental Modifications

### Lighting

**Essential:**
- Bright stairway lighting
- No shadows
- Both top and bottom
- Night lights
- Visible steps

### Stair Modifications

**Safety additions:**
- Contrasting step edges
- Non-slip strips
- Sturdy railings both sides
- Clear of clutter
- Safe conditions

### Clutter-Free

**Remove hazards:**
- Nothing on steps
- No throw rugs at base
- Clear landings
- Unobstructed path
- Hazard-free

---

## Strength for Stairs

### Leg Exercises

**Building ability:**
- Step-ups (low step)
- Chair squats
- Leg strengthening
- Maintain capability
- Ongoing exercise

### Balance Training

**Important:**
- General balance work
- Single-leg exercises
- Stability training
- Supports stair use
- Fall prevention

---

## When to Be Extra Careful

### Risk Times

**Higher risk:**
- When tired
- After alcohol
- With vision problems
- Carrying things
- Unfamiliar stairs

### Unfamiliar Stairs

**New places:**
- Extra caution
- Different heights
- Different treads
- Unknown condition
- Go slowly

---

## Carrying Things

### The Problem

**Challenges:**
- Both hands occupied
- Can't use rail
- Vision blocked
- Balance affected
- Risky

### Safer Approaches

**What to do:**
- Make multiple trips
- Keep one hand free
- Use backpack
- Have help
- Don't risk it
