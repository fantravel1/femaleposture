# Arthritis and Posture

> **Arthritis affects millions of older women, changing how you move and hold your body. Understanding how to maintain good posture while protecting arthritic joints helps preserve function and reduce pain.**

---

## How Arthritis Affects Posture

Joint changes influence how we carry ourselves.

### Pain's Postural Impact

**What happens:**
- Avoiding painful movements
- Compensating with other joints
- Reduced range of motion
- Protective positioning
- Changed movement patterns

### Joint Changes

**Physical effects:**
- Stiffness affecting mobility
- Swelling influencing position
- Deformity in severe cases
- Weakness affecting support
- Function limitations

---

## Types of Arthritis

### Osteoarthritis

**Most common:**
- Wear-and-tear arthritis
- Affects weight-bearing joints
- Cartilage breakdown
- Common in spine, hips, knees, hands
- Progressive but manageable

### Rheumatoid Arthritis

**Autoimmune:**
- Systemic inflammation
- Affects many joints
- Requires medical management
- More complex condition
- Posture still matters

### Other Types

**Also common:**
- Spinal arthritis
- Psoriatic arthritis
- Gout
- Various conditions
- Different considerations

---

## Posture with Spinal Arthritis

### Neck Arthritis

**Cervical spine:**
- Stiffness affects turning
- Pain with certain positions
- Head position changes
- Careful movement needed
- Pillows for support

### Back Arthritis

**Lumbar and thoracic:**
- Stiffness in mornings
- Pain with bending
- Posture may curve
- Support important
- Gentle movement helps

### Managing Spinal Arthritis

**Strategies:**
- Maintain what movement possible
- Gentle stretching
- Core strengthening
- Proper support
- Medical management

---

## Hip and Knee Arthritis

### Gait Changes

**Walking affected:**
- Limping or altered gait
- Avoiding full weight-bearing
- Changed stride
- Posture compensations
- Affects whole body

### Standing Posture

**Adaptations:**
- Leaning to good side
- Weight shifting
- Reduced standing duration
- Need for support
- Sitting more often

### Managing Lower Extremity Arthritis

**What helps:**
- Weight management
- Appropriate exercise
- Assistive devices when needed
- Proper footwear
- Medical care

---

## Hand and Wrist Arthritis

### Grip Changes

**Function affected:**
- Grip strength reduced
- Pain with gripping
- Fine motor challenges
- Daily tasks harder
- Compensations develop

### Posture Connection

**How it relates:**
- Holding things differently
- Changed how you carry
- Writing and typing affected
- Work adaptations needed
- Whole-body consideration

---

## Exercise with Arthritis

### Why Movement Matters

**Critical:**
- Joint lubrication
- Muscle support around joints
- Flexibility maintenance
- Function preservation
- Not moving makes it worse

### Appropriate Exercise

**Good choices:**
- Walking
- Swimming and water exercise
- Tai chi
- Gentle strength training
- Range of motion exercises

### Avoiding Aggravation

**Be careful with:**
- High impact activities
- Exercises that hurt
- Overexertion
- Wrong form
- Listen to your body

---

## Daily Strategies

### Morning Stiffness

**Managing:**
- Allow time to loosen up
- Gentle movements in bed
- Warm shower helps
- Don't rush
- Graduated activity

### Throughout Day

**Pacing:**
- Alternate activity and rest
- Change positions frequently
- Don't stay still too long
- Break up tasks
- Energy management

### Evening

**Wind-down:**
- Gentle stretching
- Comfortable positions
- Heat if helpful
- Rest
- Preparation for tomorrow

---

## Ergonomic Adaptations

### Home Modifications

**Helpful changes:**
- Appropriate seating heights
- Grab bars
- Elevated toilet seat
- Jar openers and tools
- Reduce strain

### Joint Protection

**Principles:**
- Use larger joints when possible
- Avoid sustained positions
- Respect pain
- Use assistive devices
- Reduce repetitive stress

---

## Working with Healthcare

### Medical Management

**Important:**
- Regular medical care
- Medication as prescribed
- Treatment options discussion
- Injections when appropriate
- Surgery if needed

### Physical Therapy

**Valuable:**
- Specific exercise prescription
- Joint protection education
- Manual therapy
- Assistive device training
- Ongoing guidance

---

## Posture Exercises for Arthritis

### Gentle Stretching

**Safe options:**
- Within comfortable range
- Not pushing through pain
- Slow and controlled
- Regular practice
- What you can do

### Strengthening

**Supporting joints:**
- Muscles support joints
- Strengthening helps
- Appropriate resistance
- Proper form
- Professional guidance

### Balance

**Still important:**
- Modified for abilities
- Fall prevention
- Confidence building
- Functional independence
- Part of comprehensive program

---

## When Pain Increases

### Flare Management

**During worse periods:**
- Rest inflamed joints
- Medical guidance
- Modified activity
- Not complete inactivity
- Temporary adjustments

### Returning to Activity

**After flare:**
- Gradual return
- Modified intensity
- Listen to body
- Don't rush
- Progressive rebuilding
