# Medication Effects on Posture and Balance for Seniors

> **Know Your Medications:** Many common medications affect balance, strength, and movement. Understanding these effects helps you stay safe.

---

## Why This Matters

### Multiple Medications Common

**Reality for many seniors:**
- Multiple prescriptions
- Over-the-counter additions
- Interactions possible
- Cumulative effects

### Effects on Body

**Medications can impact:**
- Balance
- Dizziness
- Muscle strength
- Alertness
- Blood pressure

---

## Common Medication Effects

### Blood Pressure Medications

**May cause:**
- Dizziness when standing (orthostatic hypotension)
- Light-headedness
- Balance issues
- Especially with position changes

**Precautions:**
- Rise slowly
- Sit before standing
- Hold support
- Wait for dizziness to pass

### Sedatives and Sleep Aids

**Effects:**
- Drowsiness
- Reduced reaction time
- Balance impairment
- May persist into morning

**Caution:**
- Night bathroom trips risky
- Morning grogginess
- Move carefully
- Use nightlights

### Pain Medications

**May cause:**
- Drowsiness
- Dizziness
- Slowed reactions
- Balance effects

**Considerations:**
- Know how they affect you
- Avoid risky activities when taking
- Use as prescribed

### Antidepressants

**Possible effects:**
- Dizziness
- Blood pressure changes
- Balance issues (some types)
- Especially when starting

### Diabetes Medications

**Watch for:**
- Low blood sugar symptoms
- Dizziness
- Weakness
- Monitor carefully

### Antihistamines

**Even OTC:**
- Drowsiness
- Balance effects
- Cognitive fog
- Choose non-drowsy options

---

## Orthostatic Hypotension

### What It Is

**Blood pressure drops when:**
- Going from lying to sitting
- Sitting to standing
- Can cause dizziness/falls

### Which Medications

**Common culprits:**
- Blood pressure medications
- Heart medications
- Some antidepressants
- Diuretics

### Managing It

**Strategies:**
- Rise in stages
- Sit on bed edge first
- Stand holding support
- Wait before walking

---

## Balance and Dizziness

### Medication-Induced

**Many medications can:**
- Affect inner ear
- Cause dizziness
- Impair balance
- Increase fall risk

### What to Do

**If experiencing:**
- Report to doctor
- Don't stop without guidance
- Use mobility aids if needed
- Extra caution with movement

---

## Polypharmacy Concerns

### Multiple Medications

**The more you take:**
- More potential interactions
- More side effects
- Cumulative impact
- Regular review important

### Medication Reviews

**Important to:**
- Review regularly with provider
- Include OTC medications
- Mention supplements
- Look for unnecessary medications

---

## Practical Adaptations

### Movement Adjustments

**When on affecting medications:**
- Move more slowly
- Use support
- Avoid sudden movements
- Plan movements

### Environmental Safety

**Home modifications:**
- Grab bars
- Good lighting
- Clear pathways
- Non-slip surfaces

### Timing Awareness

**Know when effects strongest:**
- After taking medication
- Peak effect time
- Morning vs. evening
- Plan accordingly

---

## Exercise Considerations

### Medication Timing

**Consider:**
- When you take medications
- Peak effect times
- Exercise timing
- Safety

### Modified Activities

**If affected:**
- Seated exercises safer
- Supported activities
- Avoid when most affected
- Keep moving safely

---

## Communication with Providers

### What to Report

**Tell your doctor:**
- Any balance issues
- Dizziness
- Falls or near-falls
- Concerns about medications

### Questions to Ask

**When prescribed:**
- How will this affect balance?
- Any precautions needed?
- Best time to take?
- What to watch for?

### Don't Stop on Your Own

**Important:**
- Always consult before stopping
- Gradual changes may be needed
- Can be dangerous to stop suddenly
- Work with your provider

---

## Non-Prescription Considerations

### Over-the-Counter Medications

**Still affect body:**
- Sleep aids (diphenhydramine)
- Allergy medications
- Pain relievers
- Cold medicines

### Supplements

**May interact:**
- Herbal supplements
- Vitamins
- Tell providers about all

---

## Proactive Management

### Regular Reviews

**Schedule:**
- Annual medication review
- After any changes
- If new symptoms
- With pharmacist too

### Self-Monitoring

**Pay attention to:**
- How you feel
- Timing of symptoms
- Any patterns
- Report changes
