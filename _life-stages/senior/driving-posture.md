# Driving Posture for Seniors

> **Stay Safe and Comfortable:** Proper driving position helps you drive longer into life while keeping you and others safe.

---

## Why Driving Posture Changes

### Age-Related Factors

**What may change:**
- Flexibility decreases
- Neck rotation limited
- Height may decrease
- Reaction time changes
- Vision adjustments needed

### Why It Matters

**Good driving posture:**
- Improves visibility
- Reduces fatigue
- Enables safe control
- Allows emergency response
- Comfort for longer drives

---

## Seat Position

### Distance from Steering Wheel

**Proper position:**
- At least 10 inches from airbag
- Arms slightly bent when holding wheel
- Can reach pedals comfortably
- Not cramped or stretched

### Seat Height

**Adjust for:**
- Clear view of road
- See over dashboard
- See mirrors well
- Feet reach pedals properly

### Seat Back Angle

**Recommendations:**
- Slightly reclined (100-110Â°)
- Not too upright
- Not too reclined
- Supports lower back

### Lumbar Support

**If available:**
- Use built-in support
- Or add lumbar cushion
- Reduce lower back strain
- Comfortable for drives

---

## Mirrors

### Adjusting for Limited Neck Rotation

**Compensation:**
- Mirrors slightly more outward
- Reduce blind spots
- Less head turning needed
- Still check blind spots

### Side Mirrors

**Proper position:**
- Barely see side of car
- Cover blind spots
- Both sides adjusted
- Check when parked

### Rearview Mirror

**Adjust:**
- Center on rear window
- Comfortable viewing angle
- Consider auto-dimming
- Convex mirrors for blind spots

---

## Steering Wheel Position

### Height and Tilt

**Adjust for:**
- Clear view of instruments
- Comfortable arm position
- Hands at 9 and 3 o'clock
- Not blocking view or airbag

### Holding the Wheel

**Proper grip:**
- 9 and 3 position (not 10 and 2)
- Relaxed grip
- Elbows slightly bent
- Shoulders relaxed

---

## Getting In and Out

### Entering the Car

**Technique:**
- Open door fully
- Sit on seat first
- Swing legs in together
- Use grab handles

### Exiting

**Safely:**
- Swing legs out first
- Feet on ground
- Push up using door frame/seat
- Stand slowly
- Check for traffic

### Parking Considerations

**Make it easier:**
- Park in accessible spots
- Leave room to open door
- Level ground easier
- Consider door length

---

## Stiffness Management

### Before Driving

**Preparation:**
- Gentle stretching
- Warm up joints
- Don't start cold
- Move around first

### Long Drives

**Prevent stiffness:**
- Stop every 1-2 hours
- Get out and move
- Stretch at stops
- Walk briefly

### After Driving

**Recovery:**
- Stretch after arrival
- Move gently
- Don't rush
- Allow adjustment time

---

## Head and Neck Position

### Proper Alignment

**Position:**
- Head over shoulders
- Not forward
- Chin level
- Neck relaxed

### Head Restraint

**Adjust:**
- Center of restraint at ear level
- Close to head (1-2 inches)
- Protects in collision
- Don't ignore this

---

## Vision Considerations

### Glasses

**If you wear them:**
- Correct prescription
- Clean lenses
- Consider driving glasses
- Avoid progressive lenses' distortion

### Checking Blind Spots

**If rotation limited:**
- Use mirrors well
- Turn body if needed
- Consider blind spot mirrors
- Take time

### Night Driving

**Challenges:**
- May need to limit
- Glare issues
- Reduced visibility
- Know your limits

---

## Pedal Positioning

### Foot Position

**When driving:**
- Heel on floor
- Ball of foot on pedal
- Comfortable angle
- Smooth transitions

### Footwear

**For driving:**
- Thin, firm sole
- Feel the pedals
- Secure on foot
- Not bulky

---

## Safety Considerations

### Airbag Distance

**Critical:**
- At least 10 inches from steering wheel
- Tilt wheel if needed
- Adjust seat position
- Safety first

### Seatbelt Fit

**Proper wearing:**
- Across chest (not neck)
- Low on hips (not stomach)
- Snug fit
- Adjusters available

---

## When to Reassess

### Regular Check

**Periodically:**
- Review seat position
- Check mirror alignment
- Assess comfort
- Make adjustments

### Physical Changes

**After:**
- Surgery
- Injury
- Physical therapy
- Any body changes
