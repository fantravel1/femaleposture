# Aging in Place and Physical Wellness

> **Aging in place—staying in your home as you grow older—requires attention to physical safety, mobility maintenance, and home modifications. Proactive planning supports independence.**

---

## What Aging in Place Means

Staying home as you age.

### The Goal

**Independence includes:**
- Living in your home
- Managing daily activities
- Maintaining quality of life
- Safety and comfort
- For as long as possible

### Physical Requirements

**What it takes:**
- Mobility maintenance
- Home safety
- Daily function ability
- Appropriate supports
- Ongoing attention

## Home Modifications

### Safety Basics

**Essential:**
- Grab bars in bathrooms
- Handrails on stairs
- Good lighting
- Clear pathways
- Trip hazard removal

### Kitchen Accessibility

**Modifications:**
- Frequently used items accessible
- Step stool elimination
- Anti-fatigue mats
- Easy-reach organization
- Practical changes

### Bathroom Safety

**Critical area:**
- Grab bars
- Walk-in shower
- Non-slip surfaces
- Elevated toilet seat
- Safety-focused

---

## Daily Activity Strategies

### Energy Conservation

**Managing:**
- Pace activities
- Rest between tasks
- Sit for some tasks
- Prioritize
- Sustainable approach

### Task Modification

**Easier ways:**
- Sit to dress
- Use long-handled tools
- Reorganize for access
- Simplify where possible
- Smart adaptations
