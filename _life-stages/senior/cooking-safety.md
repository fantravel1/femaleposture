# Cooking Safety for Seniors

> **Keep Cooking Safely:** Preparing your own meals is part of independence. Safe practices let you continue cooking.

---

## Kitchen Challenges

### Physical Demands

**Cooking involves:**
- Standing
- Reaching
- Lifting pots
- Fine motor tasks
- Heat exposure

### Common Risks

**Watch for:**
- Burns
- Cuts
- Falls
- Fatigue
- Overexertion

---

## Standing and Posture

### At the Stove

**Safe practice:**
- Anti-fatigue mat
- Supportive shoes
- Close to your work
- Not reaching over burners

### At the Counter

**Good setup:**
- Counter at right height
- Sit for prep if helpful
- Good lighting
- Everything within reach

### Taking Breaks

**Don't overdo:**
- Sit periodically
- Rest when needed
- Multiple shorter sessions
- Not marathon cooking

---

## Lifting and Carrying

### Heavy Pots

**Challenges:**
- Full of water = very heavy
- Hot and dangerous
- Awkward to carry

**Solutions:**
- Fill at stove (pot filler ideal)
- Or carry water in pitcher
- Lightweight cookware
- Don't carry heavy and hot

### Taking Things from Oven

**Safe technique:**
- Pull rack out first
- Lift with bent knees
- Use both hands
- Clear path to set down

### High Cabinets

**Reaching:**
- Store frequently used items lower
- Use step stool carefully
- Or reachable tools
- Ask for help for high items

---

## Equipment That Helps

### Lightweight Cookware

**Easier to manage:**
- Lighter pots and pans
- Easier to lift and pour
- Still functional
- Worth investing

### Ergonomic Tools

**Look for:**
- Large grip handles
- Easy-open items
- Electric can openers
- Jar openers
- Angled utensils

### Safety Devices

**Consider:**
- Auto-shutoff appliances
- Stove knob covers
- Timer reminders
- Smoke detector working

---

## Prep Work

### Seated Prep

**If standing difficult:**
- Sit at table for prep
- Cutting board on table
- Comfortable position
- Then move to stove

### Cutting Safely

**Sharp knives are safer:**
- Less force needed
- Better control
- Non-slip cutting board
- Proper technique

---

## Heat Safety

### Preventing Burns

**Precautions:**
- Use back burners
- Turn handles inward
- Oven mitts always
- Never reach over flames

### If Burns Happen

**Response:**
- Cool water immediately
- Cover loosely
- Seek medical attention if severe
- Know when to call for help

---

## Fire Safety

### Prevention

**In kitchen:**
- Never leave cooking unattended
- Keep flammables away from stove
- Clean grease buildup
- Working smoke detector

### If Fire Starts

**Response:**
- Small grease fire: cover with lid
- Don't use water on grease fire
- Leave and call 911 if can't control
- Know your limits

---

## Managing Fatigue

### Energy Conservation

**Strategies:**
- Sit when possible
- Prepare in stages
- Use appliances (slow cooker)
- Batch cooking
- Simple recipes

### Meal Planning

**Make it easier:**
- Plan ahead
- Use convenience items
- Double recipes for leftovers
- Accept simpler meals

---

## Nutrition Matters

### Cooking for One

**If alone:**
- Still worth cooking
- Batch and freeze
- Healthy convenience options
- Varied diet

### Physical Support

**Good nutrition:**
- Supports energy
- Bone health
- Muscle maintenance
- Overall wellbeing
