# Housework Safely for Seniors

> **Keep Your Independence:** Being able to manage your own home matters. Safe techniques let you maintain your independence.

---

## Why Housework Can Be Risky

### Physical Demands

**Chores involve:**
- Bending
- Reaching
- Lifting
- Prolonged standing
- Repetitive motions

### Common Injuries

**From housework:**
- Back strain
- Falls from reaching
- Overexertion
- Repetitive strain

---

## General Principles

### Pace Yourself

**Don't rush:**
- Break tasks into parts
- Do a little each day
- Rest between tasks
- No marathon cleaning

### Use Tools

**Helpful equipment:**
- Long-handled tools
- Lightweight equipment
- Ergonomic designs
- Anything that reduces strain

### Know Your Limits

**Be realistic:**
- What you can do safely
- What needs help
- When to stop
- Hiring help for risky tasks

---

## Cleaning

### Vacuuming

**Safe technique:**
- Lightweight vacuum
- Good posture
- Push, don't pull
- Take breaks
- Don't overreach

### Mopping

**Safer approach:**
- Lightweight mop
- Small area at a time
- Watch for wet floors
- Spray mops reduce bending

### Dusting

**High and low:**
- Long-handled duster
- Step stool only if stable
- Don't overreach
- Skip what's unsafe

### Bathrooms

**Challenging:**
- Bending to clean tub
- Wet surfaces
- Getting up and down
- Consider help for deep cleaning

---

## Laundry

### Loading/Unloading

**Washer/Dryer:**
- Front loaders may require bending
- Top loaders require reaching
- Pedestals can raise front loaders
- Consider which is easier for you

### Carrying Laundry

**Heavy loads:**
- Small loads, not big ones
- Laundry cart on wheels
- Don't carry baskets
- Multiple trips okay

### Folding

**Comfortable setup:**
- At counter height
- Not bending over bed
- Sitting is fine
- Take your time

### Putting Away

**Reaching:**
- Use step stool safely
- Or store where reachable
- Organize for accessibility
- Reduce bending and reaching

---

## Kitchen Chores

### Dishes

**Washing:**
- Don't hunch over sink
- Stand on anti-fatigue mat
- Counter height matters
- Take breaks

### Cooking Prep

**Safe approach:**
- Seated prep if helpful
- Sharp knives (safer than dull)
- Ergonomic tools
- Good lighting

### Heavy Items

**Pots and pans:**
- Lightweight cookware
- Don't carry heavy when full
- Drain at sink
- Ask for help if needed

---

## Making Beds

### The Challenge

**Bed making:**
- Bending and reaching
- Lifting mattress
- Fitted sheets difficult
- Strain potential

### Easier Approaches

**Modifications:**
- Walk around bed (don't reach across)
- Lighter bedding
- Elastic sheet straps
- Simpler bed making routine

---

## Floors

### Picking Things Up

**From floor:**
- Squat or kneel
- Use long-handled grabber
- Don't bend at waist
- Have plan to stand

### Area Rugs

**Safety note:**
- Can be trip hazards
- Secure edges
- Consider removing
- Non-slip backing essential

---

## Outdoor Chores

### Lawn and Garden

**See:**
- [Gardening Posture for Seniors](/life-stages/senior/gardening-posture/)
- Modified tools
- Hired help for heavy work
- Know your limits

### Snow and Ice

**Particularly risky:**
- Consider hiring help
- If doing, small amounts
- Take many breaks
- Avoid if unsafe

---

## Getting Help

### When to Hire

**Makes sense for:**
- Deep cleaning
- Heavy tasks
- Reaching high places
- What's too risky

### Family Help

**Asking:**
- Not defeat
- Practical wisdom
- Specify what helps
- Maintain independence for what you can do

---

## Energy Conservation

### Fatigue Management

**Work smart:**
- Sit when possible
- Spread tasks out
- Heavy days and light days
- Don't overdo

### Prioritizing

**What matters:**
- Safety first
- What you can manage
- Let some things go
- It's okay
