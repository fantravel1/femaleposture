# Seated Exercise for Seniors

> **Exercise is essential at every age, even when standing is difficult or unsafe. Seated exercises provide effective workouts that build strength, flexibility, and cardiovascular fitness from a chair.**

---

## Why Seated Exercise Matters

Exercise benefits don't require standing.

### Who Benefits

**Seated exercise for:**
- Mobility limitations
- Balance concerns
- Recovery periods
- Fatigue issues
- Preference for safety

### What's Possible

**Seated workouts can:**
- Build strength
- Improve flexibility
- Increase heart rate
- Maintain function
- Improve quality of life

---

## Chair Selection

### Best Chair for Exercise

**What to use:**
- Sturdy, stable
- No wheels
- Appropriate height
- Armrests optional
- Won't tip

### Positioning

**Setup:**
- Sit toward front
- Feet flat on floor
- Back supported when needed
- Room to move arms
- Stable and safe

---

## Upper Body Exercises

### Arm Raises

**Shoulder strength:**
- Lift arms to side
- Lift arms to front
- Overhead if comfortable
- With or without weights
- Multiple variations

### Arm Circles

**Mobility:**
- Arms out to sides
- Small circles
- Large circles
- Both directions
- Warms joints

### Bicep Curls

**Arm strength:**
- Weights or water bottles
- Curl toward shoulder
- Lower slowly
- Both arms
- Basic strengthening

### Overhead Press

**Shoulder work:**
- Press weights up
- From shoulder height
- Extend arms
- Lower controlled
- Upper body strength

### Chest Press

**Chest strength:**
- Arms out front
- Squeeze together
- With or without weights
- Controlled movement
- Works chest and arms

---

## Core Exercises

### Seated Twists

**Rotation:**
- Hands on shoulders
- Rotate side to side
- Keep hips facing front
- Gentle twist
- Spinal mobility

### Seated Knee Lifts

**Lower core:**
- Lift one knee
- Hold briefly
- Lower and switch
- Alternating
- Core engagement

### Seated Marching

**Active core:**
- Lift knees alternately
- Like marching
- Arms can swing
- Cardiovascular too
- Energizing

### Tummy Tightening

**Deep core:**
- Draw belly in
- Hold and breathe
- Release and repeat
- Subtle but effective
- Foundational

---

## Lower Body Exercises

### Leg Extensions

**Quad strength:**
- Extend one leg straight
- Hold briefly
- Lower slowly
- Alternate legs
- Thigh strengthening

### Heel Raises

**Calf work:**
- Feet flat on floor
- Raise heels
- Lower slowly
- Repeat
- Ankle strength

### Toe Raises

**Shin work:**
- Feet flat
- Raise toes
- Lower
- Balance with heel raises
- Complete lower leg

### Knee Extensions with Hold

**Quad endurance:**
- Extend leg
- Hold longer (10-30 seconds)
- Lower slowly
- Builds endurance
- Functional strength

### Hip Rotations

**Hip mobility:**
- Lift foot slightly
- Rotate ankle/foot
- Circles both directions
- Hip joint movement
- Maintains mobility

---

## Flexibility Exercises

### Neck Stretches

**Releasing tension:**
- Ear to shoulder
- Hold gently
- Both sides
- Gentle turns
- No forcing

### Shoulder Stretches

**Flexibility:**
- Arm across body
- Gentle hold
- Both sides
- Reach behind
- Multiple variations

### Seated Side Stretch

**Torso flexibility:**
- Reach arm overhead
- Lean to opposite side
- Gentle stretch
- Both sides
- Opens ribs

### Ankle Circles

**Joint mobility:**
- Lift foot slightly
- Rotate ankle
- Both directions
- Both ankles
- Maintains mobility

### Seated Forward Reach

**Back stretch:**
- Reach toward toes
- Only as far as comfortable
- Gentle stretch
- Return upright
- Spine flexibility

---

## Cardiovascular Options

### Seated Marching

**Heart rate:**
- Continuous marching
- Add arm movements
- Increase pace
- Keep going
- Cardiovascular work

### Seated Jacks

**Modified jumping jack:**
- Open and close legs
- Arms out and in
- Coordinated movement
- Continuous motion
- Gets heart pumping

### Arm Punches

**Boxing movements:**
- Alternating punches forward
- Add cross punches
- Keep moving
- Continuous
- Upper body cardio

---

## Sample Routine

### Basic Sequence

**Full workout:**
- Warm-up with movements
- Upper body exercises
- Core work
- Lower body exercises
- Stretching finish
- 15-30 minutes

### Daily Option

**Quick routine:**
- 5-10 minutes
- Mix of movements
- Something is better than nothing
- Daily habit
- Maintenance

---

## Adding Challenge

### As You Progress

**Increase difficulty:**
- Add small weights
- More repetitions
- Longer holds
- Faster pace (cardio)
- Gradual progression

### Safety Considerations

**Keep in mind:**
- Progress gradually
- Don't overdo
- Rest when needed
- Listen to body
- Safety first
