# Osteoporosis and Posture

> **Strong Posture, Fragile Bones:** With osteoporosis, posture matters more than everâ€”but you need safe approaches. Here's how to maintain upright posture while protecting your spine.

---

## Why Posture Matters More with Osteoporosis

### The Risk

**Poor posture with osteoporosis:**
- Increases fracture risk
- Worsens kyphosis
- Creates unsafe loads on spine
- Forward bending most risky

### The Opportunity

**Good posture:**
- Reduces fracture risk
- Prevents worsening curves
- Maintains function
- Preserves independence

---

## Understanding the Risks

### Vertebral Fractures

**Most common osteoporotic fracture:**
- Spine vertebrae collapse
- Can happen without trauma
- Cause increased kyphosis
- Often go undiagnosed initially

### How They Happen

**Risk activities:**
- Forward bending with load
- Twisting movements
- Heavy lifting
- Falls
- Sometimes just sneezing

---

## Movements to Avoid

### High-Risk Movements

**Do NOT do:**
- Toe touches (standing or seated)
- Sit-ups or crunches
- Loaded forward bending
- Heavy lifting
- Twisting under load
- High-impact jumping

### Why These Are Dangerous

**Forward flexion:**
- Compresses front of vertebrae
- Where bone is weakest
- Can cause compression fractures
- Even with moderate load

---

## Safe Posture Principles

### Spine Neutral or Extended

**Keep spine:**
- In neutral alignment
- Slightly extended is okay
- Never flexed under load
- Protected in all activities

### Hip Hinge, Not Spine Bend

**For reaching low:**
- Bend at hips
- Keep back straight
- Squat if needed
- Never round spine forward

### Avoid Twisting Under Load

**When turning:**
- Move feet first
- Don't twist spine
- Especially with weight

---

## Safe Exercises

### Back Extension

**Strengthening extensors:**
- Prone back extensions (gentle)
- Standing back extension
- Strengthens postural muscles
- Safe direction for spine

### Core Work (Modified)

**Safe options:**
- Planks (modified)
- Bird dogs
- Bridges
- Pelvic tilts
- NO crunches or sit-ups

### Strength Training

**Safe approach:**
- Can and should strength train
- Proper form essential
- Avoid loaded flexion
- Machines may be safer
- Professional guidance recommended

### Balance Work

**Important for fall prevention:**
- All balance exercises
- Reduces fall risk
- Prevents fractures
- See [Falls Prevention](/life-stages/senior/falls-prevention/)

---

## Daily Activities

### Getting Out of Bed

**Safe technique:**
- Roll to side first
- Push up with arms
- Don't sit straight up (crunching)
- Legs off bed, push to sitting

### Picking Things Up

**From floor:**
- Squat or golfer's pickup
- Keep back straight
- Never bend at waist
- Ask for help with heavy items

### Household Tasks

**Modifications:**
- Long-handled tools
- Avoid heavy lifting
- Break tasks into smaller parts
- Ask for help when needed

### Gardening

**Safe approach:**
- Raised beds when possible
- Kneeling instead of bending
- Light loads only
- See [Gardening Guide](/activities/gardening/)

## Posture for Kyphosis Prevention

### Why It Matters

**Vertebral fractures cause:**
- Increased forward curve
- Shortened height
- Breathing restriction
- Continued risk

### Prevention Focus

**Daily attention:**
- Frequent posture checks
- Extension exercises
- Avoid flexion
- Support when sitting

### If Kyphosis Present

**Managing existing curve:**
- Focus on what's changeable
- Prevent worsening
- Safe exercise program
- Professional guidance

---

## Sitting Safely

### Chair Choice

**Select chairs that:**
- Support upright posture
- Have firm seats
- Provide lumbar support
- Easy to get out of

### Sitting Posture

**Maintain:**
- Upright position
- Back against support
- Feet flat on floor
- Avoid slouching

---

## Standing and Walking

### Upright Alignment

**Focus on:**
- Head up, eyes forward
- Shoulders back gently
- Core engaged
- Don't look down while walking

### Walking Aids

**If needed:**
- Proper fitting important
- Reduces fall risk
- Supports better posture
- Tool for independence

---

## Exercise Program Guidelines

### What's Recommended

**Evidence-based approach:**
- Weight-bearing exercise
- Strength training
- Balance work
- Posture exercises
- Daily physical activity

### Working with Professionals

**Strongly recommended:**
- Physical therapist (osteoporosis-experienced)
- Exercise physiologist
- Safe program design
- Proper technique learning

### Programs to Seek

**Beneficial:**
- Too Fit to Fall programs
- Bone-specific exercise programs
- Physical therapy
- Supervised strength training

---

## Medical Management

### Work with Your Provider

**Osteoporosis care:**
- Medication options
- Bone density monitoring
- Fall risk assessment
- Comprehensive approach

### When to Report

**Tell provider if:**
- New back pain
- Height loss
- Worsening posture
- Falls
- Changing symptoms

## Emotional Aspects

### Fear of Movement

**Common but:**
- Inactivity worsens bone
- Movement is essential
- Safe movement exists
- Fear can be overcome

### Finding Confidence

**Build through:**
- Education about safe movement
- Working with professionals
- Gradual progression
- Celebrating capability
