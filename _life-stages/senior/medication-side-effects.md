# Medication Side Effects and Posture

> **Many medications commonly used by older adults can affect balance, muscle function, energy, and coordinationâ€”all of which impact posture and movement safety. Understanding these effects helps you stay safe.**

---

## Why This Matters

Medications affect more than their target condition.

### Common Concern

**Reality of aging:**
- More medications with age
- Side effects accumulate
- Balance often affected
- Energy impacted
- Posture influenced

### Body-Wide Effects

**What's affected:**
- Balance systems
- Muscle function
- Alertness
- Coordination
- Overall function

---

## Medications Affecting Balance

### Blood Pressure Medications

**Common effects:**
- Dizziness on standing
- Lightheadedness
- Drops when position changes
- Fall risk increase
- Careful transitions needed

### Sedatives and Sleep Aids

**Effects:**
- Drowsiness
- Coordination impaired
- Balance affected
- Morning grogginess
- Fall risk

### Antidepressants

**Possible effects:**
- Dizziness
- Drowsiness in some
- Balance changes
- Various depending on type
- Individual response

### Pain Medications

**Opioids especially:**
- Drowsiness
- Coordination effects
- Fall risk
- Alertness reduced
- Careful movement needed

---

## Medications Affecting Muscles

### Statins

**Cholesterol medications:**
- Muscle pain possible
- Weakness in some
- Affects exercise
- Report to provider
- Monitor for effects

### Steroids

**Long-term use:**
- Muscle weakness
- Bone effects
- Multiple impacts
- Posture affected
- Monitoring important

### Diuretics

**Water pills:**
- Electrolyte changes
- Muscle cramps
- Weakness possible
- Stay hydrated
- Monitor symptoms

---

## Managing Effects

### Awareness

**First step:**
- Know your medications
- Understand side effects
- Monitor for changes
- Track symptoms
- Stay informed

### Timing

**When to take:**
- Some better at bedtime
- Others with food
- Timing matters
- Follow instructions
- Ask about optimal timing

### Communication

**With providers:**
- Report side effects
- Ask about alternatives
- Discuss concerns
- Medication reviews
- Don't stop without guidance

---

## Specific Strategies

### Dizziness

**Management:**
- Rise slowly
- Sit before standing
- Hold onto support
- Wait for stability
- Know triggers

### Drowsiness

**What helps:**
- Time medication appropriately
- Limit activity when sedated
- Don't drive if affected
- Know when it peaks
- Plan around it

### Muscle Weakness

**Adaptations:**
- Extra support
- Assistive devices if needed
- Strengthening when possible
- Report to provider
- Modifications

---

## Fall Prevention

### High Risk Situations

**Be especially careful:**
- Night bathroom trips
- First thing in morning
- After medication doses
- When tired
- Position changes

### Protective Measures

**What helps:**
- Night lights
- Grab bars
- Slow transitions
- Stable furniture
- Environment modifications

### Exercise Despite Effects

**Still important:**
- Balance exercises
- Strength maintenance
- Modified as needed
- Under supervision if risky
- Don't stop moving

---

## Polypharmacy Concerns

### Multiple Medications

**The challenge:**
- Interactions between drugs
- Cumulative effects
- Complex management
- Common in seniors
- Requires attention

### Medication Reviews

**Regular evaluation:**
- With pharmacist
- With doctor
- Check for necessity
- Evaluate interactions
- Optimize regimen

### Deprescribing

**Reducing when appropriate:**
- Some can be stopped
- Gradual reduction
- Professional guidance
- May improve function
- Discuss options

---

## Specific Concerns by System

### Blood Pressure Changes

**Orthostatic hypotension:**
- Blood pressure drops on standing
- Very common with medications
- Fall risk significant
- Slow transitions essential
- Monitor and report

### Cognitive Effects

**Brain fog:**
- Some medications cause
- Affects awareness
- Balance impacted
- Safety concerns
- Discuss alternatives

### Vision Effects

**Some medications:**
- Affect vision
- Impact balance
- Change depth perception
- Report changes
- Important to address

---

## Working with Healthcare Team

### Pharmacist Consultation

**Valuable resource:**
- Drug interactions
- Side effect profiles
- Timing recommendations
- Free consultations often
- Use this resource

### Doctor Discussions

**At appointments:**
- List all medications
- Report all side effects
- Ask about necessity
- Discuss alternatives
- Be thorough

### Comprehensive Review

**Regularly:**
- Annual medication review
- After hospitalizations
- When adding new drugs
- When problems arise
- Ongoing process
