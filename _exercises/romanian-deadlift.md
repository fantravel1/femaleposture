# Romanian Deadlift: Hip Hinge Mastery

> **At a Glance:** The RDL builds posterior chain strength while reinforcing the hip hinge pattern essential for safe lifting.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Hamstrings, glutes, lower back |
| **Best For** | Posterior chain strength, hip hinge |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Equipment** | Dumbbells or barbell (optional bodyweight) |
| **Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Frequency** | 2-3 times per week |

---

## How to Perform

1. Stand with slight knee bend
2. Hold weights in front of thighs
3. Hinge at hips, pushing them back
4. Lower weights down legs
5. Feel hamstring stretch
6. Return by driving hips forward

- Keep spine neutralâ€”no rounding
- Weights stay close to body
- Knees slightly bent throughout
- Stop when hamstrings limit you

---

## Common Mistakes

- Rounding lower back
- Too much knee bend (becomes squat)
- Weight too far from body
- Looking up (neck strain)

---

## Variations

### Single-Leg RDL
- Balance and strength challenge
- Great for identifying imbalances

### Sumo RDL
- Wide stance
- More inner thigh involvement

---

## Benefits

- Strengthens entire posterior chain
- Teaches safe lifting mechanics
- Lengthens hamstrings under load
- Builds functional strength
