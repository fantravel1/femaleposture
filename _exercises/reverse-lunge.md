# Reverse Lunge: Knee-Friendly Leg Exercise

> **At a Glance:** Stepping back instead of forward reduces knee stress while still building leg and glute strength.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Glutes, quads, hamstrings |
| **Best For** | Leg strength, balance, knee-friendly option |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | None (optional weights) |
| **Time** | 5-10 minutes |
| **Frequency** | 2-3 times per week |

---

## How to Perform

1. Stand with feet together
2. Step back with one leg
3. Lower back knee toward floor
4. Front knee stays over ankle
5. Push through front heel to return
6. Alternate legs

### Why It's Easier on Knees

- Less momentum on knee joint
- Better control of movement
- Front knee stays more stable
- Natural deceleration
