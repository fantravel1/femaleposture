# Figure Four Stretch: Hip and Glute Release

> **At a Glance:** The figure four stretch (also called reclined pigeon) is a comfortable way to stretch your glutes, piriformis, and external hip rotators. Especially helpful for people who sit a lot.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Glutes, piriformis, external hip rotators |
| **Best For** | Hip tightness, back pain relief, sciatica, piriformis syndrome |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | None |
| **Time** | 3-5 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Daily |

---

## Why This Stretch Helps

Tight glutes and piriformis can:
- Contribute to lower back pain
- Cause sciatic-type symptoms
- Limit hip mobility
- Affect walking and sitting comfort

This stretch addresses these issues gently.

---

## How to Do the Figure Four Stretch

### Supine Version (Lying Down)

**Starting Position:**
1. Lie on back
2. Knees bent, feet flat on floor

**The Stretch:**
1. Cross right ankle over left knee
2. Let right knee open to side
3. Reach through gap and hold left thigh
4. Pull left thigh toward chest
5. Feel stretch in right hip/glute
6. Hold 30-60 seconds
7. Switch sides

### Key Points

- Keep crossed foot flexed (protects knee)
- Relax upper body
- Breathe into the stretch
- Don't force the stretch

---

## Variations

### Wall Version

1. Lie near wall
2. Place left foot on wall
3. Cross right ankle over left knee
4. Scoot hips closer to wall for more stretch
5. Great for tight hamstrings

### Seated Version

1. Sit in chair
2. Cross right ankle over left knee
3. Keep right knee open
4. Sit tall and lean forward gently
5. Good for office breaks

### Standing Version

1. Stand on left leg (hold support if needed)
2. Cross right ankle over left knee
3. Sit back slightly into a "squat"
4. Feel stretch in right hip
5. More challenging balance

### Pigeon Pose

The yoga version:
- More intense stretch
- Requires more hip mobility
- Good progression from figure four

## Programming

### Quick Relief

- 30-60 seconds each side
- Can do multiple times daily
- Takes 2-3 minutes

### In Stretching Routine

- Include with other hip stretches
- Pair with hip flexor stretch
- Part of lower body flexibility work

### After Exercise

- Especially after running, cycling, or leg work
- Hold 60 seconds each side
- Helps recovery

---

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Foot Not Flexed

**Problem:** Can strain knee
**Fix:** Keep crossed ankle flexed

### 2. Forcing Too Deep

**Problem:** Over-stretching
**Fix:** Go to comfortable stretch, not pain

### 3. Holding Breath

**Problem:** Creates tension
**Fix:** Breathe deeply throughout

### 4. Tensing Shoulders

**Problem:** Creates upper body tension
**Fix:** Relax shoulders and neck

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long should I hold this stretch?

30-60 seconds per side. Longer holds (up to 2 minutes) for very tight areas.

### Can I do this if I have sciatica?

It may help, but if symptoms worsen, stop. Some people find it relieves symptoms; others find it aggravating.

### Why does one side feel tighter?

Asymmetry is normal. May be from dominant leg use, sleeping position, or past injury. Work both sides, possibly extra on tight side.

### Is this the same as piriformis stretch?

Similar. Figure four targets the same area as many piriformis stretches. It's one of several effective options.
