# Lateral Lunge: Side-to-Side Strength

> **At a Glance:** Moving sideways trains often-neglected movement patterns and strengthens inner thighs.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Inner thighs, glutes, quads |
| **Best For** | Lateral strength, inner thigh flexibility |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Equipment** | None |
| **Time** | 5-10 minutes |
| **Frequency** | 2-3 times per week |

---

## How to Perform

1. Stand with feet together
2. Step wide to the right
3. Bend right knee, sit hips back
4. Keep left leg straight
5. Push off to return to start
6. Alternate sides

### Form Tips

- Keep chest up
- Toes point forward or slightly out
- Feel stretch in straight leg inner thigh
- Keep both feet flat

---

## Benefits

- Trains lateral movement plane
- Strengthens inner thighs
- Improves hip mobility
- Better lateral stability
