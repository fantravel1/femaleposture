# Split Squat: Single-Leg Strength

> **At a Glance:** Split squats isolate each leg individually, building strength and revealing imbalances.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Quads, glutes, hip flexors |
| **Best For** | Single-leg strength, balance |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Equipment** | None (optional weights) |
| **Time** | 5-10 minutes |
| **Frequency** | 2-3 times per week |

---

## How to Perform

1. Take staggered stance (front foot flat, back on toes)
2. Keep torso upright
3. Lower back knee toward ground
4. Front knee stays over ankle
5. Push through front heel to rise
6. Complete reps, then switch legs

### Form Tips

- Most weight on front leg
- Don't let front knee cave in
- Keep hips square
- Control the movement

---

## Variations

### Bulgarian Split Squat
- Back foot elevated on bench
- Increased range of motion
- More challenging

### Deficit Split Squat
- Front foot on small platform
- Deeper stretch
