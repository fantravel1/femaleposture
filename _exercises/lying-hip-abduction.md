# Lying Hip Abduction: Side-Lying Leg Raise

> **At a Glance:** The classic side-lying leg raise isolates outer hip muscles without balance challenges.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Gluteus medius, TFL |
| **Best For** | Hip strength, pelvic stability |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | Mat |
| **Time** | 5 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Daily |

---

## How to Perform

1. Lie on side, body in straight line
2. Bottom arm supports head
3. Lift top leg toward ceiling
4. Keep hips stacked (don't roll back)
5. Lower with control
6. Complete reps, then switch sides

### Form Tips

- Don't lift too high (about 45 degrees)
- Keep leg straight
- Lead with heel, not toes
- Maintain hip alignment

---

## Variations

### With Ankle Weight
- Adds resistance
- Increase weight gradually

### With Band
- Band around ankles
- Constant tension throughout
