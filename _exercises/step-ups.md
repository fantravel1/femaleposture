# Step Ups: Functional Leg Strength

> **At a Glance:** Step ups develop single-leg strength and control that transfers directly to daily activities.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings |
| **Best For** | Leg strength, balance, stair climbing |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner to Intermediate |
| **Equipment** | Sturdy step or bench |
| **Time** | 5-10 minutes |
| **Frequency** | 3 times per week |

---

## How to Perform

1. Stand facing step or bench
2. Place entire foot on step
3. Press through heel to stand up
4. Bring other foot to meet it
5. Step down with control
6. Complete reps, then switch leading leg

### Form Tips

- Drive through heel, not toe
- Keep chest up
- Control the descent
- Don't push off back foot

---

## Progressions

### Height Progression
- Start with low step (4-6 inches)
- Progress to standard step (8-12 inches)
- Challenge with higher platform

### Add Weight
- Hold dumbbells at sides
- Hold weight at chest

### Slow Negatives
- Step up normally
- Lower down for 4-5 seconds

---

## Benefits

- Builds functional leg strength
- Improves balance
- Mimics real-life activities
- Identifies strength imbalances
