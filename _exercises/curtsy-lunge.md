# Curtsy Lunge: Glute Med Activation

> **At a Glance:** The diagonal stepping pattern of curtsy lunges challenges hip stabilizers and outer glutes.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Gluteus medius, glutes, quads |
| **Best For** | Hip stability, outer glute |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Equipment** | None |
| **Time** | 5-10 minutes |
| **Frequency** | 2-3 times per week |

---

## How to Perform

1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Step right foot diagonally behind left
3. Lower into lunge (like a curtsy)
4. Keep hips facing forward
5. Push through front foot to return
6. Alternate sides

### Form Tips

- Keep torso upright
- Don't let knee cave in
- Control the diagonal step
- Feel it in outer hip

---

## Benefits

- Targets gluteus medius
- Improves hip stability
- Challenges balance
- Variety from standard lunges
