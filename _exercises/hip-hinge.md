# Hip Hinge: Movement Fundamental

> **At a Glance:** The essential movement pattern for bending that protects the back.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Hamstrings, glutes, back |
| **Best For** | Movement learning, back safety |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | Dowel or PVC pipe optional |
| **Time** | 5 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Daily until mastered |

---

## How to Perform

1. Stand with slight knee bend
2. Push hips straight back
3. Keep spine neutral
4. Lower torso (back stays flat)
5. Feel hamstrings stretch
6. Drive hips forward to stand

### With Dowel

1. Hold dowel along spine
2. Contact at head, upper back, tailbone
3. Maintain contact throughout hinge

---

## Key Points

- Bend from hips, not waist
- Spine stays neutral
- Knees soft but not deeply bent
- Foundation for lifting
