# Hip Circles: Mobility and Warm-Up Exercise

> **At a Glance:** Hip circles are a simple mobility exercise that loosens up your hip joints. Great for warm-ups, desk breaks, and maintaining hip health.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Hip joint mobility, hip flexors, glutes |
| **Best For** | Warming up, tight hips, desk workers |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | None |
| **Time** | 2-3 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Daily or as warm-up |

---

## Why Hip Circles Help

Hip circles benefit you by:
- Lubricating the hip joint with synovial fluid
- Warming up muscles around the hip
- Improving range of motion
- Releasing tension from sitting
- Preparing hips for exercise
- Maintaining mobility as you age

---

## Standing Hip Circles

### How to Do Them

**Starting Position:**
1. Stand tall with feet hip-width apart
2. Place hands on hips
3. Engage core lightly

**The Movement:**

1. Shift weight to right leg
2. Lift left knee to hip height
3. Make a circle with left knee:
   - Out to the side
   - Back behind you
   - Around to starting position
4. Complete 5-10 circles
5. Reverse direction for 5-10 circles
6. Switch legs

### Key Points

- Keep standing leg stable
- Move from the hip joint
- Make smooth, controlled circles
- Don't rush
- Keep torso relatively still

---

## Lying Hip Circles

### How to Do Them

**Starting Position:**
1. Lie on your back
2. Bring one knee toward chest
3. Hold thigh with both hands (or let it float)

**The Movement:**

1. Make circles with your knee/thigh
2. Let the hip joint move through full range
3. 5-10 circles in each direction
4. Switch legs

### Benefits

- Gravity-eliminated position
- Easier to relax into movement
- Good for very tight hips
- Gentle option

---

## Quadruped Hip Circles

### How to Do Them

**Starting Position:**
1. Start on hands and knees
2. Hands under shoulders
3. Knees under hips

**The Movement:**

1. Lift right knee off ground
2. Draw circles with knee:
   - Forward
   - Out to side
   - Back
   - Return to center
3. 5-10 circles forward
4. 5-10 circles backward
5. Switch sides

### Benefits

- Core engaged throughout
- Good hip mobility work
- Targets multiple planes of motion
- Pregnancy-friendly

---

## Large Standing Circles

### For Greater Range

**How to:**
1. Stand on right leg (hold onto something if needed)
2. Extend left leg
3. Make large circles with entire leg
4. Forward, out, back, in
5. 5-10 each direction
6. Switch legs

### Key Points

- Control the movement
- Start smaller, progress larger
- Hold support if needed for balance

---

## What You Should Feel

### Good Sensations

- Gentle loosening in hip joint
- Warmth in hip area
- Increased freedom of movement
- Mild stretching sensation

### Red Flags—Stop If:

- Sharp pain in hip
- Clicking with pain
- Pinching sensation
- Groin pain

---

## Programming Hip Circles

### As Warm-Up

**Before lower body workout:**
- Standing hip circles: 10 each way, each leg
- Progress to leg swings and dynamic stretches

### Daily Mobility

**Morning or desk break:**
- Standing circles: 5-10 each way
- Takes only 2 minutes
- Great for counteracting sitting

### In Flexibility Routine

**Combine with:**
- Hip flexor stretches
- Pigeon pose
- 90/90 stretch
- Figure four stretch

---

## Progressions

### Beginner

- Small circles
- Hands on support
- Lying version

### Intermediate

- Larger circles
- No hand support
- Standing version with knee lift

### Advanced

- Large circles with extended leg
- Add resistance band
- Single-leg balance with circles

---

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Moving Too Fast

**Problem:** Rushing defeats the purpose
**Fix:** Slow, controlled movements

### 2. Moving the Whole Body

**Problem:** Torso moves instead of hip
**Fix:** Keep core engaged, isolate hip movement

### 3. Circles Too Small

**Problem:** Not achieving full range
**Fix:** Gradually increase size of circles

### 4. Holding Breath

**Problem:** Creates tension
**Fix:** Breathe naturally throughout

---

## Who Benefits Most

Hip circles are especially helpful for:
- Anyone who sits a lot
- Before exercise or sports
- People with stiff hips
- Those with lower back issues
- Pregnancy (on hands and knees)
- Anyone wanting to maintain hip health

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should I do hip circles?

Daily is great. They're gentle enough for everyday practice.

### My hip clicks—is that okay?

Painless clicking is common. If there's pain with the click, or it's new, get it checked.

### Can hip circles help with hip pain?

They may help with stiffness. If you have pain, get a diagnosis first.

### How long until I see improvement?

You'll feel looser immediately. Lasting mobility improvements come with consistent practice over weeks.
