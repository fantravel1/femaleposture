# Single Leg Balance: Stability Foundation

> **At a Glance:** Standing on one leg challenges your balance system and strengthens stabilizing muscles from ankle to hip.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Ankle stabilizers, hip stabilizers, core |
| **Best For** | Balance, fall prevention, ankle stability |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner to Advanced |
| **Equipment** | None |
| **Time** | 3-5 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Daily |

---

## How to Perform

### Basic Single Leg Stand

1. Stand near wall or chair for safety
2. Shift weight to one leg
3. Lift other foot slightly off floor
4. Hold for 30 seconds
5. Switch sides

### Form Tips

- Keep standing knee slightly soft
- Engage core gently
- Fix gaze on stationary point
- Relax shoulders

---

## Progressions

### Level 1: With Support
- Hold chair or wall lightly

### Level 2: No Support
- Arms at sides or on hips

### Level 3: Eyes Closed
- Significantly harder
- Stay near support

### Level 4: Unstable Surface
- Pillow or foam pad
- Eyes open first

### Level 5: Movement
- Arm circles while balancing
- Leg movements of lifted leg

---

## Benefits

- Improves proprioception
- Strengthens ankle stabilizers
- Reduces fall risk
- Builds hip stability
- Functional for daily life

---

## When to Practice

- While brushing teeth
- Waiting in line
- Part of warm-up
- Daily balance training
