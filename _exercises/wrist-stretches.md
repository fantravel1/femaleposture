# Wrist Stretches for Desk Workers

> **At a Glance:** Hours of keyboard and mouse use can tighten wrist and forearm muscles. These stretches help maintain mobility and comfort.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Wrist flexors, extensors, forearms |
| **Best For** | Computer users, desk workers, typing-related tightness |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | None |
| **Time** | 2-3 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Multiple times daily |

---

## Why Wrist Health Matters

### The Problem

Desk work leads to:
- Repetitive wrist positions
- Static muscle tension
- Reduced circulation
- Potential for overuse issues

### Prevention

Regular stretching:
- Maintains flexibility
- Improves circulation
- Prevents stiffness
- Supports long-term hand health

---

## Basic Wrist Stretches

### 1. Wrist Flexor Stretch

**Stretches front of forearm:**
1. Extend arm in front, palm up
2. Use other hand to gently bend wrist down
3. Fingers point toward floor
4. Feel stretch in inner forearm
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Switch hands

### 2. Wrist Extensor Stretch

**Stretches back of forearm:**
1. Extend arm in front, palm down
2. Use other hand to gently bend wrist down
3. Fingers point toward floor
4. Feel stretch on top of forearm
5. Hold 20-30 seconds
6. Switch hands

### 3. Prayer Stretch

**Both sides at once:**
1. Press palms together at chest
2. Fingers point up
3. Lower hands while keeping palms together
4. Feel stretch in inner wrists
5. Hold 20-30 seconds

### 4. Reverse Prayer

**Backs of hands together:**
1. Press backs of hands together
2. Fingers point down
3. Raise hands upward
4. Feel stretch in outer wrists
5. Hold 20-30 seconds

---

## Wrist Mobility

### Wrist Circles

**Full range of motion:**
1. Make gentle fists
2. Circle wrists slowly
3. 10 circles in each direction
4. Both wrists together or separately

### Finger Spreads

**Opens hand:**
1. Make tight fist
2. Spread fingers wide
3. Repeat 10 times
4. Promotes circulation

### Shake It Out

**Release tension:**
1. Let hands hang loose
2. Shake gently
3. 10-15 seconds
4. Relaxes muscles

---

## Quick Desk Routine

### Every Hour (1 minute)

1. Wrist circles — 5 each way
2. Flexor stretch — 15 sec each
3. Extensor stretch — 15 sec each
4. Finger spreads — 5 times

---

## What You Should Feel

### Good Sensations

- Gentle stretch
- Increased mobility
- Relief of tightness
- Better circulation

### Stop If You Feel

- Sharp pain
- Numbness or tingling
- Pain that persists
- Increased symptoms

---

## For Specific Issues

### Tight Forearms

Focus on:
- Flexor and extensor stretches
- Longer holds (30-45 seconds)
- More frequent breaks

### After Intense Typing

- Shake out hands
- Full stretch routine
- Brief rest from keyboard

---

## Posture Connection

### Wrists Affect Posture

- Poor wrist position affects arm position
- Arm position affects shoulders
- Shoulders affect neck
- Everything connects

### Optimal Typing Posture

- Wrists neutral (not bent up or down)
- Elbows at ~90 degrees
- Shoulders relaxed
- Keyboard at appropriate height

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should I stretch my wrists?

Every hour during intensive computer work. More if you notice tightness.

### Can these stretches prevent carpal tunnel?

They may help but aren't guaranteed prevention. See a professional if you have symptoms.

### Should stretches hurt?

No. You should feel gentle stretch, not pain. Ease up if anything hurts.
