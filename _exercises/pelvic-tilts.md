# Pelvic Tilts: Core Awareness and Lower Back Relief

> **At a Glance:** Pelvic tilts are a gentle, foundational exercise that builds awareness of pelvic position and can provide lower back relief. Often used in rehabilitation and posture training.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Core awareness, lower back, pelvic position |
| **Best For** | Back pain relief, core activation, posture awareness |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | None (mat optional) |
| **Time** | 2-5 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Daily |

---

## Why Pelvic Tilts Help

Pelvic tilts:
- Build awareness of pelvic position
- Gently mobilize lower spine
- Activate core muscles
- Can relieve lower back tension
- Teach neutral spine concept
- Foundation for other exercises

---

## How to Do Pelvic Tilts

### Starting Position

1. Lie on back
2. Knees bent, feet flat on floor
3. Arms at sides
4. Spine in natural position

### Posterior Pelvic Tilt

**Flattening lower back:**
1. Gently flatten lower back toward floor
2. Tailbone lifts slightly
3. Front of pelvis tilts up
4. Core engages naturally
5. Hold 5 seconds

### Anterior Pelvic Tilt

**Arching lower back:**
1. Gently arch lower back away from floor
2. Tailbone drops toward floor
3. Front of pelvis tilts down
4. Creates space under lower back
5. Hold 5 seconds

### The Exercise

1. Move between positions slowly
2. 10-15 repetitions
3. Find your neutral in between
4. Breathe throughout

---

## What You Should Feel

### Good Sensations

- Gentle movement in lower spine
- Core engagement in posterior tilt
- Awareness of pelvic position
- Lower back relaxation

### Red Flags—Stop If:

- Sharp pain
- Pain that radiates
- Discomfort that doesn't ease
- Any concerning symptoms

---

## Understanding Pelvic Position

### Anterior Tilt

- Front of pelvis drops forward
- Lower back arches
- Common posture pattern
- Often associated with tight hip flexors

### Posterior Tilt

- Front of pelvis lifts up
- Lower back flattens
- "Tucking" the pelvis
- Engages core muscles

### Neutral

- Between the two extremes
- Natural curve in lower back
- Balanced pelvic position
- Goal for most activities

---

## Variations

### Supine (Lying Down)

Standard version as described above.
- Most accessible
- Best for learning
- Good for back pain

### Seated Pelvic Tilts

1. Sit on chair, feet flat
2. Tilt pelvis forward (arch back)
3. Tilt pelvis back (round back)
4. Find neutral
5. Great for desk workers

### Standing Pelvic Tilts

1. Stand with slight knee bend
2. Tilt pelvis (as in lying version)
3. More challenging to feel
4. Applies to real posture

### Quadruped (Hands and Knees)

1. Start on hands and knees
2. Cat-cow is essentially pelvic tilts
3. Cat = posterior tilt
4. Cow = anterior tilt

## When to Use Pelvic Tilts

### For Back Pain Relief

- Gentle movement can ease tension
- Start of many back pain routines
- Safe for most people
- Check with provider if unsure

### For Posture Training

- Learn pelvic position
- Understand neutral spine
- Build body awareness
- Foundation for other exercises

### During Pregnancy

- Generally safe
- Can relieve back discomfort
- Check with provider
- Listen to body

### Post-Partum

- Gentle core reactivation
- Can start early
- Part of recovery process

## Programming

### Quick Practice

- 10-15 tilts
- Takes 1-2 minutes
- Morning or evening

### Part of Routine

- Include with bridges and other core work
- Warm-up for exercise
- Daily is fine

### For Back Pain

- Several times daily
- Gentle and controlled
- Part of movement routine
- Combine with other exercises

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Moving Too Fast

**Problem:** Rushing through movement
**Fix:** Slow, controlled motion

### 2. Holding Breath

**Problem:** Not breathing
**Fix:** Breathe continuously

### 3. Too Much Movement

**Problem:** Over-exaggerating range
**Fix:** Gentle, small movements

### 4. Tensing Up

**Problem:** Whole body tenses
**Fix:** Only work the relevant muscles

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are pelvic tilts safe for everyone?

Generally yes—they're very gentle. Stop if painful and consult provider for specific conditions.

### How often should I do pelvic tilts?

Daily is fine and often helpful. They're gentle enough for frequent practice.

### Will pelvic tilts strengthen my core?

They're more about awareness and activation than strength. They prepare you for strengthening exercises.

### Should I stay in posterior tilt for posture?

No. Neutral spine is the goal. Excessive posterior tilt causes other problems. Pelvic tilts teach you to find neutral.
