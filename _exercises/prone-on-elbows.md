# Prone on Elbows: Gentle Extension Position

> **At a Glance:** Lying face down on your elbows is a gentle way to extend your spine. It's the same position as sphinx pose in yogaâ€”accessible and effective.

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Spine extension, front body opening |
| **Best For** | Counteracting sitting, gentle back work |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | Mat or soft surface |
| **Time** | 1-5 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Daily |

---

## Why This Position Helps

### Benefits

- Gently extends the spine
- Counteracts flexed sitting position
- Opens the chest
- Passive and relaxing
- Can be held longer than active positions

### Ideal For

- Desk workers
- After prolonged sitting
- Gentle back care
- Reading or relaxing productively

---

## How to Do It

### Setup

1. Lie face down on comfortable surface
2. Place elbows under shoulders
3. Forearms flat on floor, parallel
4. Let lower body relax

### Position

- Elbows directly under shoulders
- Forearms support upper body
- Hips and legs remain on floor
- Head neutral or looking forward

### Holding

- Breathe normally
- Let body relax into position
- Hold 1-5 minutes
- Come down when fatigued

---

## Key Points

### Form Cues

- Shoulders away from ears
- Don't shrug up
- Breathe into chest
- Relax as much as possible

### Common Errors

- Elbows too far forward
- Shoulders hunched
- Holding breath
- Straining to stay up

---

## Who Should Be Careful

### May Not Be Suitable If

- Lower back pain increases in this position
- History of disc problems (check with provider)
- Unable to lie face down
- Pregnancy (later stages)

### Modifications

- Lower: Prop only on forearms, chest lower
- With pillow under chest
- Brief holds only

---

## When to Use

### Good Times

- After sitting
- Morning upon waking (gentle)
- Evening relaxation
- While reading or watching something
- As rest position between exercises

### Duration

- Start with 30-60 seconds
- Build to several minutes
- Come down if uncomfortable

---

## Breathing in This Position

### Use the Opportunity

- Chest is open
- Practice deep breathing
- Feel ribcage expand
- Relaxing breaths

---

## Progression

### From Prone on Elbows

You can progress to:
- Press-ups (McKenzie extension)
- Cobra pose (hands pushing up)
- More active back exercises
