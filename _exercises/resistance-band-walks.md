# Resistance Band Walks: Hip Strengthening

> **At a Glance:** Walking against band resistance activates hip muscles that are often weak from prolonged sitting.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Gluteus medius, hip abductors, external rotators |
| **Best For** | Hip stability, knee alignment, glute activation |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Equipment** | Resistance band (loop) |
| **Time** | 5 minutes |
| **Frequency** | 3-4 times per week |

---

## How to Perform

### Lateral Band Walk

1. Place band around ankles or above knees
2. Stand with feet hip-width apart
3. Quarter squat position
4. Step sideways, maintaining tension
5. 10-15 steps each direction

### Monster Walk

1. Band around ankles
2. Slight squat position
3. Walk forward diagonally
4. Alternate legs with wide steps
5. 20 steps forward, 20 back

---

## Band Placement Options

- **Above knees:** Easier, targets glute med
- **Below knees:** Moderate difficulty
- **Around ankles:** Most challenging

---

## Benefits

- Activates dormant glute muscles
- Improves hip stability
- Better knee alignment
- Prevents knee valgus
- Great warm-up for exercise

---

## Common Mistakes

- Band too loose (no resistance)
- Feet coming too close together
- Leaning trunk to compensate
- Rushing the movement
