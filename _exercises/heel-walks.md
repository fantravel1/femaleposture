# Heel Walks: Shin Strengthening

> **At a Glance:** Walking on your heels strengthens the tibialis anterior muscle, important for foot clearance during walking.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Tibialis anterior (front of shin) |
| **Best For** | Shin strength, ankle dorsiflexion |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | None |
| **Time** | 2-3 minutes |
| **Frequency** | 3-4 times per week |

---

## How to Perform

1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Lift toes off floor, standing on heels only
3. Walk forward 20-30 steps
4. Rest and repeat 2-3 times

### Form Tips

- Keep good upright posture
- Take small, controlled steps
- May feel burn in front of shins

---

## Variations

### Toe Walks (Opposite)

**For calf strength:**
1. Walk on tiptoes only
2. 20-30 steps
3. Pair with heel walks

### Sideways Heel Walk

**Lateral challenge:**
1. Walk sideways on heels
2. 10 steps each direction

---

## Benefits

- Prevents shin splints
- Improves ankle control
- Better foot clearance when walking
- Balances calf-dominant legs
