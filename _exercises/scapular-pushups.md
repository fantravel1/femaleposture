# Scapular Push-Ups: Shoulder Blade Strength

> **At a Glance:** Scapular push-ups isolate the serratus anterior muscle, improving shoulder blade control and stability. Essential for addressing rounded shoulders.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Serratus anterior, shoulder stabilizers |
| **Best For** | Shoulder blade control, rounded shoulders, upper body posture |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | None (or wall for easier version) |
| **Time** | 3-5 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Daily |

---

## Why This Exercise Helps Posture

The serratus anterior:
- Holds shoulder blades flat against ribcage
- Prevents "winging" of shoulder blades
- Essential for overhead arm movement
- Works with other muscles for good posture
- Often weak in people with rounded shoulders

---

## Basic Scapular Push-Up

### Setup

1. Start in push-up position (hands under shoulders)
2. Arms straight
3. Body in straight line
4. Can modify on knees if needed

### The Movement

1. **Keep arms straight** throughout
2. Let shoulder blades squeeze together (sag between them)
3. Push through hands to spread shoulder blades apart
4. Feel the rounding of your upper back
5. Control back to start
6. Repeat 10-15 times

### Key Points

- Arms stay locked straight
- Movement only happens at shoulder blades
- Push the floor away at the top
- Control both directions

---

## Progressions

### Wall Scapular Push-Up (Easiest)

**Setup:**
1. Face wall, arms length away
2. Hands on wall at shoulder height
3. Keep body straight

**Movement:**
1. Let shoulder blades squeeze together (body moves toward wall)
2. Push away, spreading shoulder blades
3. 15-20 reps
4. Great starting point

### Kneeling Scapular Push-Up

**Modified floor version:**
1. Push-up position on knees
2. Same movement pattern
3. Easier on core
4. 12-15 reps

### Standard Scapular Push-Up

On toes, as described above.

### Elevated Scapular Push-Up

**Feet elevated for more challenge:**
1. Feet on step or bench
2. Hands on floor
3. Same movement
4. 10-12 reps

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Bending Elbows

**Problem:** Turns into regular push-up
**Fix:** Lock elbows completely

### 2. Moving Too Fast

**Problem:** Misses the muscle activation
**Fix:** Slow, controlled movement

### 3. Not Enough Range

**Problem:** Barely moves shoulder blades
**Fix:** Full squeeze together, full spread apart

### 4. Holding Breath

**Problem:** Creates tension
**Fix:** Breathe throughout

---

## Programming

### For Posture Improvement

- 2-3 sets of 10-15 reps
- Daily or every other day
- Include in warm-up or posture routine

### Pre-Workout

- 1-2 sets of 10 reps
- Activates serratus anterior
- Good before pushing exercises

### Combined with Other Exercises

Pair with:
- Wall angels
- Band pull-aparts
- Rows
