# Shoulder Circles: Simple Mobility Exercise

> **At a Glance:** Shoulder circles are a simple but effective way to maintain shoulder mobility and release tension. Perfect for desk breaks or warm-ups.

---

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Targets** | Shoulder joint mobility, upper trapezius |
| **Best For** | Tension relief, warm-up, desk workers |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Equipment** | None |
| **Time** | 1-2 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Multiple times daily |

---

## Why Shoulder Circles Help

### Benefits

- Release accumulated tension
- Improve joint lubrication
- Prepare shoulders for activity
- Break up static positions
- Maintain healthy range of motion

### Who Benefits Most

- Desk workers
- Anyone who holds tension in shoulders
- Before exercise
- Those with stiff shoulders

---

## Basic Shoulder Circles

### Setup

1. Stand or sit tall
2. Arms relaxed at sides
3. Shoulders down, away from ears

### The Movement

**Forward circles:**
1. Roll shoulders up toward ears
2. Continue forward and down
3. Complete full circles
4. 10-15 repetitions
5. Slow, controlled movement

**Backward circles:**
1. Roll shoulders up toward ears
2. Continue backward and down
3. Complete full circles
4. 10-15 repetitions
5. Focus on squeezing shoulder blades together at back

### Key Points

- Full range of motion
- Slow and controlled
- Breathe normally
- Don't rush

---

## Variations

### Large Arm Circles

**More range of motion:**
1. Arms extended to sides
2. Make large circles with entire arms
3. 10 forward, 10 backward
4. Keep core engaged

### Single Arm Circles

**Isolate each side:**
1. Circle one shoulder at a time
2. 10 each direction
3. Switch sides
4. Notice differences between sides

### Weighted Circles

**Light resistance:**
1. Hold light weights (1-3 lbs)
2. Smaller circles
3. 10 each direction
4. Adds challenge

## When to Do Shoulder Circles

### Perfect Times

- Morning wake-up
- Every hour during desk work
- Before exercise
- During work breaks
- When shoulders feel tense
- After phone or computer use

### As Part of Routine

- Include in warm-up
- Add to desk stretch routine
- Use before upper body exercises

---

## What You Should Feel

### Good Sensations

- Gentle movement through range
- Tension releasing
- Shoulders loosening
- Slight stretch

### Stop If You Experience

- Pain (not just tightness)
- Clicking with pain
- Sharp sensations
- Numbness

---

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Moving Too Fast

**Problem:** Misses mobility benefit
**Fix:** Slow, deliberate circles

### 2. Small Range

**Problem:** Limited benefit
**Fix:** Full circles through complete range

### 3. Holding Breath

**Problem:** Increases tension
**Fix:** Breathe naturally throughout

### 4. Tensing Neck

**Problem:** Adds tension instead of releasing
**Fix:** Keep neck relaxed

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should I do these?

Multiple times daily is fine and beneficial, especially during desk work.

### Are shoulder circles a good warm-up?

Yes, they prepare the shoulder joint for activity. Include before exercise.

### My shoulders make clicking sounds—is that okay?

Painless clicking is often normal. If clicking is accompanied by pain, consult a professional.

### Can these replace other shoulder exercises?

No—shoulder circles are for mobility and tension relief. Still include stretching and strengthening.
